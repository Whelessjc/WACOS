# Line Check Cards

Line checks are short oral recall and scenario questions.

They are not written quizzes.

Use them while students are standing, setting up, waiting for a demo, or resetting.

Good line checks build:

- recall
- sequencing
- safety
- service awareness
- decision-making under pressure
- calm speech

## How to Use

Use 3-5 cards at a time.

Ask one student, then another. Let students answer briefly. Correct quickly and move on.

If a card exposes a class-wide gap, reteach it in the lab.

## Scenario Cards

| Card | Scenario | Expected Move |
| --- | --- | --- |
| SC1 | Your sauce is too thick and service starts in five minutes. | lower heat, loosen with appropriate liquid, taste again |
| SC2 | Your group forgot to start rice. | communicate, adjust menu or timing, ask for support |
| SC3 | Chicken temp reads low but the outside is dark. | reduce heat, finish gently, verify temp |
| SC4 | You see unlabeled product in the cooler. | tell teacher, do not use until identified |
| SC5 | A teammate is rushing with a knife. | call it out calmly, slow the station |
| SC6 | Your station is clean but dish area is buried. | support dish flow if your work is complete |
| SC7 | A guest-facing item does not taste right. | stop, taste with teacher, adjust before service |
| SC8 | You are out of a prep item during service. | call it early, ask for backup, adjust honestly |
