# Assessment and Rubrics

Assessment in Culinary 2 should support practical kitchen performance.

It should not become paperwork-heavy.

## Assessment Philosophy

Students are evaluated mostly through:

- execution
- timing
- cleanliness
- communication
- organization
- follow-through
- composure under pressure

Short written checks are fine when they connect directly to kitchen application.

The kitchen is the main evidence.

## Evidence Sources

Use a mix of:

- teacher observation
- station checks
- practical performance tasks
- Bistro service evidence
- line checks
- marked recipes
- short scaling tasks
- brief reflections after service or practicals

## Simple Performance Scale

Use this scale for most practical work.

| Score | Meaning | Description |
| ---: | --- | --- |
| 4 | Reliable | Works cleanly, communicates, adjusts, and needs little prompting |
| 3 | Functional | Completes the task safely with some reminders or uneven timing |
| 2 | Developing | Needs repeated prompting; process breaks down under pressure |
| 1 | Not yet safe or useful | Does not follow directions, ignores safety, or leaves work incomplete |

## Daily Lab Rubric

| Category | 4 | 3 | 2 | 1 |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Preparation | Recipe read, station set, plan clear | Mostly ready | Starts without full plan | Unprepared |
| Technique | Controlled and purposeful | Mostly correct | Inconsistent | Unsafe or careless |
| Cleanliness | Cleans throughout | Cleans at major points | Waits until end | Leaves mess or ignores sanitation |
| Communication | Clear and timely | Communicates when prompted | Late or unclear | Silent, defensive, or disruptive |
| Follow-through | Food, station, and reset complete | Minor misses | Incomplete pieces | Does not finish |

## Bistro Service Rubric

| Category | Reliable | Functional | Developing | Not Yet |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Station readiness | Set before service, product checked | Mostly ready | Needs teacher push | Not ready |
| Communication | Short, clear, useful | Responds when called | Delayed or unclear | No useful communication |
| Timing | Keeps pace and communicates delays | Usually on pace | Falls behind often | Breaks service flow |
| Product quality | Taste, temp, portion, presentation controlled | Minor inconsistency | Frequent quality issues | Unsafe or unusable |
| Recovery | Names problems and keeps moving | Recovers with help | Shuts down or hides errors | Makes problem worse |
| Reset | Station fully reset | Small misses | Incomplete reset | Leaves station for others |

## Practical Check Options

Use practical checks as short performance snapshots.

Examples:

- knife cut check with station reset
- sauteed vegetable check
- rice or grain timing check
- protein doneness check
- sauce texture and seasoning check
- soup structure check
- Bistro station readiness check
- recipe scaling and mise list check

## Grading Guidance

Keep grades tied to performance, not compliance theater.

Good gradebook categories:

- labs and production
- Bistro service
- practical checks
- line checks and short applied checks
- professionalism and station habits

Avoid grades based mostly on:

- copied notes
- long packets
- decorative projects
- generic employability worksheets

## Feedback Language

Use direct, specific feedback.

Examples:

- Your station was clean, but your timing collapsed because you did not read the full method.
- Your chicken was safe and rested correctly. Next target is stronger browning.
- You communicated the delay early. That helped the station recover.
- You finished the food but left the next crew a dirty station. That is incomplete work.
- You rushed the knife work. Slow down until the cuts are controlled.

## Reflection

Reflection should be brief.

Useful prompts:

- What worked?
- What broke down?
- What did you adjust?
- What would you do differently next time?
- What does your station need from you next service?
