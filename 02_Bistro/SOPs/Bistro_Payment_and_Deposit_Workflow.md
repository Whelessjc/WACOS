# Bistro Payment and Deposit Workflow

Current as of July 22, 2026

## Purpose

Document the instructor workflow for recording Bistro payments, producing kitchen tickets, reconciling service records, and assembling the deposit packet. This SOP records current program practice and does not replace district or bookkeeper requirements.

## Accepted Payment and Redemption Types

| Type | Treatment |
|---|---|
| Cash | Accepted monetary payment; record in Square as cash. |
| Check | Accepted monetary payment; checks must be payable to **WAHS** and recorded in Square as check. |
| Approved PBIS incentive | Noncash meal redemption; record as a comp using the `PBIS Incentive` reason. |
| Bistro coin | Noncash meal redemption for one meal; record as a comp using the `Bistro Coin` reason. |

The Bistro does not accept card payments under the current instructor-confirmed workflow. Square is the sales-record and kitchen-ticket system; Square does not process the cash or checks.

## PBIS and Bistro Coin Setup

Bistro coins are wooden tokens with the West Ashley Culinary logo. They are issued through classroom-management PBIS and may be redeemed for one Bistro meal. Other approved PBIS incentives may also cover a meal.

In Square:

1. Create separate comp reasons named `PBIS Incentive` and `Bistro Coin`.
2. Ring the actual meal items so the kitchen receives an accurate ticket.
3. Send the order to the kitchen through the normal service flow.
4. Comp the item or entire ticket using the correct reason.
5. Close the ticket at a $0 balance.

Do not record these redemptions as `Other payment`, gift card, cash, or check. They are comps, not collected revenue.

Square includes comped items in gross item activity because gross sales are calculated before discounts and comps. Use net sales and payment-method totals for deposit reconciliation, and use the separate comp report to review PBIS and Bistro coin activity.

## During Service

- Enter every order in Square before or as it is sent to the kitchen.
- Select the payment type actually received: cash or check.
- Confirm checks are payable to WAHS.
- Use the correct comp reason for PBIS incentives and Bistro coins.
- Keep cash, checks, and redemption tokens controlled during service.
- Correct entry errors using the approved Square correction method; do not disguise errors as PBIS or Bistro coin comps.

## If Square Is Unavailable

Use the backup receipt system so the kitchen receives clear order information and the instructor retains a numbered or otherwise traceable sales record.

- Record each order, amount, and payment type.
- Mark PBIS incentives and Bistro coins as noncash redemptions.
- Retain all backup receipts.
- When Square becomes available, reconcile the backup receipts to cash, checks, and redemptions.
- Follow the instructor's current recovery method for any needed Square entry without creating duplicate revenue.

The exact Square re-entry method after an outage remains an instructor-controlled implementation choice because it depends on how Square reports delayed entries.

## Service Close

1. Close all Square tickets.
2. Confirm cash, check, PBIS, and Bistro coin entries use the correct type.
3. Count and secure cash and checks.
4. Confirm checks are payable to WAHS.
5. Download the detailed Square sales transactions report.
6. Review net sales and payment-method totals against cash and checks received.
7. Review the comp report for PBIS Incentive and Bistro Coin usage.
8. Reconcile any backup receipts used during an outage.
9. Complete the required bookkeeper and deposit forms.
10. Attach the detailed Square transactions report and required supporting records to the deposit.

## Deposit Timing

- Complete the deposit on Friday when time permits.
- If Friday completion is not practical, secure the funds and complete the deposit Monday.
- Follow current district and bookkeeper requirements for custody, signatures, storage, delivery, and record retention.

## Control Boundaries

This workflow does not independently establish:

- Refund or guest-comp authorization.
- Who must witness or verify a count.
- Cash-drawer starting amounts or till variance rules.
- District records-retention requirements.
- The final Square recovery method after an outage.

Use current district and bookkeeper instructions where they govern. Record future confirmed decisions through the [WACOS Update Workflow](../../00_Core/WACOS_Update_Workflow.md).
