# Leadership and Student Roles

## Purpose
Define student responsibility in daily labs, Bistro service, cleanup, and advanced work.

## Leadership Principle
Leadership should emerge through competence. Culinary 2 should feel like an honor and a higher-responsibility space, but not an elitist club and not a generic leadership camp.

## Role Framework
| Role | Duties |
|---|---|
| Station lead | Checks mise en place, assigns station tasks, watches cleanliness, reports blockers |
| Bistro manager | Tracks overall prep/service status and communicates with instructor |
| Expeditor | Coordinates timing, quality, and handoff |
| Inventory assistant | Notes shortages, labels, substitutions, and storage concerns |
| Sanitation lead | Monitors sanitizer, dish flow, trash, towels, closing checklist |
| FOH lead | Supports greeting, seating/order flow, guest recovery, table reset |
| Steward/dish lead | Runs dish system, stocks clean wares, protects flow |
| Peer mentor | Helps newer or weaker students without doing the work for them |

## Authority Boundaries
Student leaders may coordinate, remind, report, and model. They may not shame peers, approve unsafe equipment use, change grades, override instructor directions, or handle conflicts beyond their role.

## Leadership Evidence
- Arrives prepared.
- Sees work without being told.
- Communicates calmly.
- Helps the station recover.
- Protects safety and sanitation.
- Leaves clear notes.
- Makes others better without taking over.
