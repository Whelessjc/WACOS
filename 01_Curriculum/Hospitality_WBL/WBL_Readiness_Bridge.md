# Hospitality Work-Based Learning Readiness Bridge

## Why This Document Exists

`Hospitality_WBL/` has been referenced in `Curriculum_Index.md` and in the repository refactor changelog, but no file has actually existed in it until now. This document does not invent Work-Based Learning policy - hour tracking, partner agreements, on-campus vs. off-campus rules, and evaluation forms remain open (see D-numbered items in `DECISION_REGISTER.md` and the WBL line in `TODO.md`).

What this document does is answer a narrower, already-answerable question: **what does "ready for Hospitality WBL" actually mean coming out of Culinary 2?** That question is answerable now because Culinary 2 already has the evidence built in - the CKC Evidence Checklist and the tasting menu capstone. This document just connects them to the readiness question directly, the way Unit 10's build-out notes call for.

## The Readiness Question

The Master Framework's north star for Culinary 2 is: *can this student be dropped into a basic kitchen and be useful without supervision?*

For WBL specifically, that question needs one addition: *...and can they represent the program well in front of a guest, supervisor, or customer they don't already know?* Bistro service answers the first half. The tasting menu capstone - designing a dish, costing it, and describing it to a real guest - answers the second half.

A student who is CKC-ready but has never had to explain their own work to a stranger is not the same as a student who is WBL-ready. Culinary 2 already builds both; this document just says so explicitly.

## Readiness Evidence Map

This maps existing Culinary 2 evidence (nothing new required) onto the categories a workplace supervisor would actually care about.

| Readiness Category | What It Looks Like | Where the Evidence Already Exists |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Safety and reliability | Follows food safety and equipment rules without being watched | CKC Evidence Checklist; Weeks 1-9 embedded CKC work |
| Independence | Can run a station or a task start to finish with minimal prompting | Service Repetition phase, Weeks 26-28 |
| Communication | Reports problems early, asks for help, doesn't hide mistakes | Bistro service language (`04_Bistro_Integration.md`); Week 27 |
| Guest-facing hospitality | Can describe their own work to someone unfamiliar, without a script | Tasting menu Week 33 - describing a dish's "chef's message" to a real guest |
| Composure under pressure | Recovers from something going wrong live, in front of people | Tasting menu Week 33 execution; Bistro service recovery protocol |
| Ownership of a real outcome | Has planned, executed, and taken responsibility for one thing start to finish, not just followed instructions | Tasting menu unit as a whole (Weeks 29-33) |

If a student has real, specific evidence in all six rows, they are ready for a WBL placement conversation. If they're missing one or two, that's a concrete, nameable gap - not a vague "needs more experience."

## WBL Readiness Reflection (Student-Facing Draft)

A short form, filled out after the capstone, not a new assignment - it should take five minutes because the answers already exist in what the student just did.

```
Name:
Capstone course/role:

1. Describe one moment this year where you had to be reliable without anyone checking on you.

2. Describe one moment where something went wrong and you had to recover in front of someone.

3. Describe one moment where you had to explain your own work to someone who didn't already know you.

4. What is one thing about working in a real kitchen or hospitality setting that you feel ready for?

5. What is one thing you'd still want support with in a work-based learning placement?
```

Questions 1-3 map directly to the Readiness Evidence Map above. Question 5 is the honest gap-finder - it should inform what kind of WBL placement or supervision a student needs, not be treated as a disqualifier.

## What This Document Does Not Cover

- Legal/district requirements for minors in WBL placements

These are tracked in `TODO.md` ("Work-Based Learning forms and hour tracking") and should be built as separate, instructor-confirmed documents - this bridge only connects existing Culinary 2 evidence to the readiness question, and should be cited by those future documents rather than replacing the need for them.
