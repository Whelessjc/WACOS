# Semester 2 Module Map

Semester 2 should be flexible.

The point is not to march through a list of recipes. The point is to deepen precision, service readiness, timing, and independent follow-through.

## Semester 2 Priorities

- stronger Bistro execution
- more student-led prep
- more precise baking and pastry habits
- better timing across multiple components
- better recovery when something goes wrong
- more consistent station reset
- practical capstone evidence

## Suggested Semester 2 Flow

| Phase | Approx. Timing | Focus | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Reset and Rebuild | Weeks 19-20 | Re-entry, kitchen habits, Bistro reset | Rebuild routines after break |
| Baking and Pastry Foundations | Weeks 21-26 | Scaling, grams, mixing methods, doughs, custards | Keep products useful for Bistro when possible |
| Service Repetition | Weeks 27-31 | Bistro systems, menu support, production scaling | Students should carry more responsibility |
| Composed Application | Weeks 32-34 | Plates, production items, or station leadership | Choose based on class strength |
| Capstone and Final Readiness | Weeks 35-36 | Practical execution under pressure | Keep reflection short and tied to performance |

## Baking and Pastry Foundation Options

Choose based on equipment, calendar, and Bistro need.

| Skill | Possible Product | Performance Target |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Scaling by weight | Muffins, quick breads | Students weigh accurately and follow method |
| Mixing method | Biscuits, muffins, cakes | Students can explain overmixing and texture |
| Yeast fermentation | Focaccia, rolls, baguette-style bread | Students manage time, proofing, and bake cues |
| Custards | Creme anglaise, pastry cream, baked custard | Students control heat without scrambling |
| Frozen dessert base | Ice cream or sorbet | Students understand base, chilling, texture |
| Plated dessert | Custard, sauce, garnish | Students balance flavor, texture, timing |

## Bistro Application Options

Semester 2 Bistro should ask students to do more of the thinking.

Possible student responsibilities:

- write station prep lists
- check labels and storage
- call missing items before service
- run a short pre-service check
- track par levels
- taste and adjust soup or sauce
- monitor holding quality
- communicate delays clearly
- reset station for the next crew

## Composed Application Options

Choose one or two. Do not try to do all of them.

- composed protein plate
- soup and bread service
- plated dessert
- Bistro special
- small tasting menu component
- scaled production item
- station leadership rotation

## Capstone Options

The capstone should prove readiness, not create decorative paperwork.

Good options:

- lead a Bistro station through prep and service
- produce one dish scaled for service
- execute a composed plate under time limits
- plan and produce one menu component for a real event
- complete a practical kitchen readiness check

Required evidence:

- clean station
- readable prep list
- safe handling
- accurate execution
- tasting and adjustment
- communication during pressure
- full reset
- short reflection

## Semester 2 Flex Rules

When time gets tight:

- Keep Bistro prep and service.
- Keep one baking precision module.
- Keep one student-led service responsibility.
- Keep one final practical performance check.
- Cut extra products before cutting reps.
