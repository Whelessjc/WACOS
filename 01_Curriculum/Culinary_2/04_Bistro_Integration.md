# Bistro Integration Guide

Bistro is not an interruption to Culinary 2.

Bistro is applied kitchen training.

## Teams

Bistro is organized into four teams: **Dining, Kitchen, Support, and Barista.** Culinary 2 students already know these roles from Culinary 1 - use the same names consistently rather than reintroducing new ones.

## Purpose

Bistro gives students real practice with:

- prep continuity
- station ownership
- communication
- timing
- cleanliness under pressure
- guest awareness
- service recovery
- finishing what they start

## Timing

Bistro begins around Week 4.

Weeks 4-5 are Bistro systems and training weeks.

During Weeks 4-5:

- keep volume controlled
- use mock service when needed
- limit menu complexity
- teach ticket flow
- teach station language
- teach labeling and prep continuity
- debrief service without turning it into a lecture

## Weekly Bistro Rhythm

| Day | Purpose |
| --- | --- |
| Monday | Introduce or refine skills that support the week |
| Tuesday | Line checks, planning, short lab, or Hospitality Scholars |
| Wednesday | Longer lab or production repetition |
| Thursday | Bistro prep |
| Friday | Bistro service |

## Thursday Prep Structure

Thursday is not a loose prep day.

Students should begin with:

- posted menu or prep target
- station assignments
- printed prep lists
- labeling expectations
- storage plan
- dish and sanitation plan
- time check

Teacher checkpoints:

1. Station set.
2. Prep list understood.
3. Raw protein or allergen risks addressed.
4. First product checked before batch continues.
5. Labels correct.
6. Station reset before dismissal.

## Friday Service Structure

Before service:

- uniform check
- handwashing
- station setup
- towels and sanitizer
- menu review
- par check
- tasting check
- service language review

During service:

- call tickets clearly
- answer calls clearly
- communicate delays early
- keep station clean
- protect product quality
- do not abandon dish flow

After service:

- cool and label safe leftovers
- discard what should not be held
- clean equipment
- sweep and mop
- return dishes and tools
- reset stations
- brief debrief

## Bistro Roles

Common roles:

- expo or caller
- hot station
- cold station
- prep support
- dish and sanitation
- runner or service support
- quality control

Students should rotate. Strong students should not always get the visible work. Struggling students should still be taught station ownership.

## Service Language

Use short, consistent language.

Examples:

- Heard.
- Working.
- Two minutes.
- I need help on this.
- I am not ready yet.
- Holding at temp.
- Needs seasoning.
- I need a re-fire.
- Behind.
- Corner.
- Sharp.
- Hot.

## Service Recovery

When something goes wrong in the kitchen or at a station, students should learn to:

1. Say it early.
2. Name the problem.
3. Protect safety.
4. Protect the guest.
5. Ask for or offer help.
6. Reset and keep moving.

Do not let students hide mistakes until the ticket fails.

## Guest Complaint Recovery (HEARD)

Service Recovery above is for internal kitchen/station problems. When a guest brings a complaint directly, students use HEARD instead:

- **Hear** - listen to the guest's concern without interrupting.
- **Empathize** - show genuine understanding for how the guest feels.
- **Apologize** - offer a real apology, even if the issue wasn't the student's fault.
- **Resolve** - take a concrete step to fix the issue.
- **Diagnose** - identify what caused it so it doesn't happen again.

Involve the instructor for anything beyond a simple fix (comps, refunds, anything the student isn't authorized to decide alone).

## What to Assess During Bistro

Assess:

- readiness before service
- station cleanliness
- communication
- timing
- food quality
- safe handling
- recovery from mistakes
- reset after service

Avoid turning Bistro into paperwork. A short teacher observation note is enough.

## Bistro Debrief

Keep debriefs short.

Ask:

- What worked?
- What slowed us down?
- What was one quality issue?
- What communication helped?
- What has to change next service?
