# CKC Alignment

Credential focus: Certified Kitchen Cook.

The course may support credential preparation, but the kitchen remains the primary classroom.

Do not turn CKC into test prep first. Teach the kitchen well. Let the credential document the learning.

## Placement

Target window:

- embedded work Weeks 1-9
- practical push around Week 9
- formal CKC attempt around Week 9 if reasonable

If the class is not ready, keep the Week 9 practical push and move the test window.

## CKC-Aligned Skill Areas

| CKC Area | Where It Lives in Culinary 2 |
| --- | --- |
| Safety and sanitation | Daily station setup, raw protein labs, Bistro service |
| Equipment identification | Daily mise, station checks, line checks |
| Knife skills | Weeks 1-3, repeated all year |
| Recipe reading | Every lab, especially scaling and prep lists |
| Kitchen math | Week 9, production scaling, Bistro par levels |
| Cooking methods | Saute, roast, simmer, braise, fry, bake |
| Temperatures | Protein labs, holding, cooling, reheating |
| Communication | Bistro systems, service calls, group roles |
| Workflow | Mise, timing, clean-as-you-go, station reset |
| Professional readiness | Black coat expectations, Hospitality Scholars, Bistro |

## Embedded CKC Routine

Use small routines instead of long test-prep blocks:

- 3-minute line checks
- station setup checks
- recipe scaling warmups
- thermometer drills
- equipment call-outs
- safety scenario questions
- short practical demonstrations

## Week 9 CKC Practical Push

Suggested structure:

| Day | CKC Focus | Evidence |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Monday | Recipe reading and scaling | Marked recipe, scaled yield, mise list |
| Tuesday | Oral recall and safety | Line check responses |
| Wednesday | Practical station check | Knife, sanitation (hand sink/3-comp sink setup, sanitizer ppm, thermometer calibration and probe use, labeling/FIFO - named specifically, not just observed generally), cooking method, tasting, reset |
| Thursday | Bistro prep | Embedded professional standards |
| Friday | Bistro service | Communication, timing, safe service |

## CKC Evidence Checklist

A student is ready when they can consistently:

- arrive in uniform and begin without being chased
- set a station correctly
- read the recipe before starting
- identify tools and equipment needed
- scale simple quantities
- use a knife safely
- prevent cross-contamination
- use a thermometer correctly
- control basic heat
- taste and adjust
- communicate clearly
- clean and reset

## CKC Without Worksheets

Acceptable written work:

- marked recipe
- short scaling problem
- brief safety scenario response
- simple exit ticket tied to that day's lab

Avoid:

- long packets
- generic career readiness worksheets
- vocabulary lists disconnected from cooking
- copying definitions that students cannot apply in the lab

## Line Check Examples for CKC

- What is the danger zone?
- How do you check chicken temperature correctly?
- What is the first thing you do before scaling a recipe?
- What information goes on a label?
- What is cross-contamination?
- What do you do if sanitizer is missing from your station?
- What does carryover cooking mean?
- What is the difference between cleaning and sanitizing?
- What is your next move if your station is falling behind?
