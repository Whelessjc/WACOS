# West Ashley 25 Technique Map

This list is a set of dish vehicles, not a separate unit.

The West Ashley 25 is not a checklist.

Students do not need to cook every dish every year.

The list is a curriculum compass. It helps connect technique, foodways, hospitality, and community.

Use these dishes where they help students practice the technique already being taught. Do not organize the course around "Lowcountry week" or "international week." Place and culture should show up naturally through the food students cook, taste, discuss, and serve.

The goal is simple:

Students should leave Culinary Arts Management 2 with a stronger understanding of how cooking connects to where they live.

## How to Use the Map

For each dish, ask:

- What skill does this teach?
- Can it fit the class block or Bistro prep/service rhythm?
- What can students repeat?
- What quality cue can students see, taste, or feel?
- What local connection is worth naming briefly?

Keep the place note short. One or two sentences is enough.

## Additional Regional, Community, and Student-Favorite Vehicles

These are living foods.

They may not all be historically significant, but they are culturally significant to students and community.

These are not lesser dishes. They are useful because students care about them and because they teach real skills.

| Dish Vehicle | Best Technique Fit | Best Timing | Why It Belongs |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Macaroni and Cheese | roux, bechamel, pasta timing, sauce hold | Weeks 11-12 | High-interest vehicle for sauce structure |
| Banana Pudding | custard or pudding, assembly, chilling | Weeks 15-18 or Semester 2 | Dessert timing and texture |
| Chow Chow | knife work, pickling, acid balance | Weeks 3, 15, or Bistro | Knife reps plus acid and preservation talk |
| Stewed Chicken | moist heat, seasoning, rice pairing | Weeks 6 or 14 | Strong bridge from protein to braise |
| Pralines | sugar cooking, timing, temperature | Semester 2 | Good stretch item, needs close supervision |
| Shrimp Alfredo | pasta timing, shrimp cookery, sauce consistency, emulsification, holding | Weeks 8, 11-12, or Bistro | Student-requested and legitimately useful |
| Biscuits | mixing method, flour handling, baking control | Weeks 15-18 or Semester 2 | Precision without needing fancy equipment |
| Deviled Eggs | egg cookery, emulsion, seasoning, cold station | Week 15 or Bistro | Practical, affordable, service-friendly |

## Teacher Review Flags

Review these before placing them on the calendar:

- seafood cost and sourcing
- oyster safety and feasibility
- smoking equipment and time for pulled pork
- praline safety and sugar burns
- crab cost for crab cakes, deviled crab, and crab rice
- whether she-crab soup is better as a demo, full lab, or Bistro stretch item
- whether BBQ hash and rice fits your kitchen, school expectations, and available prep time
