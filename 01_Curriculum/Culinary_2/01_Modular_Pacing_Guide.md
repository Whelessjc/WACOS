# Modular Pacing Guide

This is a full-year guide for a 180-day course. It is modular on purpose.

The calendar will move. The process should not.

The year is organized across 4 quarters and about 36 instructional weeks.

The design is technique-first and place-embedded.

Do not organize the course around cuisines or regions. Organize it around techniques and professional habits. Use Lowcountry, South Carolina, Southern, student-favorite, and occasional international dishes as vehicles where they fit naturally.

## Time Structure

| Day | Class Length | Planning Note |
| --- | ---: | --- |
| Monday | 95 min | Best day for new skill plus production |
| Tuesday | 70 min | Short lab, line checks, planning, Hospitality Scholars when scheduled |
| Wednesday | 105 min | Best day for longer production and practical checks |
| Thursday | 70 min | Bistro prep |
| Friday | 95 min | Bistro service |

Build each week around about 60 minutes of real production time.

Flames off 15 minutes before the bell.

## Full-Year Module Map

| Module | Approx. Timing | Main Work | Non-Negotiables |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1. Re-establish the Baseline | Weeks 1-4 | Heat, seasoning, knife control, rice/grains, timing, workflow reset | stable daily routines, station setup, clean-as-you-go |
| 2. Bistro Systems and Service Integration | Weeks 4-5 | Ticket flow, communication, station ownership, prep continuity | Thursday prep and Friday service rhythm begins |
| 3. Proteins, Heat, and Doneness | Weeks 6-9 | Chicken, shrimp, beef, thermometers, carryover, browning | doneness judgment, safe handling, simple sauces, CKC evidence |
| 4. Sauces, Moist Heat, Rice Dishes, and Structure | Weeks 10-14 | Roux, reductions, emulsions, soups, braises, Lowcountry rice dishes | simmer control, sauce texture, seasoning correction |
| 5. Eggs, Batter, Frying, and Baking Control | Weeks 15-18 | Eggs, batters, frying systems, baking control, practical skill check | clean frying, timing, station reset, semester culmination |
| 6. Baking and Pastry Foundations | Semester 2 | Weighing, mixing, doughs, custards, temperature discipline | precision, patience, timing |
| 7. Service Repetition and Application | Semester 2 | Bistro improvement, station leadership, production scaling | students carry more of the plan |
| 8. Capstone and Readiness | Final weeks | Practical execution, service role, composed dish or production item | calm execution, reflection grounded in performance |

## Module 1: Re-establish the Baseline

Approximate timing: Weeks 1-4

Focus:

- heat control
- seasoning
- knife control
- timing
- workflow reset
- station organization

Core skills:

- medium dice
- small dice
- julienne
- chiffonade
- mise en place
- sweating vs sauteing
- seasoning in layers
- tasting and adjustment
- prep sequencing

Anchor cooks:

- sweated onion base
- sauteed vegetables
- Carolina Gold rice or basic rice
- red rice prep
- Hoppin' John prep
- vinaigrettes
- emulsified dressings

Speaking built in:

- Explain the heat choice.
- Explain one seasoning decision.
- Name one adjustment you would make next time.

Keep it short, standing, and informal.

## Module 2: Bistro Systems and Service Integration

Approximate timing: Weeks 4-5

Focus:

- service rhythm
- communication
- ownership
- awareness
- role clarity

Skills:

- ticket flow
- station communication
- prep continuity
- labeling
- timing
- graceful urgency
- service recovery
- quality control

Applications:

- mock service
- station setup drills
- Bistro prep
- printed prep lists
- labeling checks
- call-and-response communication

Students move from participating in service to helping run service.

## Module 3: Proteins, Heat, and Doneness

Approximate timing: Weeks 6-9

Focus:

- confidence through heat control and repetition
- safe handling
- doneness judgment
- browning without panic

Core skills:

- roast chicken
- pan-seared chicken breast
- steak doneness
- fish cookery
- shrimp cookery
- carryover cooking
- thermometer use
- pan sauces
- browning control

Anchor cooks:

- roast chicken
- stewed chicken
- shrimp cookery
- steak with pan sauce
- fried fish
- fried shrimp
- shrimp and grits
- sauteed vegetables
- composed protein plates

If students cannot cook proteins cleanly, nothing else matters.

## Module 4: Sauces, Moist Heat, Rice Dishes, and Structure

Approximate timing: Weeks 10-14

Focus:

- building structure
- controlling liquid
- creating texture
- correcting sauce and soup problems

Core skills:

- roux control
- reduction
- emulsions
- bechamel
- veloute
- braising
- simmer control
- stock basics
- soup structure
- stovetop mac and cheese
- rice dish structure

Anchor cooks:

- mac and cheese
- braised dishes
- soups
- shrimp Alfredo
- she-crab soup as a discussion or stretch vehicle
- chicken bog or chicken perloo
- red rice
- crab rice
- chicken with pan sauce
- vegetable finished with mounted butter

Failures are discussed openly. A broken sauce, scorched roux, or under-seasoned soup is useful if students can name what happened and correct the next attempt.

## Module 5: Eggs, Batter, Frying, and Baking Control

Approximate timing: Weeks 15-18

Focus:

- egg cookery
- batter control
- clean frying systems
- introductory baking control
- semester practical readiness

Core skills:

- egg timing and carryover
- emulsion review
- batter consistency
- dredging and breading systems
- oil temperature control
- draining and seasoning fried foods
- oven timing
- clean station reset after high-mess labs

Anchor cooks:

- stovetop eggs
- deviled eggs
- pimento cheese or egg salad as cold station practice
- fried green tomatoes
- fried shrimp or fried fish
- biscuits
- banana pudding or simple dessert component
- one reliable cake or simple custard-based dessert
- practical skill check plate

This module is about control. Frying and batter work can get messy fast. The standard is calm, clean, and organized.

## Module 6: Baking and Pastry Foundations

Approximate timing: Semester 2

Focus:

- precision
- discipline
- timing
- temperature control

Core skills:

- weighing in grams
- mixing methods
- yeasted doughs
- fermentation
- custards
- batter consistency
- ice cream bases
- temperature discipline

Anchor bakes:

- muffins
- focaccia
- baguette-style bread
- custard desserts
- ice cream

Baking reinforces patience, sequencing, precision, and composure.

## Module 7: Service Repetition and Application

Approximate timing: Semester 2

Focus:

- students increasingly carry the plan
- Thursday prep and Friday service become proof of learning

Students increasingly:

- call plans
- run timing
- explain techniques
- support teammates
- maintain standards
- solve problems calmly

Teacher role shifts from constant directing toward observation, correction, and pressure testing.

## Module 8: Capstone and Readiness

Approximate timing: Final weeks

Possible capstone formats:

- Bistro integration role
- composed plate
- tasting menu component
- service leadership
- one meaningful dish scaled for production
- practical execution under pressure

Reflection stays concise and grounded:

- What was the plan?
- What changed?
- What did you adjust?
- How did you communicate?
- What would you do next time?

## Flex Rules

When a week gets interrupted:

- Protect Thursday prep and Friday service if Bistro is active.
- Move the most important rep to Wednesday.
- Use Monday for reset and reteach.
- Use Tuesday for line checks, knife work, planning, or Hospitality Scholars.
- Drop novelty before dropping fundamentals.
- Keep the beginning and ending routines stable.
