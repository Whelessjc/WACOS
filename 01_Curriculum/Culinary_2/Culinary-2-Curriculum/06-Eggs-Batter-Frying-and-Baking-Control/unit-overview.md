# Unit 6: Eggs, Batter, Frying, and Baking Control

Approximate timing: Weeks 15-18

## Purpose

Students practice control in work that can become messy quickly. Eggs, batters, frying, and introductory baking require timing, temperature awareness, clean station habits, and calm sequencing.

## Primary Sources

- `../../01_Modular_Pacing_Guide.md`
- `../../02_Semester_1_Weekly_Guide.md`
- `../../../03_Assessment/Culinary_2_Assessment_and_Rubrics.md`

## Learning Priorities

- Cook eggs with attention to carryover.
- Maintain clean cold station work.
- Control batter and dredging systems.
- Manage oil temperature.
- Drain and season fried foods properly.
- Prepare for a semester practical.

## Suggested Recipe Vehicles

- stovetop eggs
- deviled eggs
- pimento cheese or egg salad
- fried green tomatoes
- fried shrimp or fried fish
- biscuits
- banana pudding or simple dessert component

## Evidence

Students should maintain sanitation and station order while producing food that depends on timing and temperature.

## Build-Out Notes

Create frying setup diagrams, batter consistency checks, semester practical rubrics, and cleanup standards for high-mess labs.
