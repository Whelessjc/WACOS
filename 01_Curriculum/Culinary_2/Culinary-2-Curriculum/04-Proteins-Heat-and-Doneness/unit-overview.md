# Unit 4: Proteins, Heat, and Doneness

Approximate timing: Weeks 6-10

## Purpose

Students build confidence with proteins by practicing safe handling, browning, carryover, thermometer use, doneness judgment, and gentle poaching. Broiler/grill application may be used only after equipment-specific instruction, SOP review, and individual authorization. If students cannot cook proteins cleanly, later work is fragile.

## Learning Priorities

- Handle proteins safely.
- Break down a whole chicken safely when supervision, sourcing, and budget allow.
- Choose and control heat.
- Maintain poaching liquid below a boil and judge doneness in a gentle moist-heat application.
- Use broiler/grill heat zones, tools, flare-up controls, shutdown, and cleaning procedures when specifically authorized.
- Use thermometers correctly.
- Recognize carryover cooking.
- Build simple pan sauces.
- Recover from common mistakes without panic.

## Suggested Recipe Vehicles

- roast chicken
- pan-seared chicken breast
- stewed chicken
- whole chicken breakdown feeding roast, pan-seared, or stewed applications
- steak with pan sauce
- fish cookery
- gently poached fish or eggs
- grilled or broiled chicken, fish, or vegetables when equipment, ventilation, supervision, and SOP requirements are met
- shrimp cookery
- fried fish
- shrimp and grits

## Evidence

Students should explain doneness using temperature, visual cues, timing, carryover, and method-appropriate heat cues rather than guessing. Poaching evidence includes maintaining gentle liquid movement without boiling.

## Build-Out Notes

Create protein handling checklists, thermometer checks, doneness line checks, and practical rubrics aligned to CKC evidence. Select the teacher-approved poaching vehicle. Confirm whether whole-chicken breakdown is demo-then-practice or hands-on for every student based on supervision ratio, sourcing, and cost. Do not schedule student broiler/grill operation until the equipment-specific SOP, ventilation and fire-safety checks, instruction/demo, and individual authorization are complete.
