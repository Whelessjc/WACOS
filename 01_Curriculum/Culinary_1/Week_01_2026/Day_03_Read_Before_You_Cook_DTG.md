# Day 3 Daily Teaching Guide: Read Before You Cook

- **Course:** Culinary 1
- **Unit:** Orientation and Systems
- **Week/day:** Week 1, Day 3 - Thursday, August 13, 2026
- **Weekly Anchor:** Students can enter Kitchen Mode, locate essential kitchen areas and tools, read before acting, work with a team, and help reset a station.

## Learning

- **Target:** I can read the pancake recipe, identify what we need, and prepare a simple mise en place.
- **Essential question:** What problems can we prevent by reading before we cook?
- **Knowledge:** Recipe parts, ingredients, equipment, measurements, action verbs, and basic mise en place.
- **Skill:** Reading, marking, selecting measuring tools, and staging work.
- **Professional thinking:** Planning, precision, and preparation.

## Opening

Without touching equipment, scan the pancake recipe and circle the first action verb you see.

## Core Learning

1. Read the entire recipe before discussing individual steps.
2. Identify ingredients, equipment, measurements, action verbs, heat cues, and the cleanup/reset expectation.
3. Introduce **mise en place** as having tools, ingredients, and a plan ready before cooking.
4. Demonstrate only the measuring skills required by the selected pancake recipe.
5. Have groups build a no-cook practice setup and explain the first safe step for Friday.

## Flexible Learning

- Mark the recipe with simple codes: I = ingredient, E = equipment, A = action.
- Practice dry, liquid, or spoon measurement as required by the actual recipe.
- Sequence mixed-up recipe steps.
- Rehearse where finished mise en place will sit.

**Clean stopping point:** once each group can name its ingredients, equipment, first three actions, and measuring tools.

## Closure

What will your group do before anyone turns on heat?

## Materials and Instructor Preparation

- Insert the instructor-selected pancake recipe into the student sheet.
- Confirm the recipe yield and number of groups.
- Stage only the measuring tools used Friday.
- Confirm Friday's griddle, pan, oven, holding, dish, and cleanup setup.

## Adult Roles

**Instructor:** Model recipe reading and only the measuring skills required for Friday.

**TA when available:** Check recipe markings and question groups about sequence; do not introduce alternate procedures.

## Students Will Ask

- **Do we cook today?** No. Today protects Friday by preparing the plan and the measurement skills.
- **What does mise en place mean?** Tools, ingredients, and the plan ready before cooking.
- **Do we need to memorize the recipe?** No. You need to read it, understand it, and use it.
- **Can we change the recipe or add toppings?** Only after instructor confirmation.

## Instructor Context

Recipe reading and measurement receive deeper formal instruction later. Today provides only the knowledge and practice required for successful participation in the first lab.
