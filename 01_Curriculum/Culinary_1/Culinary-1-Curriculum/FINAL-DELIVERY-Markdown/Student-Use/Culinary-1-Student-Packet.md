## Why Recipe Reading Matters Before Cooking
Good cooks do not guess their way through food. They read the recipe first so they know what they are making, what they
need, what happens first, and where mistakes could happen.

## Why Mise en Place Matters Before Action
Mise en place means your work is ready before you start. It helps your team stay calm, avoid missing ingredients, use
tools correctly, and clean as you go.

## What Success Looks Like
- I can read the whole recipe before starting.
- I can find the ingredients, equipment, yield, and steps.
- I can put steps in a useful order.

## How to Read a Recipe
Before cooking, look for:
- Title: what you are making
- Yield: how much it makes
- Ingredients: what food items and amounts are needed
- Equipment: what tools, bowls, pans, or machines are needed
- Steps: what happens and in what order
- Timing: baking, cooling, simmering, resting, or waiting time
- Safety notes: allergens, heat, raw food, sharp tools, or sanitation warnings

## How to Identify Ingredients, Equipment, and Steps
Ask your group:
- What ingredients do we need?
- What amounts do we need?
- What tools do we need?
- Which steps happen before cooking?
- Which steps need teacher approval?
- What could go wrong if we rush?

## How to Sequence Work
Sequence means order.

Before starting:
1. Read the whole recipe.
2. Find ingredients and equipment.
3. Notice timing and safety steps.
4. Decide what happens first.
5. Decide what can happen while something cooks, bakes, or rests.
6. Decide what cleanup can happen during the recipe.

## How to Build a Simple Prep List
A prep list is a short plan for what needs to happen before cooking.

Your prep list can include:
- Ingredients to gather
- Ingredients to measure
- Tools to gather
- Items to cut, mix, heat, or cook
- Team roles
- Teacher checkpoints
- Cleanup jobs during the lab

## Measuring Expectations
- Use the correct measuring tool.
- Measure dry ingredients carefully.
- Read liquid measurements at eye level when appropriate.
- Use measuring spoons for small amounts.
- Measure over a separate bowl when possible.
- Check the unit before adding ingredients.
- Slow down when reading fractions.

In every lab, the goal is the same: read, organize, measure, cook or prepare, clean, and reset.

## Why Service Has a Full Flow
Bistro service is not just cooking. It is a team system. Guests notice how we communicate, move, clean, serve, and
reset.

When one part of the flow breaks down, the whole order can slow down.

## What Life of an Order Means
Life of an Order is the path an order takes from guest arrival to farewell.

The order does not end when food leaves the kitchen. It ends when the guest space, service space, dish flow, and
station are reset.

## What Success Looks Like
- I can explain the Life of an Order.
- I can tell where an order goes next.
- I can follow my station role.
- I can use clear service communication.

- Know your role.
- Know your tools.
- Know your setup.
- Know your ticket or task flow.
- Know your reset job.
- Know who to ask when unsure.

During service:
- Stay in your role unless the teacher changes it.
- Communicate clearly.

After service:
- Complete reset.
- Close or account for tickets/tasks.

## Guest Interaction Basics
Hospitality is practiced behavior:
- Greet respectfully.
- Use a calm voice.
- Listen before responding.
- Keep the guest space clean.
- Follow through on your task.
- Say farewell when appropriate.

## Sanitation During Service
- Wash hands when needed, especially after touching your face, phone, trash, or dirty dishes.
- Keep clean tools, dirty dishes, and guest items separated.
- Report spills right away.
- Keep towels, counters, and floors under control.
- Treat dish flow and reset as part of service.

## Ticket Flow Basics
1. Guest is greeted.
2. Order is taken.
3. Ticket moves to the correct station.
4. Station confirms the order.
5. Food or service item is prepared.
6. Expo checks the order.
7. Food is run or handed off.
8. Guest is checked on.
9. Space is cleared.
10. Dishes move correctly.
11. Station and guest space reset.
12. Farewell closes the order.

## Prep and Reset Expectations
- Prep supports service.
- Dish and reset are service work.
- Cleaning is not punishment.
- A station is not finished until the next group could use it.
- If you are unsure, ask before the system falls apart.

## What We Are Learning
Over the next four weeks, we are building knife control through repeated practice. You will practice safety, board
setup, grip, claw, rough chop, dice, slice, mince, julienne, batonnet, prep lists, cooking applications, and deep clean
reset.

## Why Knife Control Matters
Knife work affects safety, cooking, timing, and teamwork. Food cooks more evenly when pieces are cut with care. Cut
size can change cooking time, texture, and browning. Bistro prep works better when containers are organized, labeled,
and ready.

Knife cuts are tools for cooking, not trophies.

## Minimum Competence
For Culinary 1, minimum competence means:
- I can set up a safe cutting station.
- I can use grip and claw.
- I can keep the blade path controlled.
- I can cut at a controlled pace.
- I can make beginner cuts that are useful for cooking.
- I can sort usable prep, scraps, and waste.
- I can clean and reset the station safely.

## What Success Looks Like
- I can keep my board stable.
- I can keep my guiding hand in claw position.
- I can keep my knife controlled.
- I can improve speed without losing safety.
- I can use a prep list.
- I can explain how cut size affects cooking.
- I can reset a knife station for the next group.

## Knife Safety Expectations
- Carry knives safely as directed.
- Keep knives down when the teacher is talking.
- Keep your board stable.
- Keep fingers tucked.
- Keep eyes on the cut.
- Stop if the blade path feels unsafe.
- Do not rush.
- Do not scrape with the knife edge.
- Clean and store knives as directed.

## Board Setup
- Damp towel under board
- Board stable
- Knife placed safely
- Towel in the correct place
- Scrap/waste plan clear
- Usable prep container ready
- Counter clean enough to work

## Grip and Claw
Grip:
- Hold the knife with control.
- Keep the knife steady.
- Do not hold the handle loosely.
- Ask for help if the knife feels unstable.

Claw:
- Tuck fingertips back.
- Use knuckles as a guide.
- Keep thumb behind fingers.
- Move slowly before trying to move faster.

## Cuts We Will Practice
Rough chop:
- Larger, practical pieces.
- Useful for some prep and cooking tasks.

Dice:
- Smaller pieces.
- Useful when food needs to cook evenly.

Slice:
- Even pieces or strips.
- Useful for vegetables, sandwiches, stir fry, and saute.

Mince:
- Very small pieces.
- Useful for aromatics like garlic, herbs, or onions.

Julienne:
- Thin matchstick-style cuts.
- Useful for quick cooking and garnish-style prep.

Batonnet:
- Larger stick-style cuts.
- Useful for potato prep, fries, and consistent roasting.

## Speed With Control
Speed is not the first goal. Control comes first.

You may increase speed only when:
- Board is stable
- Grip and claw are correct
- Blade path is controlled
- Product is organized
- Station is clean enough to work

## How Knife Work Connects to Cooking
Cut size affects:
- How fast food cooks
- Whether food cooks evenly
- Texture
- Browning
- Service timing
- How easy a station is to work

In Bistro, better knife work helps prep lists, batch containers, labels, storage, station timing, and service readiness.

# Culinary 1 Student Reference Guide

Name: ________________________________    Class Period: ________

## Kitchen Entry Routine
- Enter calmly.
- Put belongings where they belong.
- Check the agenda.
- Check uniform/readiness.
- Join the huddle.
- Wash hands when directed.
- Begin only when released.

## Uniform Check
- Hair restrained.
- Safe shoes.
- No loose items.
- Jewelry follows school policy.
- Ready to work before lab starts.

## Handwashing
Wash hands:
- Before food work.
- After restroom.
- After touching face, hair, phone, trash, floor, or dirty dishes.
- After coughing, sneezing, or blowing nose.
- After handling raw food or allergens.
- Any time the teacher calls for a reset.

## Recipe Reading Steps
1. Read the whole recipe or task.
2. Find the ingredients.
3. Find the equipment.
4. Notice timing and wait steps.
5. Look for teacher checkpoints.
6. Ask before guessing.

## Mise en Place Steps
- Recipe or task visible.
- Tools gathered.
- Ingredients gathered and measured.
- Towel, trash, dish, and sanitizer plan ready.
- Team roles clear.
- Station checked before action.

## Knife Safety Reminders
- Knife down during directions.
- Stable board first.
- Use grip and claw.
- Eyes on the blade path.
- Control before speed.
- Report cuts immediately.

## Heat Safety Reminders
- Use dry towels around heat.
- Turn pan handles in.
- Call "hot" when moving hot items.
- Ask before changing heat.
- Watch oil, steam, pans, and ovens.
- Turn off heat as directed.

## Cleanup / Reset Expectations
You are not done when the food is done.

Reset includes:
- Dishes moving correctly.
- Counters cleaned and sanitized.
- Tools returned.
- Towels/laundry handled correctly.
- Trash checked.
- Floors checked.
- Food stored or discarded as directed.
- Station ready for the next group.

## Stewardship Jobs Overview
- Laundry: towels go where directed, not on counters or floors.
- Counters: clean first, then sanitize as directed.
- Dishes: scrape, sort, wash, dry, and put away correctly.
- Storage: close, label if directed, and return items to the right place.
- Floors: report spills right away and sweep/mop as directed.
- Shared spaces: leave the room better for the next group.

## Bistro Service Reminders
- Know your role.
- Keep tickets or tasks visible.
- Use clear words: behind, hot, sharp, corner, heard, order up, I need help.
- Communicate early.
- Keep dish and reset moving.
- Hospitality is practiced behavior: notice, listen, follow through, reset.

## What To Do When Unsure
1. Pause.
2. Keep the station safe.
3. Ask the right person or teacher.
4. Use clear words.
5. Return to the task.

## Quick Self-Check
- [ ] I know the task.
- [ ] My station is set up.
- [ ] I am working safely.
- [ ] I am communicating with my team.
- [ ] I am cleaning as I go.
- [ ] My station is reset for the next group.
