# Unit 05: Bistro Systems Reinforcement

## Teacher Overview
**Focus:** Stabilize service before moving deeper into technical skills.

**Weeks covered:** Week 5
**Focus:** Stabilize service before moving deeper into technical skills.

Students debrief the soft opening, retrain stations, clarify roles, strengthen ticket flow and communication, run mock
service 2.0, prepare for Bistro, and practice service with better timing and reset.

## Student Overview
Stabilize service before moving deeper into technical skills.

## Learning Targets
- I can follow safety expectations for this unit.
- I can set up before starting work.
- I can practice the main unit skills with control.
- I can communicate with my team.
- I can clean and reset the station for the next group.

## Key Vocabulary / Key Ideas
- role clarity
- ticket flow
- communication
- mock service 2.0
- prep
- service
- reset

## Lesson Flow
- Monday: Soft opening debrief using facts, not blame.
- Tuesday: Station retraining and role clarity.
- Wednesday: Ticket flow, call-backs, expo, and communication standards.
- Thursday: Mock service 2.0 with recovery practice.
- Friday: Bistro prep/service and reset debrief.

## Lab or Service Activity
Practice the service systems that need repetition after the soft opening: roles, tickets, communication, recovery,
dish, and reset.

## Safety Reminders
- Keep movement controlled during service practice.
- Prevent crowding at expo and dish.
- Keep clean/dirty separation visible.
- Protect handwashing, sanitizer, towels, floors, and storage even when busy.

## Stewardship / Cleanup / Reset Expectations
- Assign reset ownership before service starts.
- Check dish, towels, floors, guest area, expo, and station tools.
- Use the debrief to identify one reset habit to improve next service.

## Bistro / Service Connection
Students learn that service systems are practiced habits. Better prep, clearer tickets, calmer communication, active
dish flow, and full reset create better guest experience.

## Assessment Ideas
- Observe safety, setup, and communication during lab or service work.
- Check the unit checklist for honest completion.
- Use the lab/service reflection as evidence of student understanding.
- Look for cleanup/reset completed without being chased.

## Student Reflection
1. What skill or routine did you practice?
2. What safety habit mattered most?
3. How did you support your team or station?
4. What cleanup/reset job did you complete?
5. What do you need to practice next?
