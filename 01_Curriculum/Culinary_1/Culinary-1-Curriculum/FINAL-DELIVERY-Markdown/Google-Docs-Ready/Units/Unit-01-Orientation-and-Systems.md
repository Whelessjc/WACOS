# Unit 01: Orientation and Systems

## Teacher Overview
**Focus:** Learn the room before cooking.

**Weeks covered:** Week 1
**Focus:** Learn the room before cooking.

Students learn the kitchen routines that make safe lab work possible: entry routine, agenda check, huddle, uniform
readiness, handwashing, basic safety, measuring, station setup, cleanup/reset, and the first pancake lab.

## Student Overview
Learn the room before cooking.

## Learning Targets
- I can follow safety expectations for this unit.
- I can set up before starting work.
- I can practice the main unit skills with control.
- I can communicate with my team.
- I can clean and reset the station for the next group.

## Key Vocabulary / Key Ideas
- entry routine
- uniform readiness
- huddle
- handwashing
- measuring
- station setup
- cleanup/reset

## Lesson Flow
- Monday: Course orientation, kitchen mindset, entry routine, and uniform expectations.
- Tuesday: Room tour, traffic patterns, kitchen communication, and where things belong.
- Wednesday: Handwashing, basic safety, and measuring stations.
- Thursday: Station setup, recipe reading, dry run, and cleanup/reset practice.
- Friday: Pancake lab with simple team roles and full station reset.

## Lab or Service Activity
Practice recipe reading, measuring, mise en place, teamwork, heat awareness, and full station reset through a simple
pancake lab.

## Safety Reminders
- Students walk in the kitchen and keep pathways clear.
- Spills are reported immediately.
- Students ask before using equipment or heat.
- Towels stay away from heat.
- Food and cleaning chemicals stay separate.
- Hands are washed before food work and after contamination.

## Stewardship / Cleanup / Reset Expectations
- Protect time for reset every day, especially Friday.
- Check dishes, counters, tools, towels, trash, floors, and heat source.
- Use the phrase: reset means ready for the next group.
- If cleanup is weak, simplify the next lab rather than lowering the standard.

## Bistro / Service Connection
Week 1 routines are the start of Bistro readiness. Calm entry, clear communication, handwashing, setup, and reset are
the same habits students need before service.

## Assessment Ideas
- Observe safety, setup, and communication during lab or service work.
- Check the unit checklist for honest completion.
- Use the lab/service reflection as evidence of student understanding.
- Look for cleanup/reset completed without being chased.

## Student Reflection
1. What skill or routine did you practice?
2. What safety habit mattered most?
3. How did you support your team or station?
4. What cleanup/reset job did you complete?
5. What do you need to practice next?
