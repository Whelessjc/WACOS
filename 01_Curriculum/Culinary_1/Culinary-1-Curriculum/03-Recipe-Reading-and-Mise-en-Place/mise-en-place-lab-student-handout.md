# Mise en Place Lab Student Handout

## Purpose of the Lab
The purpose of this lab is to practice reading the recipe, building a prep list, measuring carefully, setting up mise en place, working with your team, and resetting the station.

This handout can support peanut butter bars, rice cookery, fried rice, or a simple stir fry.

## Skills Practiced
- Recipe reading
- Ingredient and equipment lists
- Measuring accuracy
- Prep list creation
- Mise en place
- Team communication
- Heat and steam awareness
- Cleanup and reset

## Equipment Needed
Your teacher will confirm the equipment for the assigned lab.

Your group may need:
- Recipe copy
- Measuring cups and spoons
- Mixing bowls
- Sheet pan, pot, rice cooker, saute pan, or wok
- Spatula or spoon
- Towels
- Sanitizer and dish supplies

## Ingredients / Materials
Your teacher will provide or assign ingredients for the lab.

Possible lab ingredients may include:
- Peanut butter bar ingredients
- Rice and liquid
- Fried rice or stir fry ingredients
- Seasonings or simple sauce ingredients as directed

## Mise en Place / Setup Checklist
- [ ] Hands washed
- [ ] Recipe read completely
- [ ] Team roles assigned
- [ ] Ingredients listed
- [ ] Equipment listed
- [ ] Prep list written or discussed
- [ ] Ingredients measured or staged as directed
- [ ] Equipment gathered
- [ ] Towel in correct place
- [ ] Trash and dish plan clear
- [ ] Counter clean and usable
- [ ] Teacher checkpoint complete before cooking

## Procedure
1. Complete the entry routine, agenda check, huddle, and handwashing.
2. Read the recipe all the way through.
3. Mark ingredients, equipment, yield, and important steps.
4. Build a simple prep list with your group.
5. Gather equipment and ingredients as directed.
6. Measure carefully and keep shared ingredients clean.
7. Set up mise en place before mixing or cooking.
8. Pause for the teacher checkpoint.
9. Complete the assigned lab.
10. Clean as you go so the station stays usable.
11. Complete final cleanup and reset before leaving.

## Teamwork Expectations
- Reader: keeps the recipe visible and tracks the next step.
- Measurer: checks amounts and tools before ingredients are added.
- Equipment lead: gathers and returns tools.
- Cook or mixer: completes active cooking or mixing steps as directed.
- Steward: leads dishes, towels, trash, counters, storage, and final reset.

If your group is smaller, combine roles. Everyone still helps clean.

## Heat Awareness Reminders
- Hot pans and steam can burn quickly.
- Say "hot" when carrying or working near hot equipment.
- Do not crowd the cooking station.
- Keep handles turned in as directed.
- Ask before changing heat.
- Turn off heat only when directed or when your teacher has taught that routine.

## Cleanup and Reset Checklist
- [ ] Dishes washed, sanitized, or routed correctly
- [ ] Counters cleaned and sanitized
- [ ] Tools returned
- [ ] Ingredients closed, stored, or returned as directed
- [ ] Towels handled correctly
- [ ] Trash checked
- [ ] Floor checked
- [ ] Heat turned off as directed
- [ ] Station ready for the next group

## Bistro Connection
Bistro stations need prep lists and mise en place before service. Organized prep helps a team handle timing, tickets, batch work, and station reset without panic.

## Reflection Questions
1. What did your group do before touching ingredients?
2. Which prep step mattered most today?
3. What measuring or setup habit helped the lab?
4. How did your group share the work?
5. What did your group do to protect the next group?
