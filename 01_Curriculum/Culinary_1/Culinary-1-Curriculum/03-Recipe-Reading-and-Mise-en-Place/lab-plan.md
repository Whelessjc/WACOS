# Lab Plan: Recipe Reading and Mise en Place

## Lab Purpose
Use simple recipes to practice reading, measuring, sequencing, prep lists, and organized station setup.

## Skill Focus
- Recipe annotation
- Ingredient and equipment lists
- Measuring
- Prep list creation
- Mise en place
- Order of operations
- Cookware sizing and care
- Substitution judgment
- Clean-as-you-go

## Ingredients or Materials
- Peanut butter bar ingredients
- Rice and liquid
- Fried rice or stir fry ingredients selected by teacher
- Teacher-approved dried pasta and basic tomato-sauce ingredients
- Food Handler review materials

## Equipment
- Measuring tools
- Mixing bowls
- Sheet pans
- Rice cooker or pots
- Saute pans or woks
- Large pot, colander, tongs, and saucepan
- Spatulas and spoons
- Towels and sanitizer

## Setup
- Display or distribute recipe.
- Students identify tools and ingredients before release.
- Assign team roles.
- Stage ingredients to reduce crowding.

## Procedure
1. Read recipe completely.
2. Mark ingredients, equipment, and steps.
3. Confirm that the selected cookware fits the batch and method; flag any proposed substitution for instructor approval.
4. Build a simple prep list.
5. Gather tools and ingredients.
6. Set mise en place.
7. Cook or prepare the assigned lab.
8. Pause for teacher checks on sequence, dishes, towels, and station control.
9. Clean cookware using the correct method, store it properly, and reset the station.

## Teacher Checkpoints
- Recipe read before starting.
- Correct tools gathered.
- Cookware size matches the batch and method.
- Substitutions are approved rather than improvised.
- Prep list makes sense.
- Measuring is careful.
- Station stays organized.

## Cleanup and Reset Expectations
Tools returned, dishes routed correctly, counters sanitized, food stored or discarded as directed, towels handled, floor checked.

## Possible Variations
- Use rice only for ratio practice.
- Make fried rice teacher-led if heat control is not ready.
- Substitute another simple bar cookie if peanut allergies require it.
- Use dried pasta and basic tomato sauce to assess timing, doneness by feel, salting water, sequencing, and sauce mise en place. Confirm the formula and gluten substitution before scheduling.
