# Unit 03 Teacher Packet: Recipe Reading and Mise en Place

## Unit Overview
Week 3 focus: read before cooking. Organize before acting.

Students finish Food Handler review and assessment, then practice reading recipes, identifying ingredients and equipment, sequencing steps, building prep lists, measuring carefully, setting mise en place, and keeping a clean station while they cook.

## Week at a Glance
| Day | Focus | Main Application |
|---|---|---|
| Monday | Food Handler review and assessment | Safety review, assessment, sanitation scenarios |
| Tuesday | Recipe reading | Annotate a recipe and identify tools, ingredients, yield, steps, and timing |
| Wednesday | Measuring and sequence | Peanut butter bars with mise en place check |
| Thursday | Rice cookery and ratios | Rice practice with measuring, timing, and reset |
| Friday | Mise en place application | Fried rice or simple stir fry with prep list and station readiness |

## Daily Lesson Flow

### Monday: Food Handler Review and Assessment
- Opening: entry routine, agenda check, huddle, readiness check, handwashing.
- Mini-lesson/demo: review safety habits that must continue during recipe work.
- Student work: Food Handler review questions or assessment as planned.
- Application: quick sanitation and cross-contamination scenarios.
- Prompt: "Which safety habit still matters when the recipe looks simple?"
- Reset: return materials, wipe tables or stations, handle trash, and reset the room.

### Tuesday: Recipe Reading
- Opening: huddle on "read before cooking."
- Mini-lesson/demo: title, yield, ingredients, equipment, steps, timing, and warning signs in a recipe.
- Student work: annotate a simple recipe and build ingredient/equipment lists.
- Application: identify what is needed for peanut butter bars before touching ingredients.
- Prompt: "What should your group know before you pick up food?"
- Reset: return tools, close shared ingredients, wipe surfaces, and reset tables.

### Wednesday: Peanut Butter Bars
- Opening: entry routine, handwashing, station setup, allergen reminder.
- Mini-lesson/demo: measuring accuracy, reading the whole recipe, and following sequence.
- Student work: assign roles, make a simple prep list, and set mise en place.
- Application: prepare peanut butter bars as directed.
- Prompt: "How did mise en place prevent a mistake today?"
- Reset: dishes routed correctly, counters sanitized, ingredients stored, tools returned, towels handled, floor checked.

### Thursday: Rice Cookery and Ratios
- Opening: agenda check, huddle, handwashing, station readiness.
- Mini-lesson/demo: rice-to-liquid ratio, measuring, simmering, steam safety, and timing.
- Student work: identify or calculate the rice ratio and set up equipment.
- Application: rice cookery practice.
- Prompt: "Why does measuring matter before heat is involved?"
- Reset: pots or rice cooker cleaned as directed, counters sanitized, rice stored or discarded as directed, floor checked.

### Friday: Fried Rice or Simple Stir Fry
- Opening: handwashing, station check, prep list check.
- Mini-lesson/demo: order of operations, hot pan awareness, batch work, and keeping mise visible.
- Student work: build prep list, gather equipment, and set mise en place before cooking.
- Application: fried rice or simple stir fry.
- Prompt: "What did your prep list help your group do faster or safer?"
- Reset: full station reset with tools returned, dishes moving, shared ingredients handled correctly, counters sanitized, towels/laundry handled, trash and floor checked.

## Setup Notes
- Prepare Food Handler review or assessment materials for Monday.
- Choose simple recipes for annotation and lab use.
- Confirm peanut allergy plan before peanut butter bar day.
- Stage measuring tools, mixing bowls, sheet pans, rice cookers or pots, saute pans or woks, spatulas, spoons, towels, sanitizer, and dish supplies.
- Stage ingredients in a way that limits crowding and supports shared ingredient stewardship.
- Decide whether Friday stir fry heat work is student-managed, teacher-managed, or teacher-led.

Before each lab, confirm:
- Recipe is visible to each group.
- Ingredient pickup plan is clear.
- Equipment plan is clear.
- Safety and allergen plan is clear.
- Dish-flow, towel, trash, floor, and reset plan are clear.

## Teacher Language and Prompts
- "Read before cooking. Organize before acting."
- "A recipe is directions for action."
- "Do not touch ingredients until your group can explain the recipe."
- "Mise en place includes tools, ingredients, towels, trash, dishes, and a clean workspace."
- "Measure over a separate bowl, not over the main container."
- "Clean while the recipe is happening, not after the station falls apart."

## Safety Reminders
- Continue Food Handler habits every day.
- Check peanut allergy needs before peanut butter bar work.
- Wash hands before food work and after contamination.
- Prevent cross-contact with shared ingredients and tools.
- Use hot pan and steam language during rice and stir fry work.
- Keep handles turned in and walk carefully around cooking stations.
- Report spills and broken glass immediately.

## Classroom Management Reminders
- Require a recipe check before lab release.
- Assign a reader role so someone tracks the directions.
- Use a steward role so cleanup starts early.
- Pause the room when students start guessing or rushing.
- Keep shared ingredient pickup calm and controlled.
- Do not let one student cook while another student does all the cleanup.
- Use "station ready for the next group" as the reset standard.

## Cleanup and Reset Expectations
Students are not finished when the food is finished. Recipe work includes reset.

A finished station should include:
- Dishes washed, sanitized, or routed correctly
- Counters cleaned and sanitized
- Tools returned to the right place
- Shared ingredients closed, stored, or returned as directed
- Towels handled correctly
- Trash and floor checked
- Heat turned off as directed
- Station ready for the next group

## Unit Lab Plan
Purpose: use simple labs to practice recipe literacy, measuring, prep lists, mise en place, teamwork, and clean station control.

Skills practiced:
- Reading the whole recipe before starting
- Identifying ingredients, equipment, yield, and steps
- Measuring carefully
- Building a simple prep list
- Setting mise en place
- Communicating during cooking
- Cleaning as part of cooking

Suggested roles:
- Reader: keeps the recipe visible and tracks the next step
- Measurer: measures carefully and confirms amounts
- Equipment lead: gathers and returns tools
- Cook or mixer: completes the active cooking or mixing steps
- Steward: leads dishes, towels, trash, counters, storage, and final reset

Teacher checkpoints:
- Recipe read and annotated before students begin
- Correct tools and ingredients gathered
- Prep list makes sense
- Measuring is careful
- Mise en place is visible
- Heat or allergen risks are controlled
- Reset is complete

## Bistro Connection
Bistro service depends on students who can read before acting, build a prep list, set a station, time their work, and reset for the next group. A ticket line runs better when prep is organized before the rush starts.
