# Teacher Notes: Recipe Reading and Mise en Place

## What to Emphasize
- Recipes are directions for action, not decorations.
- Students should read before touching food.
- Mise en place reduces stress and mistakes.
- Prep lists make teamwork visible.
- Measuring and sequence affect final results.
- Clean-as-you-go should be part of the recipe process, not an ending chore.
- Stewardship means students measure, use, store, and clean shared ingredients and tools with care.

## Common Student Mistakes
- Starting at step one without reading the whole recipe.
- Ignoring yield or portion size.
- Gathering ingredients but not tools.
- Measuring over the main container.
- Mixing steps out of order.
- Leaving cleanup until the station is out of control.
- Forgetting that mise en place includes a clean, usable workspace.

## Classroom Management Notes
- Require a recipe check before lab release.
- Keep ingredient pickup organized.
- Assign a reader role in each group.
- Pause the room if students start guessing.

## Safety Concerns
- Allergens in peanut butter.
- Hot pans and steam during rice or stir fry.
- Cross-contact between ingredients.
- Crowding around cooking stations.

## Pacing Notes
Do not make the week too technical. The goal is recipe confidence and organized action.

## Repetition Needed
Recipe annotation, equipment lists, prep lists, mise en place, and clean station reset should appear in every future lab.

## Connection to Later Units
Knife prep, bistro tickets, heat control, frying, and protein cookery all require students to read, plan, and organize before acting.
