# Unit 03 Slide Outline: Recipe Reading and Mise en Place

## Slide 1: Unit 03
- Recipe Reading and Mise en Place
- Read before cooking
- Organize before acting
- Real cooking starts with preparation

## Slide 2: Why This Unit Matters
- Recipes are directions for action
- Guessing creates mistakes
- Organized stations reduce stress
- Bistro service depends on prep

## Slide 3: This Week
- Food Handler review and assessment
- Recipe reading
- Peanut butter bars
- Rice cookery
- Fried rice or simple stir fry

## Slide 4: Daily Routine
- Enter calmly
- Check the agenda
- Join the huddle
- Wash hands when directed
- Wait to begin

## Slide 5: Food Handler Habits Continue
- Wash hands at the right times
- Prevent cross-contamination
- Treat allergens seriously
- Keep stations clean and sanitized
- Store ingredients as directed

## Slide 6: Read the Whole Recipe
- Title
- Yield
- Ingredients
- Equipment
- Steps and timing

## Slide 7: Ingredient Lists
- Check amounts
- Check form: chopped, melted, cooked, cold
- Watch for allergens
- Use shared ingredients carefully
- Close and return items as directed

## Slide 8: Equipment Lists
- Gather tools before starting
- Use the right measuring tool
- Keep tools organized
- Return tools clean
- Ask before using unfamiliar equipment

## Slide 9: Step Sequencing
- Read all steps first
- Find what happens before cooking
- Notice wait time or bake time
- Keep the recipe visible
- Do not skip steps

## Slide 10: Prep Lists
- Tasks before cooking
- Who does each job
- What needs measuring
- What needs heating
- What needs cleaning during the lab

## Slide 11: Measuring Accuracy
- Level dry ingredients
- Use liquid cups for liquids
- Measure over a separate bowl
- Check the unit
- Slow down for fractions

## Slide 12: Mise en Place
- Recipe visible
- Ingredients ready
- Tools ready
- Towel, trash, and dish plan ready
- Counter clean and usable

## Slide 13: Lab Safety
- Peanut allergy awareness
- Hot pans and steam
- Crowding creates risk
- Use "hot" and "behind"
- Ask when unsure

## Slide 14: Cleanup and Reset
- Dishes moving
- Counters sanitized
- Tools returned
- Ingredients stored
- Trash and floor checked

## Slide 15: Bistro Connection
- Prep lists support service
- Station readiness saves time
- Timing matters
- Communication matters
- Reset protects the next team

## Slide 16: Exit Reflection
- What did you read before starting?
- What did your group organize first?
- What mistake did mise en place help prevent?
- What reset habit needs more practice?
