# Recipe Reading Student Handout

## Purpose
The purpose of recipe reading is to know what you are doing before you touch ingredients. A recipe should help your group plan, measure, cook, clean, and reset.

## Skills Practiced
- Reading the whole recipe
- Finding ingredients, equipment, yield, and steps
- Noticing sequence and timing
- Making a simple prep list
- Asking useful questions before cooking

## Parts of a Recipe
| Part | What to Look For |
|---|---|
| Title | What you are making |
| Yield | How much the recipe makes |
| Ingredients | What food items and amounts are needed |
| Equipment | What tools, pans, bowls, and machines are needed |
| Steps | What happens and in what order |
| Time | Mixing, cooking, baking, cooling, or holding time |
| Safety notes | Allergens, heat, raw food, sharp tools, or special directions |

## Before You Cook Checklist
- [ ] I read the whole recipe.
- [ ] I circled or marked ingredients.
- [ ] I underlined or listed equipment.
- [ ] I found the yield.
- [ ] I numbered or checked the major steps.
- [ ] I noticed measuring, cooling, baking, simmering, or waiting time.
- [ ] I asked about unclear directions.
- [ ] My group can explain what happens first.

## Build a Simple Prep List
Use the recipe to answer:
- What needs to be gathered?
- What needs to be measured?
- What needs to be mixed, cut, heated, or cooked?
- What can be done while something bakes, simmers, or rests?
- What cleaning should happen during the recipe?

## Measuring Reminders
- Use the correct tool for dry or liquid ingredients.
- Level dry ingredients when needed.
- Measure over a separate bowl when possible.
- Check the unit before pouring.
- Slow down when reading fractions.

## Safety Reminders
- Check for allergens before starting.
- Ask before using unfamiliar equipment.
- Notice any hot, sharp, raw food, or sanitation warnings in the recipe.
- Keep shared ingredients clean and covered when you are finished with them.
- Wash hands again if you contaminate them.

## Teacher Check
Before your group starts a lab, be ready to show:
- Recipe is read and marked
- Tools are listed
- Ingredients are listed
- Prep list makes sense
- Questions have been asked

## Cleanup and Reset
Recipe work is not finished until the station is reset.

- [ ] Shared ingredients closed and returned
- [ ] Tools returned clean
- [ ] Dishes routed correctly
- [ ] Counter cleaned and sanitized
- [ ] Towels handled correctly
- [ ] Trash and floor checked
- [ ] Station ready for the next class

## Bistro Connection
In Bistro, a recipe or prep list helps the station get ready before tickets start. Reading first helps the team plan timing, avoid missing ingredients, and keep service calmer.

## Reflection Questions
1. What part of the recipe helped your group the most?
2. What direction could be easy to miss?
3. What tool or ingredient did your group need to find before starting?
4. What should your group do differently before the next lab?
