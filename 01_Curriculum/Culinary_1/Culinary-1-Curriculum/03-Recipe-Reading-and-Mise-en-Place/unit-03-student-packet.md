# Unit 03 Student Packet: Recipe Reading and Mise en Place

## What We Are Learning
This week we are learning how to read before cooking and organize before acting. You will practice recipe reading, ingredient lists, equipment lists, step order, prep lists, measuring, mise en place, teamwork, and cleanup/reset.

## Why We Are Learning It
Good cooks do not guess their way through food. They read, plan, measure, set up, cook, and clean in a way that helps the whole kitchen work.

## What Success Looks Like
- I can read the whole recipe before starting.
- I can find the ingredients, equipment, yield, and steps.
- I can help put recipe steps in a useful order.
- I can help make a prep list.
- I can set up mise en place before cooking.
- I can measure carefully enough for the recipe.
- I can keep my station organized and reset it when finished.

## Daily Kitchen Entry Routine
1. Enter calmly.
2. Put belongings where they belong.
3. Check the agenda.
4. Check uniform/readiness.
5. Join the huddle.
6. Wash hands when directed.
7. Begin work only when released.

## Key Expectations
- Read the recipe before touching ingredients.
- Ask when a step, amount, or tool is unclear.
- Gather tools and ingredients before cooking.
- Keep the recipe visible while your group works.
- Clean as you go.
- Protect shared ingredients, tools, and the next group.

## Kitchen Communication
Use short, clear words so people know what is happening:
- "Behind"
- "Hot"
- "Sharp"
- "Corner"
- "Heard"
- "I need help"
- "Spill"
- "Where does this go?"

## Safety Expectations
- Use Food Handler habits every day.
- Wash hands before food work and after contamination.
- Treat allergens seriously, especially peanut butter.
- Do not crowd hot pans, rice cookers, or cooking stations.
- Use "hot" and "behind" when moving around the kitchen.
- Ask before using equipment you are not cleared to use.

## Recipe Reading Expectations
Before you cook, your group should be able to answer:
- What are we making?
- How much does it make?
- What ingredients do we need?
- What tools do we need?
- Which steps must happen first?
- Where could we make a safety or measuring mistake?

## Mise en Place Expectations
Mise en place means your work is ready before you start.

Your station should have:
- Recipe visible
- Team roles assigned
- Ingredients gathered and measured as directed
- Tools gathered
- Towel in the right place
- Trash plan clear
- Dish plan clear
- Counter clean and usable

## Unit Lab Expectations
This week may include Food Handler review, peanut butter bars, rice cookery, and fried rice or simple stir fry.

The goal is to practice:
- Reading before acting
- Measuring carefully
- Setting up before cooking
- Working as a team
- Watching heat and steam
- Cleaning during the lab
- Resetting the station fully

## Cleanup and Reset Checklist
- [ ] Dishes washed, sanitized, or routed correctly
- [ ] Counters cleaned and sanitized
- [ ] Tools returned
- [ ] Shared ingredients closed, stored, or returned as directed
- [ ] Towels handled correctly
- [ ] Trash checked
- [ ] Floor checked
- [ ] Heat turned off as directed
- [ ] Station ready for the next group

## Bistro Connection
In Bistro, prep lists and station setup matter before tickets start coming in. If a station is organized, the team can work faster, communicate better, and serve more consistent food.

## Student Reflection Questions
1. What part of the recipe did you need to read more carefully?
2. What did your group gather before starting?
3. How did mise en place help your group?
4. What measuring habit do you need to keep practicing?
5. What did your group do to leave the station ready for the next class?
