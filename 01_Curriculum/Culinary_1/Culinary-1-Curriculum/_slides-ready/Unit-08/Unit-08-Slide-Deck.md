# Unit 08 Slide Deck: Frying and Oil Management

Focus: Temperature discipline.

## Slide 1: Unit 08 Focus
- Temperature discipline
- Oil safety
- Small batches
- Crisp results
- Full reset

Teacher note:
Keep the unit calm and strict.

## Slide 2: Oil Safety
- Water stays away
- Move slowly
- Use tools
- Keep space
- Stop for spills

Teacher note:
Review the local oil policy out loud.

## Slide 3: Fryer Behavior
- No crowding
- No reaching
- No horseplay
- Eyes on task
- Teacher approval

Teacher note:
Use classroom-specific fryer rules.

## Slide 4: Batch Size
- Small batches
- Room to cook
- Temperature recovers
- Food cooks evenly

Teacher note:
Connect batch size to quality and safety.

## Slide 5: Overcrowding
- Temperature drops
- Food absorbs oil
- Cooks unevenly
- Gets greasy

Teacher note:
Explain the cause without turning it into a lecture.

## Slide 6: Crisp vs Greasy
- Dry product
- Hot enough oil
- Do not crowd
- Drain well
- Season hot

Teacher note:
Use this as the frying quality checklist.

## Slide 7: Draining
- Use rack or paper
- Let oil leave
- Do not pile
- Keep area clean

Teacher note:
Show the draining station before frying begins.

## Slide 8: Seasoning Timing
- Season while hot
- Use control
- Taste if allowed
- Do not overdo it

Teacher note:
Connect to flavor systems.

## Slide 9: Applications
- Potato batonnet
- Vegetable fritters
- Onion rings
- Similar low-cost item

Teacher note:
Use the application selected by the teacher.

## Slide 10: Oil Station Reset
- Oil handled as directed
- Tools cleaned
- Grease checked
- Floors checked
- Station safe

Teacher note:
This slide matters as much as cooking.

## Slide 11: Bistro Connection
- Batch timing
- Consistency
- Holding quality
- Service readiness

Teacher note:
Connect quality to guest timing.

## Slide 12: Exit Reflection
- One safety rule
- One batch cue
- One quality cue
- One reset job

Teacher note:
Use after frying lab.
