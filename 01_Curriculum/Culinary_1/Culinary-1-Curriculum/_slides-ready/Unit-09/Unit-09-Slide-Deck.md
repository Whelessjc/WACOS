# Unit 09 Slide Deck: Flavor Systems

Focus: Move beyond recipes and learn how flavor is built.

## Slide 1: Unit 09 Focus
- Build flavor
- Taste carefully
- Adjust small
- Respect origins

Teacher note:
Set a respectful, curious tone.

## Slide 2: Salt
- Makes flavor clearer
- Use small amounts
- Taste again
- Do not dump

Teacher note:
Use a low-risk tasting if possible.

## Slide 3: Acid
- Adds brightness
- Cuts richness
- Use a little
- Taste again

Teacher note:
Use citrus or vinegar examples.

## Slide 4: Fat
- Carries flavor
- Adds richness
- Changes texture
- Use control

Teacher note:
Connect to cooking and sauces.

## Slide 5: Aromatics
- Onion
- Garlic
- Ginger
- Smell changes
- Do not burn

Teacher note:
Connect back to heat control.

## Slide 6: Herbs and Spices
- Smell first
- Use small amounts
- Watch heat
- Store correctly

Teacher note:
Keep spices organized and closed.

## Slide 7: Tasting Rules
- Clean spoon
- No double-dip
- Name allergens
- Use respectful words

Teacher note:
Make this non-negotiable.

## Slide 8: Adjustment Loop
- Taste
- Name the need
- Add a little
- Taste again

Teacher note:
Teach a loop, not guessing.

## Slide 9: Flavor Families
- Soy and ginger
- Vinegar and herbs
- Chili and citrus
- Yogurt and spice
- Coconut and curry

Teacher note:
Use these as classroom comparisons, not claims of authenticity.

## Slide 10: Respectful Language
- Name ingredients
- Say inspired by
- Avoid claiming authentic
- Stay curious

Teacher note:
Keep cultural respect clear and simple.

## Slide 11: Cleanup and Reset
- Tasting tools handled
- Allergens cleaned
- Spices closed
- Counters sanitized
- Trash checked

Teacher note:
Flavor labs can get messy fast.

## Slide 12: Bistro Connection
- Consistent seasoning
- Tasting
- Guest experience
- Prep quality

Teacher note:
Connect adjustment to service consistency.

## Slide 13: Exit Reflection
- One flavor change
- One adjustment
- One respectful phrase
- One reset job

Teacher note:
Use after flavor labs.
