# Unit 07 Slide Deck: Heat Control

Focus: Learn how cooking actually works.

## Slide 1: Unit 07 Focus
- Heat changes food
- Watch cues
- Adjust early
- Control timing

Teacher note:
Keep students looking, smelling, and listening.

## Slide 2: Pan Preheating
- Pan first
- Then oil
- Then food
- Listen and watch

Teacher note:
Demo cold pan versus preheated pan if possible.

## Slide 3: Oil Control
- Enough oil
- Not too much
- Watch shimmer
- Avoid smoke
- Adjust heat

Teacher note:
Connect oil cues to safety and browning.

## Slide 4: Saute Basics
- Small pieces
- Hot pan
- Quick movement
- Do not crowd

Teacher note:
Use small batches so students can see the result.

## Slide 5: Browning vs Steaming
- Browning needs space
- Steam means moisture
- Crowding slows cooking
- Color adds flavor

Teacher note:
Show crowded and spaced examples if possible.

## Slide 6: Aromatics
- Pay attention
- Stir often
- Do not burn
- Smell changes

Teacher note:
Use onion, garlic, or ginger if available.

## Slide 7: Roasting
- Oven preheated
- Food spaced
- Dry surface
- Turn as needed

Teacher note:
Connect spacing to browning and texture.

## Slide 8: Caramelization and Texture
- Color builds flavor
- Texture changes
- Too hot can burn
- Timing matters

Teacher note:
Keep the explanation simple and visual.

## Slide 9: Moist Heat
- Liquid cooks food
- Simmer gently
- Do not hard boil
- Check texture

Teacher note:
Show simmer versus boil.

## Slide 10: Rice, Beans, Lentils
- Liquid ratio
- Time
- Texture
- Resting
- Seasoning

Teacher note:
Use this as a ratio and patience lesson.

## Slide 11: Hot Station Reset
- Burners off
- Pans safe
- Oil handled
- Counters cleaned
- Floors checked

Teacher note:
Reset is part of heat control.

## Slide 12: Bistro Connection
- Timing
- Batch work
- Holding quality
- Consistency

Teacher note:
Tie heat control to service readiness.

## Slide 13: Exit Reflection
- One heat cue
- One adjustment
- One texture change
- One reset job

Teacher note:
Use after any hot lab.
