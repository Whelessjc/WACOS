# Classroom Packet Standards

## Purpose
Classroom-ready packets turn the curriculum spine into usable teaching materials for a working high school culinary classroom. They should help the teacher run the week, help students understand what to do, and keep labs safe, organized, practical, and connected to real kitchen behavior.

## Required Packet Files
Each unit classroom packet should include:
- `unit-XX-teacher-packet.md`
- `unit-XX-student-packet.md`
- `unit-XX-slide-outline.md`
- `[lab-or-skill]-student-handout.md`
- `[lab-or-skill]-teacher-guide.md`

Add one extra connection or job file only when the unit genuinely needs it, such as stewardship jobs, lab connections, or station role notes.

## Tone and Style Rules
- Clear, direct, high-school appropriate.
- Practical over polished.
- Specific to a culinary classroom.
- Beginner-friendly.
- No fancy culinary school posturing.
- No corporate curriculum language.
- No long textbook-style explanations.
- Emphasize repeated practice, safety, sanitation, stewardship, teamwork, and confidence.

## Teacher-Facing Expectations
Teacher materials should include:
- Unit overview and focus
- Week-at-a-glance
- Daily lesson flow
- Setup notes
- Teacher language/prompts
- Safety reminders
- Classroom management reminders
- Cleanup/reset expectations
- Lab or application plan
- Reflection notes for reteaching

Teacher files should be usable during planning, not just nice to read.

## Student-Facing Expectations
Student materials should include:
- What students are learning
- Why it matters in the kitchen
- What success looks like
- Daily routine expectations
- Safety and communication expectations
- Unit-specific habits and skills
- Cleanup/reset checklist
- Reflection questions

Use plain student language. Students should know exactly what they are expected to do.

## Lab Handout Expectations
Lab handouts should include:
- Purpose of the lab
- Skills practiced
- Equipment needed
- Ingredients or materials
- Mise en place/setup checklist
- Procedure
- Teamwork expectations
- Safety reminders
- Cleanup/reset checklist
- Reflection questions

Labs should teach skills and systems, not just produce food.

## Slide Outline Expectations
Slide outlines should include:
- Slide titles
- 3 to 5 short bullets per slide when possible
- Clear student-facing language
- Unit focus
- Safety reminders
- Key routines or skills
- Cleanup/reset
- Bistro or real-kitchen connection
- Exit reflection

Do not write long paragraphs or decorative filler.

## Cleanup and Reset Expectations
Every packet must treat cleanup as part of cooking and professional behavior.

Include expectations for:
- Dishes
- Counters
- Tools
- Towels/laundry
- Trash
- Floors
- Storage
- Sanitizing
- Heat/equipment shutdown when relevant
- Station ready for the next group

Use language like "protect the next group" and "station ready for the next class."

## Bistro Connection Expectations
Each packet should connect skills to Bistro or service when useful. Keep connections practical:
- Prep lists
- Station setup
- Timing
- Ticket flow
- Batch work
- Communication
- Consistency
- Holding quality
- Guest safety
- Hospitality
- Reset for the next team

Do not force a Bistro connection if it does not help the unit.

## What Not to Include
- Actual slides unless requested
- Images unless requested
- Websites unless requested
- Extra folders unless requested
- Long textbook chapters
- Generic restaurant language disconnected from this classroom
- Formal quizzes or tests unless requested
- Full recipes outside the requested packet scope
- "Mastery" language
- Cleaning framed as punishment or busy work

## Review Checklist
Before a packet is considered ready, check:
- [ ] Required files are present.
- [ ] The packet follows the unit focus from the course map.
- [ ] Teacher packet is usable for planning the week.
- [ ] Student packet is clear and beginner-friendly.
- [ ] Lab handout has setup, procedure, safety, teamwork, and reset.
- [ ] Teacher guide has setup, demo points, watch-outs, checkpoints, and cleanup timing.
- [ ] Slide outline uses short student-facing bullets.
- [ ] Cleanup/reset expectations are specific.
- [ ] Safety reminders are visible.
- [ ] Stewardship is framed as real kitchen responsibility.
- [ ] Bistro/service connection is practical.
- [ ] Nothing feels generic, corporate, fancy, or too long.
