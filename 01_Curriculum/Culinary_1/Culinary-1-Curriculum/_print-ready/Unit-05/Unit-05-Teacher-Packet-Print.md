## Unit Overview
**Weeks covered:** Week 5
**Focus:** Stabilize service before moving deeper into technical skills.

Students debrief the soft opening, retrain stations, clarify roles, strengthen ticket flow and communication, run mock service 2.0, prepare for Bistro, and practice service with better timing and reset.

## Week / Day Flow
- Monday: Soft opening debrief using facts, not blame.
- Tuesday: Station retraining and role clarity.
- Wednesday: Ticket flow, call-backs, expo, and communication standards.
- Thursday: Mock service 2.0 with recovery practice.
- Friday: Bistro prep/service and reset debrief.

## Setup Notes
- [ ] Bring notes from the soft opening.
- [ ] Post revised station roles and ticket path.
- [ ] Prepare mock tickets, station signs, and reset expectations.
- [ ] Decide what one or two systems matter most this week.

## Demo Notes
- Show how a ticket should move and be called back.
- Model role clarity: what is my job, who do I tell, what do I do next?
- Practice recovery after a breakdown.
- Model debrief language that names behavior and systems, not personalities.

## Safety Watch-Outs
- Keep movement controlled during service practice.
- Prevent crowding at expo and dish.
- Keep clean/dirty separation visible.
- Protect handwashing, sanitizer, towels, floors, and storage even when busy.

## Common Student Mistakes
- Students repeat soft opening problems without naming them.
- Stations wait for someone else to solve timing.
- Tickets are not called back.
- Dish flow becomes invisible.
- Students reset only their own area and ignore shared spaces.

## Cleanup / Reset Notes
- Assign reset ownership before service starts.
- Check dish, towels, floors, guest area, expo, and station tools.
- Use the debrief to identify one reset habit to improve next service.

## Bistro / Service Connection
Students learn that service systems are practiced habits. Better prep, clearer tickets, calmer communication, active dish flow, and full reset create better guest experience.
