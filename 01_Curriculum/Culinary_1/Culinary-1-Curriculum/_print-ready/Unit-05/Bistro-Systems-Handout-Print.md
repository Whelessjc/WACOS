## Purpose
The purpose of this handout is to help you understand the systems that make Bistro service calmer and more reliable.

Systems are not paperwork. Systems are the habits that help a team cook, communicate, serve, clean, and reset without guessing.

## Why Systems Reduce Confusion
Systems help everyone know:
- What station they are working
- What task comes first
- Where the ticket goes
- What words to use
- When to ask for help
- What needs to be cleaned
- What ready looks like

## Station Roles
Each station should know:
- Setup job
- Main service job
- Timing cue
- Ticket or task flow
- Safety or sanitation watch-out
- Cleanup/reset job
- Who to ask when unsure

## Ticket Flow
1. Ticket is readable.
2. Ticket goes to the correct station.
3. Station calls back key information.
4. Work starts.
5. Expo checks before handoff.
6. Order moves out.
7. Ticket/order is completed and reset.

## Communication Standards
Good service communication is:
- Short
- Clear
- Respectful
- Confirmed
- Useful

Useful phrases:
- "Heard"
- "Order in"
- "Working"
- "Order up"
- "I need help"
- "Reset needed"

## Prep Standards
- Read the prep list before acting.
- Know what must be ready first.
- Gather only what the station needs.
- Label and store items as directed.
- Keep tools and ingredients organized.
- Clean as you go.
- Leave prep useful for the next person.

## Service Standards
- Stay in role unless reassigned.
- Keep tickets or tasks visible.
- Communicate before the station falls behind.
- Ask for help before timing becomes a problem.
- Notice guest space and team needs.
- Move calmly and safely.
- Keep the station clean enough to work.

## Dish and Reset Standards
- Start dish flow before the end.
- Reset small things while service is still moving.
- Keep clean and dirty items separated.
- Return tools to the correct place.
- Handle towels correctly.
- Check trash and floors.
- Leave the station ready for the next group.
