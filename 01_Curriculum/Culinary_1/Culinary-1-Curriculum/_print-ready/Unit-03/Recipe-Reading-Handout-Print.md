## Purpose
The purpose of recipe reading is to know what you are doing before you touch ingredients. A recipe should help your group plan, measure, cook, clean, and reset.

## Parts of a Recipe
| Part | What It Means |
|---|---|
| Title | What you are making |
| Yield | How much the recipe makes |
| Ingredient list | Food items and amounts needed |
| Equipment list | Tools, pans, bowls, and machines needed |
| Procedure | Steps to follow |
| Timing | Mixing, cooking, baking, cooling, simmering, or holding time |
| Safety notes | Allergens, heat, raw food, sharp tools, or sanitation warnings |

## Ingredient List
When reading ingredients, check:
- Amount
- Unit of measure
- Ingredient form, such as chopped, melted, cooked, cold, or softened
- Allergens
- Whether the ingredient is shared with other groups

## Equipment List
Before starting, identify:
- Measuring tools
- Mixing tools
- Bowls or containers
- Sheet pans, pots, pans, or rice cooker
- Heat equipment if used
- Towels, sanitizer, and dish supplies

## Procedure
The procedure tells you what to do and in what order.

Before starting:
1. Read all steps.
2. Mark important steps.
3. Notice what must happen before cooking.
4. Notice what needs teacher approval.
5. Ask questions before touching ingredients.

## Yield
Yield tells how much the recipe makes.

Ask:
- How many servings or portions does this make?
- Is this enough for our group?
- Does the recipe need to be divided or shared?

## Timing
Look for:
- Mixing time
- Baking time
- Simmering time
- Cooling time
- Waiting or resting time
- Cleanup time while something cooks

## Sequencing
Sequencing means putting work in the best order.

Good sequence:
1. Read recipe.
2. Gather tools.
3. Gather ingredients.
4. Measure.
5. Set mise en place.
6. Cook or prepare.
7. Clean as you go.
8. Reset fully.

## What to Look For Before Starting
- What are we making?
- What does the recipe make?
- What ingredients do we need?
- What equipment do we need?
- Which steps happen first?
- Where are the safety concerns?
- What can we clean while we work?

## Cleanup and Reset Check
Before the recipe is truly finished:
- [ ] Shared ingredients are closed and returned.
- [ ] Tools are returned clean.
- [ ] Dishes are moving.
- [ ] Counter is cleaned and sanitized.
- [ ] Towels, trash, and floor are checked.
- [ ] Station is ready for the next group.

## Common Mistakes Students Make
- Starting before reading the full recipe
- Gathering ingredients but not tools
- Missing yield or timing
- Measuring with the wrong tool
- Adding ingredients out of order
- Ignoring allergens or heat warnings
- Waiting until the end to clean

## Short Practice Activity
Use a recipe your teacher provides.

Complete the following:
- Circle or list the ingredients.
- Underline or list the equipment.
- Find the yield.
- Put a star next to any timing direction.
- Put a box around any safety warning.
- Write the first three tasks your group should do before cooking.

## Reflection
1. What part of the recipe was easiest to find?
2. What part could be easy to miss?
3. What question should your group ask before starting?
