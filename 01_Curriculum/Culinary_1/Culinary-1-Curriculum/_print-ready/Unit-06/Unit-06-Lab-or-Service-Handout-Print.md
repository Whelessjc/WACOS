## Purpose
The purpose of knife practice is to build safe, useful knife control for real prep. The goal is control first, then consistency, then speed when ready. Minimum competence means safe, steady, useful work.

## Skills Practiced
- Knife safety
- Board setup
- Grip and claw
- Blade path
- Rough chop, dice, slice, mince
- Julienne and batonnet
- Prep list execution
- Sorting usable prep, scraps, and waste
- Cleanup and reset

## Equipment
- Chef knife
- Cutting board
- Damp towel
- Bowls or containers
- Bench scraper if available
- Sheet pan if needed
- Towels
- Sanitizer and dish supplies

## Practice Sequence
1. Complete entry routine and handwashing.
2. Set up board, towel, knife, and containers.
3. Watch the teacher demo.
4. Practice slowly with eyes on the cut.
5. Pause for grip, claw, and board checkpoint.
6. Complete assigned cut or prep list.
7. Sort usable prep, scraps, and waste.
8. Label or store prep if directed.
9. Clean and reset the full station.

## Cooking Application Options
Your knife practice may support:
- Stir fry
- Sauteed vegetables
- Soup base
- Roasted vegetables
- Potato batonnet
- Fries prep

Cut size affects cooking time, texture, browning, and service timing.

## Teamwork Expectations
- Give each person safe space.
- Keep knives down when talking.
- Do not rush another student.
- Help sort prep and scraps.
- Share dish, towels, trash, floor, and final reset.
- Communicate if the station becomes unsafe.

## Safety Reminders
- Control before speed.
- Keep claw hand tucked.
- Keep board stable.
- Keep eyes on the cut.
- Stop if the blade path becomes unsafe.
- Do not scrape with the knife edge.
- Stop if the station becomes unsafe.
