## Purpose
This lab helps you practice Unit 02 skills in a controlled, useful kitchen task.
## Skills Practiced
- food safety
- personal hygiene
- cleaning and sanitation
- cross contamination
- allergens
- time and temperature control
- thermometer use
- laundry, counters, dishes, storage, floors
- three-compartment sink and dish machine
## Equipment / Materials
Your teacher will assign the tools, equipment, and products for the day. Check the board, huddle notes, and station directions before starting.
## Possible Applications
- brownie batter
- baking brownies
- dish systems training
- chicken breast temperature lab

- [ ] Hands washed

- [ ] Tools gathered

## Procedure
1. Complete entry routine and handwashing.
2. Listen to the demo or mini-lesson.
3. Set up the station before starting.
4. Practice the assigned skill at a controlled pace.
5. Pause for teacher checkpoint.
6. Complete the lab or service application.
7. Clean, store, dish, and reset fully.
8. Complete reflection or debrief.
## Teamwork Expectations
- Stay in your role unless reassigned.
- Use clear, useful communication.
- Ask for help before the station falls behind.
- Share dish, towels, trash, floor, and reset.
## Safety Reminders
- Wash hands at required times.
- Keep raw, ready-to-eat, allergen, and clean items separated.
- Use sanitizer correctly.
- Use thermometer as evidence, not guessing.
- Follow three-compartment sink or dish machine directions.
## Cleanup and Reset Checklist
- [ ] Dishes routed correctly
- [ ] Counters cleaned and sanitized
- [ ] Laundry and towels handled correctly
- [ ] Storage checked and labeled if needed
- [ ] Trash and floors checked
- [ ] Station ready for the next group
## Reflection Questions
1. What skill did you practice?
2. What did you notice while working?
3. What correction helped?
4. How could this support Bistro service?
5. What reset job did you complete?

## Lab-Specific Checkpoints
- Brownie batter: prevent cross contamination, control shared tools, and clean sticky counters fully.
- Baking brownies: use oven safety, cooling space, dish flow, and storage directions.
- Chicken temperature lab: use the thermometer as evidence, clean the probe, and record the safe reading as directed.
