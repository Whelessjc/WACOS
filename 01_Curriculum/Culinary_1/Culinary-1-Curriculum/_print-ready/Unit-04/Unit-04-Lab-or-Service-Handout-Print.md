## Purpose of a Soft Opening
A soft opening is a controlled first service used for practice and feedback.

The goal is not speed. The goal is to practice roles, communication, hospitality, ticket flow, dish flow, safety, and reset.

## Station Assignments
Before service starts, know:
- Your station
- Your main job
- Your tools
- Your ticket or task flow
- Your cleanup/reset responsibility
- Who to ask when unsure

## Before, During, After
Before service:
- Set up your station.
- Check tools, towels, sanitizer, and tickets/tasks.
- Know your first job.

During service:
- Stay in role.
- Keep tickets or tasks visible.
- Communicate early.
- Keep clean and dirty items separated.

After service:
- Finish dish flow.
- Reset guest and service spaces.
- Return tools and towels.
- Wait for inspection before leaving the station.

## Service Expectations
- Listen during the huddle.
- Stay in your role unless the teacher changes it.
- Use clear communication.
- Keep your station clean.
- Support dish and reset before being asked.
- Debrief honestly without blaming people.

## Communication Expectations
Use short, clear language:
- "Behind"
- "Hot"
- "Corner"
- "Heard"
- "Order in"
- "Order up"
- "I need help"
- "Table reset"

## What To Do When Unsure
1. Pause.
2. Keep the station safe.
3. Ask the correct person or teacher.
4. Use clear words.
5. Return to your role.

## Guest Interaction Reminders
- Greet respectfully.
- Use a calm voice.
- Listen before responding.
- Keep guest space clean.
- Follow through on your task.
- Farewell matters.

## Sanitation Reminders
- Wash hands when directed.
- Keep stations clean.
- Keep dishes moving.
- Report spills immediately.
- Keep towels and floors controlled.
- Do not let reset wait until the room is out of control.

## Reset Expectations
- Guest area reset
- Service area reset
- Tickets or tasks accounted for
- Dishes routed correctly
- Counters cleaned and sanitized
- Tools returned
- Towels handled correctly
- Trash checked
- Floor checked
- Station ready for the next group

## Self-Check Questions
1. What is my role?
2. What does my station need before service?
3. What words should I use during service?
4. What should I do if I am unsure?
5. What reset job am I responsible for?
