## What We Are Learning
This week we are learning how service works from the moment a guest arrives until the space is reset.

You will practice:
- Greeting
- Order taking
- Ticket flow
- Station roles
- Expo
- Food running
- Check back
- Clearing
- Dish
- Reset
- Farewell

## Why Service Has a Full Flow
Bistro service is not just cooking. It is a team system. Guests notice how we communicate, move, clean, serve, and reset.

When one part of the flow breaks down, the whole order can slow down.

## What Life of an Order Means
Life of an Order is the path an order takes from guest arrival to farewell.

The order does not end when food leaves the kitchen. It ends when the guest space, service space, dish flow, and station are reset.

## What Success Looks Like
- I can explain the Life of an Order.
- I can tell where an order goes next.
- I can follow my station role.
- I can use clear service communication.
- I can help move tickets through service.
- I can treat guests and teammates with respect.
- I can help reset the room after service.

## Daily Kitchen Entry Routine
1. Enter calmly.
2. Put belongings away.
3. Check the agenda.
4. Check uniform/readiness.
5. Join the huddle.
6. Wash hands when directed.
7. Begin work only when released.

## Station Expectations
Before service:
- Know your role.
- Know your tools.
- Know your setup.
- Know your ticket or task flow.
- Know your reset job.
- Know who to ask when unsure.

During service:
- Stay in your role unless the teacher changes it.
- Communicate clearly.
- Keep tickets or tasks visible.
- Help the team before the station falls behind.
- Notice guest space and kitchen space.

After service:
- Complete reset.
- Close or account for tickets/tasks.
- Return tools.
- Support dish flow.
- Leave the station ready for the next group.

## Communication Expectations
Use short, clear language:
- "Behind"
- "Hot"
- "Corner"
- "Heard"
- "Order in"
- "Order up"
- "I need help"
- "Table reset"

## Guest Interaction Basics
Hospitality is practiced behavior:
- Greet respectfully.
- Use a calm voice.
- Listen before responding.
- Keep the guest space clean.
- Follow through on your task.
- Say farewell when appropriate.

## Sanitation During Service
- Wash hands when needed, especially after touching your face, phone, trash, or dirty dishes.
- Keep clean tools, dirty dishes, and guest items separated.
- Report spills right away.
- Keep towels, counters, and floors under control.
- Treat dish flow and reset as part of service.

## Ticket Flow Basics
1. Guest is greeted.
2. Order is taken.
3. Ticket moves to the correct station.
4. Station confirms the order.
5. Food or service item is prepared.
6. Expo checks the order.
7. Food is run or handed off.
8. Guest is checked on.
9. Space is cleared.
10. Dishes move correctly.
11. Station and guest space reset.
12. Farewell closes the order.

## Prep and Reset Expectations
- Prep supports service.
- Dish and reset are service work.
- Cleaning is not punishment.
- A station is not finished until the next group could use it.
- If you are unsure, ask before the system falls apart.
