## What We Are Learning
This week we are learning how our kitchen works before we cook very much.

You will practice:
- Entering class
- Checking the agenda
- Joining the huddle
- Wearing the correct uniform
- Washing hands
- Moving safely
- Measuring
- Setting up a station
- Cleaning and resetting

## Why We Are Learning It
Culinary Arts Management 1 is about real cooking and real skills. A kitchen works better when people are safe, organized, respectful, and ready to help.

Before we cook bigger labs, we need systems.

## What Success Looks Like
- I follow the entry routine.
- I understand uniform/readiness expectations.
- I wash hands when needed.
- I use kitchen communication.
- I measure with care.
- I help set up, cook, clean, and reset.
- I leave the station ready for the next group.

## Daily Expectations
Every day, be ready to:
1. Enter calmly.
2. Put belongings where they belong.
3. Check the agenda.
4. Check uniform/readiness.
5. Go to the assigned starting area.
6. Listen during huddle.
7. Wash hands when directed.
8. Begin work only when released.

## Uniform Expectations
Your teacher will explain the school-specific uniform policy.

In general, uniforms help with:
- Safety
- Sanitation
- Professional habits
- Readiness to work

Be ready to follow expectations for:
- Hair restraint
- Shoes
- Jewelry
- Loose items
- Any school-required uniform pieces

## Handwashing Expectations
Wash hands:
- Before handling food
- After touching your face, hair, phone, trash, floor, or dirty dishes
- After using the restroom
- After coughing, sneezing, or blowing your nose
- After handling anything contaminated
- Any time the teacher tells the class to reset

## Basic Safety Expectations
- Walk in the kitchen.
- Keep pathways clear.
- Report spills right away.
- Ask before using equipment.
- Treat hot equipment as hot.
- Keep towels away from heat.
- Keep food and cleaning chemicals separate.

## Kitchen Communication
Use short, clear words so people know what is happening:
- "Behind"
- "Hot"
- "Sharp"
- "Corner"
- "Heard"
- "I need help"

## Measuring Expectations
- Use the correct tool for the ingredient.
- Measure dry ingredients carefully.
- Read liquid measurements at eye level.
- Use measuring spoons for small amounts.
- Do not rush.
- Return tools where they belong.

## First Lab Expectations
Our first lab is pancakes.

The goal is not perfect pancakes. The goal is to practice:
- Reading directions before starting
- Measuring ingredients
- Setting up mise en place
- Working in a team
- Watching heat
- Cleaning as you go
- Resetting the station

## Cleanup and Reset Checklist
- [ ] Dishes handled correctly

- [ ] Tools returned
- [ ] Towels handled correctly
- [ ] Trash checked
- [ ] Floor checked
- [ ] Heat source off
- [ ] Station ready for the next group

## Bistro Connection
The routines you learn this week will show up later in Bistro. Service works better when students know where things belong, communicate clearly, wash hands, set up before starting, and leave stations ready for the next group.
