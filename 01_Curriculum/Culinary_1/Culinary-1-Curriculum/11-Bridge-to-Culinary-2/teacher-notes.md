# Teacher Notes: Bridge to Culinary 2

## What to Emphasize
- Growth should be specific.
- Readiness is about habits and responsibility, not ego.
- Prep lists show independence.
- Ownership means following through.
- Not every student has the same next step.
- Final reset is part of readiness, not a chore after readiness.
- Bistro readiness should be practical: plan the work, communicate, finish the role, and reset.

## Common Student Mistakes
- Writing vague reflections like "I got better at cooking."
- Overrating readiness without evidence.
- Waiting for the teacher during independent tasks.
- Ignoring cleanup during final-week activities.
- Writing readiness claims without examples from actual labs.

## Classroom Management Notes
- Keep tasks simple and accountable.
- Use conferences to clarify next steps.
- Give students language for honest reflection.
- Maintain kitchen standards through the last day.
- Ask for evidence: "Where did you show that?"

## Safety Concerns
- Students relaxing expectations at the end.
- Rushed cleanup.
- Independent work without enough supervision.

## Pacing Notes
Protect time for reflection and conversations. Do not overpack the final week with new cooking.

## Repetition Needed
Prep lists, station ownership, reset, stewardship, and honest self-assessment.

## Connection to Later Units
This unit bridges into Culinary 2 by naming which students are ready for more independence in prep, service, communication, and stewardship, and what each student should practice next.
