# Unit 11 Teacher Packet: Bridge to Culinary 2

## Unit Overview
Week 18 focus: independence and readiness.

Students reflect on growth, create independent prep lists, take limited prep or service ownership, complete a skill inventory and self-assessment, and participate in a Culinary 2 interest/readiness conversation.

## Week at a Glance
| Day | Focus | Main Application |
|---|---|---|
| Monday | Skill inventory | Evidence from labs, service, cleanup, teamwork |
| Tuesday | Independent prep list | Tools, ingredients, timing, safety, cleanup |
| Wednesday | Limited service ownership | Assigned prep or service role |
| Thursday | Reflection and readiness | Self-assessment and teacher conversations |
| Friday | Course closure | Final reflection, simple review task, final reset |

## Daily Lesson Flow

### Monday: Skill Inventory
- Opening: huddle on growth and real kitchen habits.
- Mini-lesson/demo: naming specific skills with evidence.
- Student work: complete skill inventory.
- Application: quick station, tool, or routine review.
- Prompt: "Where did you show that skill?"
- Reset: materials and room reset.

### Tuesday: Independent Prep List
- Opening: agenda and task preview.
- Mini-lesson/demo: building a prep list from a simple task.
- Student work: create a prep list with tools, ingredients, timing, safety, and cleanup.
- Application: prep list conference or small prep task.
- Prompt: "What did you plan before acting?"
- Reset: tools returned, counters cleaned, station reset.

### Wednesday: Limited Service Ownership
- Opening: role huddle.
- Mini-lesson/demo: ownership before, during, and after service.
- Student work: take assigned prep or service role.
- Application: limited service ownership task.
- Prompt: "How did you support the team without being chased?"
- Reset: station, dish, towel, floor, and service area reset.

### Thursday: Reflection and Readiness Conversation
- Opening: quiet reflection setup.
- Mini-lesson/demo: honest self-assessment with evidence.
- Student work: reflect on strengths, habits, safety, stewardship, and next steps.
- Application: teacher/student readiness conversations as possible.
- Prompt: "What evidence supports your readiness?"
- Reset: materials and room reset.

### Friday: Course Closure and Transition
- Opening: final huddle.
- Mini-lesson/demo: Culinary 2 expectations and continued growth through real work.
- Student work: complete final reflection or conversation.
- Application: simple review task, service support, or final reset as schedule allows.
- Prompt: "How are you more capable now?"
- Reset: full course reset with stewardship standard.

## Setup Notes
- Prepare skill inventory, prep list format, and reflection prompts.
- Choose a simple prep/service task students can plan.
- Assign limited ownership roles.
- Keep expectations realistic.
- Protect time for individual readiness conversations.
- Maintain full kitchen standards through the last day.

## Teacher Language and Prompts
- "Readiness is about habits and responsibility."
- "Use evidence, not ego."
- "Where did you show that?"
- "Ownership means following through."
- "Final reset is part of readiness."
- "Not everyone has the same next step, and that is okay."

## Safety Reminders
- Do not relax standards during the final week.
- Supervise independent work.
- Keep cleanup from being rushed.
- Maintain handwashing, sanitation, storage, and floor checks.
- Use equipment only as approved.

## Classroom Management Reminders
- Keep final-week tasks simple and accountable.
- Ask students for evidence from real labs.
- Use conferences to clarify next steps.
- Do not let reflection replace stewardship.
- Keep the room calm and purposeful.

## Cleanup and Reset Expectations
Final-week work still requires:
- Tools returned
- Counters cleaned and sanitized
- Dishes handled correctly
- Storage checked
- Towels handled correctly
- Trash checked
- Floors checked
- Station ready for the next group
- Kitchen left better than students found it

## Unit Lab Plan
Purpose: give students a practical way to show independence, prep planning, service ownership, reflection, and final stewardship.

Skills practiced:
- Skill inventory
- Independent prep list creation
- Limited station ownership
- Service or prep responsibility
- Honest reflection
- Final reset

Suggested roles:
- Prep owner: creates and follows a prep list
- Station owner: manages tools, setup, and reset
- Service owner: completes a limited guest/team role
- Reflection owner: gathers evidence for readiness
- Steward: leads final dish, towels, trash, floor, storage, and reset

Teacher checkpoints:
- Prep list is realistic
- Student works with appropriate independence
- Safety and sanitation continue
- Role is completed
- Reflection uses evidence
- Final reset is complete

## Bistro Connection
Students show future Bistro and Culinary 2 readiness by planning work, communicating, completing a role, and resetting the kitchen without being chased.
