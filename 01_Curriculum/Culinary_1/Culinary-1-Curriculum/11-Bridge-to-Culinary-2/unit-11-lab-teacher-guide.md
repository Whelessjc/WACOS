# Unit 11 Lab Teacher Guide: Readiness and Limited Ownership

## Setup Before Class
- Choose a simple prep, service, or review task students can plan.
- Prepare skill inventory, prep list, and reflection prompts.
- Assign limited ownership roles.
- Stage only the tools and ingredients needed.
- Schedule time for short readiness conversations.
- Keep final reset expectations visible.

## Demo Points
- How to create a prep list from a simple task.
- How to name safety and cleanup in the plan.
- What limited ownership looks like.
- How to use evidence in reflection.
- What final reset should look like.

## Safety Watch-Outs
- Students relaxing standards at the end of the course.
- Independent work without enough checking.
- Rushed cleanup.
- Tools or ingredients left out after final tasks.
- Vague claims of readiness without evidence.

## Skill Checkpoints
- Prep list is realistic.
- Student can explain the plan.
- Safety and sanitation stay visible.
- Student follows through on the role.
- Reflection uses course evidence.
- Final reset is complete without repeated reminders.

## Common Student Mistakes
- Writing "I got better at cooking" without examples.
- Overrating readiness.
- Waiting for the teacher to direct every step.
- Forgetting cleanup during final-week work.
- Treating the last week as relaxed time.

## Cleanup Timing
- Leave enough time for final reset.
- Check storage before counters are wiped.
- Inspect dish, towels, trash, and floors.
- Require incomplete reset to be fixed.
- Use final reset as readiness evidence.

## What to Look For While Circulating
- Can students plan before acting?
- Are students working with appropriate independence?
- Are safety habits still present?
- Are roles completed?
- Is reflection honest and specific?
- Is reset happening without being chased?

## Bistro Connection
Limited ownership mirrors future Bistro and Culinary 2 responsibility: plan the work, communicate, complete the role, and reset the station.

## Quick Debrief Prompts
- What did you plan first?
- Where did you show independence?
- What evidence supports your readiness?
- What should you practice next?
