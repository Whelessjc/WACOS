# Unit 11: Bridge to Culinary 2

## Weeks Covered
Week 18

## Focus
Independence and readiness.

## Purpose
Students reflect on growth, create independent prep lists, take limited service ownership, complete a skill inventory and self-assessment, and participate in a Culinary 2 interest/readiness conversation. The unit closes the course by asking students to show they are safer, more aware, more responsible, and more confident in real kitchen work.

## Learning Targets
- I can identify the skills I improved in Culinary 1.
- I can name habits I still need to strengthen.
- I can create a basic prep list for a kitchen task.
- I can take ownership of a limited prep or service role.
- I can explain what readiness for Culinary 2 looks like.
- I can show stewardship by leaving the kitchen ready for the next group.

## Core Skills
- Self-assessment
- Skill inventory
- Independent prep list creation
- Limited service ownership
- Reflection
- Readiness conversation
- Final reset and stewardship

## Vocabulary
| Term | Student-Friendly Meaning |
|---|---|
| Readiness | Being prepared to work safely and responsibly. |
| Skill inventory | A list of skills you can do and skills still growing. |
| Ownership | Taking responsibility for a task or station. |
| Independence | Working with less direct help while following expectations. |
| Reflection | Thinking honestly about what happened and what to improve. |
| Transition | Moving from one level of learning to the next. |

## Lab Applications
- Independent prep list task
- Limited service ownership
- Skill review lab
- Self-assessment and reflection

## Bistro Connection
Students show whether they can carry more responsibility in future Bistro and Culinary 2 work by planning, communicating, completing a role, and resetting the kitchen without being chased.

## Assessment Ideas
- Skill inventory
- Prep list review
- Limited service role observation
- Final reflection
- Readiness conversation notes

## Teacher Notes
Keep this honest and encouraging. The goal is clear next steps, not pretending everyone is ready for the same responsibility. Readiness should be tied to evidence from real kitchen habits.

## Resources Needed
- Equipment: based on selected review or service task
- Ingredients: simple review or service ingredients
- Materials: skill inventory, reflection prompts, prep list format
