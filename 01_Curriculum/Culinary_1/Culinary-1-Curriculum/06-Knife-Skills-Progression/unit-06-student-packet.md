# Unit 06 Student Packet: Knife Skills Progression

## What We Are Learning
Over the next four weeks, we are building knife control through repeated practice. You will practice safe carrying, board setup, grip, claw, rough chop, slice, dice, mince, julienne, batonnet, prep lists, and deep clean reset.

## Why We Are Learning It
Knife skills make prep safer, faster, and more consistent. Bistro prep depends on people who can cut with control, organize product, and reset the station.

## What Success Looks Like
- I can set up a safe cutting station.
- I can use grip and claw.
- I can complete basic cuts with control.
- I can improve speed without losing safety.
- I can use a prep list.
- I can clean and reset a knife station.

## Daily Kitchen Entry Routine
1. Enter calmly.
2. Put belongings away.
3. Check the agenda.
4. Check uniform/readiness.
5. Join the huddle.
6. Wash hands when directed.
7. Begin work only when released.

## Key Expectations
- Control comes before speed.
- Knives stay down when the teacher is talking.
- Board setup matters every time.
- Claw protects your guiding hand.
- Cut size affects cooking.
- Cleanup is part of knife safety.

## Kitchen Communication
- "Sharp"
- "Behind"
- "Knife down"
- "Heard"
- "I need help"
- "Where do scraps go?"

## Safety Expectations
- Carry knives safely.
- Keep boards stable.
- Keep fingers in claw position.
- Do not rush.
- Do not scrape with the knife edge.
- Clean and store knives as directed.

## Knife Practice Expectations
- Watch the demo first.
- Practice slowly.
- Pause for teacher checkpoints.
- Sort usable prep, scraps, and waste.
- Label or store prep when directed.
- Reset before leaving the station.

## Cleanup and Reset Checklist
- [ ] Knife cleaned and stored safely
- [ ] Board washed correctly
- [ ] Counters cleaned and sanitized
- [ ] Usable prep stored or labeled as directed
- [ ] Scraps and waste handled
- [ ] Towels handled correctly
- [ ] Trash checked
- [ ] Floor checked
- [ ] Station ready for the next group

## Bistro Connection
Bistro needs consistent prep. A station with clean cuts, labeled containers, and safe tools helps the team work faster and serve food more evenly.

## Student Reflection Questions
1. Which cut felt most controlled today?
2. What safety habit did you use?
3. What cut needs more practice?
4. How did your prep support the team?
5. What did you do to reset the station?
