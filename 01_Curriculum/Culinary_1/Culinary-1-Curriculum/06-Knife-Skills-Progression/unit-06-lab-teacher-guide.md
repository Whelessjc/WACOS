# Unit 06 Lab Teacher Guide: Knife Skills Progression

## Setup Before Class
- Stage knives, boards, damp towels, bowls, containers, sanitizer, and towels.
- Space students before knives are issued.
- Prepare practice vegetables or potatoes by skill level.
- Post the prep list and stop signal.
- Decide how usable prep, scraps, and waste will be handled.
- Prepare deep clean zones for Week 9.

## Demo Points
- Safe knife carry and set-down.
- Stable board setup.
- Grip and claw.
- Slow practice before speed.
- One cut at a time: rough chop, slice, dice, mince, julienne, or batonnet.
- What a fully reset knife station looks like.

## Safety Watch-Outs
- Flat fingers.
- Students cutting too fast.
- Unstable boards.
- Crowding.
- Knife tips wandering.
- Students scraping with knife edge.
- Knives left in sinks or under towels.

## Skill Checkpoints
- Board is stable.
- Grip and claw are visible.
- Student is cutting at a controlled pace.
- Cut size fits the task.
- Usable prep is separated from scraps.
- Knife and board reset is complete.

## Common Student Mistakes
- Trying to be fast before controlled.
- Forgetting the damp towel.
- Looking away while cutting.
- Mixing waste with usable product.
- Leaving board mess until it becomes unsafe.
- Treating cleanup as separate from knife work.

## Cleanup Timing
- Stop practice early enough for safe knife cleanup.
- Collect or supervise knife washing/storage as required.
- Check boards and counters before students leave.
- Inspect floor near each station.
- Use Week 9 for deeper station, storage, and tool reset.

## What to Look For While Circulating
- Are students spaced safely?
- Are knives down during directions?
- Are cuts improving with feedback?
- Are students sorting product correctly?
- Is the station staying clean enough to keep working?
- Is reset shared?

## Bistro Connection
Connect every cut to a prep list or service use. Students should see that knife skills support timing, batch work, station setup, consistency, and storage.

## Quick Debrief Prompts
- What cut improved today?
- What safety habit did you repeat?
- What prep would be useful for Bistro?
- What needs more repetition?
