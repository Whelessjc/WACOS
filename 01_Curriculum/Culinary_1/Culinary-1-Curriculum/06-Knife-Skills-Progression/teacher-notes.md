# Teacher Notes: Knife Skills Progression

## What to Emphasize
- Control before speed.
- A safe board setup is required every time.
- Grip and claw protect students.
- Cut consistency affects cooking.
- Prep lists give knife work a real purpose.
- Clean board management is part of knife safety.
- Cut size, labeled prep, and batch containers affect Bistro timing and consistency.

## Common Student Mistakes
- Flat fingers instead of claw.
- Knife tip or handle wandering.
- Cutting too fast.
- Unstable board.
- Scraping food with knife edge.
- Ignoring cleanup around the board.
- Mixing usable prep with scraps or waste.

## Classroom Management Notes
- Stop unsafe knife behavior immediately.
- Keep students spaced out.
- Use clear start/stop signals.
- Require knives down when giving directions.
- Give short focused demos, then practice.

## Safety Concerns
- Cuts from rushing or poor hand position.
- Knives carried incorrectly.
- Crowding at sinks or ingredient stations.
- Wet cutting boards or floors.

## Pacing Notes
Four weeks allows repetition. Do not try to cover every cut at once. Revisit basics constantly.

## Repetition Needed
Board setup, grip, claw, safe carry, clean board management, scrap control, labeled prep, and controlled pace.

## Connection to Later Units
Heat control, frying, flavor systems, protein cookery, ticket flow, and Bistro prep all depend on safe, repeatable knife habits.
