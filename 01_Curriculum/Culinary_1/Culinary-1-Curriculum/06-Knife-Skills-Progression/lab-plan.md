# Lab Plan: Knife Skills Progression

## Lab Purpose
Build safe, useful knife control through repeated practice and prep-list applications.

## Skill Focus
- Knife safety
- Grip and claw
- Board setup
- Rough chop, dice, slice, mince
- Julienne and batonnet
- Prep list execution
- Sorting usable prep, scraps, and waste

## Ingredients or Materials
- Low-cost practice vegetables
- Potatoes
- Stir fry vegetables
- Soup base vegetables
- Roasting vegetables

## Equipment
- Chef knives
- Cutting boards
- Damp towels
- Bowls or hotel pans
- Bench scrapers
- Sheet pans
- Towels and sanitizer

## Setup
- Space students safely.
- Set boards with damp towel underneath.
- Stage vegetables by cut type.
- Review knife carry and stop signal.

## Procedure
1. Complete opening routine and handwashing.
2. Set up board and tools.
3. Watch teacher demo.
4. Practice slowly.
5. Pause for grip/claw checkpoint.
6. Complete assigned prep list.
7. Sort usable product, scraps, and waste as directed.
8. Label or store prep if it supports Bistro.
9. Clean knives, boards, counters, floors, and reset.

## Teacher Checkpoints
- Board stable.
- Grip and claw visible.
- Knife stays controlled.
- Cut size matches the task.
- Student slows down when needed.

## Cleanup and Reset Expectations
Knives cleaned and stored safely, boards washed, counters sanitized, food stored or discarded, floors checked, towels handled.

## Possible Variations
- Use softer produce for early practice.
- Make advanced cuts teacher-demo only for groups needing more time.
- Connect cut practice to Bistro prep when possible.
