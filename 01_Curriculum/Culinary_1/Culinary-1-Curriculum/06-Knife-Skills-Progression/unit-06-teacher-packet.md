# Unit 06 Teacher Packet: Knife Skills Progression

## Unit Overview
Weeks 6 to 9 focus: build confidence through control and repetition.

Students practice knife safety, grip, claw, board setup, rough chop, slice, dice, mince, julienne, batonnet, speed with control, prep list execution, and knife station stewardship. Applications include stir fry, sauteed vegetables, soup base, roasted vegetables, potato batonnet, fries, and a Week 9 deep clean reset.

## Weeks at a Glance
| Week | Focus | Main Application |
|---|---|---|
| Week 6 | Safety, grip, claw, rough chop, slice | Controlled vegetable prep and Bistro prep |
| Week 7 | Dice, mince, prep lists | Soup base or sauteed vegetable prep |
| Week 8 | Julienne, batonnet, roasting/fries prep | Roasted vegetables, potato batonnet, fries prep |
| Week 9 | Speed with control and deep clean | Timed prep, stir fry/saute, deep clean reset |

## Day-by-Day Teaching Flow

### Week 6: Safety, Grip, Claw, Rough Chop, Slice
- Day 1: Knife safety, carrying, board setup, stop signal, dry hand practice, full reset.
- Day 2: Grip and claw demo, slow slice/rough chop practice, teacher checkpoint, knife/board reset.
- Day 3: Controlled vegetable prep with usable prep/scrap sorting, station reset.
- Day 4: Simple Bistro prep using basic cuts, labeled storage, dish and counter reset.
- Day 5: Bistro prep/service application or review, debrief on control before speed.

### Week 7: Dice, Mince, and Prep Lists
- Day 1: Review grip/claw, dice demo, slow dice practice, reset.
- Day 2: Mince demo, safe chopping/rocking as appropriate, practice and reset.
- Day 3: Knife-to-prep-list task for soup base or sauteed vegetables.
- Day 4: Bistro prep with cut standards, containers, labels, storage, and station check.
- Day 5: Bistro service/application and debrief on how cut size affects cooking.

### Week 8: Julienne, Batonnet, Roasting, Fries Prep
- Day 1: Julienne and batonnet demo, slow practice, reset.
- Day 2: Potato batonnet practice with teacher checkpoints and scrap control.
- Day 3: Roasted vegetables or fries prep application with sheet pan/station reset.
- Day 4: Bistro prep using assigned cuts, batch containers, labels, and storage.
- Day 5: Bistro service/application and debrief on control under time pressure.

### Week 9: Speed with Control and Deep Clean Reset
- Day 1: Review all cuts, speed with control reminder, short practice and reset.
- Day 2: Timed prep list with safety stops, teacher checkpoints, reset.
- Day 3: Stir fry or sauteed vegetable application using prep list execution.
- Day 4: Week 9 deep clean reset: stations, tools, storage, floors, dish area.
- Day 5: Skill inventory, reflection, Bistro prep/service as schedule allows.

Daily pattern: opening routine, knife safety reminder, short demo, student cutting practice, teacher checkpoint, cleanup/reset, and exit reflection.

## Setup Notes
- Stage knives, cutting boards, damp towels, bowls, bench scrapers, sheet pans, sanitizer, and towels.
- Space students safely before knives are issued.
- Prepare low-cost vegetables by cut type.
- Post or review the stop signal.
- Prepare prep lists so knife work connects to real service.
- Decide how usable prep, scraps, and waste will be sorted.
- Plan Week 9 deep clean zones before the week begins.

## Teacher Language and Prompts
- "Control before speed."
- "A stable board comes before a cut."
- "Claw protects your fingers."
- "Cut size affects cooking."
- "Knife work feeds prep lists, not just practice bowls."
- "A clean board is part of knife safety."

## Safety Reminders
- Knives down when directions are given.
- Carry knives correctly.
- Keep boards stable with damp towels.
- Keep fingers in claw position.
- Do not scrape with the knife edge.
- Keep students spaced out.
- Wash and store knives safely.

## Classroom Management Reminders
- Stop unsafe knife behavior immediately.
- Keep demos short and focused.
- Use clear start/stop signals.
- Correct hand position early.
- Slow students down when control slips.
- Do not reward speed without control.

## Cleanup and Reset Expectations
Knife labs are not finished until:
- Knives are cleaned and stored safely
- Boards are washed correctly
- Counters are cleaned and sanitized
- Usable prep is stored or labeled
- Scraps and waste are handled
- Towels are handled correctly
- Floors are checked
- Station is ready for the next group

## Unit Lab Plan
Purpose: build safe, useful knife control through repeated practice and prep-list applications.

Skills practiced:
- Knife safety and carrying
- Board setup
- Grip and claw
- Rough chop, slice, dice, mince
- Julienne and batonnet
- Prep list execution
- Sorting usable prep, scraps, and waste
- Deep clean reset

Suggested roles:
- Cutter: completes assigned cuts with control
- Prep reader: tracks the prep list
- Sorter: separates usable prep, scraps, and waste
- Equipment lead: manages boards, bowls, and tools
- Steward: leads counters, towels, floors, dish, and final reset

Teacher checkpoints:
- Board is stable
- Grip and claw are visible
- Knife stays controlled
- Cut size fits the task
- Usable prep is handled correctly
- Knife station reset is complete

## Bistro Connection
Knife work supports Bistro prep through consistency, timing, batch containers, labeled storage, and station readiness. A clean knife station makes service prep safer and faster.
