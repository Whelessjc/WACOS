# Unit 09 Lab Student Handout: Flavor Systems

## Purpose of the Lab
The purpose of this lab is to learn how flavor is built through tasting, comparison, aromatics, and small adjustments.

## Skills Practiced
- Safe tasting
- Flavor vocabulary
- Seasoning gradually
- Aromatic cooking
- Balance and adjustment
- Respectful comparison
- Allergen-aware cleanup and reset

## Equipment Needed
- Tasting spoons
- Small bowls or cups
- Measuring spoons
- Saute pan if assigned
- Cutting board and knife if assigned
- Towels
- Sanitizer and dish supplies

## Ingredients / Materials
Your teacher may assign salt, vinegar or citrus, oil or fat, aromatics, herbs, spices, soy and ginger, chili and citrus, yogurt and spice, coconut and curry spices, and a simple base food.

## Mise en Place / Setup Checklist
- [ ] Hands washed
- [ ] Allergens named
- [ ] Clean tasting spoons ready
- [ ] Dirty spoon area ready
- [ ] Ingredients portioned as directed
- [ ] Recording sheet or notes ready
- [ ] Sanitizer and towel ready
- [ ] Teacher tasting rules understood

## Procedure
1. Complete the entry routine and tasting huddle.
2. Taste the base food with a clean spoon.
3. Describe the flavor using specific words.
4. Add one small teacher-approved flavor element.
5. Taste again with a clean spoon.
6. Describe what changed.
7. Compare another flavor build if assigned.
8. Clean and reset with allergen awareness.

## Teamwork Expectations
- Use clean spoons every time.
- Keep tasting tools organized.
- Add only small amounts.
- Use respectful food-focused language.
- Share cleanup.

## Safety Reminders
- Do not double-dip.
- Check allergens before tasting.
- Keep soy, dairy/yogurt, coconut, chili, and spice areas controlled.
- Wash hands if contaminated.
- Use hot-pan safety for aromatic work.

## Cleanup and Reset Checklist
- [ ] Tasting spoons/cups handled correctly
- [ ] Counters cleaned and sanitized
- [ ] Allergen surfaces cleaned
- [ ] Ingredients closed and stored
- [ ] Tools washed
- [ ] Towels handled correctly
- [ ] Trash checked
- [ ] Floor checked
- [ ] Station ready for the next group

## Bistro Connection
Flavor checks protect guests and improve consistency. A cook should taste safely, adjust carefully, communicate clearly, and keep allergens controlled.

## Reflection Questions
1. What did the base taste like?
2. What did you add?
3. What changed?
4. What flavor word best described the result?
5. How did your group taste safely?
