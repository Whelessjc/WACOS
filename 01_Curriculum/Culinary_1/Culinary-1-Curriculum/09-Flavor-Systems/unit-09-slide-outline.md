# Unit 09 Slide Outline: Flavor Systems

## Slide 1: Unit 09
- Flavor Systems
- Build flavor
- Taste safely

## Slide 2: Why This Unit Matters
- Recipes are a start
- Cooks notice flavor
- Small changes matter
- Guests need safe tasting habits

## Slide 3: Daily Routine
- Enter calmly
- Check agenda and readiness
- Join huddle
- Wash hands
- Wait for release

## Slide 4: Safe Tasting
- Clean spoon every time
- No double-dipping
- Dirty spoon area
- Allergens named

## Slide 5: Flavor Words
- Salty
- Sour or bright
- Rich or fatty
- Aromatic
- Balanced

## Slide 6: Salt, Acid, Fat
- Salt clarifies
- Acid brightens
- Fat carries flavor
- Too much can overpower

## Slide 7: Aromatics
- Onion
- Garlic
- Ginger
- Peppers
- Heat changes smell and flavor

## Slide 8: Herbs and Spices
- Smell first
- Add small amounts
- Taste again
- Keep containers clean
- Close and return shared ingredients

## Slide 9: Balance
- Taste base
- Add one thing
- Taste again
- Explain what changed

## Slide 10: Flavor Builds
- Soy and ginger
- Vinegar and herbs
- Chili and citrus
- Yogurt and spice
- Coconut and curry

## Slide 11: Respectful Comparison
- Be specific
- Be curious
- No stereotypes
- No claims of authenticity

## Slide 12: Bistro Connection
- Taste before service
- Communicate adjustments
- Protect allergen boundaries
- Keep batches consistent
- Leave tasting area safe for others

## Slide 13: Cleanup and Reset
- Tasting tools handled
- Allergens cleaned
- Ingredients stored
- Counters sanitized
- Floors checked
- Shared seasonings cared for

## Slide 14: Exit Reflection
- What changed?
- What did you add?
- What word described it?
