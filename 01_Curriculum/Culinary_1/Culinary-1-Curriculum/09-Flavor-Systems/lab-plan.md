# Lab Plan: Flavor Systems

## Lab Purpose
Help students understand how flavor is built through tasting, comparison, aromatics, and small adjustments.

## Skill Focus
- Safe tasting
- Seasoning gradually
- Aromatic cooking
- Flavor vocabulary
- Balance
- Respectful comparison
- Allergen-aware tasting and reset

## Ingredients or Materials
- Salt, vinegar or citrus, oil or fat
- Onion, garlic, ginger, peppers, or other aromatics
- Herbs and spices
- Soy and ginger
- Vinegar and herbs
- Chili and citrus
- Yogurt and spice
- Coconut and curry spices
- Simple base food selected by teacher

## Equipment
- Tasting spoons
- Small bowls
- Saute pans
- Cutting boards and knives
- Measuring spoons
- Towels and sanitizer

## Setup
- Portion strong seasonings.
- Set tasting spoon rules.
- Identify allergens.
- Prepare simple base foods for comparison.
- Set a clear discard/dirty spoon area.

## Procedure
1. Review safe tasting.
2. Taste base food.
3. Add one controlled flavor element.
4. Taste and describe.
5. Make one small adjustment.
6. Compare with another flavor build.
7. Clean and reset tasting/cooking stations with allergen awareness.

## Teacher Checkpoints
- Tasting is sanitary.
- Additions are small.
- Student descriptions are specific.
- Cultural references remain respectful.
- Station stays organized.
- Tasting tools are not reused unsafely.

## Cleanup and Reset Expectations
Tasting cups/spoons handled, counters sanitized, allergens controlled, tools washed, ingredients stored, floor checked.

## Possible Variations
- Use teacher-prepared bases if time is short.
- Make aromatic cooking a demo for large classes.
- Limit to three flavor builds if supplies are tight.
