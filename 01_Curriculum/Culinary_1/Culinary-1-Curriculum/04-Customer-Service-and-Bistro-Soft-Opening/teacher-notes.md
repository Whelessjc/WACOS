# Teacher Notes: Customer Service and Bistro Soft Opening

## What to Emphasize
- Hospitality is a skill.
- Every order has a path.
- Students need clear station roles.
- Communication should be short, respectful, and useful.
- Reset is part of service.
- Dish, clearing, and cleaning are hospitality because they shape the guest experience.
- Stewardship means students take care of the guest space, kitchen space, and dish flow together.

## Common Student Mistakes
- Forgetting to greet or acknowledge guests.
- Losing tickets or not calling back.
- Crowding expo.
- Leaving dish/reset until the end.
- Treating front and back of house as separate teams.
- Forgetting that dirty tables, full dish areas, and messy stations slow service.

## Classroom Management Notes
- Keep the soft opening limited.
- Assign roles clearly and visibly.
- Stop service practice to fix ticket flow if needed.
- Debrief with facts, not blame.

## Safety Concerns
- Crowded movement during service.
- Hot food or pans moving through the room.
- Dish area bottlenecks.
- Wet floors during cleanup.

## Pacing Notes
Do not expand the menu because students seem excited. Stabilize the flow first.

## Repetition Needed
Greeting, ticket call-back, station reset, dish flow, table clearing, and farewell need repeated rehearsal.

## Connection to Later Units
This unit sets the service structure that Weeks 5 and 6-9 will keep using during Bistro prep and service.
