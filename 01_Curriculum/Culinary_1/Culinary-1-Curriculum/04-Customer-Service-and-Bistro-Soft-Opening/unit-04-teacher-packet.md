# Unit 04 Teacher Packet: Customer Service and Bistro Soft Opening

## Unit Overview
Week 4 focus: understand the full flow of service.

Students learn the Life of an Order from guest arrival to farewell. The week is about hospitality, ticket flow, station roles, expo, food running, clearing, dish, reset, mock service, Bistro prep, and a controlled soft opening.

## Week at a Glance
| Day | Focus | Main Application |
|---|---|---|
| Monday | Life of an Order | Trace guest arrival through farewell and reset |
| Tuesday | Station assignments | Station setup, duties, and reset practice |
| Wednesday | Ticket flow and expo | Mock service with simple tickets |
| Thursday | Bistro prep | Prep list, station readiness, storage, reset |
| Friday | Soft opening | Controlled service, debrief, full reset |

## Daily Lesson Flow

### Monday: Life of an Order
- Opening: entry routine, agenda check, huddle.
- Mini-lesson/demo: guest arrival, greeting, order, ticket, cooking, expo, running, check back, clearing, dish, reset, farewell.
- Student work: trace one order through the full path.
- Application: role-play a simple service flow.
- Prompt: "Where can an order break down?"
- Reset: guest area, service area, dish path, and room reset.

### Tuesday: Station Assignments and Training
- Opening: huddle on station ownership.
- Mini-lesson/demo: what each station does before, during, and after service.
- Student work: learn assigned station expectations.
- Application: station setup and reset practice.
- Prompt: "What is your station responsible for when nobody is watching?"
- Reset: tools, towels, trash, counters, traffic flow, and floors checked.

### Wednesday: Ticket Flow and Mock Service
- Opening: agenda check and communication practice.
- Mini-lesson/demo: reading tickets, call-backs, expo basics, and respectful corrections.
- Student work: practice ticket language.
- Application: mock service with simple orders.
- Prompt: "What phrase helped the order keep moving?"
- Reset: dish, counters, service area, guest area, towels, and floor reset.

### Thursday: Bistro Prep
- Opening: prep huddle with roles and timing.
- Mini-lesson/demo: prep list, station readiness, labeling, storage, and reset.
- Student work: assigned prep and service setup.
- Application: controlled Bistro prep.
- Prompt: "What prep task supports tomorrow's service?"
- Reset: label, store, clean, dish, and reset for the next service.

### Friday: Soft Opening
- Opening: readiness check, huddle, roles confirmed.
- Mini-lesson/demo: final reminders for hospitality, ticket flow, expo, safety, and reset.
- Student work: run controlled soft opening.
- Application: guest service, ticket flow, cooking, expo, running, clearing, dish, reset, farewell.
- Prompt: "What worked, and what system needs practice?"
- Reset: full service reset before debrief.

## Setup Notes
- Keep the first soft opening small and controlled.
- Post or review station assignments.
- Prepare sample tickets or order slips.
- Limit menu or service items.
- Set clear guest/service boundaries.
- Confirm dish flow, towel plan, trash, floor, and reset roles.
- Stage sanitizer, towels, service tools, station tools, and simple prep items.

## Teacher Language and Prompts
- "Hospitality is a skill."
- "Every order has a path."
- "Expo stays calm when communication stays clear."
- "Dish and reset are part of service."
- "Front and back of house are one team."
- "Soft opening is practice with real expectations."

## Safety Reminders
- Watch movement during service.
- Use "behind," "hot," and "corner."
- Keep expo and dish areas from becoming crowded.
- Handle hot food and pans with clear communication.
- Report spills immediately.
- Keep floors, towels, and traffic paths controlled.

## Classroom Management Reminders
- Assign roles clearly and visibly.
- Stop practice if ticket flow breaks down.
- Debrief with facts, not blame.
- Keep guests limited and expectations clear.
- Do not add menu complexity to fix excitement.
- Build small resets during service, not only at the end.

## Cleanup and Reset Expectations
A finished service includes:
- Guest area reset
- Service area reset
- Dishes routed correctly
- Counters cleaned and sanitized
- Tools returned
- Towels handled correctly
- Trash and floors checked
- Stations ready for the next group

## Unit Lab Plan
Purpose: practice the full service flow in a controlled way.

Skills practiced:
- Greeting and farewell
- Order taking and ticket flow
- Station setup
- Expo and food running
- Clearing, dish, and reset
- Guest awareness
- Calm team communication

Suggested roles:
- Host/greeter: welcomes guest and starts the order path
- Order taker: records the order clearly
- Cook/station worker: completes assigned station task
- Expo: checks and organizes orders before they leave
- Runner/clearer: runs food, checks back, clears, and supports reset
- Steward: watches dish, towels, trash, floor, and final reset

Teacher checkpoints:
- Greeting is respectful
- Ticket reaches the correct station
- Students use clear call-backs
- Expo stays controlled
- Dish and reset happen throughout
- Full service reset is complete

## Bistro Connection
This is the first full Bistro service connection. Students see how safety, sanitation, cooking, communication, hospitality, dish flow, and reset work together.

## Reflection Notes
After Unit 04, note:
- Which station roles were clear
- Where ticket flow broke down
- Which hospitality habits need rehearsal
- Whether dish and reset were steady
- What must be retrained in Unit 05
