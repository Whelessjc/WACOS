# Unit 04 Student Packet: Customer Service and Bistro Soft Opening

## What We Are Learning
This week we are learning how service works from the moment a guest arrives until the space is reset. You will practice greeting, order taking, ticket flow, station roles, expo, food running, clearing, dish, reset, and farewell.

## Why We Are Learning It
Bistro service is not just cooking. It is a team system. Guests notice how we communicate, move, clean, serve, and reset.

## What Success Looks Like
- I can explain the Life of an Order.
- I can follow my station role.
- I can use clear service communication.
- I can help move tickets through service.
- I can treat guests and teammates with respect.
- I can help reset the room after service.

## Daily Kitchen Entry Routine
1. Enter calmly.
2. Put belongings away.
3. Check the agenda.
4. Check uniform/readiness.
5. Join the huddle.
6. Wash hands when directed.
7. Begin work only when released.

## Key Expectations
- Know your role before service starts.
- Use short, clear communication.
- Keep moving with purpose, not panic.
- Treat dish, clearing, and reset as service work.
- Watch the guest space and kitchen space.
- Ask for help before a system falls apart.

## Kitchen Communication
- "Behind"
- "Hot"
- "Corner"
- "Heard"
- "Order in"
- "Order up"
- "I need help"
- "Table reset"

## Safety Expectations
- Walk, do not run.
- Keep traffic paths clear.
- Use hot food language.
- Report spills right away.
- Keep dish and expo areas from getting crowded.
- Wash hands and keep stations clean.

## Life of an Order
1. Guest arrives.
2. Guest is greeted.
3. Order is taken.
4. Ticket moves to the correct station.
5. Food is prepared.
6. Expo checks the order.
7. Food is run or handed off.
8. Guest is checked on.
9. Table is cleared.
10. Dishes move correctly.
11. Space is reset.
12. Guest leaves with a farewell.

## Soft Opening Expectations
Soft opening is practice, but it is still real work.

- Know your role.
- Listen during the huddle.
- Use clear call-backs.
- Keep your station clean.
- Help dish and reset before being asked.
- Debrief honestly without blaming people.

## Cleanup and Reset Checklist
- [ ] Guest area reset
- [ ] Service area reset
- [ ] Dishes routed correctly
- [ ] Counters cleaned and sanitized
- [ ] Tools returned
- [ ] Towels handled correctly
- [ ] Trash checked
- [ ] Floor checked
- [ ] Station ready for the next group

## Bistro Connection
This unit is Bistro in slow motion. You are learning how an order moves, how stations work together, and how hospitality shows up in both service and cleanup.

## Student Reflection Questions

3. What did your station do well?
4. What communication helped your team?
