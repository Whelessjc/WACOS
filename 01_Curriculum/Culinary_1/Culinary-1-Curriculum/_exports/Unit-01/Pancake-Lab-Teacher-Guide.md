# Pancake Lab Teacher Guide

## Pre-Class Setup
- Choose a simple pancake mix or recipe.
- Stage measuring cups, measuring spoons, bowls, whisks, spatulas, towels, sanitizer, and dish supplies.
- Decide whether heat sources are teacher-managed or student-managed with approval.
- Portion ingredients if the class needs tighter control.
- Set up a clear trash, dish, and towel plan.
- Post or review team roles.

## Demo Points
- Read the directions before touching ingredients.
- Identify ingredients, equipment, and steps.
- Measure dry and liquid ingredients carefully.
- Show what a ready station looks like.
- Show where the towel belongs.
- Show heat approval procedure.
- Show pancake cues: bubbles, set edges, color, and safe flipping.
- Show what full reset looks like before students begin.

## Safety Watch-Outs
- Students turning on equipment before approval
- Towels too close to heat
- Crowding around griddles or pans
- Students rushing with hot tools
- Spills on the floor
- Students using the wrong tool near heat
- Students thinking the lab is finished when the food is finished

## Measuring Checkpoints
- Correct measuring tool selected
- Dry ingredients leveled or measured as directed
- Liquid measurements read carefully
- Small amounts measured with spoons
- Ingredients measured before mixing
- Students avoid guessing or dumping

## Heat-Control Reminders
- Keep heat moderate and controlled.
- Pancakes should not go on before the surface is ready.
- Watch bubbles and edges before flipping.
- Adjust heat if pancakes brown too fast.
- Treat equipment as hot even after it is off.
- Require "hot" communication when needed.

## Common Student Mistakes
- Skipping the directions
- Measuring with the wrong tool
- Mixing before the station is ready
- Crowding the cooking surface
- Flipping too early
- Leaving dishes until the end
- One student doing all the cleanup
- Forgetting counters, towels, trash, or floor

## Circulation Notes
While students work, look for:
- Are students reading before acting?
- Is mise en place visible?
- Are team roles being used?
- Are students measuring carefully?
- Is heat used only with approval?
- Are students noticing heat cues?
- Is cleanup shared?
- Is the final station ready for the next group?

## Cleanup Timing
- Pause midway if stations are getting messy.
- Start dishes before the final minutes.
- Give a clear final reset deadline.
- Inspect before dismissal.
- Require groups to fix incomplete reset immediately.

## Bistro Connection
Name the connection briefly: this lab is a low-pressure way to practice future service habits. Students are learning to read, set up, communicate, manage heat with approval, clean as they go, and reset so another group can trust the station.

## Quick Debrief Prompts
- What helped your group stay organized?
- What did your group do safely?
- What routine did your group forget or rush?
- What should we practice again before the next lab?
