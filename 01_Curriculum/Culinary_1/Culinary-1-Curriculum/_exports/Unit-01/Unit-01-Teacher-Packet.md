# Unit 01 Teacher Packet: Orientation and Systems

## Week Overview
Focus: learn the room before cooking.

This first week introduces Culinary Arts Management 1 as a real cooking, real skills course. Students learn the room, the routines, and the expectations that make lab work possible: entry routine, agenda check, huddle, uniform readiness, handwashing, basic safety, kitchen communication, measuring stations, station setup, cleanup/reset, and the first Friday pancake lab.

The goal is not to rush into cooking. The goal is to help students understand how to move, listen, ask, measure, set up, clean, and reset in a shared kitchen.

## Day-by-Day Flow

### Monday: Course Orientation and Kitchen Mindset
- Opening: welcome students, assign starting area, check agenda.
- Mini-lesson: Culinary Arts Management 1 is real cooking, real skills, and safe shared work.
- Student work: students name one kitchen skill or habit they want to build.
- Teacher prompt: "Why do we learn systems before we cook?"
- Reset: practice end-of-class routine.

### Tuesday: Entry Routine, Uniforms, and Room Tour
- Opening: practice entry routine and agenda check.
- Mini-lesson: uniform/readiness expectations and why they matter.
- Student work: locate sinks, sanitizer, towels, dish area, trash, storage, exits, and teacher-only areas.
- Teacher prompt: "Where does this item belong?"
- Reset: return to starting area and review traffic problems.

### Wednesday: Handwashing, Safety, and Measuring Stations
- Opening: huddle on handwashing and safety.
- Demo: handwashing, dry measuring, liquid measuring, measuring spoons.
- Student work: rotate through measuring stations.
- Teacher prompt: "Which measuring tool fits this ingredient?"
- Reset: clean stations and return tools.

### Thursday: Station Setup, Recipe Reading, and Cleanup
- Opening: huddle on read-before-cooking.
- Demo: read pancake directions, set mise en place, show ready station.
- Student work: dry station setup and cleanup/reset practice.
- Teacher prompt: "What still needs to happen before this station is ready?"
- Reset: dish flow, counters, towels, tools, floor.

### Friday: Pancake Lab
- Opening: uniform/readiness check, agenda, huddle, handwashing.
- Demo: measuring review, batter setup, heat approval, pancake cues.
- Student work: pancake lab in simple team roles.
- Teacher prompt: "What system helped your group today?"
- Reset: full station reset before dismissal.

## Setup Notes
- Post or display the daily agenda.
- Decide where students place belongings.
- Prepare school-specific uniform language.
- Stage measuring tools for Wednesday.
- Stage pancake equipment and ingredients before Friday.
- Decide whether griddles/pans are teacher-managed or student-managed with approval.
- Set dish-flow, towel, trash, and reset expectations before the first lab.

Before Friday, confirm:
- Pancake recipe or mix
- Ingredient portion plan
- Heat-source approval plan
- Dish-flow and towel plan
- Cleanup inspection plan

## Teacher Prompts
- "Learn the room before cooking."
- "Systems before speed."
- "Cooking includes cleaning."
- "Read before acting."
- "Reset means ready for the next group."
- "If you are unsure, pause and ask."
- "A useful teammate notices what needs doing."

## Safety Reminders
- Students walk in the kitchen.
- Pathways stay clear.
- Spills are reported immediately.
- Students ask before using equipment.
- Hot equipment is treated as hot even after it is turned off.
- Towels stay away from heat.
- Food and cleaning chemicals stay separate.
- Hands are washed before food work and after contamination.

## Pacing Notes
- Keep Week 1 slow and repetitive.
- Do not make the pancake lab about perfect pancakes.
- Reteach routines immediately when they wobble.
- If students struggle with movement, measuring, or cleanup, reduce the cooking task instead of rushing reset.
- Protect time for cleanup. The first lab teaches the year-long standard.

## Cleanup and Reset Expectations
Students are not done when the food is done.

A finished station has:
- Dishes handled correctly
- Counters wiped and sanitized
- Tools returned
- Towels handled correctly
- Trash checked
- Floor checked
- Heat source off
- Station ready for the next group

## Friday Pancake Lab Plan
Purpose: practice recipe reading, measuring, mise en place, station setup, teamwork, heat awareness, cleanup, and reset.

Suggested roles:
- Reader: reads directions and keeps the group on step
- Measurer: measures ingredients carefully
- Mixer: combines batter and watches texture
- Cook: works near the griddle or pan with teacher approval
- Steward: leads dishes, towels, trash, counters, and reset

Teacher checkpoints:
- Directions read before ingredients are touched
- Station set before mixing
- Measuring tools used correctly
- Heat used only with approval
- Students notice bubbles, set edges, color, and heat level
- Reset is complete

## Bistro Connection
Week 1 routines are the start of Bistro readiness. Students who can enter calmly, check the agenda, communicate, wash hands, set a station, and reset after pancakes are practicing the same habits they will need before service.
