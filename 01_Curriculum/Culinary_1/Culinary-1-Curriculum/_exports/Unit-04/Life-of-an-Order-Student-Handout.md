# Life of an Order Student Handout

## Purpose
The purpose of Life of an Order is to understand how service moves from guest arrival to farewell and reset.

An order is not just food. It is a full path through people, stations, tickets, communication, dish, and reset.

## Quick Order Path
1. Guest arrives.
2. Greeting happens.
3. Order is taken.
4. Ticket moves.
5. Station prepares the item.
6. Expo checks.
7. Food or item is run.
8. Guest is checked on.
9. Space is cleared.
10. Dishes move.
11. Station and guest area reset.
12. Farewell closes the order.

## Guest Arrives
- Notice the guest or practice guest.
- Make the space feel calm and ready.
- Stay aware of traffic and safety.

## Greeting
- Use a respectful greeting.
- Use a calm voice.
- Make eye contact if appropriate.
- Listen before moving on.

## Order Taking
- Listen carefully.
- Write or enter the order clearly.
- Ask for clarification if needed.
- Repeat important information when directed.

## Ticket Flow
- Ticket goes to the correct station.
- Station reads the ticket.
- Team uses call-backs so information is heard.
- Ticket stays visible until complete.
- Ticket/order is closed or reset when finished.

## Cooking or Preparation
- Station completes the assigned task.
- Team communicates timing or problems.
- Food safety and sanitation still matter.
- Station stays clean enough to work.

## Expo
- Expo organizes the order before it leaves.
- Expo checks that the order matches the ticket.
- Expo limits crowding and keeps the handoff calm.

## Food Running
- Move safely.
- Use "behind," "hot," and "corner" when needed.
- Deliver or hand off the correct item.
- Use respectful guest-facing language.

## Check Back
- Notice whether the guest or practice guest needs anything.
- Communicate needs to the correct person.
- Stay calm and respectful.

## Clearing
- Clear respectfully.
- Move dishes safely.
- Keep guest space clean.
- Do not let dirty items pile up.

## Dish
- Dishes move to the correct place.
- Dish flow starts before the end.
- Towels, sinks, dish machine, and floors stay controlled.

## Reset
- Guest area reset.
- Service area reset.
- Station reset.
- Tools returned.
- Tickets/tasks accounted for.
- Trash and floor checked.

## Farewell
- End with respectful language when appropriate.
- The guest should leave a space that feels cared for.

## Reflection Questions
1. Where can an order slow down?
2. Which part of the order path seems easiest to forget?
3. What communication helps the order keep moving?
4. How does reset affect the next guest or group?
