# Unit 04 Teacher Packet: Customer Service and Bistro Soft Opening

## Week Overview
Focus: understand the full flow of service.

This week introduces students to the Life of an Order and the first controlled Bistro soft opening. Students practice hospitality as a set of teachable behaviors: greeting, listening, clear communication, ticket flow, station work, expo, clearing, dish flow, reset, and farewell.

The goal is not speed or menu complexity. The goal is for students to know where an order goes next, what their station owns, how to speak during service, and how to finish with a full reset.

## Day-by-Day Flow

### Monday: Life of an Order
- Opening: entry routine, agenda check, huddle.
- Mini-lesson: guest arrival, greeting, order taking, ticket flow, cooking, expo, running, check back, clearing, dish, reset, farewell.
- Student work: trace one order through the full path and mark who touches it.
- Application: role-play one simple order from greeting to farewell.
- Prompt: "Where can an order break down?"
- Reset: guest area, service area, dish path, station, and room reset.

### Tuesday: Station Assignments and Training
- Opening: huddle on station ownership.
- Mini-lesson: what each station does before, during, and after service.
- Student work: name the station setup, service job, communication line, and reset job.
- Application: station setup and reset practice.
- Prompt: "What is your station responsible for when nobody is watching?"
- Reset: tools, towels, trash, counters, traffic flow, and floors checked.

### Wednesday: Ticket Flow and Mock Service
- Opening: agenda check and communication practice.
- Mini-lesson: reading tickets, call-backs, expo basics, and respectful corrections.
- Student work: practice ticket language and call-backs.
- Application: mock service with one simple ticket path at a time.
- Prompt: "What phrase helped the order keep moving?"
- Reset: dish, counters, service area, guest area, towels, and floor reset.

### Thursday: Bistro Prep
- Opening: prep huddle with roles and timing.
- Mini-lesson: prep list, station readiness, labeling, storage, and reset.
- Student work: assigned prep and service setup.
- Application: controlled Bistro prep.
- Prompt: "What prep task supports tomorrow's service?"
- Reset: label, store, clean, dish, return tools, and reset for the next service.

### Friday: Soft Opening
- Opening: readiness check, huddle, roles confirmed.
- Mini-lesson: final reminders for hospitality, ticket flow, expo, safety, and reset.
- Student work: run controlled soft opening.
- Application: guest service, ticket flow, cooking, expo, running, clearing, dish, reset, farewell.
- Prompt: "What worked, and what system needs practice?"
- Reset: full service reset before debrief; no station is released until dish flow, guest space, service space, and floor are checked.

## Setup Notes
- Keep the first soft opening small and controlled.
- Post station assignments clearly.
- Prepare order slips or sample tickets.
- Limit menu or service items.
- Set clear guest/service boundaries.
- Confirm dish flow, towel plan, trash, floor, and reset roles.
- Stage sanitizer, towels, service tools, station tools, and simple prep items.

## Teacher Prompts
- "Hospitality is a skill."
- "Every order has a path."
- "Know the next step before you move."
- "Expo stays calm when communication stays clear."
- "Dish and reset are part of service."
- "Front and back of house are one team."
- "Soft opening is practice with real expectations."

## Life of an Order Walkthrough Notes
Walk students through the full order path:
1. Guest arrives.
2. Guest is greeted.
3. Order is taken.
4. Ticket moves to the correct station.
5. Food is prepared.
6. Expo checks the order.
7. Food is run or handed off.
8. Guest is checked on.
9. Table or space is cleared.
10. Dishes move correctly.
11. Guest area and station reset.
12. Guest receives a farewell.

Emphasize that a service problem can happen at any point in the chain.

## Station Assignment Notes
- Keep roles simple and visible.
- Give each station a before, during, and after-service responsibility.
- Name each station's ticket or task flow.
- Name each station's safety and sanitation watch-out.
- Make stewardship part of every role.
- Assign dish/reset responsibility before service starts.
- Use short station checks before releasing students into mock service.

## Mock Service Notes
- Use simple tickets or practice orders.
- Run the mock slowly enough to stop and reteach.
- Start with one order path before adding a second.
- Pause if tickets disappear, expo crowds, or dish flow stalls.
- Debrief systems, not personalities.
- Reset after each round.

## Bistro Prep Notes
- Use a short prep list.
- Assign setup, storage, labeling, and reset jobs.
- Keep prep connected to Friday's soft opening.
- Do not add menu complexity because students are excited.
- Ask: "Would this station be useful to the next person?"

## Soft Opening Notes
- Keep guest count or practice audience small.
- Confirm roles before beginning.
- Set clear start/stop boundaries.
- Watch ticket flow, station timing, communication, dish, and reset more than speed.
- Coach hospitality in the moment: greeting, calm voice, listening, follow-through, farewell.
- Debrief with one success and one system to improve.

## Safety and Sanitation Reminders
- Students walk during service.
- Use "behind," "hot," and "corner."
- Keep expo and dish areas from crowding.
- Handle hot food and pans with clear communication.
- Report spills immediately.
- Keep hands, counters, guest areas, and stations clean.
- Keep floors, towels, and traffic paths controlled.

## Hospitality Reminders
Hospitality is practiced behavior:
- Acknowledge the guest.
- Use a calm voice.
- Listen before responding.
- Follow through on the task.
- Notice the guest space.
- Reset the space for the next guest or group.

## Cleanup and Reset Expectations
A finished service includes:
- Guest area reset
- Service area reset
- Ticket/order closed or accounted for
- Dishes routed correctly
- Counters cleaned and sanitized
- Tools returned
- Stored items labeled and put away if used
- Towels handled correctly
- Trash and floors checked
- Stations ready for the next group

## Teacher Reflection Space
- Which station roles were clear?

- Where did the Life of an Order break down?

- What communication helped service?

- Which hospitality behaviors need rehearsal?

- Were dish flow and reset steady?

- What must be retrained in Unit 05?
