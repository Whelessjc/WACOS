# Bistro Soft Opening Teacher Guide

## Pre-Class Setup
- Choose one or two simple service items or practice tasks.
- Prepare order slips or sample tickets.
- Assign stations and roles visibly.
- Set guest/service boundaries.
- Stage station tools, service tools, towels, sanitizer, and dish supplies.
- Confirm dish flow, trash, towels, floor checks, and final reset plan.

## Station Assignment Setup
For each station, clarify:
- Before-service setup
- During-service job
- Ticket or task flow
- Communication expectations
- Cleanup/reset responsibility
- Who the student checks with when unsure

## Walkthrough Plan
1. Walk through Life of an Order from guest arrival to farewell.
2. Model greeting, order taking, and farewell.
3. Show how a ticket moves.
4. Show expo expectations.
5. Show dish flow and reset path.
6. Show what a reset table, station, and dish area look like.

## Mock Service Structure
- Start with one simple order path.
- Run slowly enough to stop and correct.
- Use call-backs.
- Keep expo clear.
- Reset after each round.
- Debrief the system before adding another round.

## Soft Opening Routine
1. Pre-service huddle: confirm roles, safety, ticket flow, dish flow, and reset jobs.
2. Station check: tools, towels, sanitizer, tickets/tasks, and service space ready.
3. Controlled start: release one order or guest interaction at a time.
4. Mid-service pause if needed: fix ticket flow, crowding, dish, or sanitation.
5. Closing reset: guest space, service space, stations, dish, tools, towels, trash, and floors.
6. Debrief: one thing that worked and one system to practice next.

## Soft Opening Safety Watch-Outs
- Students rushing during service
- Crowding expo or dish
- Hot food moving without communication
- Wet floors near dish or service areas
- Students forgetting handwashing during role changes
- Students treating reset as someone else's job

## Service Coaching Prompts
- "What is your role right now?"
- "Where does that ticket go next?"
- "Who needs to hear that?"
- "What does expo need before this leaves?"
- "What can be reset while we wait?"
- "What would the next group need from this station?"
- "How can we fix the system without blaming people?"

## Common Student Mistakes
- Forgetting to greet or say farewell
- Losing or ignoring tickets
- Crowding around expo
- Treating dish as someone else's job
- Resetting only the visible guest area
- Debriefing with blame instead of facts
- Forgetting that stewardship includes guest space, kitchen space, and dish flow

## What To Observe During Service
- Are students using role language?
- Are tickets visible and moving?
- Is expo controlled?
- Are students speaking respectfully?
- Is dish flow active?
- Is reset happening throughout service?
- Are students noticing stewardship needs without being chased?

## Debrief Questions
- Where did the order move smoothly?
- Where did it slow down?
- What communication helped?
- What did guests or practice guests notice?
- Which station needs retraining next week?
- What reset habit improved the room?

## Cleanup Timing
- Pause after mock rounds for quick reset.
- Start dish flow early.
- Assign students to guest area, service area, dish, counters, towels, trash, and floor.
- Account for tickets/tasks before releasing stations.
- Inspect before debrief.
- Require incomplete stations to fix reset immediately.
