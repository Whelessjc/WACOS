# Heat Control Student Handout
## Purpose
This handout gives you the key habits for Unit 07: Learn how cooking actually works.
The goal is practical kitchen confidence. Work safely, stay organized, use clear words, and reset the station for the next group.
## Key Skills
- saute
- pan preheating
- oil control
- browning vs. steaming
- aromatics without burning
- roasting
- oven control
- spacing
- browning and caramelization
- texture
- moist heat
- rice, beans, and lentils
- simmering
- liquid ratios
## What To Watch For
- Safety before speed.
- Setup before action.
- Small corrections before big problems.
- Clean station before, during, and after work.
- Team communication that helps the task move.
## Bistro Connection
Heat control affects Bistro timing, batch work, holding quality, texture, and consistency. A hot station must be clean, calm, and ready for the next order.

- [ ] I understand today's task.

- [ ] I followed safety expectations.
- [ ] I communicated when needed.
- [ ] I supported dish flow and reset.
- [ ] My station is ready for the next group.

## Heat Cues To Watch
- Sound: a quiet pan may be too cool; aggressive sizzling may be too hot.
- Steam: too much steam can mean overcrowding or low browning.
- Color: browning and caramelization take space and patience.
- Smell: aromatics should smell fragrant, not burnt.
- Texture: rice, beans, and lentils need time and liquid control.
