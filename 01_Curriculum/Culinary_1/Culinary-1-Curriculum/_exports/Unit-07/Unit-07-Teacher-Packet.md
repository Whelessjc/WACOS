# Unit 07 Teacher Packet: Heat Control
## Unit Overview
Weeks covered: Weeks 10 to 12
Focus: Learn how cooking actually works.
This unit keeps the course practical and lab-centered. Students practice: 
- saute
- pan preheating
- oil control
- browning vs. steaming
- aromatics without burning
- roasting
- oven control
- spacing
- browning and caramelization
- texture
- moist heat
- rice, beans, and lentils
- simmering
- liquid ratios
The goal is steady beginner competence through repeated practice, safety, sanitation, stewardship, and useful kitchen habits.
## Teaching Flow
- Week 10: Pan heat, oil cues, saute, browning vs. steaming, aromatics, Bistro timing.
- Week 11: Roasting, oven control, spacing, caramelization, texture, hot-item communication.
- Week 12: Moist heat, rice, beans, lentils, simmering, liquid ratios, hot-station reset.
Daily pattern: entry routine, agenda check, huddle, short demo or mini-lesson, student practice, teacher checkpoint, cleanup/reset, and exit reflection.
## Setup Notes
- Post the unit focus and daily task.
- Stage tools and materials before students enter the lab area.
- Keep roles simple and visible.
- Prepare a reset plan before the lab starts.
- Build in one checkpoint before students move to the next task.
## Teacher Prompts
- Heat is adjustable.
- Browning needs space and patience.
- Steam tells you something.
- Simmering is not boiling.
- Hot-station reset is part of heat control.
## Demo Notes
- Keep demos short and visible.
- Model the safety habit before the skill.
- Name what students should watch, hear, smell, feel, or check.
- Show what the station should look like before and after work.
- Connect the demo to the lab task or Bistro use.
## Safety Reminders
- Use dry towels around heat.
- Keep handles turned in.
- Ask before changing heat.
- Watch oil, steam, and hot pans.
- Turn off burners and ovens as directed.
## Pacing Notes
- Do fewer tasks well instead of rushing through too many.
- Stop and reteach when safety or sanitation slips.
- Use repeated practice before adding complexity.
- Leave enough time for cleanup, dish flow, storage, and floor checks.
## Common Student Mistakes
- Rushing before the routine is clear.
- Waiting too long to ask for help.
- Treating cleanup as separate from the skill.
- Forgetting storage, labels, dish flow, towels, or floors.
- Losing the connection between practice and real service.
## Cleanup and Reset Expectations
Work is not complete until:
- Burners and ovens off as directed
- Hot pans handled safely
- Oil and spills handled
- Dishes routed correctly
- Counters cleaned and sanitized
- Towels and floors checked
- Station ready for the next group
## Bistro / Service Connection
Heat control affects Bistro timing, batch work, holding quality, texture, and consistency. A hot station must be clean, calm, and ready for the next order.
## Teacher Reflection Space
- Which habit improved?
- Which safety or sanitation routine needs reteaching?
- Which students need more support?
- What should be repeated before the next unit?
- What should be adjusted for next year?
