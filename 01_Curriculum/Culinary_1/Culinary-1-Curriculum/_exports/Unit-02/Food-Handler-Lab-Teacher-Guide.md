# Food Handler and Stewardship Teacher Guide
## Pre-Class Setup
- Stage tools, products, sanitizer, towels, dish supplies, and station signs.
- Post the daily focus and reset expectations.
- Decide which tasks require teacher approval.
- Prepare simple comparison examples when useful.
- Assign stewardship roles before the lab begins.
## Demo Points
- Model the safety habit first.
- Show the station setup.
- Name the key cue students should watch for.
- Model one controlled attempt.
- Show what cleanup/reset looks like.
## Safety Watch-Outs
- Wash hands at required times.
- Keep raw, ready-to-eat, allergen, and clean items separated.
- Use sanitizer correctly.
- Use thermometer as evidence, not guessing.
- Follow three-compartment sink or dish machine directions.
## Teacher Checkpoints
- Station is set up before work begins.
- Student can explain the task in plain language.
- Safety and sanitation are visible.
- Timing and communication are controlled.
- Product or task quality fits the beginner goal.
- Reset is complete before students leave.
## Circulation Notes
- Move early before habits drift.
- Correct the first unsafe choice, not the fifth.
- Ask students what they notice before giving the answer.
- Keep coaching short and specific.
- Use reset pauses when stations become crowded or messy.
## Common Student Mistakes
- Starting before reading or listening.
- Rushing the first attempt.
- Ignoring dish flow until the end.
- Forgetting towels, trash, floors, labels, or storage.
- Treating cleanup as punishment instead of kitchen responsibility.
## Product / Task Use Ideas
- brownie batter
- baking brownies
- dish systems training
- chicken breast temperature lab
## Cleanup Timing
- Stop work early enough for real reset.
- Check storage before counters are wiped.
- Inspect dish flow, tools, towels, trash, and floors.
- Require incomplete reset to be fixed immediately.
## Debrief Questions
- What improved today?
- What needs repetition?
- What safety habit protected the room?
- How did this connect to Bistro or future work?
- What reset habit mattered most?

## Teacher Focus Notes
- Treat stewardship jobs as real kitchen responsibility, not punishment.
- Circulate through laundry, counters, dishes, storage, floors, and dish systems instead of staying only near the food.
- Require thermometer cleaning before and after the chicken temperature lab.
