# Food Handler and Stewardship Student Handout
## Purpose
This handout gives you the key habits for Unit 02: Safety becomes behavior.
The goal is practical kitchen confidence. Work safely, stay organized, use clear words, and reset the station for the next group.
## Key Skills
- food safety
- personal hygiene
- cleaning and sanitation
- cross contamination
- allergens
- time and temperature control
- thermometer use
- laundry, counters, dishes, storage, floors
- three-compartment sink and dish machine
## What To Watch For
- Safety before speed.
- Setup before action.
- Small corrections before big problems.
- Clean station before, during, and after work.
- Team communication that helps the task move.
## Bistro Connection
Food Handler habits protect guests and classmates. Bistro service depends on clean counters, safe storage, active dish flow, controlled towels, clean floors, and students who do sanitation without being chased.
## Student Self-Check
- [ ] I understand today's task.
- [ ] My station is set up.
- [ ] I followed safety expectations.
- [ ] I communicated when needed.
- [ ] I supported dish flow and reset.
- [ ] My station is ready for the next group.

## Stewardship Job Standards
- Laundry: towels go where directed, not on counters or floors.
- Counters: clean first, then sanitize as directed.
- Dishes: scrape, sort, rack, wash, dry, and put away correctly.
- Storage: close, label, and return items to the correct place.
- Floors: report spills immediately and sweep/mop as directed.
- Dish area: keep the three-compartment sink or dish machine moving safely.
