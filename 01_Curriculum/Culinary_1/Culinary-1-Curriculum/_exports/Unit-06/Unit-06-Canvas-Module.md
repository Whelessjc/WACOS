# Canvas Module: Unit 06 Knife Skills Progression

## Module Title
Unit 06: Knife Skills Progression

## Overview Text
Weeks 6 to 9 build safe knife control through repeated practice. Students practice board setup, grip, claw, blade path, rough chop, dice, slice, mince, julienne, batonnet, prep list execution, cooking applications, Bistro prep, Bistro service, and Week 9 deep clean reset.

The Culinary 1 goal is minimum competence: safe setup, grip and claw, controlled blade path, useful beginner cuts, organized product, and a full station reset. Control comes before speed.

## Assignments

### Assignment: Knife Safety and Station Setup Check
Students identify:
- How to set up a stable board
- How to hold grip and claw
- What the stop signal means
- Where usable prep, scraps, and waste go
- What a reset knife station looks like

### Assignment: Cut Size and Cooking Reflection
Students answer:
- How does cut size affect cooking time?
- How does cut size affect texture?
- Which cut needs more practice?
- How did your prep support Bistro or a cooking task?

## Discussion / Reflection
Prompt: Why does control come before speed in a kitchen?

Students can answer in 3 to 5 sentences after a knife practice day or cooking application.

Optional sentence starters:
- Control matters because...
- One safety habit I repeated was...
- Cut size affects cooking by...

## Lab Activities

### Knife Practice Blocks
Purpose: build safe, repeated knife control. Repeat this block pattern across Weeks 6 to 9: setup, demo, slow practice, checkpoint, cooking or prep application, cleanup/reset.

Students should use:
- Knife-Skills-Student-Handout.md
- Knife-Practice-Lab-Student-Handout.md
- Unit-06-Printable-Checklist.md

Teacher should use:
- Knife-Skills-Teacher-Guide.md

### Bistro Prep and Service Connection
Purpose: use knife cuts for real prep lists and service readiness.

Examples:
- Stir fry
- Sauteed vegetables
- Soup base
- Roasted vegetables
- Potato batonnet
- Fries prep

Students should connect cut size to cooking time, texture, browning, station timing, labels, storage, and reset.

### Week 9 Deep Clean Reset
Purpose: reset stations, tools, storage, dish area, towels, floors, and knife systems before moving into heat control. Deep clean is stewardship and readiness, not punishment.
