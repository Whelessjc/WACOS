# Knife Skills Teacher Guide

## Pre-Class Setup
- Stage knives, boards, damp towels, bowls, containers, sanitizer, towels, and dish supplies.
- Space students before knives are issued.
- Prepare practice vegetables or potatoes by skill level.
- Post the prep list and stop signal.
- Decide how usable prep, scraps, and waste will be handled.
- Prepare Week 9 deep clean zones before Week 9 begins.

## Demo Points
- Safe knife carry and set-down
- Stable cutting board setup
- Grip
- Claw
- Blade path
- Slow practice before speed
- One cut at a time
- How cut size changes cooking
- Sorting usable prep, scraps, and waste
- Fully reset knife station

## Safety Watch-Outs
- Flat fingers
- Students cutting too fast
- Unstable boards
- Crowding
- Knife tips wandering
- Students scraping with the knife edge
- Knives left in sinks or under towels
- Students talking with knives in motion

## How To Correct Grip
- Stop the student before the habit repeats.
- Have the student set the knife down.
- Show the grip slowly.
- Let the student reset their hand before cutting again.
- Watch the first few cuts after the correction.

## How To Correct Claw
- Stop flat fingers immediately.
- Have the student tuck fingertips back.
- Remind them that knuckles guide the blade.
- Slow the product hand down.
- Repeat the correction every time it slips.

## How To Coach Board Setup
- Check for damp towel before knives are used.
- Press the board lightly to test stability.
- Keep containers close enough to use but out of the blade path.
- Keep scraps from crowding the board.
- Reset messy boards before they become unsafe.

## How To Watch Blade Path
- Watch the tip of the knife.
- Watch whether the blade is moving toward fingers.
- Watch whether students look away while cutting.
- Watch whether product is rolling or sliding.
- Pause the station if the blade path becomes unsafe.
- Restart only after board, product, grip, and claw are reset.

## Preventing Speed-Before-Control Problems
- Say "slow first" often.
- Use short timed pauses, not speed contests.
- Praise safe correction more than fast cutting.
- Add speed only after board, grip, claw, and blade path are steady.
- Remind students that minimum competence is safe and useful, not fast.

## Common Student Mistakes
- Forgetting the damp towel
- Looking away while cutting
- Flat guiding hand
- Mixing usable product with waste
- Letting scraps crowd the board
- Rushing because another student is faster
- Treating cleanup as separate from knife work

## Circulation Notes
Look for:
- Board stability
- Grip and claw
- Controlled pace
- Cut size for the task
- Product sorting
- Safe communication
- Knife and board reset

## Product Use Ideas
- Rough chop: stock/soup base or roasted vegetables
- Dice: soup base, sauteed vegetables, fried rice prep
- Slice: stir fry, sandwiches, sauteed vegetables
- Mince: garlic, herbs, aromatics
- Julienne: quick-cooking vegetable prep
- Batonnet: potatoes, fries, roasted vegetables

## Thursday and Friday Bistro Notes
- Thursday prep should connect cuts to a real prep list when possible.
- Friday service should show whether cut size supported cooking, timing, and station flow.
- Check batch containers, labels, storage, and station readiness.
- Use service debriefs to ask whether prep helped or slowed the team.

## Cleanup Timing
- Stop cutting early enough for safe cleanup.
- Supervise knife washing and storage as required.
- Check boards, counters, containers, towels, trash, and floors.
- Inspect stations before students leave.
- Require incomplete reset to be fixed immediately.

## Week 9 Deep Clean Reset Notes
Use Week 9 to reset:
- Knife storage
- Cutting boards
- Prep tables
- Small tools
- Containers and lids
- Storage areas
- Dish area
- Floors
- Towels/laundry

Frame deep clean as stewardship and readiness for heat control, not punishment.

## Quick Debrief Prompts
- What safety habit improved today?
- Which cut needs more repetition?
- How did cut size affect cooking?
- What prep would be useful for Bistro?
- What reset habit protected the next group?
