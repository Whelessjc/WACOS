# Unit 06 Teacher Packet: Knife Skills Progression

## Unit Overview
Weeks 6 to 9 focus: build confidence through control and repetition.

Students practice knife safety, board setup, grip, claw, rough chop, dice, slice, mince, julienne, batonnet, speed with control, prep list execution, and knife station stewardship. Knife cuts are treated as tools for cooking outcomes and Bistro prep, not trophies.

The goal for Culinary 1 is minimum competence: students can work safely, set up a stable station, use grip and claw, keep the blade path controlled, make useful beginner cuts, sort product correctly, and reset the station without being chased.

## Week-by-Week Flow
| Week | Focus | Main Application |
|---|---|---|
| Week 6 | Safety, board setup, grip, claw, rough chop, slice | Controlled vegetable prep and Bistro prep |
| Week 7 | Dice, mince, prep lists | Soup base or sauteed vegetable prep |
| Week 8 | Julienne, batonnet, roasting/fries prep | Roasted vegetables, potato batonnet, fries prep |
| Week 9 | Speed with control and deep clean | Timed prep, stir fry/saute, deep clean reset |

## Three-Day Knife Block Structure
Use this pattern each week:
1. Day 1: demo, dry practice if useful, slow cutting practice, teacher checkpoint, reset.
2. Day 2: repeat safety setup, add the new cut or correction, more practice, product sorting, reset.
3. Day 3: apply cuts to a prep list or simple cooking task, label/store usable product, reset.

Keep the rhythm predictable. Students should know that every knife block includes setup, demo, practice, checkpoint, application, and cleanup.

## Thursday Bistro Prep Connection
Thursday knife work should feed a real prep list when possible:
- Stir fry vegetables
- Sauteed vegetables
- Soup base
- Roasted vegetables
- Potato batonnet
- Fries prep

Emphasize timing, cut consistency, batch containers, labels, storage, and station readiness. Students should see how cut size affects cooking time, texture, browning, and service flow.

## Friday Bistro Service Connection
Friday connects knife work to service:
- Did the cut size cook evenly?
- Was prep organized for the station?
- Were containers labeled and easy to use?
- Did the team save time because prep was ready?
- Was the station reset for the next class or service block?

## Week 9 Deep Clean Reset
Use Week 9 to reset the knife unit before moving deeper into heat control.

Deep clean zones may include:
- Knife storage
- Cutting boards
- Prep tables
- Small tools
- Storage areas
- Dish area
- Floors and baseboards
- Towels/laundry

Use this as a systems reset, not a punishment. Students should leave the room safer, cleaner, better organized, and ready for the next unit.

## Setup Notes
- Stage knives, boards, damp towels, bowls, containers, sheet pans, sanitizer, towels, and dish supplies.
- Space students before knives are issued.
- Post the stop signal.
- Prepare low-cost vegetables or potatoes by cut type.
- Prepare a visible prep list.
- Decide how usable prep, scraps, and waste will be sorted.
- Plan Week 9 deep clean zones before the week begins.

## Teacher Prompts
- "Control before speed."
- "A stable board comes before a cut."
- "Claw protects your guiding hand."
- "Cut size affects cooking."
- "Minimum competence means safe, steady, and useful."
- "Knife work feeds prep lists, not just practice bowls."
- "A clean board is part of knife safety."

## Demo Notes
Keep demos short and repeat them often:
- Carry and set down a knife safely.
- Set a damp towel under the board.
- Show grip from more than one angle.
- Show claw slowly.
- Show the blade path before students cut: where the tip goes, where the guiding hand is, and when to stop.
- Show one cut at a time.
- Show usable prep, scraps, and waste.
- Show a fully reset knife station.

## Safety Reminders
- Knives stay down when directions are given.
- Students use the stop signal immediately.
- Boards must be stable before cutting.
- Fingers stay tucked in claw position.
- Eyes stay on the blade and product.
- Students do not scrape with the knife edge.
- Crowding is corrected immediately.
- Knives are never left in sinks or under towels.

## Pacing Notes
- Slow the room down early.
- Correct grip and claw before adding speed.
- Use short practice rounds with checkpoints.
- Do not move to advanced cuts if safety is slipping.
- Give repeated practice with the same cut before expecting consistency.
- Treat speed as a later result of control.

## Cleanup and Reset Expectations
Knife labs are not finished until:
- Knives are cleaned and stored safely
- Boards are washed correctly
- Counters are cleaned and sanitized
- Usable prep is labeled or stored as directed
- Scraps and waste are handled
- Towels are handled correctly
- Dishes are routed correctly
- Floors are checked
- Station is ready for the next group, prep block, or service block

## Teacher Reflection Space
- Which safety routines are automatic?

- Which students need grip or claw support?

- Which cuts need more repetition?

- Did prep lists make knife work purposeful?

- Did Bistro prep show improvement in timing or consistency?

- Was the Week 9 deep clean reset complete?
