# Knife Skills Student Handout

## Purpose
The purpose of knife skills is to build safe control for real cooking. We practice cuts so food can be prepared, cooked, stored, and served more consistently.

Knife cuts are tools for cooking, not trophies.

Minimum competence in Culinary 1 means safe setup, grip and claw, controlled blade path, useful beginner cuts, organized product, and full reset.

## Knife Safety
- Listen before touching the knife.
- Carry knives safely as directed.
- Keep knives down during directions.
- Keep space between students.
- Use the stop signal immediately.
- Stop if your blade path feels unsafe.
- Do not scrape the board with the knife edge.
- Never leave knives in sinks or under towels.

## Cutting Board Setup
- Damp towel under board
- Board does not slide
- Knife placed safely
- Scrap/waste bowl ready
- Usable prep container ready
- Towel in correct place
- Counter clean enough to work

## Grip
- Hold the knife with control.
- Keep your hand steady.
- Do not hold the handle loosely.
- Ask for help if the knife feels unstable.

## Claw
- Tuck fingertips back.
- Use knuckles as a guide.
- Keep thumb behind fingers.
- Move product slowly.

## Blade Path
- Keep eyes on the cut.
- Move the knife in a controlled path.
- Keep the tip from wandering.
- Keep product organized before cutting.
- Stop and reset if the knife moves toward your fingers.

## Controlled Pace
Control comes before speed.

A controlled pace means:
- Board is stable
- Grip and claw are visible
- Knife does not rush
- Cut size fits the task
- Station stays clean enough to work

## Cuts
Rough chop:
- Larger practical pieces for basic prep.

Dice:
- Smaller pieces that cook more evenly.

Slice:
- Even pieces or strips for cooking or service.

Mince:
- Very small pieces for strong-flavored ingredients.

Julienne:
- Thin stick cuts for quick cooking or neat prep.

Batonnet:
- Larger stick cuts for potatoes, roasting, or fries prep.

## How Cut Size Affects Cooking
Cut size affects:
- Cooking time
- Even cooking
- Texture
- Browning
- Service timing
- How easy the station is to work

Bistro prep depends on cut size, labels, storage, timing, and a reset station.
