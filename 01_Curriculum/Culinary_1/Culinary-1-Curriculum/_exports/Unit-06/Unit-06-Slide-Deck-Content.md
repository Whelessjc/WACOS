## Slide 2: Unit Focus
- Build confidence
- Use control
- Repeat core habits
- Prep with purpose

Speaker notes: Knife cuts are tools for cooking, not trophies.

## Slide 3: Minimum Competence
- Safe station
- Grip and claw
- Safe blade path
- Controlled pace
- Full reset

Speaker notes: Explain that Culinary 1 expects safe, useful beginner knife work.

## Slide 4: Daily Routine
- Enter calmly
- Check agenda
- Join huddle
- Wash hands
- Wait for release

Speaker notes: Keep routines steady before knives are issued.

## Slide 5: Knife Safety
- Knife down during directions
- Carry safely
- Keep space
- Use stop signal
- Ask for help

Speaker notes: Stop unsafe habits immediately.

## Slide 6: Board Setup
- Damp towel
- Stable board
- Scrap plan
- Prep container
- Clean counter

Speaker notes: A stable board comes before any cut.

## Slide 7: Grip and Claw
- Controlled grip
- Fingers tucked
- Knuckles guide
- Eyes on cut
- Slow first

Speaker notes: Repeat grip and claw correction often.

## Slide 8: Blade Path
- Tip controlled
- Knife moves forward/down
- Eyes on product
- Product stays organized

Speaker notes: Watch for wandering tips and edge scraping.

## Slide 9: Basic Cuts
- Rough chop
- Dice
- Slice
- Mince

Speaker notes: Connect each cut to a cooking use.

## Slide 10: Progression Cuts
- Julienne
- Batonnet
- Potato batonnet
- Speed with control

Speaker notes: Keep pace slow until control is steady.

## Slide 11: Cut Size Matters
- Cooks faster or slower

- Changes browning
- Affects timing

Speaker notes: Show how prep quality affects cooking.

## Slide 12: Cooking Applications
- Stir fry

## Slide 14: Cleanup and Reset
- Knives safe
