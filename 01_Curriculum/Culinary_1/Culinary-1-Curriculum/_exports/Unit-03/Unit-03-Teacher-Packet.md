# Unit 03 Teacher Packet: Recipe Reading and Mise en Place

## Week Overview
Focus: read before cooking. Organize before acting.

This week connects Food Handler habits to actual lab behavior. Students complete Food Handler review and assessment, then practice recipe reading, ingredient lists, equipment lists, step sequencing, prep lists, measuring accuracy, mise en place, and station reset.

Primary applications may include peanut butter bars, rice cookery, fried rice, or simple stir fry.

## Week at a Glance
| Day | Focus | Main Application |
|---|---|---|
| Monday | Food Handler review and assessment | Safety review, assessment, sanitation scenarios |
| Tuesday | Recipe reading | Annotate a recipe and identify ingredients, equipment, yield, steps, and timing |
| Wednesday | Measuring and sequence | Peanut butter bars with mise en place check |
| Thursday | Rice cookery and ratios | Rice practice with measuring, timing, and reset |
| Friday | Mise en place application | Fried rice or simple stir fry with prep list and station readiness |

## Day-by-Day Flow

### Monday: Food Handler Review and Assessment
- Opening: entry routine, agenda check, huddle, readiness check, handwashing.
- Mini-lesson: review the safety habits that must continue during recipe work.
- Student work: Food Handler review questions or assessment.
- Application: quick sanitation or cross-contamination scenarios.
- Prompt: "Which safety habit still matters when the recipe looks simple?"
- Reset: return materials, wipe tables or stations, handle trash, and reset the room.

### Tuesday: Recipe Reading
- Opening: huddle on "read before cooking."
- Demo: title, yield, ingredients, equipment, procedure, timing, and safety notes.
- Student work: annotate a simple recipe and build ingredient/equipment lists.
- Application: identify what is needed for peanut butter bars before touching ingredients.
- Prompt: "What should your group know before you pick up food?"
- Reset: return tools, close shared ingredients, wipe surfaces, and reset tables.

### Wednesday: Peanut Butter Bars
- Opening: entry routine, handwashing, station setup, allergen reminder.
- Demo: measuring accuracy, reading the full recipe, and following sequence.
- Student work: assign roles, build a simple prep list, and set mise en place.
- Application: prepare peanut butter bars as directed.
- Prompt: "How did mise en place prevent a mistake today?"
- Reset: dishes routed correctly, counters sanitized, ingredients stored, tools returned, towels handled, floor checked.

### Thursday: Rice Cookery and Ratios
- Opening: agenda check, huddle, handwashing, station readiness.
- Demo: rice-to-liquid ratio, measuring, simmering, steam safety, and timing.
- Student work: identify or calculate the rice ratio and set up equipment.
- Application: rice cookery practice.
- Prompt: "Why does measuring matter before heat is involved?"
- Reset: pots or rice cooker cleaned as directed, counters sanitized, rice stored or discarded as directed, floor checked.

### Friday: Fried Rice or Simple Stir Fry
- Opening: handwashing, station check, prep list check.
- Demo: order of operations, hot pan awareness, batch work, and keeping mise visible.
- Student work: build prep list, gather equipment, and set mise en place before cooking.
- Application: fried rice or simple stir fry.
- Prompt: "What did your prep list help your group do faster or safer?"
- Reset: full station reset with tools returned, dishes moving, shared ingredients handled correctly, counters sanitized, towels/laundry handled, trash and floor checked.

## Setup Notes
- Prepare Food Handler review or assessment materials for Monday.
- Choose simple recipes for annotation and lab use.
- Confirm peanut allergy plan before peanut butter bar work.
- Stage measuring tools, mixing bowls, sheet pans, rice cookers or pots, saute pans or woks, spatulas, spoons, towels, sanitizer, and dish supplies.
- Stage shared ingredients to reduce crowding.
- Decide whether Friday heat work is student-managed, teacher-approved, or teacher-led.

Before each lab, confirm:
- Recipe is visible to each group.
- Ingredient pickup plan is clear.
- Equipment plan is clear.
- Safety and allergen plan is clear.
- Dish-flow, towel, trash, floor, and reset plan are clear.

## Food Handler Review and Assessment Notes
- Keep the review connected to real lab behavior.
- Ask students to explain how handwashing, allergens, cross-contact, dish flow, and station reset will show up in this week's labs.
- Use quick scenarios rather than long lectures.
- If safety habits are weak, slow down the lab release.

## Teacher Prompts
- "Read before cooking. Organize before acting."
- "A recipe is directions for action."
- "Do not touch ingredients until your group can explain the recipe."
- "What happens first?"
- "What tools do you need before ingredients?"
- "What cleaning can happen while the recipe is happening?"

## Recipe Reading Demo Notes
Show students how to find:
- Title: what is being made
- Yield: how much it makes
- Ingredients: food items and amounts
- Equipment: tools, pans, bowls, machines
- Procedure: what happens and in what order
- Timing: cooking, baking, cooling, simmering, or waiting time
- Safety notes: allergens, heat, raw food, sharp tools, or sanitation warnings

## Mise en Place Demo Notes
Show a ready station:
- Recipe visible
- Team roles assigned
- Tools gathered
- Ingredients gathered or measured as directed
- Towel in correct place
- Trash and dish plan clear
- Counter clean and usable
- Teacher checkpoint complete before cooking

## Measuring Accuracy Reminders
- Use the correct tool for dry or liquid ingredients.
- Level dry ingredients when needed.
- Read liquid measurements at eye level when appropriate.
- Measure over a separate bowl when possible.
- Check the unit before pouring.
- Slow down for fractions.

## Pacing Notes
- Do not let students cook before they can explain the recipe.
- Keep recipe reading active and short.
- Use teacher checkpoints before ingredient pickup, before heat, and before final reset.
- Reduce the lab size if cleanup or station control begins to fail.
- Repeat the same language all week: read, list, sequence, prep, measure, cook, reset.

## Cleanup and Reset Expectations
Recipe work is not finished until the station is reset.

A finished station has:
- Dishes washed, sanitized, or routed correctly
- Counters cleaned and sanitized
- Tools returned
- Shared ingredients closed, stored, or returned as directed
- Towels handled correctly
- Trash and floor checked
- Heat turned off as directed
- Station ready for the next group

## Lab Notes

### Peanut Butter Bars
- Emphasize allergen awareness.
- Require recipe reading before ingredient pickup.
- Watch measuring accuracy and sequence.
- Keep dish flow moving before the end.

### Rice Cookery
- Emphasize ratio, timing, lid/steam safety, and texture.
- Make students identify the ratio before cooking.
- Watch students who want to open equipment repeatedly.

### Fried Rice or Simple Stir Fry
- Emphasize prep before heat.
- Require visible mise en place before cooking begins.
- Watch crowding, hot pan language, batch work, and order of operations.

## Bistro Connection
Bistro service depends on students who can read before acting, build a prep list, set a station, time their work, communicate clearly, and reset for the next group. A ticket line runs better when prep is organized before pressure starts.

## Teacher Reflection Space
- Did students read before acting?

- Which recipe parts need reteaching?

- Which measuring habits need more practice?

- Did mise en place improve the lab flow?

- Which cleanup/reset habits need repetition?

- What should be reinforced before customer service and Bistro flow?
