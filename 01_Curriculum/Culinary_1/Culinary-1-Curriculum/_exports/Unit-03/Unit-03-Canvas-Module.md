# Canvas Module: Unit 03 Recipe Reading and Mise en Place

## Module Title
Unit 03: Recipe Reading and Mise en Place

## Overview Text
This week we will practice the habit: read before cooking, organize before acting. Students will review Food Handler habits, learn how to read recipes, identify ingredients and equipment, sequence steps, build prep lists, measure carefully, set mise en place, and reset the station after lab work.

This unit supports real cooking and real skills. A kitchen works better when students read, plan, measure, organize, communicate, and clean before the station falls apart.

## Assignments

### Assignment: Recipe Reading Practice
Students use Recipe-Reading-Student-Handout.md with a teacher-provided recipe.

Students should:
- Mark ingredients
- Mark equipment
- Find yield
- Find timing
- List the first three prep tasks
- Identify one safety or measuring concern

### Assignment: Food Handler Review or Assessment
Use the teacher's Food Handler review or assessment materials.

Keep the connection practical:
- How does this safety habit show up during lab?
- What could contaminate the station?
- How will the group reset?

## Discussion / Reflection
Prompt: Why should a group read and organize before cooking?

Students can answer in 3 to 5 sentences or complete a quick exit response.

Optional sentence starters:
- Reading first helps because...
- Mise en place matters because...
- One mistake prep can prevent is...

## Lab Activity
### Mise en Place Lab
Purpose: practice recipe reading, measuring, prep lists, mise en place, teamwork, heat awareness, cleanup, and reset.

Possible applications:
- Peanut butter bars
- Rice cookery
- Fried rice
