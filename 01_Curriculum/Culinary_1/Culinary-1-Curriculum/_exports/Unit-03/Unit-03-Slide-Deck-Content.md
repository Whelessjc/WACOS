# Unit 03 Slide Deck Content: Recipe Reading and Mise en Place

Use this content to build a classroom slideshow. Keep slide text short and visual-friendly.

## Slide 1: Unit 03
- Recipe Reading
- Mise en Place
- Read before cooking
- Organize before acting

Speaker notes: Set the anchor phrase for the week.

## Slide 2: Why This Unit Matters
- Recipes are directions for action
- Guessing creates mistakes
- Organized stations reduce stress
- Bistro service depends on prep

Speaker notes: Keep this practical. Students should see recipe reading as kitchen behavior.

## Slide 3: This Week
- Food Handler review
- Recipe reading
- Peanut butter bars
- Rice cookery
- Fried rice or stir fry

Speaker notes: Preview the week without overexplaining every lab.

## Slide 4: Daily Routine
- Enter calmly
- Check the agenda
- Join the huddle
- Wash hands when directed
- Wait to begin

Speaker notes: Keep routines consistent from Units 01 and 02.

## Slide 5: Food Handler Habits Continue
- Wash hands
- Prevent cross-contact
- Treat allergens seriously
- Sanitize stations
- Store ingredients correctly

Speaker notes: Connect safety review to actual labs.

## Slide 6: Read the Whole Recipe
- Title
- Yield
- Ingredients
- Equipment
- Steps and timing

Speaker notes: Model with a simple recipe students will actually use.

## Slide 7: Ingredient Lists
- Check amounts
- Check ingredient form
- Watch allergens
- Use shared ingredients carefully
- Close and return items

Speaker notes: "Form" means chopped, melted, cooked, cold, softened, or similar directions.

## Slide 8: Equipment Lists
- Gather tools first
- Use the right measuring tool
- Keep tools organized
- Return tools clean
- Ask before equipment

Speaker notes: Students often gather food but forget tools.

## Slide 9: Step Sequencing
- Read all steps first
- Find what happens before cooking
- Notice wait time
- Keep the recipe visible
- Do not skip steps

Speaker notes: Sequence is the order that helps work make sense.

## Slide 10: Prep Lists
- Tasks before cooking
- Who does each job
- What needs measuring
- What needs heat
- What gets cleaned during lab

Speaker notes: Connect prep lists to teamwork and cleaning.

## Slide 11: Measuring Accuracy
- Use the correct tool
- Level dry ingredients
- Check liquid measurements
- Measure over a separate bowl
- Slow down for fractions

Speaker notes: Demonstrate one dry and one liquid measurement.

## Slide 12: Mise en Place
- Recipe visible
- Ingredients ready
- Tools ready
- Towel and dish plan ready
- Counter clean and usable

Speaker notes: Show a ready station before students start.

## Slide 13: Lab Safety
- Peanut allergy awareness
- Hot pans and steam
- Crowding creates risk
- Use "hot" and "behind"
- Ask when unsure

Speaker notes: Name the risks before each lab.

## Slide 14: Cleanup and Reset
- Dishes moving
- Counters sanitized
- Tools returned
- Ingredients stored
- Trash and floor checked

Speaker notes: Recipe work is not done until reset is done.

## Slide 15: Bistro Connection
- Prep lists support service
- Station readiness saves time
- Timing matters
- Communication matters
- Reset protects the next team

Speaker notes: Keep the Bistro connection concrete: prep, timing, station readiness.

## Slide 16: Exit Reflection
- What did you read first?
- What did your group organize?
- What mistake did prep prevent?
- What reset habit needs practice?

Speaker notes: Use as exit ticket or quick huddle response.
