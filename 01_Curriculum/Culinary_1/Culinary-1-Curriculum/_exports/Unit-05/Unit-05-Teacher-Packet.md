# Unit 05 Teacher Packet: Bistro Systems Reinforcement

## Week Overview
Focus: stabilize service before moving deeper into technical skills.

This week uses the Unit 04 soft opening as evidence. Students debrief what happened, retrain stations, clarify roles, practice ticket flow, run mock service 2.0, complete Bistro prep, and run a controlled Bistro service with stronger reset habits.

The goal is not to add complexity. The goal is a calmer system: clear roles, visible tickets, useful communication, steady timing, active dish flow, clean stations, and a full reset.

## Day-by-Day Flow

### Monday: Soft Opening Debrief
- Opening: entry routine, agenda check, huddle.
- Mini-lesson: debrief with facts, not blame: what worked, what slowed down, what to practice next.
- Student work: name one strength, one guest-impact issue, and one station problem.
- Application: station reset rehearsal based on soft opening problems.
- Prompt: "Which system needs to improve before the next service?"
- Reset: dish, towels, counters, floors, and station reset practice.

### Tuesday: Station Retraining
- Opening: huddle on role clarity.
- Mini-lesson: what each station does before, during, and after service.
- Student work: name station tools, tasks, timing needs, ticket/task flow, and reset job.
- Application: station setup, task rehearsal, and reset practice.
- Prompt: "What are your station's top three jobs?"
- Reset: tools, sanitizer, towels, trash, storage, and floors checked.

### Wednesday: Ticket Flow and Mock Service 2.0
- Opening: communication warm-up with call-backs.
- Mini-lesson: readable tickets, call-backs, expo communication, and respectful corrections.
- Student work: practice ticket scenarios.
- Application: mock service 2.0 with one planned reset pause and one recovery moment.
- Prompt: "What phrase helped the team stay organized?"
- Reset: dish, counters, service area, station, towels, and floors reset.

### Thursday: Bistro Prep
- Opening: prep huddle with roles and timing.
- Mini-lesson: prep lists, completion checks, labeling, storage, and usable reset.
- Student work: complete assigned prep and station setup.
- Application: Bistro prep block with teacher checkpoints.
- Prompt: "What is ready for service, and what is still unclear?"
- Reset: label, store, clean, dish, return tools, and reset.

### Friday: Bistro Service
- Opening: readiness check, huddle, roles confirmed.
- Mini-lesson: final reminders for hospitality, timing, ticket flow, communication, and clean-as-you-go.
- Student work: run controlled Bistro service in assigned roles.
- Application: service, guest awareness, ticket flow, dish flow, reset, debrief.
- Prompt: "What improved from the soft opening?"
- Reset: full room reset before reflection.

## Setup Notes
- Bring soft opening notes from Unit 04.
- Post station assignments and communication standards.
- Prepare tickets or order slips for mock service 2.0.
- Prepare a short Bistro prep list.
- Keep menu or service items simple.
- Confirm dish, towel, trash, storage, and floor reset roles before service.

## Soft Opening Debrief Structure
Use three columns:
- What worked
- What broke down
- What we will practice next

Keep debrief factual. Redirect blame into system language: role, timing, ticket, dish, sanitation, reset, or communication. End with one visible practice target for the day.

## Station Retraining Notes
- Each station needs a setup job, service job, timing cue, communication line, and reset job.
- Make students say the role before doing the work.
- Rehearse station reset before speed matters.
- Check whether the station would be useful to the next group.

## Ticket Flow Coaching Points
- Ticket must be readable.
- Ticket goes to the correct station.
- Station calls back key information.
- Ticket stays visible while work is happening.
- Expo checks before handoff.
- Ticket/order is closed or reset when complete.

## Communication Standards
- Short
- Clear
- Respectful
- Confirmed
- Useful

Useful phrases:
- "Heard"
- "Order in"
- "Working"
- "Order up"
- "I need help"
- "Reset needed"

## Mock Service 2.0 Plan
- Start with one order path.
- Use real ticket language.
- Pause once for a reset check.
- Practice one recovery: lost ticket, late call-back, crowded expo, or dish backup.
- Add a second round only when the first round is clean.
- Debrief systems, not personalities.

## Bistro Prep Notes
- Keep prep list short and visible.
- Check labeling and storage before counters are wiped.
- Connect prep to Friday service.
- Ask: "Will this help the next station work?"

## Bistro Service Notes
- Watch roles, tickets, movement, dish, sanitation, and reset more than speed.
- Coach hospitality as behavior: greeting, tone, awareness, follow-through.
- Stop service briefly if safety, ticket flow, or dish flow breaks down.

## Sanitation and Reset Expectations
Service is not complete until:
- Stations are reset
- Tickets/tasks are accounted for
- Dishes are routed correctly
- Counters are cleaned and sanitized
- Tools are returned
- Ingredients are labeled or stored as directed
- Towels are handled correctly
- Trash and floors are checked
- Service area is ready for the next group

## Teacher Reflection Space
- Which system improved from Unit 04?

- Which station still needs retraining?

- What communication phrase helped ticket flow?

- Was dish flow active during service?

- What reset habit needs repetition?

- What should be carried into the knife skills unit?
