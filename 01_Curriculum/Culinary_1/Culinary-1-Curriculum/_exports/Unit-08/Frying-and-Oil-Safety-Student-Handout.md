# Frying and Oil Management Student Handout
## Purpose
This handout gives you the key habits for Unit 08: Temperature discipline.
The goal is practical kitchen confidence. Work safely, stay organized, use clear words, and reset the station for the next group.
## Key Skills
- oil safety
- fryer safety
- temperature awareness
- batch control
- overcrowding
- crisp vs. greasy
- draining
- seasoning timing
- potato batonnet
- vegetable fritters
- onion rings or similar low-cost applications
## What To Watch For
- Safety before speed.
- Setup before action.
- Small corrections before big problems.
- Clean station before, during, and after work.
- Team communication that helps the task move.
## Bistro Connection
Frying during Bistro service depends on timing, batch size, consistency, crisp texture, safe movement, and a clean oil station. A crowded or greasy fryer slows service and creates risk.
## Student Self-Check
- [ ] I understand today's task.
- [ ] My station is set up.
- [ ] I followed safety expectations.
- [ ] I communicated when needed.
- [ ] I supported dish flow and reset.
- [ ] My station is ready for the next group.

## Oil Station Non-Negotiables
- Water stays away from hot oil.
- Only assigned students enter the oil boundary.
- Food is dried or prepared as directed before frying.
- Batches stay small enough to protect temperature and texture.
- Crisp food needs draining and seasoning at the right time.
