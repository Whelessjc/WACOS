# Lab Plan: Frying and Oil Management

## Lab Purpose
Teach safe oil habits and basic frying control through low-cost, controlled applications.

## Skill Focus
- Oil safety
- Batch control
- Temperature awareness
- Draining
- Seasoning timing
- Oil station reset

## Ingredients or Materials
- Potatoes
- Vegetables for fritters
- Onions or similar item
- Batter or breading ingredients if selected
- Oil
- Seasoning

## Equipment
- Fryer or heavy pot
- Thermometer
- Spider, tongs, or slotted spoon
- Sheet pans
- Racks or paper-lined draining area as allowed
- Towels and sanitizer

## Setup
- Establish oil station boundaries.
- Assign roles: cutter/prepper, batch manager, oil cook, drainer, steward.
- Keep water away from oil.
- Stage draining and seasoning area before cooking.

## Procedure
1. Review oil safety and temperature goal.
2. Prep item for frying.
3. Set up draining and seasoning area.
4. Teacher checks batch size.
5. Fry in controlled batches.
6. Drain and season at the correct time.
7. Compare texture.
8. Clean oil station and reset.

## Teacher Checkpoints
- Food dry enough for oil.
- Batch size controlled.
- Tools used safely.
- Students stand clear of hot oil path.
- Floor and counter stay clean.

## Cleanup and Reset Expectations
Oil handled only as teacher directs, tools cleaned, counters degreased as appropriate, floors checked carefully, towels managed, station reset.

## Possible Variations
- Teacher-led frying only.
- Use oven "fry comparison" if hot oil is not appropriate for a class.
- Limit to potato batonnet and texture comparison.
