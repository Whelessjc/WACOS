# Unit 08: Frying and Oil Management

## Weeks Covered
Week 13

## Focus
Temperature discipline.

## Purpose
Students practice oil safety, batch control, avoiding overcrowding, crisp vs. greasy texture, draining, seasoning timing, oil-station stewardship, potato batonnet, vegetable fritters, onion rings, or similar low-cost applications.

## Learning Targets
- I can explain why oil temperature matters.
- I can follow hot oil safety expectations.
- I can fry in small batches without overcrowding.
- I can drain and season fried foods correctly.
- I can describe crisp, greasy, undercooked, and overcooked results.

## Core Skills
- Oil safety
- Temperature awareness
- Batch control
- Safe loading and removal
- Draining
- Seasoning timing
- Oil station cleanup
- Floor and towel awareness

## Vocabulary
| Term | Student-Friendly Meaning |
|---|---|
| Frying | Cooking food in hot oil or fat. |
| Batch | A small amount cooked at one time. |
| Overcrowding | Adding too much food, lowering temperature. |
| Drain | Let extra oil come off food. |
| Crisp | Firm and crunchy from proper frying. |
| Greasy | Heavy with oil, often from poor temperature control. |

## Lab Applications
- Potato batonnet
- Fries
- Vegetable fritters
- Onion rings or similar low-cost item

## Bistro Connection
Frying during service requires discipline, communication, timing, safety, and a clean oil station that does not create hazards for the next person.

## Assessment Ideas
- Oil safety observation
- Batch size check
- Texture comparison
- Draining and seasoning observation
- Cleanup/reset inspection

## Teacher Notes
Keep hot oil controlled. This unit should be tightly supervised and simple.

## Resources Needed
- Equipment: fryer or pot, thermometer, spider/tongs, sheet pans, racks, towels, sanitizer
- Ingredients: potatoes, vegetables, batter or breading ingredients as selected
- Materials: oil safety reminders and batch plan
