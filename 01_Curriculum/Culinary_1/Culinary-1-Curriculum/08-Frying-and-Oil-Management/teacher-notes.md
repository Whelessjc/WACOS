# Teacher Notes: Frying and Oil Management

## What to Emphasize
- Hot oil requires discipline.
- Small batches protect texture and safety.
- Crisp food depends on temperature control.
- Seasoning timing matters.
- Cleanup around oil must be careful.
- Frying connects to service only when batch work, timing, and communication stay controlled.

## Common Student Mistakes
- Crowding the oil.
- Dropping food from too high.
- Walking too close to hot oil.
- Forgetting to drain.
- Seasoning too late.
- Leaving oily floors or counters.

## Classroom Management Notes
- Limit students at the oil station.
- Assign clear roles.
- Use teacher approval before food enters oil.
- Keep traffic away from hot oil.
- Treat oily counters and floors as immediate reset issues, not end-of-class jobs.

## Safety Concerns
- Burns from oil.
- Oil spills.
- Wet food entering oil.
- Crowding.
- Slippery floors.

## Pacing Notes
This is a one-week discipline unit. Keep applications low-cost and controlled.

## Repetition Needed
Batch size, safe tool use, draining, seasoning, communication, and floor checks.

## Connection to Later Units
Protein and Bistro service require the same temperature discipline, timing, and station control.
