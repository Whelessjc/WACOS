# Assessment Notes: Frying and Oil Management

## Formative Assessment Ideas
- Oil safety questions
- Batch size check
- Texture comparison
- Draining observation
- Seasoning timing check
- Cleanup observation
- Batch timing and communication observation

## Performance Assessment Ideas
Students demonstrate readiness by safely preparing a small batch, controlling overcrowding, draining and seasoning correctly, and resetting the oil station.

## Observation Points
- Does the student respect oil boundaries?
- Does the student wait for approval?
- Does the student control batch size?
- Does the student notice texture?
- Does the student help clean oily surfaces and floors?
- Does the student communicate when a batch is ready, waiting, or delayed?

## Simple Rubric Categories
| Category | Evidence |
|---|---|
| Safety | Works carefully around hot oil. |
| Batch Control | Avoids overcrowding. |
| Texture Awareness | Identifies crisp vs. greasy. |
| Timing | Drains and seasons appropriately. |
| Service Readiness | Uses batch timing and communication. |
| Reset | Leaves oil station clean and safe. |

## Evidence of Readiness
The student can work around frying equipment with discipline and explain how temperature and batch size affect results.
