# Unit 08 Lab Teacher Guide: Frying and Oil Management

## Setup Before Class
- Decide whether frying is teacher-led or student-managed with approval.
- Establish a clear oil station boundary.
- Stage fryer or pot, thermometer, spider/tongs, draining area, sheet pans, towels, sanitizer, and dish supplies.
- Pre-plan batch sizes and roles.
- Keep water sources away from oil work.
- Prepare floor and counter cleanup supplies.

## Demo Points
- Oil station boundary and traffic path.
- Safe loading and removal.
- Proper batch size.
- Draining and seasoning timing.
- Crisp vs. greasy cues.
- What oily counter/floor reset looks like.

## Safety Watch-Outs
- Wet food near hot oil.
- Students dropping food from too high.
- Crowding the oil station.
- Students walking behind the oil cook.
- Oily towels, counters, or floors.
- Students trying to handle oil without direction.

## Skill Checkpoints
- Food is prepared safely for oil.
- Batch size is controlled.
- Student has teacher approval before frying.
- Tools are used correctly.
- Food is drained and seasoned at the right time.
- Oil station and floor stay safe.

## Common Student Mistakes
- Overcrowding.
- Forgetting to dry food as directed.
- Standing too close to oil.
- Waiting too long to season.
- Ignoring oily floors.
- Leaving reset for the last minute.

## Cleanup Timing
- Start dish and draining-area cleanup early.
- Check counters and floors throughout.
- Keep oil handling teacher-managed.
- Inspect floor before students leave.
- Require a full oil-station reset before dismissal.

## What to Look For While Circulating
- Is the oil boundary respected?
- Are batch sizes small enough?
- Are students communicating near hot oil?
- Is texture connected to temperature and batch size?
- Are floors and towels safe?
- Is cleanup shared?

## Bistro Connection
Frying can support Bistro service only when the station is controlled. Connect batch work to ticket timing, quality, communication, and safety.

## Quick Debrief Prompts
- What made the food crisp?
- What made the station safe?
- What would slow service at this station?
- What cleanup habit matters most with oil?
