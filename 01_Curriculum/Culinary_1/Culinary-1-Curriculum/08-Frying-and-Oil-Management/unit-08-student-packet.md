# Unit 08 Student Packet: Frying and Oil Management

## What We Are Learning
This week we are learning temperature discipline with frying and oil management. You will practice oil safety, batch control, draining, seasoning timing, texture comparison, and oil-station reset.

## Why We Are Learning It
Hot oil can make food crisp, but it also creates serious safety risks. Frying only works when people move calmly, follow roles, control batch size, and clean carefully.

## What Success Looks Like
- I can explain why oil temperature matters.
- I can follow hot oil safety rules.
- I can fry in small batches without overcrowding.
- I can drain and season fried foods correctly.
- I can describe crisp, greasy, undercooked, and overcooked results.
- I can help reset an oil station safely.

## Daily Kitchen Entry Routine
1. Enter calmly.
2. Put belongings away.
3. Check the agenda.
4. Check uniform/readiness.
5. Join the huddle.
6. Wash hands when directed.
7. Begin work only when released.

## Key Expectations
- Respect the oil station boundary.
- Wait for teacher approval.
- Keep water away from hot oil.
- Work in small batches.
- Communicate before moving near oil.
- Treat oily counters and floors as safety issues.

## Kitchen Communication
- "Hot"
- "Behind"
- "Oil station"
- "Batch ready"
- "Draining"
- "Floor"
- "I need help"

## Safety Expectations
- Do not crowd the oil station.
- Do not drop food from high above oil.
- Use tools as directed.
- Keep towels and water away from oil.
- Report spills immediately.
- Handle oil only as directed by the teacher.

## Frying Practice Expectations
- Prep food before approaching oil.
- Keep batches small.
- Drain correctly.
- Season at the right time.
- Watch texture and color.
- Clean during the lab, not just at the end.

## Cleanup and Reset Checklist
- [ ] Oil handled only as teacher directs
- [ ] Tools cleaned and returned
- [ ] Draining area cleaned
- [ ] Counters cleaned/degreased and sanitized as directed
- [ ] Towels handled correctly
- [ ] Trash checked
- [ ] Floor checked carefully
- [ ] Station ready for the next group

## Bistro Connection
Fried items need timing and control. During Bistro service, small batches, clear communication, and clean floors help protect the team and keep food crisp.

## Student Reflection Questions
1. What oil safety rule mattered most today?
2. What happened when a batch was too large or just right?
3. What made the food crisp or greasy?
4. What communication helped near the oil station?
5. What reset job protected the next group?
