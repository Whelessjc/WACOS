# Unit 08 Lab Student Handout: Frying and Oil Management

## Purpose of the Lab
The purpose of this lab is to practice safe oil habits and basic frying control. The goal is temperature discipline, small batches, proper draining, seasoning timing, and a clean oil station.

## Skills Practiced
- Oil safety
- Batch control
- Temperature awareness
- Safe loading and removal
- Draining
- Seasoning timing
- Oil-station cleanup and reset

## Equipment Needed
- Fryer or heavy pot as directed
- Thermometer if assigned
- Spider, tongs, or slotted spoon
- Sheet pans
- Racks or draining area
- Towels
- Sanitizer and dish supplies

## Ingredients / Materials
Your teacher may assign potatoes, vegetables, onion rings or a similar low-cost item, batter or breading ingredients, oil, and seasonings.

## Mise en Place / Setup Checklist
- [ ] Hands washed
- [ ] Oil station boundary understood
- [ ] Role assigned
- [ ] Food prepped and dry enough as directed
- [ ] Batch size checked
- [ ] Draining area ready
- [ ] Seasoning ready
- [ ] Dish and floor plan clear
- [ ] Teacher approval received before frying

## Procedure
1. Complete the entry routine and oil safety huddle.
2. Prepare the food for frying.
3. Set up draining and seasoning area.
4. Confirm batch size with teacher.
5. Fry only as directed.
6. Drain and season at the correct time.
7. Compare texture, color, and greasiness.
8. Clean the oil station and reset.

## Teamwork Expectations
- Stay in assigned roles.
- Keep nonessential people away from the oil station.
- Communicate before moving near hot oil.
- Share dish, counter, towel, and floor cleanup.
- Stop and ask if something feels unsafe.

## Safety Reminders
- Hot oil can burn quickly.
- Keep water away from oil.
- Do not crowd or reach across the oil station.
- Use tools, not fingers.
- Report oily floors immediately.
- Handle oil only as the teacher directs.

## Cleanup and Reset Checklist
- [ ] Oil handled only as teacher directs
- [ ] Tools cleaned and returned
- [ ] Draining area cleaned
- [ ] Counters cleaned/degreased and sanitized as directed
- [ ] Towels handled correctly
- [ ] Trash checked
- [ ] Floor checked carefully
- [ ] Station ready for the next group

## Bistro Connection
Frying during service depends on small batches, timing, clear communication, and a clean floor. The station has to be safe before it can be useful.

## Reflection Questions
1. What batch choice did your group make?
2. What showed the food was crisp or greasy?
3. What safety habit did you use near oil?
4. What did your group do to keep the floor safe?
5. What would you repeat next time?
