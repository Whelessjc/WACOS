# Unit 08 Teacher Packet: Frying and Oil Management

## Unit Overview
Week 13 focus: temperature discipline.

Students practice hot oil safety, temperature awareness, batch control, avoiding overcrowding, crisp vs. greasy texture, draining, seasoning timing, oil-station stewardship, potato batonnet, vegetable fritters, onion rings, or similar low-cost applications.

## Week at a Glance
| Day | Focus | Main Application |
|---|---|---|
| Monday | Oil safety and temperature | Teacher-led frying observation |
| Tuesday | Batch control | Potato batonnet and fries practice |
| Wednesday | Crisp vs. greasy | Vegetable fritters or low-cost comparison |
| Thursday | Safe loading/removal | Onion rings or similar application |

## Daily Lesson Flow

### Monday: Oil Safety and Temperature
- Opening: huddle with hot oil expectations.
- Mini-lesson/demo: oil safety, temperature, movement, tools, and boundaries.
- Student work: identify unsafe oil-station choices.
- Application: teacher-led frying observation.
- Prompt: "What oil safety rule protects people?"
- Reset: oil station, tools, counters, towels, and floor checked.

### Tuesday: Potato Batonnet and Batch Control
- Opening: knife and oil safety review.
- Mini-lesson/demo: potato batonnet, drying food, and batch size.
- Student work: cut potatoes and prepare small batches.
- Application: fries or potato frying practice.
- Prompt: "What happens when a batch is too large?"
- Reset: draining area, tools, counters, towels, and floors reset.

### Wednesday: Crisp vs. Greasy
- Opening: temperature discipline huddle.
- Mini-lesson/demo: draining and seasoning timing.
- Student work: compare texture results.
- Application: vegetable fritters or similar low-cost item.
- Prompt: "What made the item crisp or greasy?"
- Reset: oil area safety check and station reset.

### Thursday: Onion Rings or Similar Application
- Opening: roles confirmed and boundaries reviewed.
- Mini-lesson/demo: safe loading and removing from oil.
- Student work: set up batter/breading and batches.
- Application: onion rings or teacher-selected item.
- Prompt: "What habit kept the oil station controlled?"
- Reset: full station and oil area reset.

### Friday: Review and Controlled Application
- Opening: huddle and safety review.
- Mini-lesson/demo: reteach the weakest oil system.
- Student work: small controlled frying task or station review.
- Application: teacher-selected review lab.
- Prompt: "Explain temperature discipline in one sentence."
- Reset: deep reset of oil station and nearby floor.

## Setup Notes
- Decide whether frying is teacher-led, student-managed with approval, or observation only.
- Establish oil station boundaries.
- Stage thermometer, spider/tongs, sheet pans, racks or draining area, towels, sanitizer, and dish supplies.
- Keep water away from oil.
- Pre-plan batch sizes.
- Assign oil station roles clearly.
- Prepare a floor and counter degreasing/reset plan.

## Teacher Language and Prompts
- "Hot oil requires discipline."
- "Small batches protect texture and safety."
- "Do not crowd the oil."
- "Crisp food depends on temperature."
- "Drain first, season at the right time."
- "Oily floors are immediate reset issues."

## Safety Reminders
- Limit students at the oil station.
- Keep water away from hot oil.
- Dry food as directed before frying.
- Use tools, not fingers, near oil.
- Do not drop food from high above the oil.
- Keep traffic away from the oil station.
- Handle oil only as the teacher directs.

## Classroom Management Reminders
- Use teacher approval before food enters oil.
- Assign cutter/prepper, batch manager, oil cook, drainer, and steward.
- Keep all nonessential students outside the oil boundary.
- Stop immediately for spills, crowding, or unsafe movement.
- Keep the applications low-cost and controlled.

## Cleanup and Reset Expectations
Oil-station work is complete when:
- Oil is handled only as directed
- Tools are cleaned and stored
- Counters are degreased/sanitized as appropriate
- Draining area is cleaned
- Towels are handled correctly
- Trash is checked
- Floors are checked carefully
- Station is ready for the next group

## Unit Lab Plan
Purpose: teach safe oil habits and basic frying control through low-cost, controlled applications.

Skills practiced:
- Oil safety
- Temperature awareness
- Batch control
- Safe loading and removal
- Draining
- Seasoning timing
- Oil station reset

Suggested roles:
- Cutter/prepper: prepares food for frying
- Batch manager: controls batch size
- Oil cook: works at oil station with teacher approval
- Drainer/seasoner: drains and seasons at the right time
- Steward: watches towels, counters, floor, dish, and final reset

Teacher checkpoints:
- Food is dry enough for oil
- Batch size is controlled
- Tools are used safely
- Students stand clear of hot oil path
- Food is drained and seasoned correctly
- Floor and counter stay clean

## Bistro Connection
Frying during service requires timing, communication, batch control, safety, and a clean oil station. A fryer can slow the whole service if the station becomes crowded or greasy.
