## What We Are Learning
Safety becomes behavior.
You will practice:
- food safety
- personal hygiene
- cleaning and sanitation
- cross contamination
- allergens
- time and temperature control
- thermometer use
- laundry, counters, dishes, storage, floors
- three-compartment sink and dish machine
## Why We Are Learning It
Food safety is not just something to pass on a test. It is how we handle hands, towels, dishes, counters, storage,
thermometers, and shared equipment every day.
## What Success Looks Like
- I can explain the main skill in plain language.
- I can follow safety and sanitation expectations.
- I can set up my station before working.
- I can work at a controlled pace.
- I can communicate with my team.
- I can clean and reset without being chased.
## Student Expectations
- Enter calmly and check the agenda.
- Join the huddle and listen for the task.
- Wash hands when directed.
- Use tools and equipment only as approved.
- Ask for help before the station becomes unsafe or behind.
- Treat stewardship as part of the work.
## Lab / Service Expectations
- Read the task before acting.
- Set up tools and ingredients first.
- Keep your station clean enough to keep working.
- Watch timing, communication, and safety.
- Support dish flow and reset during the task, not only at the end.
## Cleanup and Reset Checklist
- [ ] Dishes routed correctly
- [ ] Counters cleaned and sanitized
- [ ] Laundry and towels handled correctly
- [ ] Storage checked and labeled if needed
- [ ] Trash and floors checked
- [ ] Station ready for the next group
## Reflection Questions
1. What skill did you practice today?
2. What safety or sanitation habit did you use?
3. What correction helped your work?
4. How did your work support the team or Bistro?
5. What reset job did you complete?
