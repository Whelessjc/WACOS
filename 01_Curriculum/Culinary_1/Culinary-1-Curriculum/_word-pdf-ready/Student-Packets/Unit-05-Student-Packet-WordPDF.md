## What We Are Learning
This week we are strengthening Bistro systems. You will practice debriefing, station roles, ticket flow, communication,
prep timing, service, dish flow, and reset.

## Why Systems Need Repetition
Service does not get better just because we tried it once. It gets better when the team studies what happened, fixes
the system, and practices again.

Systems reduce confusion. They help people know what to do, what to say, where the ticket goes, and how to reset for
the next group.

## Debrief Routine
After service, we talk about the system:
1. What worked?
2. What slowed down?
3. What will we practice next?

Debrief is not for blaming people. It is for making service calmer, safer, and more useful for guests.

7. Begin work only when released.

## Station Responsibility
Before service:
- Know your role.
- Know your tools.
- Know your setup.
- Know your prep.
- Know what needs to be ready first.
- Know your ticket or task flow.
- Know your reset job.

During service:
- Stay in role unless reassigned.
- Keep tickets or tasks visible.
- Communicate early.
- Help before the station falls behind.
- Reset what you can while service is still moving.
- Keep clean and dirty items separated.

After service:
- Close or account for tickets/tasks.
- Route dishes correctly.
- Return tools.
- Store ingredients as directed.
- Leave the station ready for the next group.

## Role Clarity
If you are unsure, ask:
- What is my job right now?
- Who needs to hear this?
- Where does this ticket or task go next?
- What can I reset while I wait?

## Ticket Flow Expectations
1. Ticket is readable.
2. Ticket goes to the correct station.
3. Station calls back key information.
4. Work starts.
5. Expo checks before handoff.
6. Order moves out.
7. Ticket/order is completed and reset.

## Communication Expectations
Use short, useful language:
- "Heard"
- "Order in"
- "Working"
- "Order up"
- "I need help"
- "Behind"
- "Hot"
- "Reset needed"

## Prep Expectations
- Check the prep list before acting.
- Gather only what your station needs.
- Know what must be ready before service starts.
- Label and store items as directed.
- Keep the station clean enough to work.
- Ask before the prep list falls behind.

## Service Expectations
- Use your role.
- Communicate early.
- Keep tickets moving.
- Notice guest needs.
- Clean as you go.
- Support dish and reset before being asked.
- Remember that calm systems are part of hospitality.
