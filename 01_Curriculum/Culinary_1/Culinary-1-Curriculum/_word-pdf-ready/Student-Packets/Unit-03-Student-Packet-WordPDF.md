## Why Recipe Reading Matters Before Cooking
Good cooks do not guess their way through food. They read the recipe first so they know what they are making, what they
need, what happens first, and where mistakes could happen.

## Why Mise en Place Matters Before Action
Mise en place means your work is ready before you start. It helps your team stay calm, avoid missing ingredients, use
tools correctly, and clean as you go.

## How to Read a Recipe
Before cooking, look for:
- Title: what you are making
- Yield: how much it makes
- Ingredients: what food items and amounts are needed
- Equipment: what tools, bowls, pans, or machines are needed
- Steps: what happens and in what order
- Timing: baking, cooling, simmering, resting, or waiting time
- Safety notes: allergens, heat, raw food, sharp tools, or sanitation warnings

## How to Identify Ingredients, Equipment, and Steps
Ask your group:
- What ingredients do we need?
- What amounts do we need?
- What tools do we need?
- Which steps happen before cooking?
- Which steps need teacher approval?
- What could go wrong if we rush?

## How to Sequence Work
Sequence means order.

Before starting:
1. Read the whole recipe.
2. Find ingredients and equipment.
3. Notice timing and safety steps.
4. Decide what happens first.
5. Decide what can happen while something cooks, bakes, or rests.
6. Decide what cleanup can happen during the recipe.

## How to Build a Simple Prep List
A prep list is a short plan for what needs to happen before cooking.

Your prep list can include:
- Ingredients to gather
- Ingredients to measure
- Tools to gather
- Items to cut, mix, heat, or cook
- Team roles
- Teacher checkpoints
- Cleanup jobs during the lab

## Measuring Expectations
- Use the correct measuring tool.
- Measure dry ingredients carefully.
- Read liquid measurements at eye level when appropriate.
- Use measuring spoons for small amounts.
- Measure over a separate bowl when possible.
- Check the unit before adding ingredients.
- Slow down when reading fractions.

## Kitchen Communication
Use short, clear words:
- "Behind"
- "Hot"
- "Sharp"
- "Corner"
- "Heard"
- "I need help"
- "Spill"
- "Where does this go?"

## Safety Expectations
- Use Food Handler habits every day.
- Wash hands before food work and after contamination.
- Treat allergens seriously, especially peanut butter.
- Do not crowd hot pans, rice cookers, or cooking stations.
- Use "hot" and "behind" when moving around the kitchen.
- Ask before using equipment you are not cleared to use.
