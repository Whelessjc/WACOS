## Unit Connection
Unit 09: Flavor Systems

## Purpose
Practice tasting safely and making small adjustments with salt, acid, fat, aromatics, herbs, spices, or
teacher-selected flavor families.

## Skills Practiced
- clean tasting
- salt/acid/fat balance
- aromatics
- herbs and spices
- small adjustments
- respectful language
- allergen-aware reset

## Safety Reminders
- Use clean spoons every time.
- No double-dipping.
- Name allergens before tasting.
- Use respectful language about cultural flavor families.
- Sanitize tasting surfaces.

## Equipment
- small cups/spoons
- labels
- base food or sauce as directed
- measuring spoons
- sanitizer and towels

## Ingredients or Materials
Use teacher-approved ingredients, products, and quantities. Do not start until the teacher confirms the exact materials
for your station.

- Teacher-selected tasting base and flavor ingredients: [quantity placeholders]

## Mise en Place Checklist
- [ ] Read the full lab sheet before starting.
- [ ] Wash hands.
- [ ] Gather tools and materials.
- [ ] Set up towel, trash, dish, and sanitizer plan.
- [ ] Know your role.
- [ ] Wait for the teacher checkpoint before heat, knives, fryer, or raw protein work.

## Procedure
1. Set up tasting station with clean tools.
2. Name allergens before tasting.
3. Taste the base sample with a clean spoon.
4. Make one small adjustment.
5. Taste again with a clean spoon.
6. Record what changed.
7. Reset tasting tools, allergens, counters, and trash.

## Teacher Checkpoints
- [ ] Clean tasting rules are followed.
- [ ] Adjustments are small and intentional.
- [ ] Student can name what changed.
- [ ] Respectful language is used.
- [ ] Allergen/tasting reset is complete.

## Cleanup / Reset Checklist
- [ ] Dishes routed correctly.

- [ ] Tools returned clean and dry.
- [ ] Towels/laundry handled correctly.
- [ ] Trash checked.
- [ ] Floor checked.
- [ ] Food stored or discarded as directed.
- [ ] Station ready for the next group.
