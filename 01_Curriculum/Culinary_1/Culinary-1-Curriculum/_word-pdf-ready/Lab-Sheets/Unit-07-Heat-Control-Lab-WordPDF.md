## Unit Connection
Unit 07: Heat Control

## Purpose
Practice watching heat cues and adjusting cooking through saute, roasting, or moist-heat applications.

## Skills Practiced
- pan preheating
- oil control
- browning vs. steaming
- aromatics
- roasting spacing
- simmering
- liquid ratios
- hot-station reset

## Safety Reminders
- Use dry towels around heat.
- Turn handles in.
- Call hot items clearly.
- Ask before changing heat.
- Turn burners/ovens off as directed.

## Equipment
- skillet or pot
- sheet pan if roasting
- spatula/tongs/spoon
- measuring tools
- dry towels
- landing zone for hot pans

## Mise en Place Checklist
- [ ] Read the full lab sheet before starting.
- [ ] Wash hands.
- [ ] Gather tools and materials.
- [ ] Set up towel, trash, dish, and sanitizer plan.
- [ ] Know your role.
- [ ] Wait for the teacher checkpoint before heat, knives, fryer, or raw protein work.

## Procedure
1. Set up hot station before turning on heat.
2. Listen to the demo for today?s heat cue.
3. Preheat pan, oven, or liquid as directed.
4. Cook in controlled batches or ratios.
5. Watch color, steam, sound, smell, and texture.
6. Pause for teacher checkpoint.
7. Complete hot-station reset.

## Teacher Checkpoints
- [ ] Station is set before heat.
- [ ] Student can name the heat cue they are watching.
- [ ] Food is not overcrowded.
- [ ] Simmer/roast/saute technique matches the task.
- [ ] Hot reset is complete.
