## Unit Overview
**Weeks covered:** Week 18
**Focus:** Independence and readiness.

Students create independent prep lists, take limited service ownership, complete a skill inventory, reflect on
evidence, and have a Culinary 2 interest/readiness conversation. The shift is from functioning in the kitchen to
working with greater independence.

## Week / Day Flow
- Monday: Skill inventory using evidence from labs, service, cleaning, and teamwork.
- Tuesday: Independent prep list with tools, ingredients, timing, safety, and cleanup.
- Wednesday: Limited service ownership or prep ownership task.
- Thursday: Self-assessment and Culinary 2 readiness conversation.
- Friday: Final reflection, simple review task, and full course reset.

## Setup Notes
- [ ] Prepare skill inventory and readiness reflection copies.
- [ ] Choose limited ownership tasks students can complete safely.
- [ ] Plan how students will show evidence from real work.
- [ ] Set expectations for final stewardship reset.

## Demo Notes
- Model an evidence-based self-assessment.
- Show a complete prep list with timing and reset plan.
- Model ownership language: I have this, I need help, this is complete.
- Show how to reflect honestly without bragging or shutting down.

## Safety Watch-Outs
- Do not relax kitchen standards during final week.
- Supervise independent work closely.
- Maintain handwashing, sanitation, storage, and floor checks.
- Use equipment only as approved.
- Keep readiness conversations tied to real evidence.

## Common Student Mistakes
- Students confuse confidence with readiness.
- Prep lists skip tools, timing, or cleanup.
- Students wait to be chased instead of owning a role.
- Final reset is treated as less important.
- Reflection is vague and not tied to real examples.

## Cleanup / Reset Notes
- Use final reset as evidence of readiness.
- Check tools, counters, dishes, storage, towels, trash, floors, and shared spaces.
- Name the difference between finishing a task and leaving the room ready.

## Bistro / Service Connection
Students show Bistro and Culinary 2 readiness by planning work, communicating, completing a role, supporting the team,
and resetting the kitchen without being chased.

## Reflection Space
- What worked well this unit?

- What routine or skill needs reteaching?

- Which students need extra support or a different role next time?

- What should be adjusted before teaching this unit again?

## Pacing Notes
- Keep instruction short and tied to the lab or service task.
- Reteach routines when safety, cleanup, or teamwork slips.
- Protect time for cleanup/reset.
