## Unit Overview
**Weeks covered:** Weeks 16 to 17
**Focus:** Protein tests fundamentals.

Students apply previous skills to chicken breast or thigh, ground turkey, egg cookery, searing, doneness, resting,
seasoning, slicing, temperature checking, food safety, knife control, heat control, mise en place, and flavor balance.

## Week / Day Flow
- Week 16: Protein safety, mise en place, seasoning, searing, doneness, resting, and slicing.
- Week 17: Ground turkey or egg cookery, temperature checks, timing, holding quality, and Bistro service readiness.

## Setup Notes
- [ ] Confirm approved proteins, thermometers, sanitation plan, and storage rules.
- [ ] Stage separate raw/clean areas and probe-cleaning supplies.
- [ ] Post safe temperatures according to local Food Handler guidance.
- [ ] Plan small portions so students can practice carefully.

## Demo Notes
- Show raw protein station setup and clean hand habits.
- Demonstrate seasoning, pan preheat, searing, and heat adjustment.
- Show thermometer placement and probe cleaning.
- Model resting and slicing for texture and service.
- Connect doneness to guest safety and holding quality.

## Safety Watch-Outs
- Keep raw and ready-to-eat items separate.
- Wash hands and change gloves as directed.
- Clean and sanitize protein contact surfaces.
- Use thermometers as evidence.
- Store and cool protein only as directed.

## Common Student Mistakes
- Students cross-contaminate tools or towels.
- Protein moves before it sears.
- Temperature is guessed by color only.
- Protein is sliced before resting.
- Seasoning and flavor balance are forgotten.
- Cleanup misses raw-protein contact points.

## Cleanup / Reset Notes
- Clean and sanitize all raw-protein surfaces, tools, boards, and handles.
- Route dishes carefully and keep clean/dirty separation visible.
- Check storage, labels, thermometers, towels, trash, and floors.
- Do not dismiss until protein safety reset is complete.

## Bistro / Service Connection
Protein connects to Bistro through timing, doneness, consistency, holding quality, guest safety, and service readiness.
This is where fundamentals have to work together.
