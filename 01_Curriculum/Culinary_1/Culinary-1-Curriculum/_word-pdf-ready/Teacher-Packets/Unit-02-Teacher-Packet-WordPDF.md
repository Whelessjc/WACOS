## Unit Overview
**Weeks covered:** Week 2
**Focus:** Safety becomes behavior.

Students connect Food Handler ideas to real kitchen behavior: hygiene, sanitation, cross contamination, allergens, time
and temperature control, laundry, counters, dishes, storage, floors, three-compartment sink, dish machine, thermometer
use, brownie labs, and chicken temperature checks.

## Week / Day Flow
- Monday: Food safety habits, personal hygiene, handwashing, and contamination risks.
- Tuesday: Stewardship jobs: laundry, counters, dishes, storage, floors, and dish systems.
- Wednesday: Brownie batter lab with sanitation, shared tools, and clean-as-you-go habits.
- Thursday: Baking brownies, oven safety, storage, and dish flow.
- Friday: Chicken breast temperature lab, thermometer use, and food-safety reflection.

## Setup Notes
- [ ] Post stewardship job assignments and dish-flow directions.
- [ ] Stage sanitizer, towels, dish racks, thermometers, and labels.
- [ ] Clarify three-compartment sink or dish machine expectations before lab work.
- [ ] Prepare brownie and chicken temperature lab materials according to local rules.

## Demo Notes
- Show handwashing and when to rewash.
- Model cleaning first, then sanitizing.
- Demonstrate thermometer placement, reading, and probe cleaning.
- Walk through dish machine or three-compartment sink flow.
- Show how sticky batter changes counter and dish cleanup.

## Safety Watch-Outs
- Separate raw, ready-to-eat, allergen, clean, and dirty items.
- Use sanitizer correctly and only where directed.
- Handle hot pans and ovens carefully.
- Use thermometer readings as evidence, not guessing.
- Keep floors dry and towels off counters/floors.

## Common Student Mistakes
- Students wipe but do not sanitize.
- Towels are used for too many jobs.
- Thermometers touch food and are not cleaned.
- Dish flow stops because no one owns it.
- Students treat stewardship as punishment instead of kitchen work.

## Cleanup / Reset Notes
- Make laundry, counters, dishes, storage, and floors visible jobs.
- Check dish area before dismissal, not after the bell.
- Require labeled/closed storage where needed.
- Name stewardship as professional responsibility.

## Bistro / Service Connection
Bistro service depends on safe hands, clean counters, active dish flow, controlled towels, safe storage, and students
who protect guests without being reminded.

## Pacing Notes
- Keep instruction short and tied to the lab or service task.
- Reteach routines when safety, cleanup, or teamwork slips.
- Protect time for cleanup/reset.
