# Unit 01: Orientation and Systems

## Weeks Covered
Week 1

## Focus
Learn the room before cooking.

## Purpose
Students learn how Culinary Arts Management 1 works before they begin regular cooking labs. The week introduces course expectations, uniforms, daily entry routine, agenda check, huddle, handwashing, basic safety, kitchen communication, measuring stations, station setup, cleanup, and a Friday pancake lab.

## Learning Targets
- I can explain the purpose of Culinary Arts Management 1.
- I can follow the daily entry routine, agenda check, and huddle.
- I can describe basic uniform and readiness expectations.
- I can use simple kitchen communication to move safely.
- I can measure basic ingredients with care.
- I can help my team set up, cook, clean, and reset.

## Core Skills
- Entry routine and class huddle
- Uniform readiness
- Handwashing expectations
- Basic kitchen safety
- Measuring dry and liquid ingredients
- Mise en place and station setup
- Cleanup and reset

## Vocabulary
| Term | Student-Friendly Meaning |
|---|---|
| System | A routine that helps the kitchen run smoothly. |
| Station | A work area set up for a specific task. |
| Uniform | Clothing required for safety, sanitation, and professionalism. |
| Huddle | A short group meeting before work begins. |
| Mise en place | Tools, ingredients, and plans ready before cooking. |
| Reset | Returning a station to clean, organized, ready condition. |

## Lab Applications
- Measuring stations
- Dry station setup practice
- Cleanup/reset practice
- Friday pancake lab

## Bistro Connection
Bistro service only works when students know the room, follow routines, communicate, and reset the space for the next group.

## Assessment Ideas
- Entry routine observation
- Measuring station check
- Station setup check
- Pancake lab teamwork and cleanup observation
- Short exit reflection on one routine students can now follow

## Teacher Notes
Keep the week calm and direct. The pancake lab is a systems lab, not a fancy cooking lab. The most important evidence is whether students can prepare, communicate, measure, work safely, and clean.

## Resources Needed
- Equipment: measuring cups, measuring spoons, bowls, whisks, griddle or pans, spatulas, towels, sanitizer
- Ingredients: teacher-selected pancake ingredients or mix
- Materials: agenda display, uniform expectations, station labels if available
