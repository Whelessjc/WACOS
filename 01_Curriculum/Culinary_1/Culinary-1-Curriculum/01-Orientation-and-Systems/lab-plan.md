# Lab Plan: Pancakes and First-Week Systems

## Lab Purpose
Use pancakes on Friday to practice first-week kitchen systems: recipe reading, basic measuring, mise en place, station setup, teamwork, heat awareness, cleanup, and reset.

## Skill Focus
- Read directions before acting
- Measure dry and liquid ingredients
- Set up a simple station
- Work in team roles
- Notice heat cues
- Clean as you go

## Ingredients or Materials
- Teacher-selected pancake mix or simple pancake recipe
- Flour, baking powder, sugar, and salt if using a scratch recipe
- Milk or water as needed
- Eggs or oil if the selected recipe requires them
- Small amount of cooking fat or spray for the cooking surface if used

## Equipment
- Mixing bowls
- Whisks or mixing spoons
- Measuring cups and spoons
- Liquid measuring cups
- Rubber spatulas
- Ladles, scoops, or portion cups if available
- Griddle or pans
- Spatulas
- Towels
- Sanitizer and dish supplies
- Trash access

## Setup
- Review uniform/readiness and handwashing.
- Review safe movement, kitchen communication, and heat source rules.
- Stage measuring tools and mixing tools.
- Keep heat sources off until teacher approval.
- Assign simple roles: reader, measurer, mixer, cook, steward.

## Procedure
1. Complete entry routine, agenda check, huddle, and handwashing.
2. Read the pancake directions all the way through.
3. Gather tools and ingredients.
4. Measure ingredients carefully.
5. Mix batter according to directions.
6. Get teacher approval before turning on heat.
7. Cook pancakes while watching bubbles, edges, color, and heat level.
8. Keep the station controlled while cooking.
9. Turn off equipment as directed.
10. Wash dishes, wipe and sanitize, manage towels, check floor, and reset.
11. Debrief what system helped the group most.

## Teacher Checkpoints
- Students read before starting.
- Station is set up before ingredients are mixed.
- Measuring tools match the task.
- Heat source is used only with approval.
- Cleanup is shared by the whole team.
- Students notice heat cues without rushing.
- Final station is actually reset, not just mostly clean.

## Heat Awareness Teaching Points
- Equipment must be approved before use.
- Pancakes show bubbles and set edges before flipping.
- Too much heat browns the outside too fast.
- Hot pans and griddles stay hot after use.
- Handles and towels need attention.

## Cleanup and Reset
Station is not done until dishes are handled, counters are sanitized, tools are returned, towels are managed, floor is checked, and the station is ready for the next class.

## Debrief Questions
- What helped your group stay organized?
- What did your group do safely?
- What routine did your group forget or rush?
- How did heat affect the pancakes?
- What should we do better in the next lab?

## Possible Variations
- Use teacher-portioned ingredients for classes needing more control.
- Run griddles as teacher-managed stations if students are not ready.
- Use pancake mix for a shorter class period.
