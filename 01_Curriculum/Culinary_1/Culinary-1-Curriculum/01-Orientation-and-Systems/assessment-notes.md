# Assessment Notes: Orientation and Systems

## Formative Assessment Ideas
- Entry routine observation
- Uniform/readiness check
- Kitchen area call-outs
- Measuring station check
- Handwashing observation
- Station setup check
- Pancake lab debrief response

## Performance Assessment Ideas
Students show first-week readiness by completing the entry routine, participating in huddle, measuring accurately enough for pancakes, setting up a station, working safely with a team, and completing cleanup/reset.

## Observation Points
- Does the student wait for directions before using equipment?
- Does the student communicate when moving around others?
- Does the student wash hands at expected times?
- Does the student measure with care?
- Does the student help clean, or leave cleanup to others?

## Simple Rubric Categories
| Category | Evidence |
|---|---|
| Safety | Walks, listens, communicates, waits for equipment approval. |
| Preparation | Follows entry routine, checks agenda, sets up station. |
| Measuring | Uses basic measuring tools with care. |
| Teamwork | Takes a role and supports the group. |
| Cleanup | Completes dishes, counters, towels, floor, and reset. |

## Evidence of Readiness
The student can work through a simple lab without unsafe behavior, can explain why systems come before cooking, and can leave the station ready for the next class.
