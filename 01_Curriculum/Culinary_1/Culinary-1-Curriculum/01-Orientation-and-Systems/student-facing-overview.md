# Student Overview: Orientation and Systems

## Week Focus
Learn the room before cooking.

## What This Week Is About
This week you will learn how our kitchen works. Before we cook a lot, we need to know how to enter, dress, move, communicate, wash hands, measure, set up, clean, and reset.

Culinary Arts Management 1 is about real cooking and real skills. You do not need to already be a chef. You do need to be willing to learn, practice, stay safe, help your team, and take care of the kitchen.

## What You Will Learn
- What this course is about
- What the uniform expectations are
- How to follow the daily entry routine
- How to communicate in the kitchen
- When and how to wash your hands
- Basic safety expectations
- How to measure ingredients
- How to set up a station
- How to clean and reset a station
- How to complete a simple first lab using pancakes

## Learning Targets
- I can explain the purpose and expectations of Culinary Arts Management 1.
- I can follow the daily entry routine.
- I can describe basic uniform and readiness expectations.
- I can use basic kitchen communication.
- I can measure simple ingredients with care.
- I can help my team set up, cook, clean, and reset.

## Key Words
| Word | Meaning |
|---|---|
| System | A routine or process that helps the kitchen run smoothly. |
| Station | A work area set up for a specific task. |
| Uniform | Required clothing that supports safety, sanitation, and professionalism. |
| Mise en place | Having ingredients, tools, and plans ready before working. |
| Reset | Returning a station to clean, organized, ready condition. |
| Stewardship | Taking care of shared spaces, tools, food, and people. |

## Friday Lab
On Friday, we will use pancakes for the first lab.

The pancake lab will help you practice:
- Reading directions before starting
- Measuring ingredients
- Setting up mise en place
- Working with a team
- Paying attention to heat
- Cleaning as you go
- Resetting your station

## What Success Looks Like
By the end of the week, you should be able to:
- Enter class correctly
- Understand the basic uniform expectations
- Move safely in the kitchen
- Wash hands when needed
- Use basic kitchen communication
- Find important kitchen areas
- Measure simple ingredients
- Set up and clean a station
- Explain why systems matter before cooking

## Remember
Cooking includes cleaning. Safety comes first. Organization makes cooking easier. This week is about building the habits that will make every future lab better.
