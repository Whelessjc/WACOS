# Unit 01 Teacher Packet: Orientation and Systems

## Unit Overview
Week 1 focus: learn the room before cooking.

Students learn how Culinary Arts Management 1 works before regular cooking labs begin. The week introduces course expectations, uniforms, daily entry routine, agenda check, huddle, handwashing, basic safety, kitchen communication, measuring stations, station setup, cleanup/reset, and a Friday pancake lab.

## Week at a Glance
| Day | Focus | Main Application |
|---|---|---|
| Monday | Course orientation and kitchen mindset | Entry routine, course purpose, uniform expectations |
| Tuesday | Entry routine, uniforms, room tour | Kitchen walk-through and communication practice |
| Wednesday | Handwashing, safety, measuring | Measuring stations |
| Thursday | Station setup, recipe reading, cleanup | Pancake lab dry run |
| Friday | First cooking lab | Pancakes and full station reset |

## Daily Lesson Flow

### Monday: Course Orientation and Kitchen Mindset
- Opening: welcome students, seat/starting area, agenda check.
- Mini-lesson: Culinary Arts Management 1 is real cooking, real skills, and safe shared work.
- Student work: name one kitchen skill or habit they want to build.
- Prompt: "Why do we learn systems before we cook?"
- Reset: practice end-of-class routine.

### Tuesday: Entry Routine, Uniforms, and Room Tour
- Opening: practice entry routine and agenda check.
- Mini-lesson: uniform/readiness expectations and why they matter.
- Student work: kitchen walk-through; locate sinks, sanitizer, towels, dish area, trash, storage, exits, and teacher-only areas.
- Prompt: "Where does this item belong?"
- Reset: return to starting area and review traffic problems.

### Wednesday: Handwashing, Safety, and Measuring Stations
- Opening: huddle on handwashing and safety.
- Demo: handwashing, dry measuring, liquid measuring, measuring spoons.
- Student work: rotate through measuring stations.
- Prompt: "Which measuring tool fits this ingredient?"
- Reset: clean stations and return tools.

### Thursday: Station Setup, Recipe Reading, and Cleanup
- Opening: huddle on read-before-cooking.
- Demo: read pancake directions, set mise en place, show ready station.
- Student work: dry station setup and cleanup/reset practice.
- Prompt: "What still needs to happen before this station is ready?"
- Reset: dish flow, counters, towels, tools, floor.

### Friday: Pancake Lab
- Opening: uniform/readiness check, agenda, huddle, handwashing.
- Demo: measuring review, batter setup, heat approval, flipping cues.
- Student work: pancake lab in simple team roles.
- Prompt: "What system helped your group today?"
- Reset: full station reset before dismissal.

## Setup Notes
- Post or display the daily agenda.
- Decide where students place belongings.
- Have uniform expectations ready in school-specific language.
- Stage measuring tools for Wednesday.
- Stage pancake equipment and ingredients before Friday.
- Decide whether griddles/pans are student-managed or teacher-managed.

Before Friday, confirm:
- Pancake recipe or mix
- Ingredient portion plan
- Heat-source approval plan
- Dish-flow and towel plan

## Teacher Language and Prompts
- "Learn the room before cooking."
- "Systems before speed."
- "Cooking includes cleaning."
- "Read before acting."
- "Reset means ready for the next group."
- "If you are unsure, pause and ask."
- "A useful teammate notices what needs doing."

## Safety Reminders
- Walk in the kitchen.
- Keep pathways clear.
- Report spills immediately.
- Ask before using equipment.
- Treat hot equipment as hot.
- Keep towels away from heat.
- Keep food and cleaning chemicals separate.
- Wash hands before food work and after contamination.

## Classroom Management Reminders
- Teach routines as repeated systems, not one-time directions.
- Stop the room early if movement becomes unsafe.
- Keep the first lab simple; the goal is systems.
- Give every student a job during lab.
- Watch for one student doing all the cleanup.
- Require full reset before dismissal.

## Cleanup and Reset Expectations
Students are not done when the food is done. A finished station has:
- Dishes handled correctly
- Counters wiped and sanitized
- Tools returned
- Towels handled correctly
- Trash and floor checked
- Heat source off
- Station ready for the next group

## Friday Pancake Lab Plan
Purpose: practice recipe reading, measuring, mise en place, station setup, teamwork, heat awareness, cleanup, and reset.

Suggested roles:
- Reader: reads directions and keeps the group on step
- Measurer: measures ingredients carefully
- Mixer: combines batter and watches texture
- Cook: works near the griddle or pan with teacher approval
- Steward: leads dishes, towels, trash, counters, and reset

Teacher checkpoints:
- Directions read before ingredients are touched
- Station set before mixing
- Measuring tools used correctly
- Heat used only with approval
- Students notice bubbles, set edges, color, and heat level
- Reset is complete

## Bistro Connection
Week 1 routines are the start of Bistro readiness. Students who can enter calmly, check the agenda, communicate, wash hands, set a station, and reset after pancakes are practicing the same habits they will need before service.

## Reflection Notes
After Week 1, note:
- Which routines students can follow now
- Which routines need reteaching in Week 2
- Whether measuring stations prepared students for the lab
- Whether pancake lab pacing was manageable
- What cleanup issue needs attention first
