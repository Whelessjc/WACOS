# Pancake Lab Student Handout

## Purpose of the Lab
This first lab uses pancakes to practice kitchen systems. The goal is not perfect pancakes. The goal is to read directions, measure carefully, set up the station, work with a team, pay attention to heat, and clean/reset correctly.

## Skills Practiced
- Recipe reading
- Basic measuring
- Mise en place
- Station setup
- Teamwork
- Heat awareness
- Cleanup and reset

## Equipment Needed
- Mixing bowl
- Whisk or mixing spoon
- Measuring cups
- Measuring spoons
- Liquid measuring cup
- Rubber spatula
- Ladle, scoop, or portion cup if used
- Griddle or pan
- Spatula
- Towel
- Sanitizer and dish supplies

## Ingredients
Use the pancake recipe or mix provided by your teacher.

Your group may need:
- Pancake mix or dry ingredients
- Milk or water
- Egg or oil if required
- Small amount of cooking fat or spray if directed

## Mise en Place Checklist
- [ ] Hands washed
- [ ] Directions read completely
- [ ] Team roles assigned
- [ ] Bowl and mixing tool ready
- [ ] Measuring tools ready
- [ ] Ingredients gathered or received
- [ ] Towel in correct place
- [ ] Trash and dish plan clear
- [ ] Heat source off until teacher approval

## Procedure
1. Complete the entry routine, agenda check, huddle, and handwashing.
2. Read the directions all the way through.
3. Gather tools and ingredients.
4. Measure ingredients carefully.
5. Mix batter according to directions.
6. Get teacher approval before turning on heat.
7. Cook pancakes while watching bubbles, edges, color, and heat level.
8. Keep the station controlled while cooking.
9. Turn off equipment as directed.
10. Wash dishes, wipe and sanitize counters, handle towels, check trash and floor, return tools, and reset the station.

## Teamwork Expectations
- Reader: reads directions and keeps the group on step.
- Measurer: measures ingredients carefully.
- Mixer: mixes batter and watches texture.
- Cook: works near heat with teacher approval.
- Steward: leads dishes, towels, trash, counters, and reset.

If your group is smaller, combine roles. Everyone still helps.

## Heat Awareness Reminders
- Do not turn on heat without approval.
- Hot pans and griddles stay hot after use.
- Watch for bubbles and set edges before flipping.
- Too much heat can brown the outside too fast.
- Keep towels away from heat.
- Say "hot" when moving near hot equipment.

## Cleanup and Reset Checklist
- [ ] Heat source off
- [ ] Dishes handled correctly
- [ ] Counters wiped and sanitized
- [ ] Tools returned
- [ ] Towels handled correctly
- [ ] Trash checked
- [ ] Floor checked
- [ ] Station ready for the next group

## Bistro Connection
Pancakes are not Bistro service, but the habits are connected. Reading directions, setting the station, communicating near heat, cleaning as you go, and resetting for the next group are the same habits service will need later.

## Reflection Questions
1. What system helped your group today?
2. What did your group do safely?
3. What measuring step required the most care?
4. How did heat affect the pancakes?
5. What cleanup job should your group improve next time?
