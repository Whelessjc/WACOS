# Unit 02 Teacher Packet: Food Handler and Stewardship

## Unit Overview
Week 2 focus: safety becomes behavior.

Students connect food safety to daily kitchen stewardship. This unit turns safety language into visible habits: personal hygiene, handwashing, cleaning and sanitation, cross-contamination prevention, allergen awareness, time and temperature control, laundry, counters, dishes, storage, floors, three-compartment sink, dish machine, and thermometer use.

## Daily Lesson Flow

### Monday: Food Safety and Personal Hygiene
- Opening: entry routine, uniform/readiness, agenda check, huddle, handwashing.
- Mini-lesson/demo: food safety protects classmates, guests, and the kitchen.
- Student work: identify contamination risks in common kitchen situations.
- Application: handwashing practice and station sanitation.
- Prompt: "What habit protects someone else?"
- Reset: counters, sinks, towels, trash, and floor check.

### Tuesday: Cleaning, Sanitizing, and Dish Systems
- Opening: huddle with stewardship jobs assigned.
- Mini-lesson/demo: cleaning vs. sanitizing, three-compartment sink, dish machine flow.
- Student work: rotate through dish, counter, laundry, and floor systems.
- Application: brownie batter lab with dish-flow emphasis.
- Prompt: "What is the difference between clean and sanitized?"
- Reset: full dish system and station reset.

### Wednesday: Cross-Contamination, Allergens, and Storage
- Opening: agenda check and food safety reminder.
- Mini-lesson/demo: cutting boards, clean tools, storage order, allergens, cross-contact.
- Student work: sort storage examples and identify allergen risks.
- Application: bake brownies while maintaining clean stations.
- Prompt: "How could this food become unsafe?"
- Reset: counters sanitized, tools returned, storage checked, floor checked.

### Thursday: Time, Temperature, and Thermometers
- Opening: huddle on temperature as evidence.
- Mini-lesson/demo: danger zone, internal temperature, thermometer placement and probe cleaning.
- Student work: practice thermometer reading and cleaning.
- Application: chicken breast temperature lab or teacher-led temperature demo.
- Prompt: "Why is temperature a safety tool, not just a number?"
- Reset: sanitize probes, reset protein station, handle towels, check floor.

### Friday: Stewardship Review and Skill Check
- Opening: students complete routine with less prompting.
- Mini-lesson/demo: reteach the weakest stewardship system from the week.
- Student work: food safety and stewardship scenario practice.
- Application: station reset challenge or dish-system timed practice.
- Prompt: "Which system does our class still need to improve?"
- Reset: full kitchen stewardship sweep.

## Setup Notes
- Prepare sanitizer according to school/district procedure.
- Decide how students rotate through dish, counter, laundry, floor, and storage jobs.
- Stage brownie ingredients or mix, pans, bowls, towels, and dish supplies.
- Label or clarify dish-flow steps if students are new to the sink or dish machine.
- Confirm whether students use the three-compartment sink, dish machine, or both.
- Keep raw protein work separate from brownie or ready-to-eat work.
- Stage thermometers and probe-cleaning supplies for temperature work.

Before the chicken breast temperature lab, confirm:
- Protein sample plan or teacher-demo plan
- Internal temperature target required by school guidance
- Thermometer cleaning procedure
- Raw/clean station boundaries
- Dish-flow and reset plan

## Teacher Language and Prompts
- "Safety becomes behavior."
- "Cleaning and dishes are real culinary work."
- "Sanitize after cleaning, not instead of cleaning."
- "Allergens are safety issues, not preferences."
- "Temperature is evidence."
- "Stewardship means taking care of the shared kitchen."
- "Do the job so the next group can trust the station."

## Safety Reminders
- Wash hands before food work and after contamination.
- Keep raw and ready-to-eat items separate.
- Treat allergens seriously.
- Use sanitizer correctly.
- Report spills immediately.
- Clean thermometer probes before and after use.
- Keep dish, laundry, and floor areas controlled.
- Follow teacher directions for the three-compartment sink and dish machine.

## Classroom Management Reminders
- Assign stewardship jobs early and rotate them fairly.
- Stop the room if sink, dish machine, or towel systems get messy.
- Use short resets during class, not only at the end.
- Keep protein temperature work tightly supervised.
- Watch for towel misuse and wet floors.
- Do not let students frame cleaning as punishment.
- Do not let one student become the whole group's cleaner.
- Use "protect the next group" as the reset standard.

## Cleanup and Reset Expectations
Students are not finished when the food is finished. A safe reset includes:
- Counters cleaned and sanitized
- Dishes washed, sanitized, or routed correctly
- Towels/laundry handled correctly
- Ingredients stored safely
- Thermometers cleaned and returned
- Trash and floors checked
- Station ready for the next group

## Unit Lab Plan
Purpose: use brownie batter, baking brownies, dish systems, and chicken temperature work to turn food safety into daily behavior.

Skills practiced:
- Handwashing and personal hygiene
- Cleaning and sanitizing
- Dish flow
- Allergen and cross-contamination prevention
- Thermometer use
- Full station reset

Suggested roles:
- Safety lead: watches handwashing and contamination risks
- Dish lead: tracks dish flow
- Counter lead: checks cleaning and sanitizing
- Storage lead: checks ingredients and storage
- Steward: leads towels, trash, floor, and final reset

Teacher checkpoints:
- Hands washed before food work
- Sanitizer used after cleaning
- Dish flow followed
- Allergens and cross-contact controlled
- Raw protein handled separately
- Thermometers inserted and cleaned correctly
- Reset is complete

## Bistro Connection
Guests trust the Bistro because students handle food safely, clean correctly, communicate concerns, and take stewardship jobs seriously before service ever begins.
