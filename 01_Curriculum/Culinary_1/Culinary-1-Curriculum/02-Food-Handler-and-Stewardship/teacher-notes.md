# Teacher Notes: Food Handler and Stewardship

## What to Emphasize
- Safety is behavior, not just vocabulary.
- Cleaning and dishes are real culinary work.
- Cross-contamination and allergens affect real people.
- Thermometers help students make safer decisions.
- Stewardship means taking care of shared spaces without being chased.

## Common Student Mistakes
- Rinsing instead of washing and sanitizing.
- Using towels for too many jobs.
- Leaving wet counters or dirty sink areas.
- Forgetting to clean thermometer probes.
- Treating allergens like preferences.
- Thinking food is safe because it "looks done."

## Classroom Management Notes
- Assign stewardship roles early: dishes, counters, towels, floors, storage.
- Stop the room when sink or dish flow gets messy.
- Use short resets during class, not only at the end.
- Keep protein temperature work tightly supervised.

## Safety Concerns
- Raw protein handling.
- Sanitizer strength and contact time.
- Wet floors.
- Hot pans from brownie baking.
- Dish machine heat or chemicals if used.

## Pacing Notes
Do not rush this week. Later labs depend on these systems. If students cannot handle dishes, towels, and counters now, every later unit becomes harder.

## Repetition Needed
Handwashing, towel use, three-compartment sink, dish return, counter sanitizing, and thermometer cleaning need repeated practice.

## Connection to Later Units
Knife work, bistro service, protein cookery, and frying all depend on safe habits and stewardship routines built here.
