# Unit 02: Food Handler and Stewardship

## Weeks Covered
Week 2

## Focus
Safety becomes behavior.

## Purpose
Students connect food safety concepts to daily kitchen stewardship: personal hygiene, cleaning and sanitation, cross-contamination, allergens, time and temperature control, laundry, counters, dishes, storage, floors, three-compartment sink, dish machine, and thermometer use.

## Learning Targets
- I can explain how food safety protects guests, classmates, and the kitchen.
- I can prevent cross-contamination and respond to allergen concerns.
- I can clean and sanitize counters, dishes, and equipment correctly.
- I can use dish, laundry, floor, and storage systems responsibly.
- I can use a thermometer to check food temperature safely.

## Core Skills
- Personal hygiene and handwashing
- Cleaning vs. sanitizing
- Three-compartment sink and dish machine procedures
- Laundry, counters, floors, and storage systems
- Allergen and cross-contamination awareness
- Thermometer use and temperature control

## Vocabulary
| Term | Student-Friendly Meaning |
|---|---|
| Sanitize | Reduce germs on a clean surface. |
| Cross-contamination | Germs or allergens moving from one food or surface to another. |
| Allergen | A food that can cause a serious reaction for some people. |
| Danger zone | Temperature range where bacteria can grow quickly. |
| Internal temperature | Temperature inside food. |
| Stewardship | Taking care of shared kitchen spaces, tools, and people. |

## Lab Applications
- Brownie batter lab
- Baking brownies
- Dish systems training
- Chicken breast temperature lab

## Bistro Connection
Guests trust the bistro because students handle food safely, clean correctly, and take stewardship jobs seriously.

## Assessment Ideas
- Handwashing and sanitation observation
- Dish system check
- Thermometer technique check
- Cross-contamination scenario response
- Stewardship role completion

## Teacher Notes
Treat cleaning, dishes, laundry, floors, and storage as culinary skills. Students need practice doing these systems, not just hearing rules about them.

## Resources Needed
- Equipment: sinks, dish machine if available, sanitizer, towels, thermometers, sheet pans, bowls
- Ingredients: brownie ingredients, chicken breast samples or teacher-selected temperature lab protein
- Materials: food safety notes, sanitizer directions, thermometer guidelines
