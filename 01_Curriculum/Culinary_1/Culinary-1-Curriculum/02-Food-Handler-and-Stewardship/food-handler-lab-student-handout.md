# Food Handler and Stewardship Lab Student Handout

Use this handout for the full Unit 02 lab week: brownie batter, baking brownies, dish systems, and chicken temperature work.

## Purpose of the Labs
This week uses simple labs and kitchen systems practice to turn food safety into daily behavior. The goal is to work cleanly, prevent contamination, use dish systems correctly, and understand why temperature matters.

## Skills Practiced
- Handwashing
- Cleaning and sanitizing
- Dish flow
- Towel and laundry systems
- Storage habits
- Allergen awareness
- Cross-contamination prevention
- Thermometer use
- Station reset

## Equipment Needed
- Mixing bowls
- Spoons or spatulas
- Sheet pans or baking pans
- Oven mitts or dry towels as directed
- Thermometers
- Sanitizer and cleaning supplies
- Dish area or dish machine
- Three-compartment sink if directed
- Towels/laundry area

## Ingredients or Materials
Your teacher will provide the materials for the day's application.

Possible applications:
- Brownie batter ingredients or mix
- Brownie baking supplies
- Chicken breast sample or teacher-demo protein
- Sanitizer and towel supplies
- Storage containers or practice storage examples

## Mise en Place / Setup Checklist
- [ ] Hands washed
- [ ] Station clear
- [ ] Sanitizer ready as directed
- [ ] Tools gathered
- [ ] Ingredients or materials received
- [ ] Dirty dish plan clear
- [ ] Towel/laundry plan clear
- [ ] Raw protein area separate if used

## Procedure
1. Complete the entry routine, agenda check, huddle, and handwashing.
2. Listen for the day's safety focus.
3. Set up the station with clean tools and clear dish/towel plans.
4. Complete the assigned lab or systems rotation.
5. Pause for teacher checkpoints on sanitation, dish flow, or thermometer use.
6. Clean, sanitize, manage dishes, handle towels, check floors, and reset.
7. Reflect on one safety habit your group improved.

## Teamwork Expectations
- Everyone helps protect the station.
- Do not leave dishes, towels, or floors for one person.
- Speak up if something is unsafe.
- Ask before using chemicals, equipment, or thermometers.
- Treat stewardship jobs as real culinary work.
- Leave the station safe for the next group.

## Safety Reminders
- Wash hands before food work.
- Keep raw and ready-to-eat foods separate.
- Treat allergens seriously.
- Clean before sanitizing.
- Report spills immediately.
- Clean thermometer probes before and after use.
- Follow the three-compartment sink or dish machine procedure your teacher gives you.
- Do not guess if food is safe.

## Cleanup and Reset Checklist
- [ ] Counters cleaned and sanitized
- [ ] Dishes washed, sanitized, or routed correctly
- [ ] Towels/laundry handled correctly
- [ ] Ingredients stored or discarded as directed
- [ ] Thermometers cleaned and returned if used
- [ ] Trash checked
- [ ] Floor checked
- [ ] Station ready for the next group

## Bistro Connection
Food Handler habits protect future Bistro guests. Clean hands, safe storage, correct dish flow, thermometer use, and full reset help the kitchen earn trust before service starts.

## Reflection Questions

2. What stewardship job did you complete?
3. What is one way contamination could have happened today?
4. How did your group prevent that problem?
