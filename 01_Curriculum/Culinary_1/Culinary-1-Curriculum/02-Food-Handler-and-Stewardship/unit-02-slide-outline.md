# Unit 02 Slide Outline: Food Handler and Stewardship

## Slide 1: Food Handler and Stewardship
- Safety becomes behavior
- Real kitchen habits
- Cleaning is culinary work
- Learn by doing

## Slide 2: Why This Unit Matters
- Food safety protects people
- Guests trust clean kitchens
- Unsafe habits spread quickly
- Bistro service starts with sanitation

## Slide 3: What We Are Practicing
- Handwashing
- Cleaning and sanitizing
- Dish systems
- Storage habits
- Thermometer use

## Slide 4: Daily Routine
- Enter calmly
- Check the agenda
- Join the huddle
- Wash hands when directed
- Wait to begin

## Slide 5: Safety Reminders
- Wash hands
- Separate raw and ready-to-eat
- Report spills
- Treat allergens seriously
- Ask before equipment or chemicals

## Slide 6: Kitchen Communication
- Behind
- Hot
- Sharp
- Spill
- Heard

## Slide 7: Clean vs. Sanitized
- Clean removes food and dirt
- Sanitize reduces germs
- Clean first
- Sanitize second
- Follow class procedure

## Slide 8: Cross-Contamination
- Raw to ready-to-eat
- Dirty tool to clean food
- Hands to food
- Board to food
- Prevent it early

## Slide 9: Allergens
- Allergens can be serious
- Do not guess
- Avoid cross-contact
- Use clean tools
- Tell the teacher

## Slide 10: Stewardship Jobs
- Professional responsibility
- Not punishment
- Dishes
- Counters
- Towels and laundry

## Slide 11: Dish Systems
- Scrape
- Wash
- Rinse
- Sanitize
- Dish machine if directed

## Slide 12: Time and Temperature
- Danger zone
- Internal temperature
- Thermometers give evidence
- Clean the probe
- Do not guess

- Dishes routed
