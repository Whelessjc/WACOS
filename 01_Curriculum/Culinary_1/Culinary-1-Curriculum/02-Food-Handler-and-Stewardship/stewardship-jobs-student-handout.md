# Stewardship Jobs Student Handout

Use this handout when your class is assigning or practicing stewardship jobs.

## Purpose
Stewardship means taking care of the shared kitchen. These jobs are not punishment or busy work. They are professional kitchen responsibilities that make food safer, service smoother, and the next class more successful.

## What Stewardship Looks Like
- Notice what needs doing.
- Take care of shared tools and spaces.
- Clean before the station is out of control.
- Help the team instead of waiting to be asked.
- Leave the kitchen ready for the next group.

## Stewardship Jobs
| Job | What It Includes | Why It Matters |
|---|---|---|
| Dishes | Scrape, wash, rinse, sanitize, dry/store as directed | Dirty dishes slow the kitchen and spread germs. |
| Counters | Remove food mess, clean, sanitize, keep clear | Food-contact surfaces must be safe before and after work. |
| Towels/Laundry | Use towels correctly, send dirty towels where directed | Towels can spread germs when misused. |
| Floors | Check spills, dropped food, trash, and traffic areas | Wet or messy floors cause slips and slow service. |
| Storage | Put ingredients and tools where they belong | Good storage prevents waste and contamination. |
| Trash | Keep trash controlled and remove as directed | Overflowing trash creates mess, odor, and safety problems. |

## Daily Stewardship Habits
- Wash hands before food work.
- Keep towels in the correct place.
- Report spills immediately.
- Keep dirty and clean items separate.
- Ask before using chemicals or equipment.
- Reset before leaving.

## Dish Flow Reminder
Follow the system your teacher shows you.

Basic flow:
1. Scrape.
2. Wash.
3. Rinse.
4. Sanitize.
5. Dry or store as directed.

If your kitchen uses a dish machine, follow the teacher's loading, unloading, and safety directions.

## Cleanup and Reset Checklist
- [ ] Dishes routed correctly
- [ ] Counters cleaned and sanitized
- [ ] Towels/laundry handled correctly
- [ ] Ingredients stored correctly
- [ ] Trash checked
- [ ] Floor checked
- [ ] Tools returned
- [ ] Station ready for the next group

## Reflection Questions
1. Which stewardship job did you do today?
2. How did that job help the kitchen?
3. What system did your group handle well?
4. What system needs more practice?
5. What did you do without being reminded?
