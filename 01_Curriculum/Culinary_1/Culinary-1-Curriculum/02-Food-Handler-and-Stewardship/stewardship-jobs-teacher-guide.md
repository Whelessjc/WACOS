# Stewardship Jobs Teacher Guide

Use this guide when assigning, rotating, or reteaching stewardship jobs.

## Purpose
Use stewardship jobs to make cleaning, dishes, laundry, counters, storage, and floors feel like real kitchen responsibility. The goal is not to keep students busy. The goal is to build dependable habits that make labs and Bistro service safer.

## Setup Before Class
- Decide which stewardship jobs are needed that day.
- Post or announce job assignments.
- Stage sanitizer, towels, dish supplies, trash access, and storage areas.
- Clarify three-compartment sink or dish machine expectations.
- Identify any teacher-only chemical or equipment procedures.
- Decide what counts as "ready for the next group" before students start.

## Job Rotation Suggestions
- Dish lead
- Counter lead
- Towel/laundry lead
- Floor lead
- Storage lead
- Trash/reset lead

Rotate jobs so students understand the whole kitchen system over time.

## Demo Points
- Cleaning vs. sanitizing.
- Correct towel use.
- Dish-flow sequence.
- Dish machine loading/unloading expectations if students use it.
- How to handle wet floors.
- Where clean tools belong.
- What a reset station looks like.

## Safety Watch-Outs
- Students using sanitizer on dirty counters.
- Towels used for hands, counters, and hot pans interchangeably.
- Wet floors near sinks or dish machine.
- Dirty dishes mixed with clean tools.
- Students crowding the sink.
- Chemicals used without permission.

## Classroom Management Notes
- Assign jobs before the lab gets busy.
- Pause halfway for a quick reset.
- Praise students who notice needs before being asked.
- Do not let one student become the group's cleaner.
- Reframe cleaning as "protecting the next group."
- Make students fix incomplete reset before dismissal.

## What to Look For While Circulating
- Are students doing their assigned stewardship job?
- Is dish flow moving?
- Are towels controlled?
- Are counters cleaned before sanitizing?
- Are floors checked before dismissal?
- Are students taking care of shared tools?
- Is the station ready for the next class?

## Common Student Mistakes
- Waiting until the end to start dishes.
- Wiping instead of cleaning and sanitizing.
- Leaving ingredients out.
- Forgetting floors.
- Putting wet tools away.
- Walking away from trash or towels.

## Quick Debrief Prompts
- Which stewardship job protected the kitchen today?
- Where did the system slow down?
- What should we do earlier next time?
- What job needs reteaching?
