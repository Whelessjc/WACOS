# Lab Plan: Food Handler and Stewardship

## Lab Purpose
Use simple labs and systems training to turn food safety into daily kitchen behavior.

## Skill Focus
- Handwashing and hygiene
- Cleaning and sanitizing
- Dish flow
- Allergen and cross-contamination prevention
- Thermometer use
- Full station reset

## Ingredients or Materials
- Brownie batter ingredients or mix
- Chicken breast samples or teacher-selected protein for temperature lab
- Sanitizer, towels, gloves if used, storage containers

## Equipment
- Mixing bowls, spoons, pans, ovens
- Three-compartment sink and/or dish machine
- Thermometers
- Sheet pans
- Sanitizer buckets or spray bottles as allowed

## Setup
- Assign stewardship jobs.
- Stage sanitizer and towels.
- Set up dish flow labels if useful.
- Keep raw protein temperature lab separate from brownie work.

## Procedure
1. Complete handwashing and station setup.
2. Review the safety focus for the day.
3. Complete the assigned lab or systems rotation.
4. Pause for teacher checkpoints on sanitation or thermometer use.
5. Clean, sanitize, handle dishes, manage towels, check floors, and reset.
6. Debrief one safety behavior that improved.

## Teacher Checkpoints
- Handwashing before food contact.
- Correct dish flow.
- Sanitizer used after cleaning.
- Raw protein handled separately.
- Thermometer inserted and cleaned correctly.

## Cleanup and Reset Expectations
No group leaves until counters are sanitized, dishes are routed correctly, towels are handled, ingredients are stored, floors are checked, and tools are returned.

## Possible Variations
- Use a teacher demo for chicken temperature if class readiness is low.
- Use brownie batter as a no-pressure sanitation practice.
- Rotate groups through stewardship stations instead of running one full lab.
