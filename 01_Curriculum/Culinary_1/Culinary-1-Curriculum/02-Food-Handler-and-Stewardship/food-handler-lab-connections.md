# Food Handler Lab Connections

## Purpose
This file connects Unit 02 labs and systems practice to the food safety habits students need all semester. Each application should feel like real kitchen behavior, not an isolated activity.

## Brownie Batter Lab
### Skills Practiced
- Handwashing
- Measuring and mixing with clean tools
- Counter control
- Dish flow
- Towel use
- Clean-as-you-go

### Teacher Emphasis
The brownie batter lab is not only about batter. It is a low-pressure way to practice clean hands, clean tools, organized stations, and shared cleanup.

### Checkpoints
- Hands washed before food contact
- Ingredients handled cleanly
- Dishes started before the end
- Counters cleaned and sanitized
- Towels handled correctly

## Baking Brownies
### Skills Practiced
- Oven safety
- Hot pan communication
- Timer awareness
- Dish and counter reset
- Storage or holding as directed

### Teacher Emphasis
Baking brownies gives students a reason to practice hot equipment safety and cleanup while food is in the oven.

### Checkpoints
- Dry towels or approved mitts used safely
- Hot pans communicated
- Counters reset while baking
- Tools returned
- Floor checked

## Dish Systems Training
### Skills Practiced
- Scraping
- Washing
- Rinsing
- Sanitizing
- Drying or storing as directed
- Dish machine use if available

### Teacher Emphasis
Dish systems are professional kitchen systems. Students should learn the flow well enough that dishes do not stop the lab.

### Checkpoints
- Dirty and clean items separated
- Sink or dish machine used correctly
- Wet floors managed
- Clean tools stored correctly

## Chicken Breast Temperature Lab
### Skills Practiced
- Raw protein boundaries
- Cross-contamination prevention
- Thermometer placement
- Internal temperature reading
- Probe cleaning
- Protein station reset

### Teacher Emphasis
Temperature is evidence. Students should not rely on appearance alone to decide whether food is safe.

### Checkpoints
- Raw protein handled separately
- Thermometer inserted correctly
- Temperature read carefully
- Probe cleaned before and after use
- Raw-contact surfaces sanitized

## Bistro Connection
Guests trust the Bistro because students practice these habits before service:
- Clean hands
- Safe tools
- Controlled allergens
- Correct dish flow
- Safe temperatures
- Clean counters
- Reset stations

## Student Reflection Questions
1. Which lab helped you understand food safety most clearly?
2. What stewardship job affected the whole group?
3. What does temperature tell us that our eyes cannot?
4. What dish or cleanup habit needs more repetition?
5. How does this work protect future Bistro guests?
