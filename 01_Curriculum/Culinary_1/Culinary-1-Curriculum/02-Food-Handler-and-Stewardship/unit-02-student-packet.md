# Unit 02 Student Packet: Food Handler and Stewardship

## What We Are Learning
This week we are learning how safety becomes behavior. You will practice food safety, personal hygiene, cleaning and sanitation, dish systems, laundry, storage, floors, allergen awareness, cross-contamination prevention, time and temperature control, and thermometer use.

## Why We Are Learning It
A kitchen only works when people can trust each other. Food safety is not just something you say on a test. It is how you wash, store, clean, cook, check temperature, and reset every day.

## What Success Looks Like
- I can wash hands at the right times.
- I can keep raw and ready-to-eat foods separate.
- I can treat allergens seriously.
- I can clean and sanitize correctly.
- I can help with dishes, towels, counters, storage, and floors.
- I can use and clean a thermometer correctly when asked.
- I can reset the station without waiting to be chased.

## Daily Kitchen Entry Routine
1. Enter calmly.
2. Put belongings where they belong.
3. Check the agenda.
4. Check uniform/readiness.
5. Join the huddle.
6. Wash hands when directed.
7. Begin work only when released.

## Key Expectations
- Food safety protects real people.
- Cleaning and dishes are culinary work.
- Allergens are safety concerns.
- Temperature gives evidence.
- Stewardship means taking care of the shared kitchen.

## Kitchen Communication
Use short, clear words so people know what is happening:
- "Behind"
- "Hot"
- "Sharp"
- "Corner"
- "Heard"
- "I need help"
- "Spill"
- "Dirty"

## Safety Expectations
- Wash hands before food work and after contamination.
- Keep raw and ready-to-eat foods separate.
- Treat allergens seriously.
- Clean before sanitizing.
- Ask before using chemicals, dish equipment, or thermometers.
- Report spills right away.

## Stewardship Job Expectations
Stewardship jobs are professional kitchen responsibilities. They are not punishment or busy work.

These jobs protect the next group and help the whole kitchen work safely.

This week, you may help with:
- Dishes
- Counters
- Towels and laundry
- Floors
- Storage
- Trash
- Sanitizer setup or station reset as directed

## Unit Lab Expectations
This week includes brownie batter, baking brownies, dish systems training, and a chicken breast temperature lab or demo.

The goal is to practice:
- Clean hands
- Clean tools
- Safe storage
- Dish flow through the three-compartment sink or dish machine as directed
- Thermometer use
- Cleaning as you go
- Resetting the station

## Cleanup and Reset Checklist
- [ ] Counters cleaned and sanitized
- [ ] Dishes washed, sanitized, or routed correctly
- [ ] Towels/laundry handled correctly
- [ ] Ingredients stored correctly
- [ ] Thermometers cleaned and returned if used
- [ ] Trash checked
- [ ] Floor checked
- [ ] Station ready for the next group

## Bistro Connection
Bistro guests trust the kitchen because students handle food safely, clean correctly, and take stewardship jobs seriously.

## Student Reflection Questions
1. What safety habit did you practice this week?
2. What is the difference between cleaning and sanitizing?
3. Which stewardship job did you do well?
4. What is one way to prevent cross-contamination?
5. Why is temperature more reliable than guessing by appearance?
