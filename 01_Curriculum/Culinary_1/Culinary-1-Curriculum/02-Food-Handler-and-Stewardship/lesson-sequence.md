# Lesson Sequence: Food Handler and Stewardship

## Day 1: Food Safety and Personal Hygiene
- Opening routine: entry, uniform/readiness, agenda check, huddle, handwashing.
- Demo or mini-lesson: why food safety matters and when to wash hands.
- Student work: identify contamination risks in common kitchen situations.
- Lab/application: practice handwashing and station sanitation.
- Cleanup/reset: counters, sinks, towels, floor check.
- Exit check: name one habit that protects other people.

## Day 2: Cleaning, Sanitizing, and Dish Systems
- Opening routine: huddle with focus on stewardship jobs.
- Demo or mini-lesson: cleaning vs. sanitizing, three-compartment sink, dish machine flow.
- Student work: rotate through dish, counter, laundry, and floor systems.
- Lab/application: brownie batter lab with dish-flow emphasis.
- Cleanup/reset: full dish system and station reset.
- Exit check: explain one difference between washing and sanitizing.

## Day 3: Cross-Contamination, Allergens, and Storage
- Opening routine: agenda check and safety reminder.
- Demo or mini-lesson: cutting boards, storage order, allergens, clean tools.
- Student work: sort storage examples and identify allergen risks.
- Lab/application: bake brownies while maintaining clean stations.
- Cleanup/reset: counters sanitized, tools returned, storage checked.
- Exit check: one way to prevent cross-contact with allergens.

## Day 4: Time, Temperature, and Thermometers
- Opening routine: huddle with thermometer purpose.
- Demo or mini-lesson: danger zone, internal temperature, thermometer placement and cleaning.
- Student work: practice reading thermometers safely.
- Lab/application: chicken breast temperature lab or teacher-led temperature demo.
- Cleanup/reset: sanitize probe thermometers and reset protein station.
- Exit check: why temperature is a safety tool, not just a number.

## Day 5: Stewardship Review and Skill Check
- Opening routine: students complete routine with less prompting.
- Demo or mini-lesson: review systems that were weak this week.
- Student work: food safety and stewardship scenario practice.
- Lab/application: station reset challenge or dish-system timed practice.
- Cleanup/reset: complete full kitchen stewardship sweep.
- Exit check: name one system the class must keep improving.
