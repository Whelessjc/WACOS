# Unit 04: Customer Service and Bistro Soft Opening

## Teacher Overview
**Focus:** Understand the full flow of service.

**Weeks covered:** Week 4
**Focus:** Understand the full flow of service.

Students learn Life of an Order, guest arrival, greeting, order taking, ticket flow, cooking, expo, food running, check
back, clearing, dish, reset, farewell, station assignments, mock service, Bistro prep, and soft opening routines.

## Student Overview
Understand the full flow of service.

## Learning Targets
- I can follow safety expectations for this unit.
- I can set up before starting work.
- I can practice the main unit skills with control.
- I can communicate with my team.
- I can clean and reset the station for the next group.

## Key Vocabulary / Key Ideas
- Life of an Order
- greeting
- order taking
- ticket flow
- expo
- dish
- reset

## Lesson Flow
- Monday: Life of an Order walkthrough from guest arrival to farewell.
- Tuesday: Station assignments, role expectations, communication, and guest interaction basics.
- Wednesday: Ticket flow, expo, food running, clearing, dish, and reset practice.
- Thursday: Mock service with coaching, stop points, and debrief.
- Friday: Bistro prep and soft opening with controlled expectations.

## Lab or Service Activity
Practice a controlled soft opening where students learn the full flow of service from guest arrival to farewell and
reset.

## Safety Reminders
- Keep guest areas clear and clean.
- Control hot food movement and traffic paths.
- Keep dish and service items separated.
- Wash hands and reset after contamination.
- Do not let service pressure override sanitation.

## Stewardship / Cleanup / Reset Expectations
- Build reset into service, not only after service.
- Check guest area, service area, dish, counters, towels, trash, and floors.
- Debrief without blame: name the system that needs practice.

## Bistro / Service Connection
This unit is the first full service picture. Students see how prep, ticket flow, cooking, expo, dish, reset, and
hospitality all connect.

## Assessment Ideas
- Observe safety, setup, and communication during lab or service work.
- Check the unit checklist for honest completion.
- Use the lab/service reflection as evidence of student understanding.
- Look for cleanup/reset completed without being chased.

## Student Reflection
1. What skill or routine did you practice?
2. What safety habit mattered most?
3. How did you support your team or station?
4. What cleanup/reset job did you complete?
5. What do you need to practice next?
