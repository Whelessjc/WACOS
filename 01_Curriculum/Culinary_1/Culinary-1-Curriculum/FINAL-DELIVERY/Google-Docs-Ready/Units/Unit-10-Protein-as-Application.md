# Unit 10: Protein as Application

## Teacher Overview
**Focus:** Protein tests fundamentals.

**Weeks covered:** Weeks 16 to 17
**Focus:** Protein tests fundamentals.

Students apply previous skills to chicken breast or thigh, ground turkey, egg cookery, searing, doneness, resting,
seasoning, slicing, temperature checking, food safety, knife control, heat control, mise en place, and flavor balance.

## Student Overview
Protein tests fundamentals.

## Learning Targets
- I can follow safety expectations for this unit.
- I can set up before starting work.
- I can practice the main unit skills with control.
- I can communicate with my team.
- I can clean and reset the station for the next group.

## Key Vocabulary / Key Ideas
- protein safety
- searing
- doneness
- resting
- slicing
- thermometer use
- station reset

## Lesson Flow
- Week 16: Protein safety, mise en place, seasoning, searing, doneness, resting, and slicing.
- Week 17: Ground turkey or egg cookery, temperature checks, timing, holding quality, and Bistro service readiness.

## Lab or Service Activity
Apply food safety, mise en place, heat control, seasoning, doneness, resting, slicing, and thermometer use to a
teacher-approved protein task.

## Safety Reminders
- Keep raw and ready-to-eat items separate.
- Wash hands and change gloves as directed.
- Clean and sanitize protein contact surfaces.
- Use thermometers as evidence.
- Store and cool protein only as directed.

## Stewardship / Cleanup / Reset Expectations
- Clean and sanitize all raw-protein surfaces, tools, boards, and handles.
- Route dishes carefully and keep clean/dirty separation visible.
- Check storage, labels, thermometers, towels, trash, and floors.
- Do not dismiss until protein safety reset is complete.

## Bistro / Service Connection
Protein connects to Bistro through timing, doneness, consistency, holding quality, guest safety, and service readiness.
This is where fundamentals have to work together.

## Assessment Ideas
- Observe safety, setup, and communication during lab or service work.
- Check the unit checklist for honest completion.
- Use the lab/service reflection as evidence of student understanding.
- Look for cleanup/reset completed without being chased.

## Student Reflection
1. What skill or routine did you practice?
2. What safety habit mattered most?
3. How did you support your team or station?
4. What cleanup/reset job did you complete?
5. What do you need to practice next?
