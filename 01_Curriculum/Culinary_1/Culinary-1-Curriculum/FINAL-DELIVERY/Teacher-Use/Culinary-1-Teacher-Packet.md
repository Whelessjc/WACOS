# Unit 01 Teacher Packet: Orientation and Systems


Teacher: ________________________________    Date(s): ________________________________

---

## Unit Overview
**Weeks covered:** Week 1
**Focus:** Learn the room before cooking.

Students learn the kitchen routines that make safe lab work possible: entry routine, agenda check, huddle, uniform
readiness, handwashing, basic safety, measuring, station setup, cleanup/reset, and the first pancake lab.

## Week / Day Flow
- Monday: Course orientation, kitchen mindset, entry routine, and uniform expectations.
- Tuesday: Room tour, traffic patterns, kitchen communication, and where things belong.
- Wednesday: Handwashing, basic safety, and measuring stations.
- Thursday: Station setup, recipe reading, dry run, and cleanup/reset practice.
- Friday: Pancake lab with simple team roles and full station reset.

## Setup Notes
- [ ] Post the agenda and starting location before students enter.
- [ ] Decide where belongings, uniforms, towels, tools, trash, and dishes belong.
- [ ] Stage measuring stations for midweek practice.
- [ ] Stage pancake tools and ingredients before Friday.
- [ ] Decide how heat approval will work before the first lab.

## Demo Notes
- Model entry routine and huddle expectations.
- Show correct handwashing and when students must rewash.
- Demonstrate dry, liquid, and spoon measuring.
- Show a ready station before food work begins.
- For pancakes, show batter setup, heat approval, bubbles, set edges, and safe flipping.

## Safety Watch-Outs
- Students walk in the kitchen and keep pathways clear.
- Spills are reported immediately.
- Students ask before using equipment or heat.
- Towels stay away from heat.
- Food and cleaning chemicals stay separate.
- Hands are washed before food work and after contamination.

## Common Student Mistakes
- Students rush to touch tools before hearing directions.
- Belongings or towels land in work areas.
- Students measure with the wrong tool.
- Cleanup starts too late.
- Groups think the lab is finished when the food is done.

## Cleanup / Reset Notes
- Protect time for reset every day, especially Friday.
- Check dishes, counters, tools, towels, trash, floors, and heat source.
- Use the phrase: reset means ready for the next group.
- If cleanup is weak, simplify the next lab rather than lowering the standard.

## Bistro / Service Connection
Week 1 routines are the start of Bistro readiness. Calm entry, clear communication, handwashing, setup, and reset are
the same habits students need before service.

## Reflection Space
- What worked well this unit?

- What routine or skill needs reteaching?

- Which students need extra support or a different role next time?

- What should be adjusted before teaching this unit again?

## Pacing Notes
- Keep instruction short and tied to the lab or service task.
- Reteach routines when safety, cleanup, or teamwork slips.
- Protect time for cleanup/reset.

# Unit 02 Teacher Packet: Food Handler and Stewardship


Teacher: ________________________________    Date(s): ________________________________

---

## Unit Overview
**Weeks covered:** Week 2
**Focus:** Safety becomes behavior.

Students connect Food Handler ideas to real kitchen behavior: hygiene, sanitation, cross contamination, allergens, time
and temperature control, laundry, counters, dishes, storage, floors, three-compartment sink, dish machine, thermometer
use, brownie labs, and chicken temperature checks.

## Week / Day Flow
- Monday: Food safety habits, personal hygiene, handwashing, and contamination risks.
- Tuesday: Stewardship jobs: laundry, counters, dishes, storage, floors, and dish systems.
- Wednesday: Brownie batter lab with sanitation, shared tools, and clean-as-you-go habits.
- Thursday: Baking brownies, oven safety, storage, and dish flow.
- Friday: Chicken breast temperature lab, thermometer use, and food-safety reflection.

## Setup Notes
- [ ] Post stewardship job assignments and dish-flow directions.
- [ ] Stage sanitizer, towels, dish racks, thermometers, and labels.
- [ ] Clarify three-compartment sink or dish machine expectations before lab work.
- [ ] Prepare brownie and chicken temperature lab materials according to local rules.

## Demo Notes
- Show handwashing and when to rewash.
- Model cleaning first, then sanitizing.
- Demonstrate thermometer placement, reading, and probe cleaning.
- Walk through dish machine or three-compartment sink flow.
- Show how sticky batter changes counter and dish cleanup.

## Safety Watch-Outs
- Separate raw, ready-to-eat, allergen, clean, and dirty items.
- Use sanitizer correctly and only where directed.
- Handle hot pans and ovens carefully.
- Use thermometer readings as evidence, not guessing.
- Keep floors dry and towels off counters/floors.

## Common Student Mistakes
- Students wipe but do not sanitize.
- Towels are used for too many jobs.
- Thermometers touch food and are not cleaned.
- Dish flow stops because no one owns it.
- Students treat stewardship as punishment instead of kitchen work.

## Cleanup / Reset Notes
- Make laundry, counters, dishes, storage, and floors visible jobs.
- Check dish area before dismissal, not after the bell.
- Require labeled/closed storage where needed.
- Name stewardship as professional responsibility.

## Bistro / Service Connection
Bistro service depends on safe hands, clean counters, active dish flow, controlled towels, safe storage, and students
who protect guests without being reminded.

## Reflection Space
- What worked well this unit?

- What routine or skill needs reteaching?

- Which students need extra support or a different role next time?

- What should be adjusted before teaching this unit again?

## Pacing Notes
- Keep instruction short and tied to the lab or service task.
- Reteach routines when safety, cleanup, or teamwork slips.
- Protect time for cleanup/reset.

# Unit 03 Teacher Packet: Recipe Reading and Mise en Place


Teacher: ________________________________    Date(s): ________________________________

## Unit Overview
**Weeks covered:** Week 3
**Focus:** Read before cooking. Organize before acting.

Students review Food Handler expectations, read recipes before acting, identify ingredients/equipment/steps, sequence
work, build prep lists, measure accurately, and practice mise en place through peanut butter bars, rice cookery, fried
rice, or stir fry.

## Week / Day Flow
- Monday: Food Handler review and assessment notes; recipe parts and before-you-start reading.
- Tuesday: Ingredient lists, equipment lists, yield, timing, and sequencing practice.
- Wednesday: Measuring accuracy and mise en place setup with peanut butter bars or similar lab.
- Thursday: Rice cookery, liquid ratios, timing, and station readiness.
- Friday: Fried rice or stir fry application using prep lists, teamwork, and reset.

## Setup Notes
- [ ] Prepare sample recipes for marking ingredients, tools, and steps.
- [ ] Stage measuring tools and simple mise en place containers.
- [ ] Post a visible prep-list model.
- [ ] Confirm rice/stir fry equipment and dish/reset plan.

## Demo Notes
- Annotate a recipe before touching ingredients.
- Show how to pull equipment from a recipe.
- Build a short prep list from steps.
- Demonstrate accurate measuring and organized mise en place.
- Connect prep list order to cooking timing.

## Safety Watch-Outs
- Review Food Handler habits before labs.
- Keep allergens named and separated.
- Prevent rushing from recipe reading into food handling.
- Keep cutting/cooking stations organized and clear.
- Store ingredients and leftovers as directed.

## Common Student Mistakes
- Students read only the first line and start too soon.
- Equipment is missing after cooking begins.
- Measurements are guessed or leveled poorly.
- Prep is scattered across the station.
- Cleanup waits until the recipe is finished.

## Cleanup / Reset Notes
- Make reset part of mise en place: tools returned, ingredients closed, counters sanitized, dishes moving.
- Use prep containers responsibly and return them clean.
- Check that rice/stir fry stations are dry, organized, and ready for the next group.

## Bistro / Service Connection
Bistro prep starts with reading, sequencing, and station readiness. Prep lists help students work on time, communicate
needs, and avoid service delays.

## Week / Day Flow

## Demo Notes
- Walk a sample order through the room.
- Model greeting, order taking, check back, clearing, and farewell.

# Unit 06 Teacher Packet: Knife Skills Progression


Teacher: ________________________________    Date(s): ________________________________

---

## Unit Overview
**Weeks covered:** Weeks 6 to 9
**Focus:** Build confidence through control and repetition.

Students build minimum competence with knife safety, grip, claw, board setup, rough chop, dice, slice, mince, julienne,
batonnet, prep-list execution, cooking applications, Thursday Bistro prep, Friday Bistro service, and Week 9 deep clean
reset.

## Week / Day Flow
- Weeks 6-8: Three-day knife skill block with repeated safety, setup, grip, claw, and cut practice.
- Thursdays: Bistro prep connection using prep lists, timing, and station setup.
- Fridays: Bistro service connection through consistency, communication, and product use.
- Week 9: Speed with control, potato batonnet/fries application, and deep clean reset.

## Setup Notes
- [ ] Prepare cutting boards, towels, knives, containers, compost/trash, and product-use plan.
- [ ] Post minimum competence language and control-before-speed reminder.
- [ ] Plan how usable product will become stir fry, sauteed vegetables, soup base, roasted vegetables, potato batonnet, or fries.
- [ ] Schedule extra reset time for Week 9 deep clean.

## Demo Notes
- Show board setup with damp towel and stable work area.
- Demonstrate knife carry, set down, grip, claw, and blade path.
- Model each cut slowly before asking students to practice.
- Show how cut size affects cooking time and texture.
- Model sorting usable product from waste.

## Safety Watch-Outs
- Control before speed, every time.
- Knives stay down when listening.
- Eyes stay on the blade path.
- Claw hand protects fingertips.
- Boards must be stable before cutting.
- Students report cuts immediately.

## Common Student Mistakes
- Students chase speed before control.
- Claw hand opens flat.
- Board slides or station is crowded.
- Knife tip or blade path is uncontrolled.
- Cut product is not sorted for cooking use.
- Deep clean is treated as optional.

## Cleanup / Reset Notes
- Clean and store knives correctly.
- Scrape boards before washing; sanitize as directed.
- Sort usable product, compost/trash, towels, floors, and containers.
- Week 9 deep clean should include stations, shelves, floors, tools, and neglected corners.

## Bistro / Service Connection
Knife work supports Bistro prep lists, timing, consistency, batch work, and service readiness. Cuts are tools for
cooking, not trophies.

## Reflection Space

- What should be adjusted before teaching this unit again?

## Pacing Notes

# Unit 07 Teacher Packet: Heat Control


Teacher: ________________________________    Date(s): ________________________________

---

## Unit Overview
**Weeks covered:** Weeks 10 to 12
**Focus:** Learn how cooking actually works.

Students practice pan preheating, oil control, saute, browning vs. steaming, aromatics, roasting, oven spacing,
caramelization, moist heat, rice, beans, lentils, simmering, texture control, and liquid ratios.

## Week / Day Flow
- Week 10: Pan heat, oil cues, saute, browning vs. steaming, aromatics, and timing.
- Week 11: Roasting, oven control, spacing, browning, caramelization, and texture.
- Week 12: Moist heat, rice, beans, lentils, simmering, liquid ratios, and hot-station reset.

## Setup Notes
- [ ] Stage pans, sheet pans, towels, utensils, oil, aromatics, and tasting tools.
- [ ] Post heat words students should use: low, medium, high, simmer, steam, brown, burn.
- [ ] Plan small batches so students can see cause and effect.
- [ ] Prepare hot-item traffic and landing zones.

## Demo Notes
- Show pan preheat cues before oil or food goes in.
- Compare browning and steaming with crowded vs. spaced food.
- Demonstrate aromatics cooked without burning.
- Show roasting spacing and turning decisions.
- Show simmer vs. boil and how liquid ratio affects texture.

## Safety Watch-Outs
- Use dry towels around heat.
- Turn handles in and keep hot paths clear.
- Ask before changing heat.
- Watch steam, oil, and hot pans.
- Turn burners and ovens off as directed.
- Call hot items clearly.

## Common Student Mistakes
- Food goes into a cold pan.
- Pans are overcrowded, causing steaming.
- Aromatics burn while students look away.
- Students stir too much and prevent browning.
- Rice/beans/lentils boil too hard or run dry.
- Hot station reset is rushed.

## Cleanup / Reset Notes
- Cool and handle hot pans safely.
- Degrease and sanitize counters as directed.
- Check burners, ovens, oil, spills, towels, dish flow, and floors.
- Store moist-heat products safely if directed.

## Bistro / Service Connection
Heat control affects Bistro timing, batch work, holding quality, texture, and consistency. A hot station must stay
clean, calm, and ready for the next order.

## Reflection Space

- What should be adjusted before teaching this unit again?

# Unit 08 Teacher Packet: Frying and Oil Management


Teacher: ________________________________    Date(s): ________________________________

---

## Unit Overview
**Weeks covered:** Week 13
**Focus:** Temperature discipline.

Students practice oil safety, fryer safety, batch control, overcrowding, crisp vs. greasy results, draining, seasoning
timing, potato batonnet, vegetable fritters, onion rings, or another low-cost frying application.

## Week / Day Flow
- Monday: Oil safety, temperature discipline, fryer expectations, and emergency stop points.
- Tuesday: Potato batonnet, drying, batch size, and draining setup.
- Wednesday: Vegetable fritters or onion rings with controlled loading and removal.
- Thursday: Batch timing, seasoning timing, holding quality, and station reset.
- Friday: Low-cost frying application or Bistro prep/service connection with debrief.

## Setup Notes
- [ ] Confirm fryer policy, oil disposal system, and student access rules.
- [ ] Stage dry towels, sheet pans, racks/paper, spiders/tongs, seasoning, and safety zones.
- [ ] Keep product dry and batches small.
- [ ] Post oil-station roles and limit crowding.

## Demo Notes
- Show safe approach to hot oil and what students may not do.
- Demonstrate loading away from the body and removing with the correct tool.
- Compare crisp vs. greasy using temperature and batch size.
- Show draining and seasoning while hot.
- Model oil-station cleanup without rushing.

## Safety Watch-Outs
- Keep water away from hot oil.
- Limit students at the oil station.
- Dry food before frying as directed.
- Use tools, not fingers, near oil.
- Stop immediately for spills, crowding, splatter, or unsafe movement.
- Dispose of oil only as directed.

## Common Student Mistakes
- Students overcrowd the oil.
- Wet product causes splatter.
- Food is not drained before seasoning.
- Seasoning happens too late.
- Students crowd the fryer to watch.
- Oil cleanup is rushed or unclear.

## Cleanup / Reset Notes
- Follow the local oil-handling and disposal plan exactly.
- Clean draining area, tools, counters, towels, trash, and floors.
- Check for greasy surfaces and slippery spots.
- Reset fryer/oil station only when safe and approved.

## Bistro / Service Connection
Frying connects to Bistro through timing, batch size, consistency, holding quality, and safe service readiness. Fried
food needs discipline, not guessing.

## Reflection Space

- Which students need extra support or a different role next time?

- What should be adjusted before teaching this unit again?

# Unit 09 Teacher Packet: Flavor Systems


Teacher: ________________________________    Date(s): ________________________________

---

## Unit Overview
**Weeks covered:** Weeks 14 to 15
**Focus:** Move beyond recipes and learn how flavor is built.

Students learn how salt, acid, fat, aromatics, herbs, and spices affect food. They practice tasting and adjustment with
flavor families such as soy and ginger, vinegar and herbs, chili and citrus, yogurt and spice, and coconut and curry
while respecting cultural origins without claiming authenticity.

## Week / Day Flow
- Week 14: Salt, acid, fat, aromatics, tasting rules, and small adjustments.
- Week 15: Herbs, spices, flavor family comparisons, respectful language, and simple Bistro application.

## Setup Notes
- [ ] Stage small tasting portions, clean spoons/cups, allergen labels, and sanitizer.
- [ ] Prepare a tasting chart or board notes for observations.
- [ ] Plan clear limits for chili, salt, dairy, soy, coconut, and spice allergens.
- [ ] Choose simple base foods for comparison.

## Demo Notes
- Model clean tasting with no double-dipping.
- Show how a small amount of salt, acid, or fat changes food.
- Demonstrate smelling aromatics and spices before using them.
- Model respectful language: inspired by, similar flavors, not claiming authenticity.
- Show how to adjust once, taste, and decide.

## Safety Watch-Outs
- Use clean tasting spoons every time.
- Name allergens before tasting.
- Keep chili, dairy, soy, coconut, and spice risks organized.
- Prevent cross contact between tasting samples.
- Sanitize tasting surfaces.

## Common Student Mistakes
- Students add too much at once.
- Students taste with dirty spoons.
- Allergens are not named clearly.
- Students describe foods carelessly or claim authenticity.
- Cleanup leaves sticky or strongly scented surfaces.

## Cleanup / Reset Notes
- Control tasting trash, spoons, cups, allergen surfaces, counters, towels, and floors.
- Close and store seasonings correctly.
- Check that strong-smelling ingredients are sealed and returned.

## Bistro / Service Connection
Flavor systems support Bistro consistency, tasting, seasoning, prep, and guest experience. Students learn to adjust
with care instead of guessing.

## Reflection Space

- What should be adjusted before teaching this unit again?

Students create independent prep lists, take limited service ownership, complete a skill inventory, reflect on

working with greater independence.

## Week / Day Flow
- Monday: Skill inventory using evidence from labs, service, cleaning, and teamwork.
- Tuesday: Independent prep list with tools, ingredients, timing, safety, and cleanup.
- Wednesday: Limited service ownership or prep ownership task.
