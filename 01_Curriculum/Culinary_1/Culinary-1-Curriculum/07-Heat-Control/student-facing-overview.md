# Student Overview: Heat Control

## What You Are Learning
You will learn how heat changes food and how cooks adjust heat to control color, texture, flavor, and doneness.

## Why It Matters
Most cooking mistakes come from poor heat control. Good cooks watch food and make adjustments.

## What Success Looks Like
- I can preheat a pan safely.
- I can manage oil without rushing.
- I can brown food instead of steaming it.
- I can cook aromatics without burning.
- I can roast with spacing.
- I can simmer grains or legumes with correct liquid.
- I can clean and reset a hot station safely.

## Applications
Sauteed vegetables, roasted vegetables, aromatics, rice, beans, lentils, and moist heat practice.

## Bottom Line
Cooking is not just heat on or heat off. It is watching, adjusting, cleaning as you go, and leaving the hot station ready for the next cook.
