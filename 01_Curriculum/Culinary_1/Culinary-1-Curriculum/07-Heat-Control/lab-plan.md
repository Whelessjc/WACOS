# Lab Plan: Heat Control

## Lab Purpose
Use repeated cooking applications to help students see how heat affects texture, color, doneness, and flavor.

## Skill Focus
- Pan preheating
- Oil control
- Saute
- Browning vs. steaming
- Roasting
- Simmering
- Liquid ratios
- Hot-station stewardship

## Ingredients or Materials
- Vegetables
- Aromatics
- Rice
- Beans or lentils
- Cooking oil or fat
- Seasonings

## Equipment
- Saute pans
- Pots
- Burners
- Sheet pans
- Ovens
- Spoons and spatulas
- Towels and sanitizer

## Setup
- Review heat safety.
- Stage ingredients by lab.
- Keep oil amounts controlled.
- Assign hot-station roles.

## Procedure
1. Complete opening routine and safety huddle.
2. Watch demo for the heat skill.
3. Set mise en place.
4. Cook with teacher checkpoints.
5. Observe color, sound, steam, smell, texture, and timing.
6. Adjust heat as directed.
7. Pause for a mid-lab wipe, dish, or towel reset if needed.
8. Clean hot stations and reset.

## Teacher Checkpoints
- Pan properly preheated.
- Oil not smoking.
- Food spaced correctly.
- Simmer maintained.
- Texture checked before stopping.
- Hot station is safe and clean before students leave.

## Cleanup and Reset Expectations
Burners and ovens off, hot pans handled safely, dishes routed, counters sanitized, floors checked, towels managed.

## Possible Variations
- Teacher-led oil demo for classes needing more control.
- Use one vegetable across several heat methods.
- Use rice only if beans/lentils need more time than schedule allows.
