# Lesson Sequence: Heat Control

## Weeks Covered
Weeks 10 to 12

## Bistro / Service Connection
Heat control supports service timing, holding quality, consistent texture, and safe hot-station work. Students should connect every heat lab to prep lists, station setup, communication, and reset.

## Week 10: Saute and Pan Heat

### Day 1: Pan Preheating and Oil Cues
- Opening routine: huddle on hot-station safety and clean towels.
- Demo or mini-lesson: cold pan vs. preheated pan; oil shimmer vs. smoke.
- Student work: observe cues and sketch or describe what changed.
- Lab/application: teacher-led pan heat observation.
- Bistro/service connection: hot stations must be ready before service work starts.
- Cleanup/reset: burners off, pans cooled safely, counters sanitized.
- Exit check: one sign a pan is ready or too hot.

### Day 2: Saute Basics
- Opening routine: review heat approval and station spacing.
- Demo or mini-lesson: saute with small batches and controlled movement.
- Student work: set mise en place and identify safe pan roles.
- Lab/application: sauteed vegetables.
- Bistro/service connection: small-batch saute supports consistent prep and service timing.
- Cleanup/reset: hot pans handled safely, dishes routed, station reset.
- Exit check: what heat adjustment did your group make?

### Day 3: Browning vs. Steaming
- Opening routine: huddle on spacing and patience.
- Demo or mini-lesson: crowded pan vs. spaced pan.
- Student work: compare color, steam, texture, and smell.
- Lab/application: browning/steaming vegetable comparison.
- Bistro/service connection: crowded pans slow service and weaken food quality.
- Cleanup/reset: pan wash, counter sanitize, floor check.
- Exit check: how does spacing affect food?

### Day 4: Aromatics Without Burning
- Opening routine: review low/medium heat and stirring with purpose.
- Demo or mini-lesson: sweating aromatics and recognizing burning.
- Student work: prep aromatics and watch color/smell changes.
- Lab/application: simple aromatic base.
- Bistro/service connection: burned aromatics can affect an entire batch or service item.
- Cleanup/reset: odor control, dishes, counters, trash, floor.
- Exit check: what told you the aromatics were ready?

### Day 5: Bistro Prep Application
- Opening routine: prep huddle with timing goal.
- Demo or mini-lesson: using saute skills for service prep.
- Student work: complete assigned prep with heat checkpoints.
- Lab/application: Bistro or prep application using saute skills.
- Cleanup/reset: label, store, clean, reset.
- Exit check: how did heat control support service?

## Week 11: Roasting and Oven Control

### Day 1: Oven Safety and Spacing
- Opening routine: huddle on hot pans, towels, and oven traffic.
- Demo or mini-lesson: rack placement, tray spacing, and safe loading.
- Student work: plan tray setup.
- Lab/application: dry run with sheet pans or simple roasting setup.
- Bistro/service connection: oven traffic and hot pan communication protect the team.
- Cleanup/reset: sheet pans returned, oven area checked.
- Exit check: one oven safety habit.

### Day 2: Roasted Vegetables
- Opening routine: review cut size, oil amount, and spacing.
- Demo or mini-lesson: coating vegetables without drowning them in oil.
- Student work: set up roasting tray.
- Lab/application: roasted vegetables.
- Bistro/service connection: spacing and cut size help food finish together.
- Cleanup/reset: sheet pans handled safely, counters sanitized.
- Exit check: what helped browning happen?

### Day 3: Browning, Caramelization, and Texture
- Opening routine: huddle on doneness cues.
- Demo or mini-lesson: color, texture, and caramelization.
- Student work: compare underdone, ready, and overdone examples if available.
- Lab/application: roasting adjustment or texture comparison.
- Bistro/service connection: cooks need cues when holding or finishing food for service.
- Cleanup/reset: oven off, station reset.
- Exit check: one cue besides the timer.

### Day 4: Oven Timing for Prep
- Opening routine: prep huddle.
- Demo or mini-lesson: timing sheet pans for service or class deadlines.
- Student work: plan when items should enter and leave the oven.
- Lab/application: roasting or oven prep task.
- Cleanup/reset: label/store, clean, floor check.
- Exit check: how did timing affect readiness?

### Day 5: Bistro Service/Application
- Opening routine: roles and hot-station reminders.
- Demo or mini-lesson: holding and communicating hot items.
- Student work: complete assigned service/prep role.
- Lab/application: Bistro service or heat-control application.
- Cleanup/reset: full hot-station reset.
- Exit check: one service habit connected to heat.

## Week 12: Moist Heat, Ratios, and Texture

### Day 1: Simmer vs. Boil
- Opening routine: huddle on steam safety.
- Demo or mini-lesson: bubble size and movement.
- Student work: observe and describe simmer/boil differences.
- Lab/application: water or stock observation.
- Cleanup/reset: pots cooled and washed, counters sanitized.
- Exit check: why does simmering matter?

### Day 2: Rice and Liquid Ratios
- Opening routine: ratio check.
- Demo or mini-lesson: measuring liquid and managing lid/steam.
- Student work: calculate or confirm rice ratio.
- Lab/application: rice cookery.
- Bistro/service connection: ratios and timing help sides stay consistent.
- Cleanup/reset: pot/rice cooker cleaned, station reset.
- Exit check: what changed the rice texture?

### Day 3: Beans or Lentils
- Opening routine: huddle on simmering and patience.
- Demo or mini-lesson: texture checks and liquid control.
- Student work: monitor doneness and liquid.
- Lab/application: beans or lentils.
- Bistro/service connection: simmered items need texture checks before holding or serving.
- Cleanup/reset: storage, dishes, counters, floor.
- Exit check: how did liquid affect texture?

### Day 4: Combined Heat-Control Application
- Opening routine: prep and heat plan.
- Demo or mini-lesson: choosing heat method for the task.
- Student work: set mise en place and identify heat checkpoints.
- Lab/application: teacher-selected combined heat task.
- Cleanup/reset: full station reset.
- Exit check: one heat decision you made.

### Day 5: Review, Skill Check, and Reset
- Opening routine: huddle on growth.
- Demo or mini-lesson: reteach the weakest heat concept.
- Student work: short heat-control skill check or reflection.
- Lab/application: simple review application if time allows.
- Cleanup/reset: hot station deep reset.
- Exit check: what heat cue will you watch for next time?

## Teacher Pacing Notes
- Keep demos short and visual. Students learn heat best by comparing color, sound, steam, smell, and texture.
- Build in reset pauses during longer labs so hot stations do not become unsafe.
- If students are rushing, reduce the lab size rather than adding more tasks.
- Reuse the same vocabulary across weeks: preheat, space, simmer, brown, adjust, reset.
