# Unit 07 Student Packet: Heat Control

## What We Are Learning
We are learning how cooking works through heat. You will practice saute, preheating, oil control, browning vs. steaming, aromatics, roasting, oven control, simmering, rice, beans, lentils, ratios, texture, and hot-station reset.

## Why We Are Learning It
Heat changes food. If you can control heat, you can make better decisions instead of guessing or turning everything to high.

## What Success Looks Like
- I can explain how heat changes food.
- I can preheat a pan and manage oil safely.
- I can tell browning from steaming.
- I can cook aromatics without burning them.
- I can use oven spacing and timing.
- I can use simmering and liquid ratios.
- I can reset a hot station safely.

## Daily Kitchen Entry Routine
1. Enter calmly.
2. Put belongings away.
3. Check the agenda.
4. Check uniform/readiness.
5. Join the huddle.
6. Wash hands when directed.
7. Begin work only when released.

## Key Expectations
- Watch before reacting.
- Heat is adjustable.
- Higher is not always better.
- Use dry towels near heat.
- Clean spills right away.
- Reset hot stations carefully.

## Kitchen Communication
- "Hot"
- "Behind"
- "Corner"
- "Heard"
- "Burner on"
- "Oven opening"
- "I need help"

## Safety Expectations
- Ask before using burners or ovens.
- Keep handles turned in.
- Use dry towels.
- Watch steam and oil.
- Keep traffic paths clear.
- Turn off heat as directed.

## Heat Practice Expectations
- Set mise en place before heat starts.
- Notice color, sound, steam, smell, texture, and time.
- Cook in small enough batches.
- Keep pans and trays spaced.
- Ask before changing heat if that is the class rule.
- Clean while the station is still under control.

## Cleanup and Reset Checklist
- [ ] Burners/ovens off as directed
- [ ] Hot pans handled safely
- [ ] Dishes routed correctly
- [ ] Counters cleaned and sanitized
- [ ] Oil or spills handled
- [ ] Towels handled correctly
- [ ] Trash checked
- [ ] Floor checked
- [ ] Station ready for the next group

## Bistro Connection
Bistro food needs timing and consistency. Heat control helps food brown, simmer, roast, hold, and reach guests with better texture.

## Student Reflection Questions
1. What heat cue did you notice today?
2. Did your group need more or less heat?
3. What helped browning happen?
4. What made texture better or worse?
5. How did your group reset the hot station?
