# Unit 07 Lab Teacher Guide: Heat Control

## Setup Before Class
- Stage pans, pots, sheet pans, spoons, spatulas, towels, sanitizer, and ingredients.
- Decide which stations may use heat and which need approval.
- Pre-portion oil when useful.
- Prepare comparison setups for browning vs. steaming or simmer vs. boil.
- Set clear towel, dish, spill, and floor expectations.

## Demo Points
- Cold pan vs. preheated pan.
- Oil shimmer vs. smoke.
- Crowded pan vs. spaced pan.
- Aromatics ready vs. burned.
- Roasting tray spacing.
- Simmer vs. boil.
- What a safe hot-station reset looks like.

## Safety Watch-Outs
- Wet towels near heat.
- Handles turned out.
- Students changing heat constantly.
- Smoke or oil splatter.
- Steam burns.
- Crowding around burners or ovens.
- Hot pans placed in unsafe areas.

## Skill Checkpoints
- Mise en place is ready before heat begins.
- Pan is preheated appropriately.
- Oil is controlled.
- Food is spaced correctly.
- Aromatics are watched closely.
- Simmer is gentle, not rolling.
- Texture is checked before stopping.
- Hot station is reset safely.

## Common Student Mistakes
- Turning heat too high.
- Stirring constantly before browning.
- Crowding pans or trays.
- Burning aromatics.
- Boiling instead of simmering.
- Ignoring liquid ratios.
- Leaving oily pans, wet towels, or hot surfaces.

## Cleanup Timing
- Build in one mid-lab reset pause.
- Have students start dish before final minutes.
- Cool and wash pans safely.
- Check burners/ovens before dismissal.
- Inspect floors around hot stations.

## What to Look For While Circulating
- Are students watching cues or guessing?
- Is heat being adjusted with a reason?
- Is the station safe enough to keep cooking?
- Are towels dry and controlled?
- Are students communicating around heat?
- Is cleanup shared?

## Bistro Connection
Connect heat cues to service timing and holding quality. Students should see that hot stations need prep, communication, consistency, and a careful reset.

## Quick Debrief Prompts
- What cue told you to adjust heat?
- What caused browning or steaming?
- How did spacing affect texture?
- What hot-station habit needs practice?
