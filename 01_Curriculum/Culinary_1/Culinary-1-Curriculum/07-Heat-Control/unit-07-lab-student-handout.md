# Unit 07 Lab Student Handout: Heat Control

## Purpose of the Lab
The purpose of heat-control labs is to learn how heat changes food. You will watch color, sound, steam, smell, texture, and time so you can make better cooking decisions.

## Skills Practiced
- Pan preheating
- Oil control
- Saute
- Browning vs. steaming
- Aromatics without burning
- Roasting and oven spacing
- Simmering and liquid ratios
- Hot-station cleanup and reset

## Equipment Needed
- Saute pan or pot
- Burner or oven as assigned
- Sheet pan if roasting
- Spoon or spatula
- Measuring tools
- Towels
- Sanitizer and dish supplies

## Ingredients / Materials
Your teacher may assign vegetables, aromatics, rice, beans, lentils, cooking oil or fat, and seasonings.

## Mise en Place / Setup Checklist
- [ ] Hands washed
- [ ] Recipe or task read
- [ ] Ingredients ready
- [ ] Tools ready
- [ ] Heat role assigned
- [ ] Dry towel ready
- [ ] Trash and dish plan clear
- [ ] Teacher approval received if needed

## Procedure
1. Complete the entry routine and safety huddle.
2. Watch the heat demo.
3. Set mise en place before turning on heat.
4. Cook with teacher checkpoints.
5. Observe color, sound, steam, smell, texture, and timing.
6. Adjust heat only as directed.
7. Pause for reset if the station becomes messy or unsafe.
8. Turn off heat as directed.
9. Clean and reset the hot station.

## Teamwork Expectations
- One person manages heat unless the teacher changes roles.
- Communicate before moving hot pans.
- Keep the station clear.
- Share dish and reset work.
- Ask for help before smoke, spills, or crowding get worse.

## Safety Reminders
- Use dry towels around heat.
- Say "hot" and "behind."
- Keep handles turned in.
- Watch steam when lids open.
- Keep oil controlled.
- Report spills right away.

## Cleanup and Reset Checklist
- [ ] Burner/oven off as directed
- [ ] Hot pans handled safely
- [ ] Dishes routed correctly
- [ ] Counters cleaned and sanitized
- [ ] Oil or spills handled
- [ ] Towels handled correctly
- [ ] Floor checked
- [ ] Station ready for the next group

## Bistro Connection
Heat control helps Bistro stations serve food with better timing, texture, and consistency. A clean hot station is safer for the next task.

## Reflection Questions
1. What heat cue did you notice?
2. What adjustment did your group make?
3. What happened when food was crowded or spaced?
4. What did texture tell you?
5. How did your group reset safely?
