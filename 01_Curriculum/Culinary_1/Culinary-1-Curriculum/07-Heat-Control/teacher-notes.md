# Teacher Notes: Heat Control

## What to Emphasize
- Heat is adjustable.
- Students should observe before reacting.
- Browning needs space and patience.
- Aromatics need control.
- Simmering is not boiling.
- Texture tells a story.
- Hot-station reset is part of heat control.

## Common Student Mistakes
- Turning heat too high.
- Crowding pans.
- Stirring constantly before browning can happen.
- Burning aromatics.
- Boiling when asked to simmer.
- Ignoring liquid ratios.
- Leaving oily pans, wet towels, or hot stations for someone else.

## Classroom Management Notes
- Keep hot stations spaced.
- Require teacher approval for burner use until ready.
- Use side-by-side comparisons when possible.
- Stop labs when smoke, crowding, or unsafe movement begins.
- Build short reset pauses into longer labs.

## Safety Concerns
- Hot pans and ovens.
- Oil splatter.
- Steam burns.
- Wet towels near heat.
- Crowding around burners.

## Pacing Notes
Three weeks allows heat concepts to repeat in different foods. Keep returning to cues, not just times.

## Repetition Needed
Pan preheat, oil control, spacing, simmering, oven safety, towel control, and hot-station reset.

## Connection to Later Units
Frying, flavor systems, protein cookery, and Bistro timing all depend on heat control and clean hot stations.
