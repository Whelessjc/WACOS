# Assessment Notes: Heat Control

## Formative Assessment Ideas
- Heat cue exit ticket
- Pan spacing check
- Simmer vs. boil observation
- Roasting tray review
- Ratio calculation
- Lab reflection on adjustment
- Hot-station reset check
- Timing/holding quality observation

## Performance Assessment Ideas
Students demonstrate readiness by completing a heat-control application, naming the cues they watched, adjusting heat appropriately, and resetting the hot station safely.

## Observation Points
- Does the student manage heat safely?
- Does the student overcrowd the pan?
- Does the student notice browning, steam, bubbles, or texture?
- Does the student adjust instead of guessing?
- Does the student handle hot cleanup safely?
- Does the student understand when food is ready, holding, or falling behind?

## Simple Rubric Categories
| Category | Evidence |
|---|---|
| Safety | Handles heat, oil, steam, and pans safely. |
| Observation | Notices visual and sensory cues. |
| Control | Adjusts heat and spacing appropriately. |
| Service Readiness | Connects timing and holding quality to service. |
| Product | Produces reasonable texture and doneness. |
| Stewardship | Cleans hot station safely and leaves it ready. |

## Evidence of Readiness
The student can explain how heat affected the food and can make at least one appropriate adjustment during cooking.
