# Unit 07 Teacher Packet: Heat Control

## Unit Overview
Weeks 10 to 12 focus: learn how cooking actually works.

Students practice pan preheating, oil control, saute, browning vs. steaming, aromatics without burning, roasting, oven control, spacing, caramelization, texture, moist heat, rice, beans, lentils, simmering, liquid ratios, and hot-station stewardship.

## Weeks at a Glance
| Week | Focus | Main Application |
|---|---|---|
| Week 10 | Saute and pan heat | Sauteed vegetables, browning vs. steaming, aromatics |
| Week 11 | Roasting and oven control | Roasted vegetables, spacing, caramelization, timing |
| Week 12 | Moist heat and ratios | Rice, beans, lentils, simmering, texture control |

## Day-by-Day Teaching Flow

### Week 10: Saute and Pan Heat
- Day 1: Pan preheat and oil cues, teacher-led comparison, hot-station reset.
- Day 2: Saute basics, small batches, sauteed vegetables, reset.
- Day 3: Browning vs. steaming comparison with spacing and patience.
- Day 4: Aromatics without burning, smell/color cues, pan and counter reset.
- Day 5: Bistro prep/application using saute skills and timing reflection.

### Week 11: Roasting and Oven Control
- Day 1: Oven safety, rack placement, tray spacing, hot pan traffic.
- Day 2: Roasted vegetables with cut size, oil amount, and spacing check.
- Day 3: Browning, caramelization, texture cues, compare doneness examples if possible.
- Day 4: Oven timing for prep or service deadlines, label/store/reset.
- Day 5: Bistro service/application with hot-item communication and full hot-station reset.

### Week 12: Moist Heat, Ratios, and Texture
- Day 1: Simmer vs. boil, steam safety, observation and reset.
- Day 2: Rice and liquid ratios, measuring, steam/lid management.
- Day 3: Beans or lentils, simmering, liquid control, texture checks.
- Day 4: Combined heat-control application with heat checkpoints.
- Day 5: Review, skill check, reteach weak concept, hot-station deep reset.

Daily pattern: opening routine, heat safety reminder, visual demo, mise en place, cooking/application, teacher checkpoint, hot-station reset, exit reflection.

## Setup Notes
- Stage pans, pots, burners, sheet pans, spoons, spatulas, towels, sanitizer, and ingredients.
- Keep oil amounts controlled.
- Decide which heat tasks require teacher approval.
- Prepare comparison examples when possible.
- Build in reset pauses during longer labs.
- Keep hot-station roles simple and visible.

## Teacher Language and Prompts
- "Heat is adjustable."
- "Watch color, sound, steam, smell, texture, and time."
- "Browning needs space and patience."
- "Simmering is not boiling."
- "Hot-station reset is part of heat control."
- "If the station is unsafe, we reset before cooking continues."

## Safety Reminders
- Use dry towels around heat.
- Keep handles turned in.
- Ask before changing heat.
- Watch oil and steam.
- Keep oven and burner traffic clear.
- Report spills immediately.
- Turn off heat as directed.

## Classroom Management Reminders
- Require teacher approval for burners until students are ready.
- Use side-by-side comparisons.
- Stop the room for smoke, crowding, or unsafe movement.
- Keep demos short and visual.
- Reduce lab size if students are rushing.
- Use mid-lab reset pauses.

## Cleanup and Reset Expectations
Hot-station work is complete when:
- Burners and ovens are off as directed
- Hot pans are cooled/handled safely
- Dishes are routed correctly
- Counters are cleaned and sanitized
- Oil or spills are handled
- Towels are handled correctly
- Floors are checked
- Station is ready for the next group

## Unit Lab Plan
Purpose: help students see how heat affects texture, color, doneness, and flavor.

Skills practiced:
- Pan preheating
- Oil control
- Saute
- Browning vs. steaming
- Aromatic control
- Roasting and oven control
- Simmering and ratios
- Hot-station reset

Suggested roles:
- Heat lead: watches heat setting and teacher checkpoints
- Mise lead: confirms ingredients and tools are ready
- Timer/observer: tracks time and sensory cues
- Cook: handles food as directed
- Steward: leads dishes, towels, spills, floor, and reset

Teacher checkpoints:
- Pan is properly preheated
- Oil is not smoking
- Food is spaced correctly
- Aromatics are not burning
- Simmer is controlled
- Texture is checked before stopping
- Hot station is safe and reset

## Bistro Connection
Heat control affects service timing, holding quality, texture, and consistency. Bistro hot stations must be clean, safe, and ready for the next task.
