# Unit 07: Heat Control

## Weeks Covered
Weeks 10 to 12

## Focus
Learn how cooking actually works.

## Purpose
Students practice saute, pan preheating, oil control, browning vs. steaming, aromatics without burning, roasting, oven control, spacing, browning, caramelization, texture, moist heat, rice, beans, lentils, simmering, texture control, liquid ratios, and hot-station stewardship.

## Learning Targets
- I can explain how heat changes food.
- I can preheat a pan and manage oil safely.
- I can tell the difference between browning and steaming.
- I can cook aromatics without burning them.
- I can use oven spacing and timing for roasting.
- I can use liquid ratios and simmering for grains or legumes.
- I can clean and reset a hot station safely.

## Core Skills
- Saute
- Pan preheating
- Oil control
- Browning and spacing
- Aromatics
- Roasting and oven control
- Moist heat, simmering, and ratios
- Hot-station cleanup and reset

## Vocabulary
| Term | Student-Friendly Meaning |
|---|---|
| Saute | Cook quickly in a small amount of fat. |
| Preheat | Heat equipment before food is added. |
| Browning | Color and flavor developed through heat. |
| Steaming | Cooking with water vapor. |
| Caramelization | Browning of natural sugars. |
| Simmer | Cook liquid with small gentle bubbles. |

## Lab Applications
- Sauteed vegetables
- Aromatic base
- Roasted vegetables
- Rice, beans, or lentils
- Moist heat texture practice

## Bistro Connection
Heat control affects service timing, food quality, texture, and consistency. Bistro prep only works when hot stations are clean, safe, and ready for the next task.

## Assessment Ideas
- Pan preheat observation
- Browning vs. steaming comparison
- Roasting tray check
- Simmer and ratio check
- Lab reflection on heat adjustment

## Teacher Notes
Students often think higher heat is better. Teach them to watch color, sound, steam, smell, texture, and timing.

## Resources Needed
- Equipment: pans, pots, burners, ovens, sheet pans, spoons, thermometers if needed
- Ingredients: vegetables, aromatics, rice, beans, lentils, cooking fat, seasonings
- Materials: ratio examples and heat cue notes
