# Unit 07: Heat Control

## Weeks Covered
Weeks 10 to 12

## Focus
Learn how cooking actually works.

## Purpose
Students practice saute, pan preheating, oil control, browning vs. steaming, aromatics without burning, roasting, oven control, spacing, browning, caramelization, texture, moist heat, rice, beans, lentils, simmering, texture control, liquid ratios, and hot-station stewardship. Broiler/grill fundamentals are limited to instructor demonstration and heat-cue recognition until the equipment-specific SOP and student authorization process are complete.

## Learning Targets
- I can explain how heat changes food.
- I can preheat a pan and manage oil safely.
- I can tell the difference between browning and steaming.
- I can cook aromatics without burning them.
- I can use oven spacing and timing for roasting.
- I can use liquid ratios and simmering for grains or legumes.
- I can identify broiler/grill heat zones, safe positioning, and shutdown expectations after an instructor demonstration.
- I can clean and reset a hot station safely.

## Core Skills
- Saute
- Pan preheating
- Oil control
- Browning and spacing
- Aromatics
- Roasting and oven control
- Moist heat, simmering, and ratios
- Broiler/grill fundamentals through instructor demonstration; no independent use without authorization
- Hot-station cleanup and reset

## Vocabulary
| Term | Student-Friendly Meaning |
|---|---|
| Saute | Cook quickly in a small amount of fat. |
| Preheat | Heat equipment before food is added. |
| Browning | Color and flavor developed through heat. |
| Steaming | Cooking with water vapor. |
| Caramelization | Browning of natural sugars. |
| Simmer | Cook liquid with small gentle bubbles. |

## Lab Applications
- Sauteed vegetables
- Aromatic base
- Roasted vegetables
- Rice, beans, or lentils
- Moist heat texture practice
- Instructor demonstration of broiler/grill heat zones, flare-up prevention, safe tools, and shutdown when approved equipment and an SOP are available

## Bistro Connection
Heat control affects service timing, food quality, texture, and consistency. Bistro prep only works when hot stations are clean, safe, and ready for the next task.

## Assessment Ideas
- Pan preheat observation
- Browning vs. steaming comparison
- Roasting tray check
- Simmer and ratio check
- Lab reflection on heat adjustment

## Teacher Notes
Students often think higher heat is better. Teach them to watch color, sound, steam, smell, texture, and timing. Broiler/grill exposure does not authorize student use; keep it demonstration-only until equipment instruction, ventilation and fire-safety checks, the local SOP, and individual approval are complete.

## Resources Needed
- Equipment: pans, pots, burners, ovens, sheet pans, spoons, thermometers if needed; broiler/grill and heat-resistant tools only for an authorized instructor demonstration
- Ingredients: vegetables, aromatics, rice, beans, lentils, cooking fat, seasonings
- Materials: ratio examples and heat cue notes
