# Unit 10 Teacher Packet: Protein as Application

## Unit Overview
Weeks 16 to 17 focus: protein tests fundamentals.

Students apply safety, sanitation, stewardship, knife control, heat management, doneness, resting, seasoning, slicing, and temperature checking to chicken breast or thigh, ground turkey, egg cookery, searing, and simple protein applications.

## Weeks at a Glance
| Week | Focus | Main Application |
|---|---|---|
| Week 16 | Protein safety, eggs, ground turkey, thermometer use | Egg cookery, ground turkey, temperature checks |
| Week 17 | Chicken, searing, resting, slicing | Chicken breast or thigh, searing, doneness, slicing |

## Day-by-Day Teaching Flow

### Week 16: Safety, Eggs, Ground Turkey, Temperature
- Day 1: Protein safety reset, raw/clean station demo, contamination scenario practice.
- Day 2: Egg cookery review with gentle heat, doneness cues, egg-contact reset.
- Day 3: Ground turkey application with raw/clean separation and seasoning.
- Day 4: Thermometer placement, stable reading, probe cleaning, temperature as evidence.
- Day 5: Simple protein application tying safety, heat, seasoning, and reset together.

### Week 17: Chicken, Searing, Resting, Slicing
- Day 1: Chicken setup and sanitation, raw poultry boundary, board/tool control.
- Day 2: Searing and heat control, browning without burning or crowding.
- Day 3: Chicken breast or thigh lab with temperature, resting, and sanitation checkpoints.
- Day 4: Seasoning, resting, slicing, and simple side/prep connection.
- Day 5: Readiness practice, reteach weakest checkpoint, reflection, full protein reset.

Daily pattern: opening routine, protein safety reminder, demo, student application, teacher sanitation/temperature checkpoint, cleanup/reset, exit reflection.

## Setup Notes
- Keep protein work simple and supervised.
- Prepare raw and clean station boundaries.
- Stage thermometers, sanitizer, towels, tongs, pans, boards, knives, sheet pans, and dish supplies.
- Decide temperature targets based on school guidance.
- Use color-coded boards if available.
- Build sanitation checkpoints before students move from raw handling to cooked handling.

## Teacher Language and Prompts
- "Protein requires evidence, not guessing."
- "Raw and clean do not cross."
- "Temperature is a safety tool."
- "Resting and slicing affect quality."
- "This unit uses everything we have practiced."
- "A protein station must be safe for the next group."

## Safety Reminders
- Wash hands after raw protein contact.
- Keep raw and cooked tools separate.
- Clean thermometer probes before and after use.
- Verify doneness when required.
- Watch hot pans and oil.
- Use knife safety when slicing.
- Sanitize raw-contact surfaces completely.

## Classroom Management Reminders
- Use teacher checkpoints before students move on.
- Limit movement during raw protein handling.
- Stop and reset if sanitation slips.
- Keep chicken work small and controlled.
- Do not add advanced protein techniques.
- Make students explain their evidence for doneness.

## Cleanup and Reset Expectations
Protein work is complete when:
- Raw-contact surfaces are sanitized
- Thermometers are cleaned and returned
- Raw tools and clean tools are separated and washed
- Boards and knives are handled safely
- Counters are cleaned and sanitized
- Towels are handled correctly
- Trash and floors are checked
- Station is safe for the next group

## Unit Lab Plan
Purpose: use simple proteins to apply Culinary 1 fundamentals in a controlled way.

Skills practiced:
- Protein sanitation
- Cross-contamination prevention
- Seasoning
- Searing and heat control
- Doneness and temperature checking
- Resting and slicing
- Raw/clean station reset

Suggested roles:
- Safety lead: watches raw/clean boundaries
- Thermometer lead: manages temperature check with teacher approval
- Cook: manages heat and cooking as directed
- Prep/slice lead: handles seasoning or slicing tasks
- Steward: leads dish, towels, trash, floor, and final sanitation reset

Teacher checkpoints:
- Raw protein is contained
- Thermometer is used and cleaned correctly
- Heat level is controlled
- Doneness is verified
- Resting/slicing is appropriate
- Station is sanitized after protein work

## Bistro Connection
Protein work connects directly to guest safety, timing, service quality, and trust. Bistro cannot serve protein well without clean stations, accurate temperatures, and calm communication.

## Reflection Notes
After Unit 10, note:
- Whether students maintained raw/clean separation
- Whether thermometer use was correct
- Which heat-control habits held up
- Whether students connected earlier skills to protein
- Which students are ready for more independent responsibility
