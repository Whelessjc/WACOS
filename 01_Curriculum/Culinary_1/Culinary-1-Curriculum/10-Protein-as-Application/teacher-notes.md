# Teacher Notes: Protein as Application

## What to Emphasize
- Protein requires strong sanitation.
- Temperature checks are evidence, not guessing.
- Heat control affects texture.
- Resting and slicing matter.
- Earlier skills should show up here.
- Raw/clean separation and reset are as important as the cooked product.
- Protein service needs timing, temperature evidence, communication, and holding awareness.

## Common Student Mistakes
- Touching clean items after raw protein.
- Forgetting thermometer sanitation.
- Cutting protein immediately after cooking.
- Overcooking from high heat.
- Seasoning randomly.
- Using visual doneness only.
- Leaving raw-contact surfaces for someone else.

## Classroom Management Notes
- Keep raw protein stations controlled.
- Use color-coded boards if available.
- Require teacher checkpoints before moving on.
- Limit movement during protein handling.
- Build a sanitation checkpoint before students move from raw prep to cooked handling.

## Safety Concerns
- Cross-contamination.
- Undercooked poultry.
- Hot pans and oil.
- Knife use for slicing.

## Pacing Notes
Two weeks gives time to connect safety, heat, and doneness. Keep applications simple.

## Repetition Needed
Handwashing, board control, raw/clean separation, thermometer use, resting, and cleanup.

## Connection to Later Units
This unit shows whether students can apply Culinary 1 fundamentals before moving toward Culinary 2 responsibility, especially when food must be safe before it enters service.
