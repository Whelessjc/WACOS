# Lesson Sequence: Protein as Application

## Weeks Covered
Weeks 16 to 17

## Bistro / Service Connection
Protein work connects directly to guest safety, timing, ticket flow, holding quality, and trust. Students should see protein as an application of sanitation, heat control, seasoning, communication, and station reset.

## Week 16: Safety, Eggs, Ground Turkey, Temperature

### Day 1: Protein Safety Reset
- Opening routine: huddle on raw protein boundaries and handwashing.
- Demo or mini-lesson: clean station vs. contaminated station.
- Student work: identify cross-contamination risks.
- Lab/application: protein safety setup practice or teacher demo.
- Bistro/service connection: raw/clean boundaries protect guests and teammates.
- Cleanup/reset: sanitize raw-contact surfaces, handle towels, check floor.
- Exit check: one habit that protects the next person.

### Day 2: Egg Cookery Review
- Opening routine: handwashing and gentle heat reminder.
- Demo or mini-lesson: eggs, coagulation, doneness, and carryover.
- Student work: set mise en place and identify heat cues.
- Lab/application: simple egg cookery review.
- Bistro/service connection: eggs require timing, gentle heat, and quick reset in service.
- Cleanup/reset: egg-contact tools washed, counters sanitized.
- Exit check: what heat cue told you the egg was ready?

### Day 3: Ground Turkey Application
- Opening routine: raw protein safety huddle.
- Demo or mini-lesson: breaking up ground turkey, cooking through, and seasoning.
- Student work: prep station with raw/clean separation.
- Lab/application: ground turkey cooking task.
- Bistro/service connection: ground protein must be cooked and held safely for service-style work.
- Cleanup/reset: raw station sanitized, thermometer cleaned if used.
- Exit check: how did your group prevent cross-contamination?

### Day 4: Thermometer Use
- Opening routine: huddle on temperature as evidence.
- Demo or mini-lesson: thermometer placement, reading, and probe cleaning.
- Student work: practice placement on teacher-approved samples or models.
- Lab/application: temperature checking practice.
- Bistro/service connection: temperature checks are evidence before food moves forward.
- Cleanup/reset: thermometers sanitized and stored.
- Exit check: why is "looks done" not enough?

### Day 5: Simple Protein Application
- Opening routine: station roles and safety check.
- Demo or mini-lesson: connect safety, heat, seasoning, and doneness.
- Student work: prepare mise en place and call for checkpoints.
- Lab/application: teacher-selected simple protein task.
- Bistro/service connection: protein tasks require clear calls for checkpoints and timing.
- Cleanup/reset: full protein station reset.
- Exit check: name one earlier skill you used.

## Week 17: Chicken, Searing, Resting, Slicing

### Day 1: Chicken Setup and Sanitation
- Opening routine: huddle on raw poultry boundaries.
- Demo or mini-lesson: chicken station setup, board control, and handwashing.
- Student work: identify clean tools, raw tools, and reset steps.
- Lab/application: teacher demo or supervised setup practice.
- Bistro/service connection: station setup prevents cross-contamination during busy prep.
- Cleanup/reset: sanitize station and check trash/towels.
- Exit check: what must be cleaned before moving on?

### Day 2: Searing and Heat Control
- Opening routine: hot pan and oil safety reminder.
- Demo or mini-lesson: searing without burning, crowding, or guessing.
- Student work: observe browning and heat cues.
- Lab/application: searing practice or teacher-led comparison.
- Bistro/service connection: searing needs batch control and communication.
- Cleanup/reset: hot pans handled safely, counters sanitized.
- Exit check: what does searing add besides color?

### Day 3: Chicken Breast or Thigh Lab
- Opening routine: roles, thermometer, and sanitation check.
- Demo or mini-lesson: doneness, resting, and carryover.
- Student work: season, cook, check temperature, rest.
- Lab/application: chicken breast or thigh.
- Bistro/service connection: doneness, resting, and timing affect guest safety and quality.
- Cleanup/reset: raw and cooked stations fully reset.
- Exit check: what evidence showed the protein was done?

### Day 4: Seasoning, Resting, and Slicing Application
- Opening routine: huddle on knife safety and resting.
- Demo or mini-lesson: slicing across the grain if appropriate and protecting juices.
- Student work: connect protein to a simple side or prep list.
- Lab/application: protein application with seasoning and side/prep connection.
- Bistro/service connection: slicing and holding affect consistency and service readiness.
- Cleanup/reset: knife, board, counter, dish, and floor reset.
- Exit check: how did resting or slicing affect the final product?

### Day 5: Readiness Practice and Reflection
- Opening routine: final protein safety reminder.
- Demo or mini-lesson: reteach the weakest checkpoint.
- Student work: short performance practice or station conference.
- Lab/application: teacher-selected review task.
- Bistro/service connection: readiness means protein can move safely from prep to service.
- Cleanup/reset: full protein station reset.
- Exit check: what protein habit are you ready to carry into future service?

## Teacher Pacing Notes
- Keep raw protein work controlled and small.
- If sanitation slips, stop and reset before continuing.
- Do not add advanced protein skills; this unit is about applying fundamentals.
- Use teacher checkpoints for raw/clean separation, temperature, resting, slicing, and final reset.

## Daily Required Elements
- Opening routine and huddle
- Protein safety reminder
- Demo or mini-lesson
- Student application
- Teacher temperature/sanitation checkpoints
- Cleanup/reset
- Exit reflection or check for understanding
