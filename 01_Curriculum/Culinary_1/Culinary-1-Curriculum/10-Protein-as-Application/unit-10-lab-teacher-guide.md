# Unit 10 Lab Teacher Guide: Protein as Application

## Setup Before Class
- Choose protein application: eggs, ground turkey, chicken breast/thigh, or teacher demo.
- Confirm temperature guidance required by school procedures.
- Stage thermometers, sanitizer, towels, boards, knives, tongs, pans, and dish supplies.
- Set raw and clean boundaries.
- Decide teacher checkpoints for raw handling, cooking, temperature, resting, slicing, and reset.

## Demo Points
- Raw/clean station setup.
- Proper handwashing moments.
- Thermometer placement and probe cleaning.
- Searing with controlled heat.
- Resting before slicing.
- Full protein station reset.

## Safety Watch-Outs
- Students touching clean tools after raw protein.
- Thermometers not cleaned.
- Visual doneness guesses.
- Raw boards used for cooked food.
- Hot pan and oil risks.
- Rushed cleanup after protein work.

## Skill Checkpoints
- Raw protein is contained.
- Raw and clean tools stay separate.
- Thermometer is used and cleaned correctly.
- Temperature or doneness evidence is checked.
- Heat is controlled.
- Resting/slicing is appropriate.
- Station is sanitized after protein work.

## Common Student Mistakes
- Forgetting handwashing after raw contact.
- Moving cooked food onto contaminated surfaces.
- Overcooking with high heat.
- Cutting immediately after cooking.
- Seasoning randomly.
- Leaving raw-contact surfaces for someone else.

## Cleanup Timing
- Build in a sanitation checkpoint before cooked handling.
- Start dish flow early.
- Clean thermometers immediately after use.
- Inspect raw-contact areas before dismissal.
- Require incomplete sanitation reset to be fixed immediately.

## What to Look For While Circulating
- Are raw/clean boundaries visible?
- Are students calling for checkpoints?
- Are thermometers cleaned correctly?
- Are students using heat control from Unit 07?
- Is cleanup shared?
- Is the station safe for the next group?

## Bistro Connection
Protein is where guest safety and kitchen trust become obvious. Connect sanitation, temperature evidence, timing, communication, and holding quality to Bistro service.

## Quick Debrief Prompts
- What evidence proved doneness?
- How did your group prevent cross-contamination?
- What heat-control habit mattered?
- What reset step is non-negotiable?
