# Unit 10: Protein as Application

## Weeks Covered
Weeks 16 to 17

## Focus
Protein tests fundamentals.

## Purpose
Students apply safety, sanitation, stewardship, knife control, heat management, doneness, resting, seasoning, slicing, temperature checking, and basic pan-sauce construction to chicken breast or thigh, ground turkey, egg cookery, tofu, searing, and other simple protein applications. Tofu is the required plant-protein vehicle and is taught with allergen awareness and separate-tool controls where needed.

## Learning Targets
- I can handle protein safely and prevent cross-contamination.
- I can season protein with purpose.
- I can use heat control for searing or gentle cooking.
- I can press, portion, season, and cook tofu for a purposeful texture while controlling soy cross-contact.
- I can check temperature correctly when required.
- I can rest and slice protein appropriately.
- I can connect protein work to earlier course skills.
- I can reset a protein station so it is safe for the next group.

## Core Skills
- Protein sanitation
- Temperature checking
- Doneness awareness
- Searing
- Egg cookery
- Ground turkey cooking
- Tofu preparation and plant-protein texture control
- Resting and slicing
- Fond, deglazing, reduction, and basic pan sauce
- Raw/clean station reset

## Vocabulary
| Term | Student-Friendly Meaning |
|---|---|
| Protein | Food such as chicken, turkey, eggs, beans, or meat that needs careful handling. |
| Doneness | How fully cooked a food is. |
| Internal temperature | Temperature inside food. |
| Sear | Brown the surface with controlled heat. |
| Rest | Let cooked protein sit before cutting. |
| Carryover cooking | Food keeps cooking after leaving heat. |

## Lab Applications
- Chicken breast or thigh
- Ground turkey
- Egg cookery
- Searing practice
- Temperature checks
- Resting and slicing
- Basic pan sauce paired with seared chicken
- Teacher-approved tofu application

## Bistro Connection
Protein work connects directly to guest safety, timing, service quality, and trust. A Bistro kitchen cannot serve protein well without clean stations, accurate temperatures, and calm communication.

## Assessment Ideas
- Protein sanitation observation
- Thermometer check
- Searing and doneness observation
- Resting/slicing check
- Reflection connecting earlier skills

## Teacher Notes
Keep protein work simple and supervised. This is an application unit, not an advanced meat course. Use a teacher-approved tofu recipe and identify soy as an allergen before the lab; follow the program's allergen separation and cleaning procedure.

## Resources Needed
- Equipment: thermometers, pans, sheet pans, cutting boards, knives, tongs, towels, sanitizer
- Ingredients: chicken breast or thigh, ground turkey, eggs, tofu, seasonings, stock or another instructor-approved deglazing liquid
- Materials: temperature guidelines and sanitation reminders
