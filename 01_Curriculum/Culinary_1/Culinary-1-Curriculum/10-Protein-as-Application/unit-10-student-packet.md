# Unit 10 Student Packet: Protein as Application

## What We Are Learning
We are applying Culinary 1 fundamentals to protein. You will practice protein safety, raw/clean separation, seasoning, searing, egg cookery, ground turkey, chicken breast or thigh, temperature checking, resting, slicing, and full station reset.

## Why We Are Learning It
Protein shows whether kitchen habits are real. Safety, sanitation, heat control, knife skills, timing, and communication all matter when protein is involved.

## What Success Looks Like
- I can handle protein safely.
- I can prevent cross-contamination.
- I can season with purpose.
- I can use heat control.
- I can check temperature correctly when required.
- I can rest and slice protein appropriately.
- I can reset a protein station safely.

## Daily Kitchen Entry Routine
1. Enter calmly.
2. Put belongings away.
3. Check the agenda.
4. Check uniform/readiness.
5. Join the huddle.
6. Wash hands when directed.
7. Begin work only when released.

## Key Expectations
- Raw and clean do not mix.
- Wash hands at the right times.
- Temperature gives evidence.
- "Looks done" is not enough when temperature is required.
- Resting matters.
- Cleanup is part of protein safety.
- Stewardship means leaving no raw-protein risk for the next group.

## Kitchen Communication
- "Raw"
- "Clean"
- "Hot"
- "Behind"
- "Temperature check"
- "Thermometer clean"
- "I need help"

## Safety Expectations
- Keep raw protein contained.
- Keep raw tools away from clean food.
- Clean thermometer probes before and after use.
- Use hot pan safety.
- Use knife safety when slicing.
- Sanitize raw-contact surfaces fully.

## Protein Practice Expectations
- Set up raw and clean areas before starting.
- Season as directed.
- Cook with teacher checkpoints.
- Check temperature when required.
- Rest before slicing when directed.
- Reset completely before leaving.
- Treat thermometers, boards, knives, towels, and sanitizer as shared safety tools.

## Cleanup and Reset Checklist
- [ ] Raw-contact surfaces sanitized
- [ ] Thermometers cleaned and returned
- [ ] Boards washed correctly
- [ ] Knives handled safely
- [ ] Raw and clean tools separated and washed
- [ ] Counters cleaned and sanitized
- [ ] Towels handled correctly
- [ ] Trash checked
- [ ] Floor checked
- [ ] Station safe for the next group

## Bistro Connection
Bistro guests trust the kitchen when protein is handled safely, cooked with evidence, timed well, and served from a clean station. A protein station that is fully reset protects the next team and the next guest.

## Student Reflection Questions
1. How did your group prevent cross-contamination?
2. What evidence showed doneness?
3. What heat-control habit mattered today?
4. How did resting or slicing affect the protein?
5. What reset step protected the next group?
