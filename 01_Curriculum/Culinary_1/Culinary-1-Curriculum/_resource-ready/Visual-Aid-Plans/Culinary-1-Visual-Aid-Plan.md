# Culinary 1 Visual Aid Plan

These are plans only. Do not treat them as finished posters or images until the teacher confirms classroom layout, equipment, and school-specific language.

## Kitchen Entry Routine
- **Purpose:** Help students start class without confusion.
- **Where used:** Unit 01, posted by entrance
- **Suggested format:** Poster or slide
- **What it should show:** belongings, agenda, huddle, wash hands, wait for release
- **Student-facing language:** Enter. Check. Huddle. Wash. Wait.
- **Teacher notes:** Use during first two weeks and after breaks.
- **Priority:** high

## Handwashing
- **Purpose:** Make handwashing triggers visible.
- **Where used:** Near handwashing sink
- **Suggested format:** Sink poster
- **What it should show:** when to wash and what contaminates hands
- **Student-facing language:** Wash before food work and after contamination.
- **Teacher notes:** Keep wording short and point to it often.
- **Priority:** high

## Uniform Expectations
- **Purpose:** Show readiness before kitchen work.
- **Where used:** Entrance or changing area
- **Suggested format:** Poster/checklist
- **What it should show:** hair, shoes, jewelry, loose items, school policy placeholder
- **Student-facing language:** Ready means safe, clean, and prepared to work.
- **Teacher notes:** Add school-specific requirements before printing.
- **Priority:** high

## Daily Huddle
- **Purpose:** Clarify huddle behavior.
- **Where used:** Class starting area
- **Suggested format:** Poster or slide
- **What it should show:** agenda, roles, safety focus, release to work
- **Student-facing language:** Listen first. Ask early. Move when released.
- **Teacher notes:** Use before labs and service.
- **Priority:** medium

## Measuring Stations
- **Purpose:** Support first measuring practice.
- **Where used:** Measuring area
- **Suggested format:** Station card set
- **What it should show:** dry measure, liquid measure, spoons, return tools
- **Student-facing language:** Choose the right tool. Measure carefully. Return it clean.
- **Teacher notes:** Use real tool photos later.
- **Priority:** high

## Stewardship Jobs
- **Purpose:** Frame cleaning as real kitchen work.
- **Where used:** Dish/laundry/storage zones
- **Suggested format:** Job board
- **What it should show:** laundry, counters, dishes, storage, floors
- **Student-facing language:** Stewardship protects the next group.
- **Teacher notes:** Rotate jobs visibly.
- **Priority:** high

## Three-Compartment Sink
- **Purpose:** Show sink order.
- **Where used:** Dish area
- **Suggested format:** Waterproof poster
- **What it should show:** wash, rinse, sanitize, air dry
- **Student-facing language:** Wash. Rinse. Sanitize. Air dry.
- **Teacher notes:** Confirm local process before final art.
- **Priority:** high

## Dish Machine Flow
- **Purpose:** Prevent dish bottlenecks.
- **Where used:** Dish machine area
- **Suggested format:** Flow poster
- **What it should show:** scrape, rack, wash, unload, put away
- **Student-facing language:** Keep dish flow moving safely.
- **Teacher notes:** Match actual machine rules.
- **Priority:** high

## Storage Zones
- **Purpose:** Help students return items correctly.
- **Where used:** Storage shelves/pantry
- **Suggested format:** Zone labels/map
- **What it should show:** dry, cold, tools, towels, chemicals separate
- **Student-facing language:** Return items where they belong.
- **Teacher notes:** Use actual classroom zones.
- **Priority:** medium

## Thermometer Use
- **Purpose:** Support temperature labs.
- **Where used:** Near thermometers or protein station
- **Suggested format:** Step poster
- **What it should show:** clean probe, insert, read, record, clean probe
- **Student-facing language:** Use the thermometer as evidence.
- **Teacher notes:** Include local safe-temp language later.
- **Priority:** high

## Recipe Reading

- **Where used:** Recipe station or binder
