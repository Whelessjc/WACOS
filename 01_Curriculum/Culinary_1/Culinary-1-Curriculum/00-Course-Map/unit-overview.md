# Course Map and Kitchen Readiness

## Unit Purpose
This opening unit helps students understand the purpose, expectations, routines, and major skill areas of Culinary Arts Management 1. Students learn that the course is built around fundamentals, safety, organization, teamwork, and steady growth rather than advanced cooking.

## Time Frame
Estimated number of class periods: 2-4

## Learning Targets
- I can describe the main goals of Culinary Arts Management 1.
- I can explain why safety, sanitation, and professionalism come before cooking.
- I can identify the major units and skills in the course.
- I can begin using classroom routines for responsibility, teamwork, and reflection.

## Essential Skills
- Understanding course expectations
- Following classroom routines
- Using respectful kitchen communication
- Connecting units to long-term skill growth
- Reflecting on personal readiness and goals

## Key Vocabulary
| Term | Student-Friendly Definition |
|---|---|
| Fundamentals | The basic skills and habits that support all future cooking. |
| Professional habits | Dependable behaviors such as being prepared, clean, safe, and respectful. |
| Lab | A hands-on kitchen class activity. |
| Readiness | Being prepared to work safely and responsibly. |
| Teamwork | Working with others to complete tasks safely and successfully. |
| Hospitality | Treating others with respect, care, and awareness. |

## Teacher Notes
Keep the opening practical and reassuring. Students may arrive with very different cooking experience. Emphasize that everyone will be expected to grow from their starting point and that the course rewards safe, organized, responsible work.

## Student-Facing Overview
In this course, you will learn the basic habits and skills that make someone useful in a kitchen. You do not need to already cook like a chef. You need to be willing to learn, practice, communicate, clean, and work safely.

## Demonstrations
| Demo | Purpose | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Kitchen walk-through | Introduce major areas, boundaries, and expectations. | Keep it focused on safety and routines. |
| How class begins and ends | Show entry, setup, cleanup, and dismissal expectations. | Repeat in later units. |
| Professional communication | Model respectful questions, call-backs, and asking for help. | Use short role-play examples. |

## Labs / Recipes
| Lab | Skill Focus | Time Needed | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Course routines practice | Entry, setup, cleanup, reflection | 1 class | No cooking required. |
| Kitchen orientation scavenger check | Equipment awareness and room familiarity | 1 class | Keep movement controlled and purposeful. |
| Personal readiness reflection | Goal setting and responsibility | 1 class | Connect to future lab expectations. |

## Formative Checks
Use short reflection prompts, routine practice observation, course map questions, kitchen area identification, and student goal statements.

## Performance Assessment
Students must demonstrate basic course readiness by explaining expectations, following opening and closing routines, using respectful communication, and identifying how the major units build toward final practical readiness.

## Bistro Connection
The course map introduces the habits students will need for any bistro-style work: safety, sanitation, preparation, timing, teamwork, and hospitality.

## Resources Needed

- Ingredients: none

- Videos: optional course introduction or kitchen professionalism clips
- Images: optional classroom layout or station examples
