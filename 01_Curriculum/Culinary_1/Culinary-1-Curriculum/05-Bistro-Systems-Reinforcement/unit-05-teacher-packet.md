# Unit 05 Teacher Packet: Bistro Systems Reinforcement

## Unit Overview
Week 5 focus: stabilize service before moving deeper into technical skills.

Students debrief the soft opening, retrain stations, clarify roles, practice ticket flow and communication standards, run mock service 2.0, complete Bistro prep, and run a controlled Bistro service with stronger reset habits.

## Week at a Glance
| Day | Focus | Main Application |
|---|---|---|
| Monday | Soft opening debrief | Facts, fixes, station reset rehearsal |
| Tuesday | Station retraining | Role clarity and station setup/reset |
| Wednesday | Ticket flow | Mock service 2.0 and communication standards |
| Thursday | Bistro prep | Prep list, labeling, storage, reset |
| Friday | Bistro service | Controlled service, debrief, full reset |

## Daily Lesson Flow

### Monday: Soft Opening Debrief
- Opening: huddle with service reflection.
- Mini-lesson/demo: debrief with facts, not blame.
- Student work: name one strength, one guest-impact issue, and one station problem.
- Application: station reset rehearsal using soft opening problems.
- Prompt: "Which system needs to improve before the next service?"
- Reset: dish, towels, counters, floors, and station reset practice.

### Tuesday: Station Retraining
- Opening: agenda check and role focus.
- Mini-lesson/demo: station expectations before, during, and after service.
- Student work: revise station responsibilities and name one stewardship job.
- Application: setup, task rehearsal, and reset practice.
- Prompt: "What are your station's top three jobs?"
- Reset: tools, sanitizer, towels, trash, and floor checked.

### Wednesday: Ticket Flow and Communication Standards
- Opening: call-back practice.
- Mini-lesson/demo: clear ticket language, expo communication, and respectful corrections.
- Student work: practice ticket scenarios.
- Application: mock service 2.0 with a planned reset pause.
- Prompt: "What phrase helped the team stay organized?"
- Reset: dish, counter, service area, station, towel, and floor reset.

### Thursday: Bistro Prep
- Opening: prep huddle.
- Mini-lesson/demo: prep lists, checking completion, labeling, storage, and usable reset.
- Student work: complete assigned prep and station setup.
- Application: Bistro prep block with teacher checkpoints.
- Prompt: "What is ready for service?"
- Reset: label, store, clean, dish, and reset.

### Friday: Bistro Service
- Opening: readiness check, huddle, roles confirmed.
- Mini-lesson/demo: final reminders for hospitality, timing, communication, and clean-as-you-go.
- Student work: run controlled service in assigned roles.
- Application: service, dish flow, guest awareness, reset, debrief.
- Prompt: "What improved from the soft opening?"
- Reset: full room reset before reflection.

## Setup Notes
- Review Unit 04 soft opening notes.
- Keep station assignments visible.
- Prepare tickets for mock service 2.0.
- Post or review communication expectations.
- Prepare prep list and simple Bistro items.
- Confirm dish, towel, trash, storage, and floor reset roles.

## Teacher Language and Prompts
- "Debrief is for improvement, not blame."
- "Know your role before service starts."
- "Prep and reset are service tasks."
- "Call back what matters."
- "Would this station be useful to the next person?"
- "Guest awareness includes what guests never see."

## Safety Reminders
- Watch movement under service pressure.
- Keep hot food paths clear.
- Prevent crowding at expo and dish.
- Report spills immediately.
- Keep floors and towels controlled.
- Maintain handwashing and sanitation during role changes.

## Classroom Management Reminders
- Use short stoppages to correct service flow.
- Make students name one improvement target.
- Assign reset captains if needed.
- Do not add complexity yet.
- Keep mock service focused on systems, not speed.
- Use mid-service resets so students learn recovery.

## Cleanup and Reset Expectations
Service is not complete until:
- Stations are reset
- Dishes are routed correctly
- Counters are cleaned and sanitized
- Tools are returned
- Towels are handled correctly
- Storage is checked
- Trash and floors are checked
- Service area is ready for the next group

## Unit Lab Plan
Purpose: strengthen service systems before technical skills increase.

Skills practiced:
- Debrief and improvement
- Station retraining
- Ticket communication
- Mock service 2.0
- Bistro prep and storage
- Guest awareness
- Service reset

Suggested roles:
- Station lead: owns role clarity and tools
- Ticket/expo lead: watches ticket movement
- Prep lead: checks prep list completion
- Guest/service lead: watches guest experience
- Steward: leads dish, towels, trash, floors, and final reset

Teacher checkpoints:
- Students can state station role
- Ticket language is clear
- Prep list is complete
- Dish and reset happen during service
- Student improvement target is visible
- Station is ready for the next group

## Bistro Connection
This week keeps Bistro from becoming chaotic. Clear roles, clean stations, steady dish flow, and calm communication make service safer and more respectful.

## Reflection Notes
After Unit 05, note:
- Which station roles are stable
- Which communication habits improved
- What still breaks down under service pressure
- Which stewardship jobs need repetition
- How Bistro systems should support knife skills next
