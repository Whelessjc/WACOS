# Unit 05 Student Packet: Bistro Systems Reinforcement

## What We Are Learning
This week we are strengthening Bistro systems. You will practice debriefing, station roles, ticket flow, communication, prep, guest awareness, service, and reset.

## Why We Are Learning It
Service gets better when the team studies what happened, fixes the system, and practices again. We are making Bistro calmer and more reliable before technical skills get harder.

## What Success Looks Like
- I can name what worked and what needs improvement.
- I can explain my station role.
- I can use clear ticket language.
- I can complete prep and reset tasks.
- I can notice how my work affects guests and teammates.
- I can improve one service habit based on feedback.

## Daily Kitchen Entry Routine
1. Enter calmly.
2. Put belongings away.
3. Check the agenda.
4. Check uniform/readiness.
5. Join the huddle.
6. Wash hands when directed.
7. Begin work only when released.

## Key Expectations
- Talk about systems, not blame.
- Know your role before work starts.
- Use call-backs and "heard."
- Keep your station useful for the next person.
- Help dish and reset during service.
- Improve one habit from the soft opening.

## Kitchen Communication
- "Heard"
- "Order in"
- "Order up"
- "Working"
- "I need help"
- "Behind"
- "Hot"
- "Reset needed"

## Safety Expectations
- Move calmly during service.
- Keep expo and dish paths clear.
- Report spills right away.
- Keep towels and floors controlled.
- Wash hands when needed.
- Keep stations clean and sanitized.

## Station Expectations
Your station should be clear before service:
- What tools do you need?
- What prep do you need?
- What tickets or tasks come to you?
- What do you say when you receive work?
- What do you clean and reset?
- How do you know your station is ready?

## Bistro Service Expectations
- Use your role.
- Communicate early.
- Keep tickets moving.
- Keep guest needs in mind.
- Clean as you go.
- Reset before the room falls behind.

## Cleanup and Reset Checklist
- [ ] Station tools returned
- [ ] Counters cleaned and sanitized
- [ ] Dishes routed correctly
- [ ] Ingredients labeled or stored as directed
- [ ] Towels handled correctly
- [ ] Trash checked
- [ ] Floor checked
- [ ] Service area reset
- [ ] Station ready for the next group

## Bistro Connection
This unit is about making Bistro systems stronger. A station that is clear, clean, and ready helps tickets move and helps the team serve guests with more confidence.

## Student Reflection Questions
1. What improved from the soft opening?
2. What is your station responsible for?
3. What communication helped your team?
4. What reset habit did you practice?
5. What should our class keep working on?
