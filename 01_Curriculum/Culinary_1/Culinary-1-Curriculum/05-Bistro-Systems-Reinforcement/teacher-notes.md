# Teacher Notes: Bistro Systems Reinforcement

## What to Emphasize
- Debrief is for improvement, not blame.
- Roles need to be clear before service starts.
- Ticket flow depends on communication.
- Prep and reset are service tasks.
- Students should improve from Week 4 evidence.
- Hospitality includes what guests feel and what teammates can rely on.

## Common Student Mistakes
- Talking about problems vaguely.
- Waiting for the teacher instead of owning a role.
- Forgetting call-backs.
- Crowding ticket or expo areas.
- Treating cleanup as someone else's job.
- Forgetting that messy stations affect service even when guests cannot see them.

## Classroom Management Notes
- Keep station assignments visible.
- Use short service stoppages to correct flow.
- Assign a reset captain if needed.
- Make students name their improvement target.
- Use quick mid-service resets so students learn to recover while work is still happening.

## Safety Concerns
- Movement under service pressure.
- Hot food or equipment.
- Dish area congestion.
- Wet floors during reset.

## Pacing Notes
This week should feel repetitive in a good way. Stabilize the system before new technical skills begin.

## Repetition Needed
Station setup, ticket communication, dish flow, guest awareness, clean-as-you-go, and final reset.

## Connection to Later Units
Knife prep and heat-control labs will plug into these Bistro systems. Students need service habits ready before technical skills increase.
