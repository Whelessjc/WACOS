# Culinary 1 Curriculum

## Purpose
Define the one-semester Culinary 1 course as the foundational and exploratory entry point into West Ashley Culinary.

## Course Identity
Culinary 1 is a double-pathway, one-semester course. It should give every student a skill set and a nationally recognized Food Handler credential target whether or not the student continues in the culinary pathway.

The message to students is simple: if they love it, they can request Level 2 through their IGP. If they do not, the skill set still belongs to them forever.

## Design Principles
- Foundations before expensive product.
- Cook what you cut so knife practice becomes food, not waste.
- Bistro readiness requires dish systems, sanitation, menu knowledge, stocking, guest flow, and station responsibility before complex cooking.
- Culinary 1 should prepare many students for life and some students for Culinary 2.
- A semester course needs task-based targets, not abstract units.

## Pre-Bistro Fundamentals
Students should not be treated as Bistro-ready until they can demonstrate:
- Food Handler safety mindset and test preparation.
- Handwashing, hygiene, uniform, and personal readiness.
- Triple sink setup.
- Dish machine setup and basic operation.
- Clean and sanitize procedures.
- Laundry and towel systems.
- Storage, labeling, dating, FIFO, and cooler awareness.
- Notebook and pen every day.
- Station checklists every time.
- Basic menu knowledge.
- Flash card and vocabulary practice.
- Kitchen math foundations: fractions, conversions, scaling.
- Basic equipment identification and safe use.
- Knife handling 101 and safe cutting posture.
- Communication: behind, corner, hot, sharp, heard.
- Awareness: trash, sanitizer, labels, stocked tools, missing product.
- Clean-as-you-go discipline.
- FOH basics: greet, bus, reset, stock, guest courtesy.
- Life of an order.

## Suggested Semester Sequence
| Phase | Weeks | Focus | Evidence |
|---|---:|---|---|
| Orientation and safety | 1-2 | Culture, safety, sanitation, ServSafe/Food Handler foundations, notebook, uniform, emergency basics | Safety checks, sanitation setup, vocabulary |
| Systems before cooking | 3-5 | Dish systems, triple sink, storage, labeling, station checklists, recipe reading, kitchen math, menu awareness | Station audits, dish setup practical, math checks |
| Bistro launch foundations | 4-6 | Life of an order, mock service, customer service, stocking, role training, prep lists | Mock service, menu quiz, role checklist |
| Basic knife and cooking | 6-10 | Safe cuts, onion/garlic, carrots, potatoes, saute, roast, steam, boil, stir fry, simple seasoning | Cook-what-you-cut labs, practical checks |
| Applied labs | 10-14 | Pancakes, brownies, salads, rice/fried rice, eggs, pasta, simple vegetables, basic baking | Lab rubrics and reflection |
| Bistro reinforcement | After launch through Week 18 | Weekly prep/service cycle as calendar allows | Live service grading, self-reflection |
| Bridge to Level 2 | 17-18 | Practical review, portfolio/checklist, Culinary 2 interest guidance | Final practical, self-assessment |

## Core Competencies
- Safety and sanitation.
- Station setup and reset.
- Recipe reading.
- Measuring and scaling.
- Basic knife handling.
- Basic cooking methods.
- Dish and cleaning systems.
- Guest service basics.
- Professional communication.
- Personal responsibility.

## Assessment Anchors
- Food Handler credential target.
- Pre-Bistro fundamentals practical.
- Bistro role performance after launch.
- Skills checklist or portfolio evidence.
- Lab execution and cleanup.
- Reflection/self-grade after service.

Grade weights are not finalized in this manual. See [DECISION_REGISTER.md](DECISION_REGISTER.md).
