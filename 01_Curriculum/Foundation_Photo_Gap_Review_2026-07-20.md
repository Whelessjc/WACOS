# Culinary 1 and 2 Foundation Photo Gap Review

Date: July 20, 2026

## Purpose

Compare the photographed chapter overview from *The New Cooking School Cookbook: Fundamentals* against the current WACOS Culinary 1 and Culinary 2 foundations. The book is comparison evidence, not program authority. No photographed pages or copyrighted recipes were imported into WACOS.

## Already Incorporated

The photographs support the July 20 instructor decision already applied to WACOS:

- Culinary 1: dried pasta with a basic tomato sauce.
- Culinary 1: basic vinaigrette with a simple green salad.
- Culinary 1: basic pan sauce.
- Culinary 2: whole-chicken breakdown, subject to supervision, sourcing, budget, and calendar confirmation.

## Procedural Gaps Incorporated Now

The photographs exposed two small procedural gaps that fit existing Culinary 1 work without adding a new unit:

1. **Cookware selection and care:** students should select an appropriate pan/pot size, explain how crowding or excessive surface area changes results, and clean/store cookware correctly.
2. **Substitution judgment:** students should identify when a substitution changes function, safety, allergens, or expected results and obtain instructor approval before changing a formula.

These have been added to Culinary 1 Unit 03, where students already identify equipment and annotate recipes.

## Additions Confirmed and Incorporated

The instructor confirmed all four additions on July 20, 2026, and subsequently approved the tofu, poaching, quick-pickle, and broiler/grill recommendations as written. They are now part of the current curriculum with the boundaries below:

| Addition | Placement | Current boundary |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Tofu as a basic plant-protein vehicle | Culinary 1 Unit 10 | Required plant-protein vehicle using a teacher-approved recipe and soy-allergen controls. Tempeh is not required. |
| Poaching as an explicit gentle moist-heat method | Culinary 2 Unit 4 and Semester 1 Week 10 | Required method; teacher selects fish or egg vehicle based on cost and scheduling. |
| Broiler and grill fundamentals | Culinary 1 Unit 7 exposure; Culinary 2 Unit 4 application | Culinary 1 is instructor-demo exposure. Any student use requires equipment access, ventilation/fire checks, the equipment-specific SOP, instruction/demo, supervision, and individual authorization. |
| Quick pickling | Culinary 1 Unit 9 | Teacher-approved acid formula, labeled and refrigerated for same-day classroom use only. No canning, shelf-stable preservation, or fermentation. |

## Not Recommended as New Foundation Requirements

The photographed source includes many useful recipe categories that do not need to become separate WACOS requirements. They may remain optional recipe vehicles:

- specialty vegetable applications such as eggplant or cauliflower
- every salad subtype
- one-pot, baked, fresh, or specialty noodles
- risotto
- every steak, pork, roast, stew, or seafood subtype
- compound butters and spice rubs as standalone topics
- a comprehensive bread or dessert survey

The July 20 instructor decision already excludes fresh pasta, risotto, whole turkey, bivalves, fermentation, and a full pastry/baking program from required course scope.

## Remaining Implementation Work

The curriculum decisions and instructor approvals are complete. The remaining work is operational documentation: publish the final tofu and poaching recipe vehicles, the approved quick-pickle formula/label/discard procedure, and the broiler/grill SOP and authorization record before student operation.
