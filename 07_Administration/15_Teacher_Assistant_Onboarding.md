# Teacher Assistant Onboarding

## Purpose
Give a teacher assistant enough context to support the culinary program consistently and safely.

## TA Role
The TA helps the room function. The TA supports routines, safety observation, documentation, student redirection, Bistro preparation, and closing systems. The TA does not replace instructor judgment.

## First Week Priorities
- Learn emergency basics and escalation paths.
- Learn room map, storage, dish flow, laundry, trash, and sanitation systems.
- Learn uniform/material expectations.
- Shadow entry, briefing, work period, and closing.
- Observe Bistro roles before directing students independently.

## Daily Support Tasks
- Help monitor readiness and materials.
- Support station checklists.
- Watch for safety and sanitation concerns.
- Help students find the system before finding the answer for them.
- Support live grading evidence collection if assigned.
- Help close and reset.
- Report recurring issues to instructor.

## Boundaries
The TA should not:
- Authorize equipment use.
- Change grades.
- Handle major discipline alone.
- Give unapproved food safety guidance.
- Override instructor priorities during service.

## Escalation
Escalate immediately for injuries, unsafe equipment, chemical issues, suspected contamination, serious conflict, repeated refusal, guest complaints, or anything involving school policy.
