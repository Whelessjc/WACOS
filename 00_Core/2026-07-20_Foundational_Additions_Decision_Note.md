# Decision Note

**Decision:** Add three foundational skill vehicles to Culinary 1 - dried pasta with a basic tomato sauce, a standalone basic sauce/pan sauce lab, and a basic vinaigrette with simple salad. Add whole chicken breakdown to Culinary 2 instead of Culinary 1.

**Status:** Confirmed by instructor (2026-07-20 conversation) - incorporated into the repository on 2026-07-20.

**Source:** Instructor review of *The New Cooking School Cookbook: Fundamentals* (America's Test Kitchen) course structure, cross-referenced against the current `culinary-1-course-map.md`, `Culinary-1-Recipe-Index.md`, and `culinary-2-course-map.md` (S013, current WACOS GitHub repository).

**Reason:** Gap analysis against the cookbook's course list showed Culinary 1 currently has no salad/vinaigrette work, no pasta work, and no standalone sauce-construction lab. Instructor confirmed these three are appropriately scoped as one-semester foundation competencies using the standard: "at the end of a semester of Culinary 1, can this student make dried pasta, cook rice, handle a knife safely, run a Bistro station, work clean, and build a basic sauce?" All three passed that test.

Whole chicken breakdown was initially proposed for Culinary 1 but reclassified to Culinary 2 on instructor review: whole birds are a real product-cost line, and Culinary 1 is a one-semester course without the budget runway or repetition time that a full year gives Culinary 2. This puts whole-bird cost in the same bucket as the other cost-driven Culinary 1 exclusions already confirmed (seafood, pork as standalone cuts). Culinary 2 already has established chicken cookery (roast, pan-seared, stewed) to build the breakdown skill onto, and more calendar room to absorb the product cost.

Items explicitly considered and rejected as out of scope for either course: fresh pasta, risotto, whole turkey, bivalves, fermentation, and a full pastry/baking program.

## Culinary 1 Recipe Index Additions

Ready to paste into `Culinary-1-Recipe-Index.md` in the existing format. Treat all bracketed items as placeholders pending instructor-selected formula, cost, and district food-safety confirmation - consistent with current index practice.

### Dried Pasta with Basic Tomato Sauce
- **Unit connection:** Unit 03
- **Skill focus:** liquid ratio, timing, doneness by feel, basic sauce construction, salting pasta water
- **Approximate difficulty:** Beginner
- **Equipment needed:** large pot, colander, tongs, saucepan, knife setup for aromatics
- **Food safety concern:** boiling water burns, shared tools, allergen (gluten) awareness
- **Cleanup/reset concern:** starchy pot, sauce splatter, colander/strainer flow
- **Possible Bistro connection:** Direct Bistro menu item or side; sauce base reusable for other labs
- **Notes for future recipe development:** Use teacher-approved dried pasta shape and a simple canned or fresh tomato sauce; exact formula and allergen substitution plan still needed.

### Basic Vinaigrette and Simple Green Salad
- **Unit connection:** Unit 09
- **Skill focus:** emulsion, acid/fat balance, seasoning to taste, knife cuts for salad components
- **Approximate difficulty:** Beginner
- **Equipment needed:** whisk or jar, measuring tools, knife/board for greens and add-ins
- **Food safety concern:** produce washing, allergen awareness (nuts, cheese if used)
- **Cleanup/reset concern:** oily bowls/jars, produce scraps, compost/trash flow
- **Possible Bistro connection:** Direct Bistro side or starter; reinforces guest-facing plating standards
- **Notes for future recipe development:** Confirm base vinaigrette ratio and approved salad components/allergens.

### Basic Pan Sauce
- **Unit connection:** Unit 10
- **Skill focus:** fond, deglazing, reduction, seasoning, timing off protein rest
- **Approximate difficulty:** Beginner/medium
- **Equipment needed:** skillet, tongs, whisk, liquid for deglazing, thermometer (carryover from protein lab)
- **Food safety concern:** hot pan/liquid splatter, raw-protein cross-contact if sequenced tightly
- **Cleanup/reset concern:** hot pan handling, sauce reduction mess, station reset timing
- **Possible Bistro connection:** Elevates existing chicken breast/thigh Bistro items with a finished sauce
- **Notes for future recipe development:** Confirm deglazing liquid (stock, wine substitute, or approved alternative) and pairing with existing Unit 10 protein.

## Culinary 2 Addition

Ready to add to the Culinary 2 Unit 4 (Proteins, Heat, and Doneness) suggested recipe vehicles list, or to `10_West_Ashley_25_Technique_Map.md` as a technique entry.

### Whole Chicken Breakdown
- **Unit connection:** Culinary 2, Unit 4 (Proteins, Heat, and Doneness)
- **Skill focus:** knife control applied to protein, anatomy awareness, yield, safe raw-poultry handling
- **Approximate difficulty:** Medium
- **Equipment needed:** boning or chef's knife, cutting board (raw-only), sanitizer, storage containers, scale if tracking yield
- **Food safety concern:** raw poultry cross-contamination, dedicated board/tool sanitation, thorough handwashing
- **Cleanup/reset concern:** raw-contact station breakdown, immediate sanitation before any other work resumes at that station
- **Possible Bistro connection:** Breakdown parts feed directly into existing Culinary 2 roast/pan-seared/stewed chicken work - no product waste
- **Notes for future recipe development:** Confirm whether this is demo-then-practice or fully hands-on for every student given knife supervision ratios; confirm sourcing (whole birds vs. parts) and cost - this is the reason it's a Culinary 2 item rather than Culinary 1.
