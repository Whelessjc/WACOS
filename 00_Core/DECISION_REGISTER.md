## Confirmed Standards
- Version 1.0 remains historical baseline architecture.
- Version 2.1 is the current cleaned operating manual.
- Raw evidence belongs in the research archive, not the operating repo.
- Student-facing AI content must be reviewed before use.
- Certified Kitchen Cook (CKC) fully replaces ServSafe Manager as Culinary 2's credential pathway. CKC is embedded across Weeks 1-9 with a Week 9 practical push; ServSafe Manager test prep is explicitly listed as something Culinary 2 is not. (Resolves former D004. See `01_Curriculum/Culinary_2/00_Master_Framework.md` and `05_CKC_Alignment.md`.)
- Culinary 2 follows an 8-module full-year pacing arc (`01_Modular_Pacing_Guide.md`). Semester 1 is written day-by-day (`02_Semester_1_Weekly_Guide.md`). Semester 2 has a day-by-day draft in `06_Semester_2_Weekly_Guide.md`, pending instructor confirmation of the items in D005, D009, and D010.
- Guest complaint recovery uses the HEARD method (Hear, Empathize, Apologize, Resolve, Diagnose), replacing the older "listen, apologize, thank, act" language. See `West_Ashley_Culinary_Core_Manual.md` (Guest Recovery), `05_Bistro_Operations.md`, and `04_Bistro_Integration.md` (which also keeps a separate, distinct internal Service Recovery protocol for kitchen/station mistakes - HEARD is specifically for guest-facing complaints).
- **E.P.I.C. (Excellence, Passion, Innovation, Collaboration) is retired and is not current program identity.** It appeared in older dining-room training materials as a mission/values framework but is confirmed no longer in use (2026-07). Do not reintroduce it into Program Identity, Mission/Vision, or any current-facing document. The Core Manual's existing "Classroom to Table" / "High School, High Standards" identity language remains current and unaffected.
- **The four Bistro teams/rotations are Dining, Kitchen, Support, and Barista** (resolves former D011). "Management Staff" and "Service Staff" are not current team names - use Dining, Kitchen, Support, Barista consistently across Culinary 1, Culinary 2, and all Bistro documentation.
- Foundational additions confirmed 2026-07-20: Culinary 1 adds dried pasta with basic tomato sauce, basic vinaigrette with simple salad, and a basic pan sauce. Whole-chicken breakdown belongs in Culinary 2, subject to calendar, supervision, sourcing, and budget checks. Fresh pasta, risotto, whole turkey, bivalves, fermentation, and a full pastry/baking program were not added.
- Foundation photo-review additions instructor-approved 2026-07-20: tofu is the required Culinary 1 Unit 10 plant-protein vehicle; a teacher-approved quick pickle is included in Culinary 1 Unit 09 for labeled, refrigerated, same-day use only; poaching is explicit in Culinary 2 Unit 4; and broiler/grill fundamentals are introduced in Culinary 1 and applied in Culinary 2 only after equipment-specific SOP, ventilation/fire-safety, instruction/demo, supervision, and individual-authorization gates are met. Quick pickling does not add canning or fermentation, and tempeh is not a required vehicle. The instructor subsequently confirmed the tofu, poaching, quick-pickle, and broiler/grill recommendations as written.
