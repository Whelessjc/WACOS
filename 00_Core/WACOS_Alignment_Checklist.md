# WACOS Alignment Checklist

Use this checklist before approving or publishing any West Ashley Culinary lesson plan, SOP, Bistro document, assessment tool, AI assistant, syllabus, handbook, recipe packet, or administrative guide.

## 1. Authority Check

- [ ] The document aligns with `West_Ashley_Culinary_Core_Manual.md`.
- [ ] Current WACOS documentation is treated as the operating source.
- [ ] Archived material is used only as historical context unless specifically reapproved.
- [ ] Retired systems are not presented as current practice.
- [ ] Any unresolved conflict is flagged instead of silently resolved.

## 2. Program Identity

- [ ] The document supports West Ashley Culinary as a working kitchen inside a school.
- [ ] The document reflects "Classroom to Table" where appropriate.
- [ ] The document reflects "High School, High Standards" where appropriate.
- [ ] The Bistro is treated as the central instructional model, not an add-on.
- [ ] The tone is practical, professional, and clear.

## 3. Enduring Principles

- [ ] Authentic work is connected to authentic learning.
- [ ] Students learn through responsibility, not adult rescue.
- [ ] Fundamentals are treated as the highest standard.
- [ ] Professional habits are assessed or reinforced.
- [ ] Hospitality is treated as a mindset, not only a front-of-house task.
- [ ] Cleanliness, reset, and room stewardship are included where relevant.

## 4. Current Operating Decisions

- [ ] The grading structure uses 30% Employability, 30% Labs, and 40% Summative if grade weights are included.
- [ ] The certification pathway emphasizes ServSafe Food Handler and Certified Kitchen Cook if credentials are mentioned.
- [ ] The National ProStart Certificate of Achievement is not presented as current program policy.
- [ ] Thursday Bistro Prep and Friday Bistro Service are reflected correctly if weekly rhythm is included.
- [ ] Bistro launch is treated as readiness-based unless a current calendar decision says otherwise.

## 5. Curriculum and Assessment

- [ ] Culinary 1 is treated as foundation and exploration.
- [ ] Culinary 2 is treated as depth, operations, and fundamentals under pressure.
- [ ] Recipes are used as vehicles for skill practice, not as the curriculum itself.
- [ ] Assessment includes practical performance, visible habits, safety, cleanup, and reflection where appropriate.
- [ ] Written tests support safety, credentials, vocabulary, kitchen math, or applied understanding.
- [ ] Student reflection supports improvement rather than paperwork for its own sake.

## 6. Bistro and Kitchen Operations

- [ ] Safety and sanitation are explicit where needed.
- [ ] Mise en place, station readiness, communication, and closing/reset are addressed.
- [ ] Front-of-house, back-of-house, support, and leadership roles are bounded appropriately.
- [ ] Student leaders are allowed to coordinate, remind, report, and model, but not override instructor authority.
- [ ] Guest recovery or service language is calm, respectful, and instructor-escalated when needed.

## 7. AI Use

- [ ] AI is used as support, not authority.
- [ ] AI does not replace instructor judgment, safety decisions, grading, allergen verification, or district policy.
- [ ] Student-facing AI coaches thinking rather than simply providing answers.
- [ ] AI-generated recipes, assessments, safety instructions, and communications require instructor review.
- [ ] The document names its source base when AI is used to generate or revise policy-like content.

## 8. Documentation Quality

- [ ] The document has a clear purpose and audience.
- [ ] It does not duplicate another current document unnecessarily.
- [ ] It avoids obsolete terminology unless marked historical.
- [ ] It distinguishes enduring principles from operational decisions.
- [ ] It is concise enough to be used in real work.
- [ ] It names any documents that should be updated because of this document.

- [ ] Needs revision before use.
