# Program Standards

## Purpose
Establish common standards for professionalism, safety, sanitation, communication, teamwork, and quality.

## Operating Standard
Students are expected to be ready, clean, organized, safe, useful, and coachable. Technical talent does not cancel out poor safety, poor communication, or unreliable work habits.

## Core Standards
| Area | Standard |
|---|---|
| Preparedness | Arrive on time with uniform, notebook, pen, required materials, and readiness to work |
| Safety | Follow knife, heat, equipment, movement, chemical, and emergency procedures |
| Sanitation | Wash hands, prevent contamination, use sanitizer correctly, label and store food safely |
| Mise en place | Set the station before work begins; keep tools, product, and timeline visible |
| Communication | Use kitchen calls such as behind, corner, hot, sharp, heard, and walking in |
| Awareness | Notice empty sanitizer, trash, missing labels, low supplies, unstocked spoons, unsafe movement, and unfinished closing tasks |
| Teamwork | Complete assigned work and help the team without taking over irresponsibly |
| Hospitality | Treat guests, classmates, staff, and the room with respect |
| Quality | Follow recipes, taste thoughtfully, check doneness, plate intentionally, and correct errors |
| Reflection | Identify what worked, what failed, and what changes next time |

## Non-Negotiables
- No unauthorized equipment use.
- No unsafe knife handling.
- No food service without required sanitation controls.
- No unlabeled stored food.
- No wet towels as pot holders.
- No leaving stations for others to reset without approval.
- No using AI output as operational fact without instructor review.

## Professional Language
Preferred language in the program:
- Station, role, prep list, pass, ticket, fire, hold, eighty-six, mise en place, FIFO, clean-as-you-go, reset, service, guest recovery.

## Conflict Notes
The source history contains both aspirational professional standards and practical frustration with inconsistent student fundamentals. Version 2.1 treats fundamentals as explicitly teachable and assessable rather than assuming students will absorb them.
