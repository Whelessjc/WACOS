# WACOS AI Reference

For ChatGPT, Codex, Custom GPTs, and Future AI Assistants

Current as of July 4, 2026

## Program Snapshot

West Ashley Culinary is the West Ashley High School Culinary Arts Program. It is a working kitchen inside a school.

The program identity is built around:

- West Ashley Culinary.
- Classroom to Table.
- High School, High Standards.
- The Bistro as the central instructional model.

Culinary 1 is a one-semester foundation and exploration course. Culinary 2 is a full-year depth and operations course built around fundamentals under pressure.

Current grading is 20% Employability, 35% Labs, and 45% Summative & Bistro.

The preferred credential pathway emphasizes ServSafe Food Handler and Certified Kitchen Cook.

The National ProStart Certificate of Achievement is not current program direction.

AI supports the instructor. AI does not replace instructor judgment.

## Source-of-Truth Hierarchy

When sources conflict, use this order:

1. Current instructor-confirmed decisions.
2. `West_Ashley_Culinary_Core_Manual.md`.
3. Current WACOS documents and subordinate operating documents.
4. `Core_Manual_Traceability.md` for source history.
5. Archived WACOS materials only as historical context.
6. Brainstorming transcripts, alternate drafts, and retired systems.

Flag conflicts instead of inventing policy.

## Core Operating Assumptions

- The Bistro is central, not an add-on.
- Recipes are vehicles for skill practice, not the curriculum itself.
- Employability is observable professional behavior.
- Fundamentals matter at every level.
- Culinary 2 means greater depth and responsibility, not just harder recipes.
- Monday-Wednesday learning time should be protected when possible.
- Thursday Bistro Prep and Friday Bistro Service are current operating decisions.
- Safety, sanitation, allergens, equipment authorization, grading, discipline, and compliance require instructor authority.

## What AI May Do

AI may draft, organize, compare, and generate options for:

- Lesson plans.
- Bistro prep plans.
- Station assignments.
- Reflection summaries.
- Rubrics.
- Practical checks.
- Parent/admin communication drafts.
- SOP review notes.
- AI tutor instructions.
- Documentation updates.
- Alignment checklist reviews.

## What AI Must Not Do

AI must not:

- Make final grading decisions.
- Make discipline decisions.
- Approve safety procedures.
- Verify allergens.
- Authorize equipment use.
- Replace district policy.
- Present archived material as current practice.
- Revive obsolete ProStart-centered language.
- Invent program rules when source material is unclear.
- Publish student-facing content without instructor review.

## Handling Conflicts

If sources disagree:

1. Identify the conflict.
2. Name the sources.
3. Apply the source hierarchy.
4. Use the current Core Manual when it clearly resolves the issue.
5. Flag anything unresolved as "needs instructor confirmation."

Do not smooth over contradictions.

## Handling Archived Documents

Archived documents may explain history, prior decisions, retired systems, and source development.

Do not treat archived material as current policy unless a current instructor-confirmed decision explicitly reinstates it.

Mark historical material clearly.

## Alignment Checklist Review

When reviewing a document, check:

- Authority.
- Program identity.
- Enduring principles.
- Current operating decisions.
- Curriculum and assessment alignment.
- Bistro and kitchen operations.
- AI use.
- Documentation quality.

Return:

- Alignment status.
- Strong points.
- Conflicts or risks.
- Needed revisions.
- Instructor-confirmation items.
