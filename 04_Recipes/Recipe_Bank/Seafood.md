# Seafood

## Fish Fumet

- **Course/use case**: Culinary 2, Seafood unit - foundational stock.
- **Source and revision date**: `8 - Stocks & Sauces/FISH FUMET.docx`, standardized 2026-07.
- **Yield and portion size**: Not a portioned dish - a base stock (batch as written uses 5 lb fish bones and yields roughly 3+ quarts after reduction/straining). Scales by batch size as needed; no serving count applies.
- **Equipment**: Rondeau, fine strainer/chinois.
- **Ingredients**: 5 lb fish bones (chopped; a whole skate can substitute if there's an outlet for the meat), 1 tsp vegetable oil, 1/2 lb onion (small dice), 4 oz carrots (small dice), 4 oz celery, 1 oz mushroom trim, 2 oz fennel trim (small dice, optional), 12 oz dry white wine, 1 lemon, 3.5 qt water or fish stock, 1 sachet/bouquet garni.
- **Procedure**:
  1. Heat oil in a rondeau.
  2. Sweat the mirepoix, including mushrooms and fennel, for 10 minutes.
  3. Add the bones; cook 5 minutes.
  4. Add white wine; reduce all liquids by half.
  5. Add the juice and micro-zest of the lemon (not the rind/pith).
  6. Add the sachet and water/stock.
  7. Simmer gently 30 minutes.
  8. Strain.
- **Food safety controls**: Standard seafood/stock handling; cool and refrigerate promptly if not used same-day.
- **Allergens**: Fish; contains alcohol (white wine, cooked down).
- **Quality indicators**: Clean, light seafood flavor without bitterness (avoid boiling hard or over-simmering, which turns fish stock cloudy/bitter), pale golden color.
- **Holding/storage**: Refrigerate or freeze; use as a base for fish velouté, chowders, or poaching liquid.
- **Scaling notes**: Scales by weight; keep the same short simmer time regardless of batch size (fish stock, unlike meat stock, doesn't benefit from long cooking).
- **Skill focus**: Building a proper fumet (short-simmer stock with wine and aromatics), sweating vs. browning technique, straining for clarity.

## Shellfish Fumet/Stock White

- **Course/use case**: Culinary 2, Seafood unit - foundational stock.
- **Source and revision date**: `8 - Stocks & Sauces/SHELLFISH FUMET_STOCK WHITE.docx`, standardized 2026-07.
- **Yield and portion size**: Not a portioned dish - a base stock. No serving count applies.
- **Equipment**: Appropriately sized stock pot (source notes: too large a vessel dilutes the stock).
- **Ingredients**: 4 lb quahogs, 8 oz mussels, 1 lb mirepoix (cut 1", about 20% of shellfish weight), 1 cup white wine, 1 bouquet garni, water to cover.
- **Procedure**:
  1. Wash shellfish well.
  2. Sweat the mirepoix 10 minutes.
  3. Add white wine; reduce by half.
  4. Add shellfish and bouquet garni; cover with water.
  5. Simmer 30 minutes.
  6. Reserve the clams and mussels (for another use).
  7. Strain the stock.
- **Food safety controls**: Standard shellfish handling; discard any shellfish that don't open, or that were dead/damaged before cooking.
- **Allergens**: Shellfish; contains alcohol (white wine, cooked down).
- **Quality indicators**: Clean, briny-sweet flavor, not watered down (source flags vessel size as the main risk for a weak stock).
- **Holding/storage**: Refrigerate or freeze; use as a base for shellfish nage, bisques, or chowders.
- **Scaling notes**: Scales by weight - keep the ~20% mirepoix-to-shellfish ratio and appropriately sized pot as batch size changes.
- **Skill focus**: Shellfish stock technique (a close relative of fish fumet, per the source's own note), vessel-sizing for stock strength, reserving cooked shellfish meat for a second use.

## Shellfish Nage

- **Course/use case**: Culinary 2, Seafood unit - aromatic poaching liquid.
- **Source and revision date**: `8 - Stocks & Sauces/SHELLFISH NAGE.docx`, standardized 2026-07.
- **Yield and portion size**: Not a portioned dish - roughly 1 qt as written. No serving count applies.
- **Equipment**: Sauce pot, bain-marie (for cooling/steeping).
- **Ingredients**: 4 stalks lemongrass (thinly sliced), 1 qt shellfish stock (clam is best - see Shellfish Fumet/Stock White above), 2 sprigs lemon thyme, 6 sprigs parsley (including stems), 1/2 cup tomato consommé (if available), 1 clove garlic (crushed), 1 bay leaf.
- **Procedure**:
  1. Place sliced lemongrass in a sauce pot.
  2. Add remaining ingredients; bring to a simmer.
  3. Simmer/reduce briefly - do not cook for a prolonged period.
  4. Do not strain; let the lemongrass steep in the stock while it cools in a bain-marie.
  5. Finish with butter or olive oil and an appropriate herb before use.
- **Food safety controls**: Standard stock handling; cool promptly (the bain-marie cooling step also serves this purpose).
- **Allergens**: Shellfish (via the base stock).
- **Quality indicators**: Fragrant, neutral-but-aromatic flavor (lemongrass forward, not muddy) - the source notes this neutrality is what makes it versatile across different fish/shellfish preparations.
- **Holding/storage**: Refrigerate; finish with fat just before service, not during storage.
- **Scaling notes**: Scales linearly from the 1 qt base.
- **Skill focus**: Building an aromatic nage (a light, herbed poaching/finishing liquid distinct from a heavier fumet), steeping vs. simmering for aromatic extraction, finishing with fat at the point of service.

## Crawfish Velouté

- **Course/use case**: Culinary 2, Seafood unit; Bistro sauce for fish or pasta.
- **Source and revision date**: `8 - Stocks & Sauces/CRAWFISH VELOUTE.docx`, standardized 2026-07.
- **Yield and portion size**: Already at production scale per source: batch size 2 quarts, yielding 100 portions per batch.
- **Equipment**: Large sauté pan.
- **Ingredients**: 1/4 cup olive oil, 10 minced shallots, 10 cloves chopped garlic, 10 lb crawfish tails, 1 cup chopped green onions, 2 qt heavy cream, 2.5 tsp paprika, 2 tsp salt, 2 tsp garlic powder, 1 tsp black pepper, 1 tsp onion powder, 1 tsp cayenne pepper, 1 tsp fresh oregano (chopped), 1 tsp fresh thyme (chopped).
- **Procedure**:
  1. Heat olive oil in a large sauté pan.
  2. Add shallots; sauté.
  3. Add garlic, crawfish tails, and the herbs/spices; continue to sauté.
  4. Add green onions.
  5. Stir in the cream; bring to a boil.
  6. Reduce heat to a simmer; cook until the liquid thickens and coats the back of a spoon, about 4 minutes.
- **Food safety controls**: Standard shellfish/dairy handling.
- **Allergens**: Shellfish, dairy.
- **Quality indicators**: Sauce coats the back of a spoon (nappe consistency), crawfish tails tender (not rubbery from overcooking), balanced Cajun spice.
- **Holding/storage**: Hold hot for service in a bain-marie; cool and refrigerate leftovers promptly.
- **Scaling notes**: Already a full production batch (2 qt, 100 portions). Scale down proportionally for smaller runs.
- **Skill focus**: Cream reduction to nappe consistency, building a Cajun spice blend, timing shellfish additions to avoid overcooking.

## Beer-Battered Fish & Chips

- **Course/use case**: Culinary 2, Seafood unit - frying technique.
- **Source and revision date**: `14 - Seafood/Beer-battered fish n chips.pdf` (source: Rouxbe). Standardized 2026-07.
- **Yield and portion size**: Serves 4 (source yield). Scaled 5x below to ~20 portions.
- **Equipment**: Deep pot or fryer, spider, deep-fry thermometer, baking trays.
- **Ingredients (5x batch)**: French fries: 20 large russet potatoes, frying oil (grapeseed, peanut, or canola). Batter: 5 cups all-purpose flour, 5 cups beer, 10 large egg whites, 1.25-2.5 tsp black pepper (optional), 2.5 tsp kosher salt. Fish: 20 fillets halibut, cod, or haddock (6-8 oz each), 5 cups all-purpose flour (for dusting), kosher salt to taste. To finish: salt, pepper, lemon wedges, malt vinegar, tartar sauce (all optional).
- **Procedure**:
  1. Peel potatoes; cut into 3/8" x 3/8" strips. Hold in water to prevent browning (can be done up to a day ahead).
  2. Heat frying oil to 300-325°F. Blanch potatoes in small batches, 1.5-2 minutes, until just softened but not colored. Drain and chill at least 1 hour (up to a day).
  3. Just before frying, mix batter: flour, salt, pepper, and beer. Whip egg whites to stiff peaks; fold into the batter.
  4. Raise oil to 350-375°F. Dust fish lightly in flour, shake off excess, dip in batter, and fry until golden and cooked through, flipping as needed.
  5. Drain and season fish with salt while hot; hold in a warm oven or serve immediately.
  6. Raise oil to 375-400°F. Fry the blanched potatoes a second time, 1.5-5 minutes depending on desired crispness, until golden. Season immediately.
  7. Serve fish and chips together with lemon wedges, malt vinegar, and tartar sauce as desired.
- **Food safety controls**: Standard seafood handling; confirm fish reaches a safe internal temperature/opaque, flaky texture throughout.
- **Allergens**: Gluten, fish, egg; contains alcohol (beer).
- **Quality indicators**: Crisp, not greasy batter; fries golden and crisp outside, fluffy inside (double-fry technique); fish flaky and moist inside the crust.
- **Holding/storage**: Best fresh; batter-fried items lose crispness quickly if held.
- **Scaling notes**: Scaled 5x from the source's 4-serving yield.
- **Skill focus**: Double-fry (blanch then fry) potato technique, beer-batter mixing with whipped egg whites for lightness, staged frying/holding logistics for two components served together.

## Fried Calamari

- **Course/use case**: Culinary 1/2, Seafood unit; Bistro appetizer.
- **Source and revision date**: `14 - Seafood/Fried calamari.pdf` (source: Dinner at the Zoo). Standardized 2026-07.
- **Yield and portion size**: Not explicitly stated in source (1 lb calamari; the "Servings" field in the source actually lists a calorie count rather than a serving count). Scaled 4x below to 4 lb calamari, ~16 appetizer portions at 4 oz raw each. **Needs confirmation** on actual portion count in production.
- **Equipment**: Large deep pot, paper towels for draining.
- **Ingredients (4x batch)**: 4 lb calamari rings and tentacles, 4 cups buttermilk, 6 cups all-purpose flour, 8 tsp salt (plus more for serving), 2 tsp paprika (smoked or regular), 1 tsp pepper, 1 tsp garlic powder, vegetable oil for frying, 8 tsp chopped fresh parsley.
- **Procedure**:
  1. Combine calamari and buttermilk; cover and refrigerate at least 30 minutes.
  2. Heat 3-4" of oil in a large deep pot to 375°F.
  3. Combine flour, salt, paprika, pepper, and garlic powder.
  4. Remove calamari from the buttermilk and dredge in the flour mixture; repeat until all pieces are coated.
  5. Fry in batches (8-10 pieces at a time, scaling batch size up with total quantity), 2-3 minutes, until golden brown.
  6. Drain on paper towels; season with additional salt and parsley. Serve immediately.
- **Food safety controls**: Standard seafood/dairy handling; don't overcrowd the fryer (drops oil temperature and produces greasy, unevenly cooked calamari).
- **Allergens**: Gluten, dairy, mollusk (squid).
- **Quality indicators**: Light, crisp coating; tender (not rubbery) squid - a sign it wasn't overcooked.
- **Holding/storage**: Best immediately after frying; does not hold well.
- **Scaling notes**: Scaled 4x from the source's 1 lb batch; confirm actual portion count from a full batch in production.
- **Skill focus**: Buttermilk soak for tenderizing, dredge-and-fry technique, batch frying without overcrowding, timing to avoid rubbery squid.

## Blackened Shrimp

- **Course/use case**: Culinary 1/2, Seafood unit - Cajun/Creole flavor profile.
- **Source and revision date**: `14 - Seafood/Seafood/Blackened Shrimp - Will Cook For Smiles.pdf` (source: Will Cook For Smiles). Standardized 2026-07.
- **Yield and portion size**: Servings 2 (1 lb shrimp, source yield). Scaled 8x below to ~16 servings.
- **Equipment**: Heavy skillet or cast iron pan, small saucepan (for clarified butter, optional).
- **Ingredients (8x batch)**: 8 lb uncooked shrimp (shelled and deveined), 16 Tbsp butter (melted), 8 Tbsp butter (for cooking). Blackening seasoning: 8 Tbsp paprika, 16 tsp garlic powder, 16 tsp onion powder, 4 tsp dried oregano, 4 tsp dried basil, 8 tsp dried thyme, 8 tsp salt, 2 tsp black pepper, 2 tsp cayenne pepper (more to taste). *Optional clarified butter, for a cleaner sear: 32 oz (2 lb) unsalted butter, melted and skimmed, with milk solids left behind.*
- **Procedure**:
  1. Pat shrimp dry; place in a mixing bowl.
  2. Mix together all blackening seasoning ingredients in a separate bowl.
  3. Toss shrimp with melted butter to coat.
  4. Sprinkle blackening seasoning over the shrimp, mixing gently to coat evenly.
  5. Preheat a pan over medium-high heat until fully hot. Add cooking butter (or clarified butter, if using) and shrimp in a single layer once melted.
  6. Cook until pink and opaque on one side, flip, and cook until opaque on the other, 4-7 minutes total depending on size.
  7. Remove from the pan as soon as cooked; serve with favorite sides or over pasta.
  8. *(Optional clarified butter method: melt butter over medium heat, skim off the foam, pour into a measuring cup, and let the milk solids settle to the bottom. Pour off the clear golden butter, leaving the solids behind, for a cleaner-tasting sear.)*
- **Food safety controls**: Standard shellfish handling; confirm shrimp are fully opaque throughout before removing from heat.
- **Allergens**: Shellfish, dairy.
- **Quality indicators**: Dark, spiced crust without a burnt/acrid taste, shrimp opaque and just cooked through (not rubbery from overcooking).
- **Holding/storage**: Best fresh; shrimp toughens quickly if held or reheated.
- **Scaling notes**: Scaled 8x from the source's 2-serving yield.
- **Skill focus**: Building a blackening spice blend, high-heat pan searing without burning the spices, clarifying butter for a cleaner cook (a technique contrast against the Blackened Chicken entry in `Poultry.md`, which finishes in the oven rather than staying entirely stovetop).

## Ahi Poke

- **Course/use case**: Culinary 2, Seafood unit - raw preparation/Garde Manger crossover.
- **Source and revision date**: `14 - Seafood/Seafood/Poke.pdf` ("Ahi Poke," source: A Spicy Perspective). Standardized 2026-07.
- **Yield and portion size**: Servings 8 (1.5 lb tuna, source yield). Scaled 2x below to ~16 servings.
- **Equipment**: Sharp knife, mixing bowl.
- **Ingredients (2x batch)**: 4 large sashimi-grade Ahi tuna steaks (about 3 lb total), 2 shallots (sliced; or Maui onions), 1 cup chopped green onion, 6 Tbsp soy sauce (look for a gluten-free brand if needed), 2 tsp sesame oil, 2 tsp chili garlic sauce, 2 Tbsp sesame seeds. *(Variation: omit chili sauce and add 1 tsp wasabi paste and 2 tsp honey; fresh avocado may be added.)*
- **Procedure**:
  1. Pat the tuna dry; cut into neat 1/2" cubes. Place in a bowl.
  2. Add shallots, green onion, soy sauce, sesame oil, chili garlic sauce, and sesame seeds.
  3. Gently toss. Serve immediately, or cover and refrigerate briefly.
- **Food safety controls**: Sashimi-grade fish only, from a supplier verified for raw consumption; keep well chilled until service; use within the same day.
- **Allergens**: Fish, soy, sesame.
- **Quality indicators**: Clean-tasting, bright tuna (no fishy odor - a sign of freshness), even seasoning distribution, neat uniform dice.
- **Holding/storage**: Serve same-day; hold well chilled (below 41°F) until service, consistent with raw-fish food safety practice.
- **Scaling notes**: Scaled 2x from the source's 8-serving yield.
- **Skill focus**: Sourcing and handling sashimi-grade fish safely, clean knife cuts for raw fish, balancing a soy-sesame-chili dressing.

## Don Ceviche (Peruvian Sea Bass Ceviche)

- **Course/use case**: Culinary 2, Seafood unit - International/raw preparation technique.
- **Source and revision date**: `14 - Seafood/Seafood/don-ceviche-sea-bass-ceviche-from-ceviche-peruvian-kitchen.pdf` (source: Serious Eats, adapted from the cookbook "Ceviche: Peruvian Kitchen"). Standardized 2026-07. Kept as a separate entry from the Panamanian-Style Ceviche below - genuinely different technique (the "tiger's milk"/leche de tigre method with amarillo chili paste, single fish type) and a different national cuisine, not a minor variant.
- **Yield and portion size**: Serves 4 (source yield). Scaled 4x below to ~16 portions.
- **Equipment**: Fine sieve, large mixing bowl, metal spoon.
- **Ingredients (4x batch)**: Amarillo chili tiger's milk: 1" piece fresh ginger (cut in half), 4 small cloves garlic (cut in half), 16 roughly chopped cilantro sprigs, 32 limes (juiced), 2 tsp salt, 8 tsp Amarillo chile paste. Ceviche: 4 large red onions (very thinly sliced), 5.3 lb sea bass fillet (or other white fish, skinned and trimmed), the tiger's milk (above), a few cilantro sprigs (leaves finely chopped), 4 limo chilies (seeded, finely chopped - substitute another mild-hot fresh chili if unavailable), 4 sweet potatoes (cooked, cut into small cubes), fine sea salt.
- **Procedure**:
  1. Make the tiger's milk: combine ginger, garlic, cilantro sprigs, and lime juice in a bowl. Stir and let infuse 5 minutes. Strain through a sieve. Add salt and Amarillo chile paste; mix well. (Keeps up to 4 hours refrigerated.)
  2. Rinse the sliced onion; soak in iced water 10 minutes. Drain thoroughly, spread out to remove excess water, and refrigerate until needed - this crisps the onion and tames its bite.
  3. Cut the fish into uniform strips (about 1.25" x 0.75"). Place in a large bowl, add a pinch of salt, and mix gently with a metal spoon; let sit 2 minutes (the salt helps open the fish's pores).
  4. Pour the tiger's milk over the fish; combine gently. Let the fish "cook" in the marinade 2 minutes.
  5. Add the onion, cilantro, chili, and sweet potato to the fish. Mix gently; taste and adjust the salt/sour/heat balance.
  6. Divide among serving bowls; serve immediately.
- **Food safety controls**: Use fish suitable for raw/marinated consumption (from a supplier verified for this use, same standard as sashimi-grade fish); keep well chilled through prep; serve immediately once dressed (the acid "cooks" the fish's texture but doesn't replace food-safe sourcing/handling).
- **Allergens**: Fish.
- **Quality indicators**: Fish opaque and firm (from the acid marinade) but not mushy from over-marinating, balanced salt/sour/heat, crisp (not harsh) onion.
- **Holding/storage**: Serve immediately after dressing; the tiger's milk base can be made up to 4 hours ahead and refrigerated separately.
- **Scaling notes**: Scaled 4x from the source's 4-serving yield.
- **Skill focus**: Building a "leche de tigre" (tiger's milk) marinade, acid-marination technique and timing, tempering raw onion in ice water, balancing salt/acid/heat by taste.

## Panamanian-Style Ceviche

- **Course/use case**: Culinary 2, Seafood unit - International/raw preparation technique.
- **Source and revision date**: `14 - Seafood/Seafood/Panamanian style Ceviche recipe.docx` (source: recetasgratis.net, translated from Spanish - some ingredient names are approximate translations, noted below). Standardized 2026-07.
- **Yield and portion size**: Not specified in source. Scaled 2x below (1 kg each of shrimp, fish, and clams). **Needs confirmation** on actual appetizer-portion count from this batch size.
- **Equipment**: Glass or non-reactive container, wooden or plastic spatula (source specifies non-metal, likely to avoid a metallic taste/reaction with the acid).
- **Ingredients (2x batch)**: 1 kg shrimp (deveined, cleaned), 1 kg fish (preferably sea bass or grouper), 1 kg fresh clams in their juice, 4 onions (finely chopped, large), 2 leaves/sprigs culantro (long-leaf coriander) or cilantro, minced, 2 habanero peppers (or "chombo ajà­" if available - a Panamanian habanero variety) to taste, minced, 2 tsp mustard, 2 cups lemon juice (about 14 lemons), a splash of vinegar to taste (source: "1 chorro" - an imprecise "pour" measurement, **needs confirmation** on exact quantity), 2 tsp paprika (optional - source notes the original cook, "Enrique," omits it), a pinch of salt to taste.
- **Procedure**:
  1. Combine the seafood in a glass or other non-reactive container; handle with a wooden or plastic utensil to keep the product fresh.
  2. Add the lemon juice and vinegar to the seafood.
  3. Add the onion, culantro/cilantro, mustard, minced chili, and salt.
  4. Stir; let stand at least 4 hours in the refrigerator. (Source note: this recipe improves resting overnight - taste again before serving and adjust spice/heat as needed, since the fish can absorb flavor and need re-seasoning.)
  5. Serve with plain or soda crackers.
- **Food safety controls**: Use seafood suitable for raw/marinated consumption from a verified supplier; keep refrigerated throughout the marination; the acid marinates/firms the seafood but doesn't replace food-safe sourcing.
- **Allergens**: Shellfish (shrimp, clams), fish, mustard.
- **Quality indicators**: Seafood firm and opaque from the citrus marinade, balanced heat and acidity, fresh clean seafood flavor (not "fishy," a sign of freshness).
- **Holding/storage**: Best after resting at least 4 hours, up to overnight, refrigerated; serve chilled.
- **Scaling notes**: Scaled 2x from the source (no stated original yield); confirm actual portion count in production.
- **Skill focus**: Multi-seafood ceviche technique (shrimp, fish, and clams together, vs. the single-fish approach in Don Ceviche), non-reactive equipment handling, working from a translated/imprecise source recipe and flagging what needs confirmation rather than guessing at ambiguous measurements.

## Dover Sole Meunière

- **Course/use case**: Culinary 2, Seafood unit - classical French technique.
- **Source and revision date**: `16 - Rice, Pasta, & Grains/Meuniere.pdf` (source: Food Network). Standardized 2026-07. Kept separate from Poisson Dugléré below - meunière (dredge, pan-brown, brown-butter-caper pan sauce) is a distinct classical technique from Dugléré's poach-and-veloute method, not a variant of the same dish.
- **Yield and portion size**: Source lists "Yield: 4 servings" at the top but the nutrition footer separately says "Serving Size: 1 of 8 servings" for the same recipe - an inconsistency in the source, not resolved by guessing; 4 servings (whole fish per source) is used as the base here. Scaled 4x below to ~16 servings (4 whole soles).
- **Equipment**: Shallow dish for dredging, sauté pan large enough for a whole fish, sheet tray fitted with a rack, oven.
- **Ingredients (4x batch)**: 4-8 cups quick-mixing flour (such as Wondra), kosher salt and black pepper, 4 whole Dover sole (1-2 lb each, peeled and skirts trimmed), 8 Tbsp clarified butter or canola oil, 12 oz high-fat French butter, 12 lemons (juiced), 8 Tbsp capers, 8 Tbsp fresh parsley (finely minced).
- **Procedure**:
  1. Preheat oven to 400°F.
  2. Season flour with salt and pepper in a shallow dish. Dredge each fish lightly; set aside.
  3. Heat clarified butter in a sauté pan large enough for a whole fish, over medium-high heat, until it just begins to lightly smoke.
  4. Place the fish in the pan; cook without moving until browned, about 4 minutes. Turn and repeat on the other side.
  5. Transfer to the oven on a rack-fitted sheet tray; cook until tender and cooked through at the thickest part of the spine, 8-10 minutes.
  6. In the same pan, heat the high-fat butter until it begins to lightly brown. Add lemon juice to stop the browning. Add capers; finish with parsley. Taste and season with pepper only (not salt, due to the capers' saltiness).
  7. Rest the fish briefly, then serve whole with the sauce poured over the top.
- **Food safety controls**: Standard seafood handling; confirm the fish is opaque and flakes at the thickest part.
- **Allergens**: Gluten, dairy, fish.
- **Quality indicators**: Golden, evenly browned exterior, moist and tender flesh, nutty brown-butter sauce (not burnt) with bright lemon-caper balance.
- **Holding/storage**: Best fresh; the brown butter sauce doesn't hold or reheat well.
- **Scaling notes**: Scaled 4x from the source's 4-serving figure - see the yield discrepancy noted above before finalizing a production portion count.
- **Skill focus**: Classical meunière technique (dredge, pan-brown, deglaze into a brown-butter pan sauce), whole-fish handling, judging doneness in a large whole fish via a pan-to-oven finish.

## Poisson Dugléré (Fish in Tomato-Wine Velouté)

- **Course/use case**: Culinary 2, Seafood unit - classical French technique.
- **Source and revision date**: `20 - Nutritional Cooking/From TTC/Sole Duglere.docx`. Standardized 2026-07. Uses a fish velouté as a component - the Fish Fumet in this same file can serve as the stock base for building that velouté (fumet plus a light roux), if a from-scratch velouté isn't already on hand.
- **Yield and portion size**: Yield 10 (source yield; already within the program's target range). Scaled 2x below to ~20 portions.
- **Equipment**: Shallow poaching pan or dish, oven, sauce pan.
- **Ingredients (2x batch)**: 20 fish portions (4-6 oz each), 4 fl oz fish velouté, 2 oz onion (finely minced), 2 oz shallots (finely minced), 1 tsp salt, 1/4 tsp white pepper (or less), 8 fl oz white wine, 2 oz butter, 1/2 tsp lemon juice, 8 oz tomato fillet (small dice, i.e. peeled/seeded tomato concassé), 2 Tbsp parsley (finely chopped).
- **Procedure**:
  1. Combine onions, shallots, salt, white pepper, white wine, and fish in a pan; shallow-poach in the oven.
  2. Drain off the cooking liquid and reduce by half.
  3. Keep the fish covered and warm.
  4. Combine the fish velouté with the reduced poaching liquid; finish the sauce with butter and a few drops of lemon juice.
  5. Right before service, add the diced tomato and chopped parsley to the sauce; toss gently and adjust thickness if needed.
  6. Nappe (coat) the fish with the finished sauce.
- **Food safety controls**: Standard seafood handling; hold poached fish covered and warm, not at room temperature, until sauced and served.
- **Allergens**: Fish, dairy; contains alcohol (white wine, cooked down).
- **Quality indicators**: Fish moist and just cooked through, sauce glossy and nappe-consistency (coats the back of a spoon), tomato added late enough to stay bright rather than breaking down into the sauce.
- **Holding/storage**: Best fresh, sauced just before service.
- **Scaling notes**: Scaled 2x from the source's yield of 10.
- **Skill focus**: Shallow-poaching technique, building a velouté-based sauce from a reduced poaching liquid, timing a delicate garnish (fresh tomato and herbs) so it doesn't overcook.

## Chile Shrimp with Butter Beans and Lemony Couscous

- **Course/use case**: Culinary 2, Seafood unit - composed plate/grain pairing.
- **Source and revision date**: `16 - Rice, Pasta, & Grains/Pasta, Potatoes & Grains/couscous with shrimp.pdf` (source: Food & Wine). Standardized 2026-07.
- **Yield and portion size**: Serves 4 (source yield). Scaled 4x below to ~16 portions.
- **Equipment**: Heatproof bowl with lid (for steaming couscous), nonstick skillet.
- **Ingredients (4x batch)**: 2.67 cups couscous, 12 Tbsp extra-virgin olive oil (divided), 4 lb shelled and deveined medium shrimp, 2 tsp crushed red pepper, 8 Tbsp unsalted butter, 4 (15 oz) cans butter beans (rinsed and drained), 8 Tbsp capers, 6 Tbsp fresh lemon juice, 4 Tbsp chopped parsley.
- **Procedure**:
  1. Stir couscous with 3 cups boiling water in a bowl; cover and steam 5 minutes. Fluff with a fork.
  2. Meanwhile, heat 2/3 of the olive oil in a nonstick skillet. Add shrimp and crushed red pepper; cook over moderately high heat until golden, 2-3 minutes. Transfer to a plate.
  3. Add butter to the skillet. Add the beans, capers, and lemon juice; cook, stirring, 2 minutes.
  4. Fold the bean mixture, parsley, and remaining olive oil into the couscous; season with salt and pepper.
  5. Serve topped with the shrimp.
- **Food safety controls**: Standard shellfish handling; confirm shrimp are opaque throughout.
- **Allergens**: Shellfish, dairy, gluten (couscous is a wheat product).
- **Quality indicators**: Shrimp golden and just cooked through, couscous fluffy (not gummy), bright lemon-caper flavor balance.
- **Holding/storage**: Best fresh; couscous can be held briefly covered to stay warm.
- **Scaling notes**: Scaled 4x from the source's 4-serving yield.
- **Skill focus**: Quick couscous steaming technique, sequencing a quick-cooking protein with a pan sauce/bean ragout, building a composed one-bowl plate.

## Lowcountry Shrimp and Grits

- **Course/use case**: Culinary 1/2, Seafood unit - Lowcountry regional technique.
- **Source and revision date**: `6 - Breakfast/Breakfast/shrimp and grits.docx`, standardized 2026-07.
- **Yield and portion size**: Not specified in source. Scaled 4x below. **Needs confirmation** on actual portion count achieved.
- **Equipment**: Saucepan (for grits), sauté pan (for shrimp).
- **Ingredients (4x batch)**: Grits: 12 cups water, 4 cups cream, 16 Tbsp butter, 4 cups grits, salt and pepper to taste. Shrimp: 8 Tbsp butter, 8 Tbsp flour, 4 onions (diced), 4 stalks celery (diced), 4 bell peppers (diced), 8 cloves garlic (minced), 8 cups broth, 4 lb shrimp, seasoning to taste.
- **Procedure**:
  1. Boil water, cream, and butter together.
  2. Add grits, whisking until smooth.
  3. Season to taste and adjust liquid consistency as needed.
  4. For the shrimp: melt butter and whisk in flour to make a roux.
  5. Add the diced vegetables and sweat until tender.
  6. Add broth; cook until saucy.
  7. Add shrimp; poach lightly until just opaque.
  8. Season and serve over the grits.
- **Food safety controls**: Standard shellfish/dairy handling; confirm shrimp are opaque throughout.
- **Allergens**: Shellfish, dairy, gluten.
- **Quality indicators**: Smooth, creamy grits (no graininess), shrimp just cooked through (not rubbery), roux-based sauce with good body (coats the shrimp, not watery).
- **Holding/storage**: Best fresh; grits thicken as they cool and may need loosening with a splash of liquid if held.
- **Scaling notes**: Scaled 4x from the source (no stated original yield); confirm actual portion count in production.
- **Skill focus**: Roux-building for a Lowcountry-style shrimp gravy, grits cookery (liquid ratio and whisking for smoothness), vegetable sweating (the "holy trinity" of onion/celery/pepper) as a flavor base.

## Thai Scallop and Mango

- **Course/use case**: Culinary 2, Seafood unit; composed appetizer/competition plate.
- **Source and revision date**: Condensed from two documents on the same dish: `14 - Seafood/Seafood/Scallop appetizer.docx` (gives the vinaigrette recipe) and `14 - Seafood/Seafood/Scallops.doc` (a formal recipe/food-cost worksheet, West Ashley High, Educator: Jason Wheless, with slightly refined quantities and two additional garnishes - chili oil and Thai chili pepper - not in the first version). Standardized 2026-07 by combining both, using the worksheet's quantities as primary.
- **Yield and portion size**: 2 portions at 2 oz per the worksheet source. Scaled 8x below to ~16 portions.
- **Equipment**: Small pot (for poaching), small pan (for toasting peanuts).
- **Ingredients (8x batch)**: 32 oz scallops, 32 oz coconut water, salt to taste, 4 oz carrot (fine julienne), 2 oz peanuts, 1/2 oz chipotle powder, 8 mangoes (sliced and cut into rounds), 8 Thai chili peppers (finely sliced), 1 oz chili oil, 1 bunch basil, 1 bunch cilantro. Vinaigrette: 8 tsp fish sauce, 8 tsp white vinegar, 8 tsp poaching water (reserved from the scallops), 4 tsp sugar in the raw, 8 cloves garlic, 8 tsp shallot, 4 tsp lime juice.
- **Procedure**:
  1. Poach the scallops in coconut water. Remove, season with salt, and chill in the vinaigrette (reserve some poaching liquid for the vinaigrette).
  2. Make the vinaigrette: combine fish sauce, vinegar, reserved poaching water, sugar, garlic, shallot, and lime juice.
  3. Toss the carrot julienne with the vinaigrette; set aside.
  4. Toast the peanuts with chipotle powder for a few minutes; cool.
  5. Slice the chilled scallops in half; season lightly with salt.
  6. Shingle the mango and scallops in a circle on the plate.
  7. Top with the vinaigrette, toasted peanuts, sliced Thai chili, dressed carrots, chili oil, basil, and cilantro.
- **Food safety controls**: Standard shellfish handling; confirm scallops are fully cooked through in the poaching step, then chilled promptly before plating.
- **Allergens**: Shellfish, fish (fish sauce), tree nut (peanuts - note: peanuts are a legume, but flag per standard allergen labeling as a peanut allergen).
- **Quality indicators**: Scallops tender (not rubbery from overcooking), bright and balanced sweet-sour-salty-spicy vinaigrette, attractive shingled presentation.
- **Holding/storage**: Best assembled just before service; scallops can be poached and chilled slightly ahead.
- **Scaling notes**: Scaled 8x from the worksheet's 2-portion yield.
- **Skill focus**: Poaching scallops in a flavored liquid (coconut water) rather than water or stock, building a balanced Southeast Asian-style vinaigrette, plate composition/shingling technique, combining a formal recipe/cost worksheet with its referenced sub-recipe (the same pattern as the Green Curry Duck entry in `Poultry.md`).
