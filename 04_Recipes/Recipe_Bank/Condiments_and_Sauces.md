## The Best Salsa in the Universe

- **Course/use case**: Bistro condiment (chips, tacos, general use).
- **Source and revision date**: Program recipe. Standardized 2026-07.
- **Yield and portion size**: 1 quart.
- **Equipment**: Sheet pan (for roasting), food processor or blender, saucepan.
- **Ingredients**: 2 lb fresh tomatoes, 2 cups crushed canned tomatoes, 0.5 large red onion (quartered), 1 red bell pepper, 2-3 canned jalapenos (seeded for milder heat), 3 cloves garlic (minced), 0.5 bunch fresh cilantro (stems and leaves), juice of 2 limes, 2 tsp kosher salt, 1 tsp black pepper, 1.5 tsp ground cumin, 1 tsp chili powder, 1 tsp oregano.
- **Procedure**:
  1. Toss tomatoes, onion, bell pepper, and garlic with a little salt; roast at 425F until lightly blistered, about 20 minutes (adds char and sweetness - skip and go raw for a brighter salsa if time is short).
  2. Add roasted vegetables, cilantro, lime juice, jalapenos, and seasonings to a food processor; pulse until mostly smooth but still textured. Add a splash of crushed tomatoes if it needs more body.
  3. Simmer in a pot 20-30 minutes to marry the flavors.
  4. Taste and adjust: more salt if flat, more lime if dull, more cilantro if you want it grassier, a splash of water if it feels heavy.
- **Food safety controls**: Standard produce handling; cool before refrigerating.
- **Allergens**: None inherent.
- **Quality indicators**: Balanced heat, acid, and salt; texture still has some chunk, not a puree.
- **Holding/storage**: Refrigerate in a covered container.
- **Scaling notes**: Scales linearly by the quart.
- **Skill focus**: Roasting for flavor development, balancing acid/heat/salt by taste, food processor texture control (pulsing vs. pureeing).

## Roasted Tomatillo Salsa (Salsa Verde)

- **Course/use case**: Bistro condiment (pairs with Smoked Carnitas Tacos in Lunch.md).
- **Source and revision date**: Adapted from an external source; standardized 2026-07.
- **Yield and portion size**: About 2 cups.
- **Equipment**: Baking sheet, blender or food processor.
- **Ingredients**: 6 medium tomatillos (husked and rinsed), 1 small white onion (quartered), 2 cloves garlic (peeled), 1-2 jalapenos (stemmed, seeds removed for less heat), 0.5 cup chopped fresh cilantro, 1 Tbsp fresh lime juice, 0.5 tsp salt, 1 Tbsp olive oil.
- **Procedure**:
  1. Preheat oven to 450F or set a broiler on high.
  2. Place tomatillos, onion, garlic, and jalapenos on a baking sheet; drizzle with olive oil.
  3. Roast 10-12 minutes, flipping halfway, until slightly charred.
  4. Transfer to a blender or food processor; add cilantro, lime juice, and salt.
  5. Pulse until smooth but still slightly chunky.
  6. Taste and adjust seasoning with more salt or lime as needed.
- **Food safety controls**: Standard produce handling.
- **Allergens**: None inherent.
- **Quality indicators**: Balanced tang from tomatillos and lime, visible slight char flavor, not over-blended.
- **Holding/storage**: Refrigerate in an airtight container up to 5 days.
- **Scaling notes**: Scales linearly by tomatillo count.
- **Skill focus**: Roasting/charring for flavor, blending to a controlled texture, tasting and adjusting acid/salt balance.

## Savory Onion Jam (Chicken + Mozzarella Focaccia)

- **Course/use case**: Bistro condiment (sandwich component, pairs with Focaccia in Lunch.md).
- **Source and revision date**: Program recipe, written in a student-friendly, batch-consistent style. Standardized 2026-07.
- **Yield and portion size**: About 2.5-3 cups (600-700 g), depending on reduction.
- **Equipment**: Wide saute pan or rondeau.
- **Ingredients**: 1.5 lb yellow onions (thinly sliced, 680 g), 2 Tbsp olive oil, 1 Tbsp unsalted butter, 1 tsp kosher salt, 1 Tbsp granulated sugar, 3 Tbsp white wine or champagne vinegar, 2 Tbsp water, 0.5 tsp black pepper, optional 1 tsp fresh thyme (finely chopped).
- **Procedure**:
  1. Heat olive oil and butter in a wide pan over medium-low heat.
  2. Add onions and salt; cook 30-40 minutes, stirring every few minutes, until fully soft and lightly golden - no aggressive browning; the goal is tenderness and natural sweetness, not deep caramel bitterness.
  3. Add sugar; cook 5 more minutes to build gentle sweetness without making it dessert-like.
  4. Add vinegar and water; stir and scrape the pan well. Reduce on low heat 15-20 minutes until jammy but still soft and spreadable.
  5. Stir in black pepper and thyme if using. Taste and adjust salt or add a splash more vinegar if it feels flat.
- **Food safety controls**: Standard produce handling; cool before refrigerating.
- **Allergens**: Dairy (butter).
- **Quality indicators**: Jammy, spreadable texture - not caramelized to a deep brown or bitter; balanced sweet/acid.
- **Holding/storage**: Refrigerate in a covered container.
- **Scaling notes**: Scales linearly by onion weight; the long, low cook time is the real constraint on batch timing, not the ratios.
- **Skill focus**: Low-and-slow sweating (distinct from caramelizing), deglazing and reduction, tasting for balance rather than following a fixed cook time.

## Preserved Cherry Reduction Sauce (for Duck)

- **Course/use case**: Bistro/plated-entree sauce; pairs with duck dishes in `Poultry.md` (e.g., Green Curry Duck, Grilled Duck Breasts with Cranberries use their own integrated sauces, but this stock-based reduction is a separate, more classical option for a duck entree).
- **Source and revision date**: `8 - Stocks & Sauces/PRESERVED CHERRY REDUCTION SAUCE FOR DUCK.docx`, standardized 2026-07.
- **Yield and portion size**: Not specified in source - it's written as a mother-sauce-style base with "as needed" quantities for several components. **Needs confirmation**: source itself notes "yield will vary, a reduction sauce is finished when flavors have developed and the proper balance is achieved," so an exact yield isn't really applicable without a fixed batch test.
- **Equipment**: Saucepan, fine strainer.
- **Ingredients**: 1 cup sliced shallots, 1 cup small-diced onions, 2 oz unsalted butter, 2 cups dried cherries (optional), 1.5 cups brandy, loose bouquet garni (as needed), 3 qts duck stock, duck glace (as needed - may be necessary to fortify the sauce), salt and black pepper to taste, arrowroot (as needed, if not finishing with butter), preserved cherries (as needed, quartered, for garnish/finish).
- **Procedure**:
  1. Sweat shallots and onions in butter.
  2. Add dried cherries, brandy, and bouquet garni; reduce au sec (nearly dry).
  3. Add wine and reduce by 3/4. *(Note: source lists "wine" in this step but doesn't include a wine quantity in the ingredient list - likely the brandy itself, or a wine addition dropped from the source. Confirm before production.)*
  4. Add duck stock; reduce until flavors have developed.
  5. Season; adjust consistency with arrowroot if not finishing with butter.
  6. Strain; add the quartered preserved cherries.
- **Food safety controls**: Standard stock/sauce handling; cool and refrigerate promptly if not used same-day.
- **Allergens**: Dairy (butter, if used to finish); contains alcohol (brandy, cooked down).
- **Quality indicators**: Glossy, well-balanced reduction; cherry sweetness balanced against the savory duck stock, not cloying.
- **Holding/storage**: Refrigerate; reheat gently before service.
- **Scaling notes**: **Needs confirmation** - source is written as a flexible base recipe rather than a fixed batch; establish a standard batch size in production.
- **Skill focus**: Classical reduction-sauce technique (sweat, deglaze/reduce, stock reduction, finish/strain), using dried and preserved fruit in a savory sauce, working from a "to taste"/"as needed" style professional recipe format.

## Basic Vinaigrette (Stock-Thickened)

- **Course/use case**: Bistro salad dressing - base technique.
- **Source and revision date**: `8 - Stocks & Sauces/BASIC VINAIGRETTE.docx`, standardized 2026-07.
- **Yield and portion size**: Not specified in source; roughly 45 calories per 2 oz serving per the source's own note.
- **Equipment**: Small saucepan, whisk.
- **Ingredients**: 8 oz vegetable stock or tomato broth, 1 tsp cornstarch, 2 cloves roasted garlic, 4 oz vinegar (any type - source notes most have similar acidity), 2 oz olive oil, salt and pepper to taste.
- **Procedure**:
  1. Mix cornstarch with 1 tsp cold water or stock to form a slurry; reserve.
  2. Bring the stock to a simmer.
  3. Whisk in the slurry; return to a simmer until thickened enough to coat the back of a spoon.
  4. Cool to room temperature.
  5. Add remaining ingredients; whisk hard to break up the garlic.
  6. Taste and adjust seasoning/consistency.
- **Food safety controls**: Standard stock handling; cool before combining with other ingredients.
- **Allergens**: Gluten (if using a wheat-based stock product - confirm); none inherent otherwise.
- **Quality indicators**: Stable, lightly thickened body (a lower-fat vinaigrette style, thickened by starch rather than heavy emulsification), balanced acidity.
- **Holding/storage**: Refrigerate; a versatile base for herb/flavor variations.
- **Scaling notes**: Scales linearly; fat content (and calories) can be reduced further by cutting back the olive oil.
- **Skill focus**: Starch-thickening a vinaigrette base (an alternative to oil-heavy emulsification), building a base recipe designed for variation (herbs, other flavorings).

## Bruschetta in a Jar

- **Course/use case**: Bistro condiment/canned preserve.
- **Source and revision date**: `8 - Stocks & Sauces/BRUSCHETTA IN A JAR.docx`, standardized 2026-07.
- **Yield and portion size**: About 2 (8 oz) jars.
- **Equipment**: Boiling water canner, 2 half-pint glass preserving jars with lids and bands.
- **Ingredients**: 3 cloves garlic (minced), 1/4 cup dry white wine, 1/4 cup white wine vinegar, 1/8 cup water, 1 Tbsp sugar, 2 Tbsp basil, 2 Tbsp oregano, 1 Tbsp balsamic vinegar, 3.5 cups chopped cored plum tomatoes.
- **Procedure**:
  1. Set up a boiling water canner; heat jars and lids in simmering (not boiling) water until ready to use. Set bands aside.
  2. Combine garlic, wine, wine vinegar, water, sugar, basil, oregano, and balsamic vinegar. Bring to a full rolling boil, stirring occasionally. Reduce heat, cover, and simmer about 5 minutes.
  3. Pack tomatoes into hot jars, leaving 1/2" headspace.
  4. Ladle the hot vinegar mixture over the tomatoes, leaving 1/2" headspace.
  5. Remove air bubbles, wipe rims, center lids, and apply bands fingertip-tight.
  6. Process in a boiling water canner 20 minutes, adjusting for altitude.
  7. Remove and cool; check the seal after 24 hours (lid should not flex when pressed).
- **Food safety controls**: Proper home-canning/water-bath process is essential here - correct headspace, processing time, and altitude adjustment all affect seal safety; verify seal before storing at room temperature, and discard any jar that doesn't seal properly.
- **Allergens**: None inherent to this recipe.
- **Quality indicators**: Properly sealed lid (doesn't flex), bright acidic tomato-herb flavor.
- **Holding/storage**: Shelf-stable once properly sealed and processed; refrigerate after opening.
- **Scaling notes**: Scales linearly by jar count; canner capacity may limit batch size per run.
- **Skill focus**: Water-bath canning technique, headspace and seal-testing practice, balancing an acidified tomato preserve for safe shelf storage.

## Coulis de Tomate

- **Course/use case**: Bistro sauce - classical technique.
- **Source and revision date**: `8 - Stocks & Sauces/COULIS DE TOMATE.docx`, standardized 2026-07. Kept separate from the Mother Sauce Tomato Sauce in `Soups_and_Stocks.md` - this is a smooth, blended coulis built with classical French technique terms (émonder/concasser), a different result (silky purée vs. a rustic simmered sauce) from that recipe.
- **Yield and portion size**: Not specified in source.
- **Equipment**: Blender or immersion blender, appropriately sized covered cooking vessel.
- **Ingredients**: 1 lb tomatoes, 1/2 cup carrot (small dice), 1/2 cup onion (small dice), 2 cloves garlic, 1/4 cup white wine, vegetable stock as needed (tomato consommé - see `Soups_and_Stocks.md` - may substitute).
- **Procedure**:
  1. Blanch and peel (émonder) the tomatoes; roughly chop (concasser).
  2. Sweat carrots, onions, and garlic until soft.
  3. Increase heat to brown slightly.
  4. Add tomatoes; cook 2 minutes.
  5. Deglaze with wine.
  6. Add stock just to cover.
  7. Cook covered, on the stovetop or in the oven, on low heat, until vegetables are tender.
  8. Purée until very smooth, adding more stock as needed for consistency.
  9. Return to the stove; simmer, adjust consistency, and season.
- **Food safety controls**: Standard produce handling.
- **Allergens**: None inherent to this recipe (contains alcohol from the wine, cooked down).
- **Quality indicators**: Silky, fully smooth purée (no fibrous texture), balanced consistency (not too thin - source notes an oversized cooking vessel requiring too much stock will make it watery).
- **Holding/storage**: Refrigerate or freeze.
- **Scaling notes**: Scales by weight; match pot size to batch size to keep the coulis from becoming too thin.
- **Skill focus**: Classical tomato-peeling and dicing technique (émonder/concasser), building a smooth coulis by puréeing rather than leaving it rustic, vessel-sizing for proper consistency.

## Grilled Romaine "Vinaigrette"

- **Course/use case**: Bistro salad dressing - unconventional technique.
- **Source and revision date**: `8 - Stocks & Sauces/GRILLED ROMAINE "VINAIGRETTE".docx`, standardized 2026-07.
- **Yield and portion size**: Not specified in source.
- **Equipment**: Grill, shocking station (ice bath), high-powered blender (e.g., Vitamix).
- **Ingredients**: 1 head romaine, 1/2 bunch parsley (blanched and shocked), 6 oz spinach (blanched and shocked), 3 oz apple cider vinegar, salt and white pepper to taste.
- **Procedure**:
  1. Bring a pot of well-salted water to a boil ("tastes like the ocean"); set up a shocking (ice water) station.
  2. Trim bad leaves from the romaine; slice lengthwise.
  3. Brush with oil, season, and grill until wilted with good grill marks.
  4. Blanch the parsley and spinach in the boiling water; shock in ice water.
  5. Blend all the greens (grilled romaine, blanched parsley, blanched spinach) until smooth, like butter.
  6. Season with vinegar and salt to taste.
- **Food safety controls**: Standard produce handling.
- **Allergens**: None inherent to this recipe.
- **Quality indicators**: Completely smooth, vivid green color (a sign of proper blanch-and-shock to preserve color), balanced acid.
- **Holding/storage**: Refrigerate in a covered container; color fades with time, so use promptly.
- **Scaling notes**: Scales by weight; other lettuces/greens may be substituted per the source's own note.
- **Skill focus**: Grilling a lettuce for a smoky base flavor, blanch-and-shock technique for color retention, turning cooked greens into a smooth "vinaigrette" purée rather than a classic oil-based dressing.

## Ranch Dressing

- **Course/use case**: Bistro dip/dressing; also referenced as a Buffalo Wings accompaniment in `Poultry.md`.
- **Source and revision date**: `8 - Stocks & Sauces/Homemade Ranch Dressing.docx`, standardized 2026-07. Kept as its own entry here (rather than only living inside the Buffalo Wings sauce list) since it's a genuinely standalone condiment; the version bundled into the Buffalo Wings entry uses real buttermilk, while this one builds a DIY soured-milk substitute - a minor technique variance, noted below rather than treated as a separate dish.
- **Yield and portion size**: Not specified in source; a small single-batch dressing.
- **Equipment**: Wide-mouth bowl, fork.
- **Ingredients**: 1/2 cup organic whole milk, 1 Tbsp raw apple cider vinegar, 2 cloves garlic, 1/2 tsp salt, 1 Tbsp fresh chopped parsley, 1 tsp fresh chopped chives, 2 Tbsp mayonnaise, 2 Tbsp sour cream, black pepper to taste.
- **Procedure**:
  1. Pour vinegar into the milk; let sit to sour (a DIY buttermilk substitute).
  2. In a separate bowl, mash garlic and salt together with a fork until it forms a paste.
  3. Add chopped herbs, mayonnaise, sour cream, and black pepper.
  4. Blend in the soured milk; combine well.
- **Food safety controls**: Standard dairy handling.
- **Allergens**: Dairy, egg (mayonnaise).
- **Quality indicators**: Creamy consistency (source notes the original recipe's 1 cup milk was corrected down to 1/2 cup after producing too thin a result), balanced garlic and herb flavor.
- **Holding/storage**: Best served immediately; refrigerated leftovers keep up to a week.
- **Scaling notes**: Scales linearly.
- **Skill focus**: Building a soured-milk buttermilk substitute, garlic-paste technique (mashing with salt rather than mincing, for smoother distribution), balancing a creamy dressing base.

## Lemon Caper Brown Butter Sauce

- **Ingredients**: 8 oz whole capers, 1/4 oz chopped parsley, 1/8 oz chopped tarragon, 16 oz butter, 4 oz lemon juice.

## Light Lemon Vinaigrette

- **Quality indicators**: Bright green flecks of parsley (fades with prolonged acid exposure, so best used fresh), stable temporary emulsion (shake before use if it sits).
- **Holding/storage**: Best used within an hour of adding the parsley/shallot; hold those additions back if the dressing won't be used right away.
- **Scaling notes**: Scales linearly; a stick processor can substitute for a full food processor.
- **Skill focus**: Broth-thickening technique for a lower-fat vinaigrette (a direct application of the Basic Vinaigrette technique above), timing delicate ingredient additions to preserve color and flavor.

## Orange Chili Marmalade

- **Course/use case**: Bistro condiment/canned preserve.
- **Source and revision date**: `8 - Stocks & Sauces/Orange Chili Marmalade.docx`, standardized 2026-07.
- **Yield and portion size**: 2 (8 oz) jars.
- **Equipment**: Boiling water canner, deep stainless steel saucepan, 2 half-pint glass preserving jars with lids and bands.
- **Ingredients**: 9 oz oranges (unpeeled, seeded, thinly sliced), grated zest and juice of 1 lemon, 1.5 cups water, 2 dried habanero chili peppers, 2.25 cups granulated sugar.
- **Procedure**:
  1. Prepare a boiling water canner; heat jars and lids in simmering (not boiling) water. Set bands aside.
  2. Combine oranges, lemon zest/juice, and water in a saucepan; bring to a boil over high heat, stirring constantly.
  3. Reduce heat; boil gently, stirring occasionally.
  4. Add chili peppers; partially cover and boil gently until the fruit is very soft. Remove and discard the chilies.
  5. Maintaining a boil, gradually stir in sugar. Boil hard, stirring occasionally, until the mixture reaches gel stage, about 15 minutes. Test the gel; skim off foam.
  6. Ladle hot marmalade into hot jars, leaving 1/4" headspace. Wipe rims, apply lids and bands fingertip-tight.
  7. Process in a boiling water canner 10 minutes, adjusting for altitude. Cool; check seals after 24 hours.
- **Food safety controls**: Sanitize jars even if new/unused; always fill to the recommended headspace to reduce oxidation risk; verify the seal before storing at room temperature.

## Southern-Style Chili Crunch with Szechuan Peppercorns and Benne Seeds

- **Course/use case**: Bistro condiment - fusion flavor profile.
- **Source and revision date**: `9 - Flavor Profiles & Recipe Development/Southern-Style Chili Crunch with Szechuan Peppercorns and Benne Seeds.docx`, standardized 2026-07.
- **Yield and portion size**: Not specified in source; keeps refrigerated up to 1 month per the source's own storage note.
- **Equipment**: Dry skillet, medium saucepan, slotted spoon, heatproof bowl, sterilized storage jars.
- **Ingredients**: 1 cup grapeseed oil (or other neutral oil), 1/2 cup roasted peanuts (coarsely chopped), 1/4 cup benne seeds (or sesame seeds, toasted), 2 medium Vidalia onions (thinly sliced), 6 cloves garlic (thinly sliced), 3 dried puya chilies (crushed), 3 dried árbol chilies (crushed), 3 dried japones chilies (crushed), 2 tsp Szechuan peppercorns (toasted, coarsely crushed), 1 tsp smoked paprika, 1 tsp cayenne pepper (optional), 2 Tbsp brown sugar or sorghum syrup, 1 tsp apple cider vinegar, 1 tsp sea salt, 1 tsp mustard seeds (toasted), 1 tsp dried thyme.
- **Procedure**:
  1. Toast the benne seeds and mustard seeds in a dry skillet over medium heat until aromatic and lightly golden. Set aside.
  2. Heat the grapeseed oil in a saucepan over low-medium heat. Add sliced onions and garlic; cook, stirring frequently, 15-20 minutes, until golden brown and crispy. Remove with a slotted spoon; drain on a paper towel.
  3. In the same oil, add the Szechuan peppercorns; toast 1-2 minutes until fragrant - careful not to burn.
  4. Add the crushed chilies, smoked paprika, and cayenne (if using) to the hot oil; stir 30-60 seconds to bloom the spices.
  5. Turn off heat; stir in brown sugar/sorghum syrup, vinegar, and salt until dissolved.
  6. In a large heatproof bowl, combine the crispy onions, garlic, toasted benne seeds, peanuts, mustard seeds, and thyme. Pour the spiced oil over; stir to coat evenly.
  7. Cool completely; transfer to sterilized jars. Refrigerate up to 1 month. Stir before use, as the oil may separate.
- **Food safety controls**: Standard produce/nut handling; cool completely before jarring to avoid condensation/spoilage risk; use sterilized jars.
- **Allergens**: Peanut, sesame (benne seeds), soy (if a soy-based mustard seed product is substituted - unlikely here but confirm), mustard.
- **Quality indicators**: Crispy (not soggy) onion and garlic bits, fragrant toasted spices without a burnt taste, good balance of crunch and heat.
- **Holding/storage**: Refrigerate up to 1 month in sterilized jars; oil may separate on standing - stir before use.
- **Scaling notes**: Scales linearly; toast spices/seeds in batches if scaling up significantly, to avoid uneven toasting.
- **Skill focus**: Frying aromatics to a crisp without burning, blooming spices in hot oil, building a textured condiment (as opposed to a smooth sauce), balancing a Southern flavor base against a Szechuan-numbing element.
