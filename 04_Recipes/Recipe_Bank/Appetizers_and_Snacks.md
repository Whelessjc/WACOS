## Pimento Cheese

- **Course/use case**: Culinary 1/2, Appetizers unit - Southern cheese spread/dip.
- **Source and revision date**: Cookieandkate.com, "Pimento Cheese." Standardized 2026-07.
- **Yield and portion size**: Serves 2 (source yield, about 1/4 cup servings); a small-batch condiment/appetizer yield, left as written.
- **Equipment**: Stand mixer or hand mixer, mixing bowl.
- **Ingredients**: 2 cups freshly grated extra-sharp cheddar cheese, 8 oz cream cheese (cut into 1" cubes, softened at room temperature), 4 oz pimento peppers (well drained, chopped if in strips), 2 Tbsp mayonnaise (Duke's traditional, per source), 1/4 tsp garlic powder, 1/4 tsp onion powder, 1/4 tsp ground cayenne pepper (decrease or omit if sensitive to spice), 1/2 medium jalapeño (ribs and seeds removed, minced, about 1 Tbsp - omit if sensitive to spice), freshly ground black pepper to taste, salt to taste.
- **Procedure**: Combine the cheddar, cream cheese, pimento peppers, mayonnaise, garlic powder, onion powder, cayenne, and jalapeño (if using) in a large mixing bowl or stand mixer bowl, with several twists of black pepper. Beat together with a hand mixer, stand mixer, or by hand with a sturdy wooden spoon/spatula until thoroughly combined. Taste and add more black pepper and/or salt as desired (add more cayenne for extra heat). Transfer to a serving bowl; serve immediately or chill up to 1 week.
- **Food safety controls**: Standard dairy handling.
- **Allergens**: Dairy, egg (mayonnaise).
- **Quality indicators**: Smooth, well-combined spread (not streaky), balanced sharp-cheddar and pimento flavor with a gentle kick if jalapeño/cayenne are used.
- **Holding/storage**: Refrigerate airtight up to 1 week; the spread hardens when cold - let rest 30 minutes at room temperature to soften before serving.
- **Scaling notes**: Source yield of 2 servings is a small-batch condiment yield; scales linearly by ingredient ratio for a larger batch.
- **Skill focus**: Building a smooth cold cheese spread by full incorporation (mixer or hand-beating) rather than a rough fold, balancing cream cheese richness against sharp cheddar and pimento acidity.

## Spinach Artichoke Dip

- **Course/use case**: Culinary 1/2, Appetizers unit - hot baked cheese dip.
- **Source and revision date**: Program recipe. Standardized 2026-07.
- **Yield and portion size**: Not specified in source. **Needs confirmation** for a target portion count.
- **Equipment**: Oven, mixing bowl, baking dish.
- **Ingredients**: 1 (13.75 oz) can artichoke hearts, 24 oz cream cheese (softened), 1/4 cup mayonnaise, 2 Tbsp garlic, 1 tsp kosher salt, 1/2 tsp pepper, 1 cup cheddar cheese, 1 cup Parmesan cheese, 24 oz cooked, chopped spinach.
- **Procedure**: Roast the artichoke hearts at 400°F for 10 minutes. Blend the cream cheese, mayonnaise, garlic, salt, pepper, cheddar, and Parmesan. Chop the roasted artichoke hearts and blend them in. Lightly combine in the cooked spinach. When ready to cook, add the remaining cheddar and Parmesan on top and bake at 425°F until golden and bubbly.
- **Food safety controls**: Standard dairy handling; confirm spinach is fully cooked and squeezed of excess moisture before combining.
- **Allergens**: Dairy, egg (mayonnaise).
- **Quality indicators**: Smooth, well-blended base (not watery from under-drained spinach/artichokes), golden bubbling cheese top.
- **Holding/storage**: Refrigerate before baking; best served hot, fresh from the oven.
- **Scaling notes**: No yield stated in source - needs confirmation on a target portion count; scales linearly by ingredient ratio and baking-dish size.
- **Skill focus**: Roasting artichoke hearts for concentrated flavor before blending, properly draining/squeezing high-moisture vegetables (spinach, artichoke) to avoid a watery dip.
