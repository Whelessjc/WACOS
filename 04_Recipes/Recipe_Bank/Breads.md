## Baguette

- **Course/use case**: Culinary 2, Baking - Lean Dough unit; Bistro bread service.
- **Source and revision date**: Standardized 2026-07 from two sources: the program's own "Artisan Bread I" board notes (`4 - Baking - Lean Dough` / `17 - Advanced Baking & Pastry` folders - the whiteboard photo and the transcribed board-notes PDF are the same formula, duplicated) and French Baguettes, Gozney (us.gozney.com/blogs/recipes/french-baguettes-recipe). Both use the same lean-dough technique (~70% hydration, mix-fold-shape-steam-bake) with only minor timing differences, so they're condensed into one entry; procedure follows Gozney's more complete shaping/scoring/baking detail, ingredient ratios cross-checked against the program's own board formula.
- **Yield and portion size**: Scaled 2x from the Gozney source to 16 baguettes (~215 g raw dough each). *Note: the Gozney source itself says "cut the dough into four pieces (about 215g/7.5 Oz each)," which doesn't reconcile with its own total dough weight (1,740 g per batch, which is ~8 pieces at 215 g) - the 215 g per-piece figure was used for scaling math here rather than the "four pieces" count, which appears to be a source error.*
- **Equipment**: Stand mixer with dough hook, couche or floured kitchen towel, baking stone/steel or sheet pan, steam source (water pan or steam injector), razor or sharp knife for scoring.
- **Ingredients**: 2000 g strong bread flour, 40 g fresh yeast (or 20 g instant/dried), 1400 g water, 40 g sea salt.
- **Procedure**:
  1. Add water to the mixer bowl, then flour and yeast. Mix on the slowest speed for 4 minutes, adding the salt halfway through.
  2. Lift the dough out and spin it over itself into a ball. Rest 1 hour at room temperature, then refrigerate overnight.
  3. Turn dough onto a floured surface. Divide into 16 pieces (~215 g each). Round each piece with thumb/fingers; rest 5 minutes.
  4. Shape each piece into a baguette: flatten into an oval, fold in thirds, roll and extend to the length of the couche or baking tray.
  5. Lay shaped baguettes on a floured couche or towel, pleating fabric between them so they don't touch. Cover and proof 45-60 minutes until visibly puffy.
  6. Transfer to a floured peel or upturned sheet pan. Score 3 diagonal cuts per loaf with a razor or sharp knife.
  7. Add water to a steam injector or a water pan in the oven just before baking.
  8. Bake at 240°C/464°F (or 425-480°F per the program's own board notes) for 12-20 minutes until deep golden and crusty. Don't open the oven for the first 4-5 minutes.
  9. Cool on a rack before serving.
- **Food safety controls**: Standard yeast/gluten handling; refrigerate bulk dough promptly during the overnight rest.
- **Allergens**: Gluten.
- **Quality indicators**: Deep golden, blistered crust; open, irregular crumb; hollow sound when tapped on the bottom.
- **Holding/storage**: Best same-day; store at room temperature loosely wrapped (plastic bags soften the crust). Not recommended for refrigeration.
- **Scaling notes**: Scaled 2x from the original 1,000 g flour source recipe to reach 16 loaves. Scales cleanly by weight (baker's %: flour 100%, water 70%, yeast 2%, salt 2%).
- **Skill focus**: Lean-dough hydration and gluten development, bulk fermentation timing, shaping technique, scoring, steam-baking for crust development.

## Ciabatta with Biga

- **Course/use case**: Culinary 2, Baking - Lean Dough / Advanced Baking & Pastry unit.
- **Source and revision date**: Program "Artisan Bread I" board notes (`17 - Advanced Baking & Pastry/artisanBread.boardNotes.pdf`), standardized 2026-07. *This is a shorthand classroom board formula, not a full written recipe - it gives ingredient weights and a step outline (biga, mix, stretch & fold, divide, garnish, proof, bake) but no detailed mixing speeds, biga composition, or shaping instructions. Flagged below rather than filled in with assumptions.*
- **Yield and portion size**: Source batch yields ~2.2 lb finished dough (roughly 2 standard ciabatta loaves). **Needs confirmation**: scaling to the program's 16-30 portion convention wasn't specified in the source - recommend batch-testing at a 4-6x multiplier (forming ~350-400 g rolls) and recording the actual portion count/weight achieved.
- **Equipment**: Stand mixer with dough hook, couche or floured proofing cloth, baking stone/steel, steam source.
- **Ingredients (as sourced, ~1 kg batch)**: 454 g bread flour, 346 g water, 11 g salt, 8.5 g instant yeast, 181 g biga (pre-ferment; composition/ratio not specified in source - **needs confirmation**).
- **Procedure**:
  1. Prepare the biga 12 hours ahead (composition not specified in source - use a standard biga ratio, e.g., equal parts flour and water by weight with a pinch of yeast, and confirm against program practice).
  2. Mix flour, water, salt, yeast, and biga for about 6 minutes.
  3. Stretch and fold at 45-minute intervals, twice.
  4. Divide, garnish (per source notation - likely a flour/semolina dusting; not detailed), and proof about 45 minutes.
  5. Bake at 480°F for 20+ minutes with steam, until deeply golden.
- **Food safety controls**: Standard yeast/gluten handling; biga should be made and held under refrigeration if prepared more than a few hours ahead.
- **Allergens**: Gluten.
- **Quality indicators**: Open, irregular holey crumb; crisp crust; good oven spring.
- **Holding/storage**: Best same-day; store at room temperature.
- **Scaling notes**: See Yield note above - source batch is small and scaling factor to reach program portion targets needs to be established in production.
- **Skill focus**: Pre-ferment (biga) use, high-hydration dough handling, stretch-and-fold technique, steam-baking.

## Challah

- **Equipment**: Stand mixer with dough hook, sheet pan, pastry brush for egg wash.
- **Ingredients (as sourced, ~1 kg batch)**: 366 g bread flour, 181 g high-gluten flour, 43 g sugar, 43 g egg yolks, 77 g whole eggs, 43 g oil, 176 g water, 11 g salt, 17 g yeast.
- **Procedure**:
  1. Mix all ingredients about 6 minutes to a smooth dough.
  2. Bulk ferment (time not specified in source - standard practice is 1-1.5 hours until doubled).
  3. Divide and shape (braiding method not detailed in source - confirm program's preferred braid, e.g., 3-strand or 6-strand).
  4. Proof and egg-wash, about 45 minutes.
  5. Bake at 380°F for 20+ minutes until deep golden and internal temperature reaches ~190°F.
- **Food safety controls**: Standard egg/yeast/gluten handling.
- **Allergens**: Gluten, egg.
- **Quality indicators**: Deep golden, glossy egg-washed crust; soft, rich, slightly sweet crumb; even braid definition.
- **Holding/storage**: Best same-day to next-day; store wrapped at room temperature.
- **Scaling notes**: See Yield note above - source batch is small and scaling factor needs to be established in production.

## Sourdough Bread (Bread Flour, Hearth-Baked)

- **Course/use case**: Culinary 2, Baking - Lean Dough unit; Bistro bread.
- **Source and revision date**: `Sourdough.docx` (root of archive), standardized 2026-07.
- **Yield and portion size**: One large hearth loaf per batch. Scaled 3x below to reach ~3 loaves (~16-18 portions if sliced 6-8 ways per loaf - **needs confirmation** on actual slice count used in production).
- **Equipment**: Bench scraper, banneton/floured bread basket, Dutch oven or hotel pan of water for steam, pizza stone.
- **Ingredients (3x batch)**: 1200 g sourdough starter, 2400 g warm water (100°F), 3900 g AP or bread flour, 105 g salt.
- **Procedure**:
  1. Mix starter, water, and flour until a smooth, wet dough forms. Rest covered 30 minutes.
  2. Add salt and stretch-and-fold the dough. Rest covered 30 minutes.
  3. Stretch and fold five more times at 30-minute intervals.
  4. Transfer to an oiled container; cold-ferment in the fridge overnight.
  5. The next day, divide and shape into loaves. Rest in floured bread baskets, covered, 2-3 hours.
  6. Heat oven to 425°F, low fan, with a hotel pan of water on the bottom rack for steam.
  7. Flip loaves onto a pizza stone and bake until internal temperature reaches 210°F (10-15 minutes; larger scaled loaves may need longer - confirm by temperature, not time).
- **Food safety controls**: Standard yeast/gluten handling; confirm doneness by internal temperature.
- **Allergens**: Gluten.
- **Quality indicators**: Blistered, deeply colored crust; open, irregular crumb; distinct sour tang.
- **Holding/storage**: Best same-day to next-day; store cut-side down at room temperature, not refrigerated (accelerates staling).
- **Scaling notes**: Scaled 3x from the 1-loaf source recipe. Scales by weight; starter should be active/recently fed before mixing.
- **Skill focus**: Wild-yeast leavening, stretch-and-fold gluten development, cold fermentation timing, hearth/steam baking.

## Sourdough Bread (Whole Wheat Levain, Dutch Oven)

- **Course/use case**: Culinary 2, Baking - Lean Dough unit.
- **Source and revision date**: `4 - Baking - Lean Dough/Sourdough.pdf` ("Basic sourdough," source credited as "Jaxler12" in the original file). Standardized 2026-07. **Kept separate from the Bread-Flour Sourdough above** - this uses a whole-wheat flour blend, a different hydration ratio, and a covered-Dutch-oven bake instead of an open hearth/steam-pan bake, a genuinely different technique and crust result, not a minor variant.
- **Yield and portion size**: One loaf. Scaled 3x below to ~3 loaves. **Needs confirmation** on portions-per-loaf for production counts.
- **Equipment**: Dutch oven (must be large enough to cover the loaf), bench scraper, banneton.
- **Ingredients (3x batch)**: 975 g bread flour, 225 g whole wheat flour, 900 g water, 210 g levain, 21 g sea salt.
- **Procedure**:
  1. Combine flours, water, and levain; mix until well incorporated.
  2. Autolyse 30 minutes.
  3. Mix in salt thoroughly; rest 30 minutes.
  4. Perform 6 stretch-and-folds, spaced 30-45 minutes apart.
  5. Rest 30 minutes after the last fold.
  6. Shape and refrigerate 12 hours.
  7. Preheat oven with a covered Dutch oven inside to a constant 425°F (218°C).
  8. Score the dough, place in the preheated Dutch oven, cover, and bake 30 minutes.
  9. Uncover and continue baking at 425°F for another 30 minutes, until deeply colored.
  10. Cool completely before slicing.
- **Food safety controls**: Standard yeast/gluten handling; handle the preheated Dutch oven with appropriate protection.
- **Allergens**: Gluten.
- **Quality indicators**: Deep, even crust color; open crumb; good oven spring from the covered bake.
- **Holding/storage**: Best same-day to next-day; store cut-side down at room temperature.
- **Scaling notes**: Scaled 3x from the 1-loaf source recipe.
- **Skill focus**: Levain maintenance and use, whole-grain hydration, long cold fermentation, covered-vessel (Dutch oven) steam-trapping technique - a contrast to the open-hearth method in the other Sourdough entry.

## Sourdough Crumpets

- **Course/use case**: Culinary 2, Baking - Lean Dough unit - a quick griddled sourdough-discard item, a good use for levain byproduct alongside the two loaf-style Sourdough entries above. Previously unstandardized - the archived screenshot (`17 - Advanced Baking & Pastry/Baking & Pastry/Sourdough Crumpets _ King Arthur Flour.png`) had quantities that were unreadable at its resolution, so the recipe was pulled fresh from the publisher's site per the recommendation logged in `_OPEN_QUESTIONS.md`.
- **Source and revision date**: [King Arthur Baking, "Sourdough Crumpets Recipe"](https://www.kingarthurbaking.com/recipes/sourdough-crumpets-recipe). Standardized 2026-07.
- **Yield and portion size**: 20-25 crumpets, scaled 5x from the source's 4-5 crumpet yield (1 crumpet per portion).
- **Equipment**: Mixing bowl, griddle, English muffin rings (1" high, 4" across - at least 4-6 to work in batches), muffin scoop (recommended).
- **Ingredients (5x batch)**: 5 cups (1135 g) sourdough starter (unfed/discard), 5 tsp granulated sugar, 1.25-2.5 tsp table salt, 1.875 tsp baking soda (increase toward 2.5 tsp if the starter is particularly sour).
- **Procedure**:
  1. Combine the sourdough starter with the sugar and salt in a bowl, then stir in the baking soda - the mixture should turn bubbly and billowy as it reacts.
  2. Heat the griddle to 300°F over medium-low heat. Lightly grease with cooking oil and add a pat of butter.
  3. Grease the muffin rings and place on the griddle. Distribute batter evenly among the rings, about 1/4 cup per ring (a generous muffin scoop works well).
  4. Cook about 5 minutes, until the tops are set and full of small holes. Flip carefully, remove the rings, and continue cooking about 3 minutes, until golden on the bottom.
  5. Repeat in batches until all batter is used, re-greasing rings as needed.
  6. Serve warm, split and topped with butter and jam, or toasted.
- **Food safety controls**: Standard griddle/egg-free batter handling; hold cooked crumpets warm if not serving immediately.
- **Allergens**: Gluten.
- **Quality indicators**: Distinctive small holes across the top surface (the signature crumpet texture, from the baking-soda reaction), golden-brown bottom, billowy/bubbly batter before cooking as a sign the starter and baking soda reacted properly.
- **Holding/storage**: Room temperature for several days, or freeze well-wrapped for longer storage; split and toast to refresh before serving.
- **Scaling notes**: Scaled 5x from the source's 4-5 crumpet yield to reach the program's 16-30 portion target; ring-batch cooking doesn't scale by simply multiplying batter in one pan, so plan to cook in successive rounds as rings free up.
- **Skill focus**: Using sourdough discard productively, timing a baking-soda reaction for the crumpet's signature holes, ring-molded griddle cookery, batch-cooking with a limited number of rings.

## Pita Bread

- **Course/use case**: Culinary 2, Baking - Lean Dough unit.
- **Source and revision date**: `17 - Advanced Baking & Pastry/Baking & Pastry/Pita Bread.docx` and `Pita Bread(1).docx` - identical recipes, true duplicates, condensed into one entry. Standardized 2026-07.
- **Yield and portion size**: 16 pieces (source yield; already within the program's target range).
- **Equipment**: Mixing bowl, rolling pin, sheet pan (used inverted as a baking surface), 500°F-capable oven.
- **Ingredients**: 2 1/4 cups water, 6 cups bread flour, 2 tsp salt, 2 Tbsp vegetable oil, 1 Tbsp sugar, 1 Tbsp active dry yeast.
- **Procedure**:
  1. Dissolve yeast and sugar in warm water.
  2. Combine bread flour and salt in a bowl.
  3. Stir in the yeast mixture and knead. As the dough comes together, slowly add the oil. Add flour as needed until the dough is no longer sticky.
  4. Place in an oiled bowl, cover with a damp cloth, and let rise in a warm place until doubled, about 2 hours.
  5. Divide into 16 equal pieces; form into rounds. Rest 20 minutes.
  6. Roll into 1/4"-thick disks. Let rise about 20 minutes more.
  7. In a preheated 500°F oven, place disks directly on an inverted sheet pan; bake 3 minutes or until the bread begins to color and puffs.
  8. Cool on a rack.
- **Food safety controls**: Standard yeast/gluten handling; properly wrap, label, store, chill, and rotate per program procedure.
- **Allergens**: Gluten.
- **Quality indicators**: Even puff (pocket forms from steam), light color with only slight browning at edges, soft texture.
- **Holding/storage**: Cool before storing; wrap well to prevent drying out; best within 1-2 days.
- **Scaling notes**: Already at target batch size (16 portions). Scales by weight for larger batches.
- **Skill focus**: High-heat flatbread baking, steam-pocket formation, portioning consistency.

## Cinnamon Rolls (Classic, Cream Cheese-Free Glaze)

- **Course/use case**: Culinary 2, Baking - Enriched Dough unit; Bistro bake-sale item.
- **Source and revision date**: `17 - Advanced Baking & Pastry/Baking & Pastry/Cinnamon Rolls doubled.docx`, `Cinnamon Rolls(1).docx`, and `Cinnamon Rolls.docx` - the same Fleischmann's-style recipe at three batch scales (roughly 4x, 2x, and 1x of each other), condensed into one entry using the largest ("doubled") version as the base. **Note**: all three source files print "Yield: 24 rolls" despite the differing ingredient quantities - this looks like a copy-paste artifact where the yield line wasn't updated when the batch was scaled. The 24-roll yield is used here as-given, but actual roll count should be confirmed in production against the ingredient scale actually used.
- **Yield and portion size**: 24 rolls (source-stated; see note above on inconsistent scaling across source copies).
- **Equipment**: Stand mixer with dough hook, two 13x9" pans (or four 8x8" pans).
- **Ingredients**: Dough: 1 oz active dry yeast, 1 cup warm water (100-110°F), 3 cups warm milk (100-110°F), 3 sticks butter, 1 1/3 cups sugar, 1 Tbsp salt, 4 eggs, 14 cups all-purpose flour. Filling: 1 cup sugar, 3 Tbsp ground cinnamon, 1 stick softened butter. Glaze: 2 cups powdered sugar, 2 Tbsp butter, 2 Tbsp milk, 1/2 tsp vanilla.
- **Procedure**:
  1. Dissolve yeast in warm water in a large mixer bowl. Warm milk and butter together (butter doesn't need to fully melt); add to the yeast mixture.
  2. Beat in sugar, salt, eggs, and 8 cups of the flour. Mix well, adding remaining flour until a soft dough forms.
  3. Knead (by hand or dough hook) until smooth and elastic, 6-8 minutes.
  4. Place in a greased bowl, cover, and let rise in a warm, draft-free place until doubled, about 1 hour.
  5. Mix filling sugar and cinnamon; set aside. Grease the pans.
  6. Punch down dough, divide in half. Roll each half into a 15x10" rectangle. Spread with half the softened butter, then half the cinnamon-sugar filling. Roll up tightly, seal the edge, and cut into 12 slices. Place in pans; repeat with remaining dough.
  7. Cover and let rise until nearly doubled, about 1 hour.
  8. Bake at 350°F for 25-30 minutes, until internal temperature reaches 170°F.
  9. Cool slightly; mix glaze ingredients and drizzle over warm rolls.
- **Food safety controls**: Standard egg/dairy/yeast handling; confirm doneness by internal temperature.
- **Allergens**: Gluten, dairy, egg.
- **Quality indicators**: Even spiral, soft pull-apart texture, golden top, filling melted but not runny out the sides.
- **Holding/storage**: Best same-day; store loosely covered at room temperature.
- **Scaling notes**: Source is already at the 24-roll batch scale. See yield note above regarding the halved/quartered variant files if a smaller batch is ever needed.
- **Skill focus**: Enriched-dough mixing and kneading, laminated roll-and-slice technique, glaze consistency.

## Ultimate Cinnamon Rolls (Tangzhong Method)

- **Course/use case**: Culinary 2, Baking - Enriched Dough (advanced) unit.
- **Source and revision date**: `5 - Baking - Enriched Dough/Cinnamon rolls with tangzhong.pdf` ("The Ultimate Cinnamon Rolls," source: Cleobuttera.com). Standardized 2026-07. **Kept separate from the Classic Cinnamon Rolls above** - the tangzhong (cooked flour-paste) method and cream-cheese frosting are a genuinely different technique and result (softer, more tender crumb) from the classic straight-dough version, not a minor variant.
- **Yield and portion size**: 12 rolls (source yield). Scaled 2x below to 24 rolls to match the program's other cinnamon roll batch size.
- **Equipment**: Small saucepan (for tangzhong), stand mixer with dough hook, 15x13" rimmed baking sheet or two 13x9" pans.
- **Ingredients (2x batch)**: Tangzhong: 10 Tbsp water, 10 Tbsp whole milk, 6 Tbsp + 2 tsp flour. Dough: all of the tangzhong, 12 Tbsp melted unsalted butter, 1.5 cups whole milk, 4 eggs, 8 1/4 cups flour, 7 Tbsp powdered milk, 2 Tbsp instant yeast, 1/2 cup granulated sugar, 3.5 tsp salt. Filling: 1 cup unsalted butter (very soft), 2.5 cups packed light brown sugar, 4 Tbsp cornstarch, 5 Tbsp ground cinnamon, 1 tsp fine salt. Frosting: 1/2 cup butter, 4 oz cream cheese, 2 cups powdered sugar, 2 Tbsp milk, 2 tsp lemon juice, 1 tsp vanilla, pinch of salt.
- **Procedure**:
  1. Whisk tangzhong ingredients (water, milk, flour) in a saucepan; cook over medium heat, whisking constantly, until it thickens to a smooth paste, 2-3 minutes. Remove from heat.
  2. Whisk melted butter and milk into the tangzhong, then whisk in eggs until smooth.
  3. In a mixer bowl, whisk together flour, powdered milk, and yeast. Pour in the tangzhong/egg mixture; mix on low until a shaggy dough forms. Rest, covered, 20 minutes.
  4. Add sugar and salt; mix on medium-low 10 minutes until smooth, elastic, and slightly tacky (it will be sticky at first - don't add extra flour).
  5. Shape into a ball with oiled hands; place in a greased bowl, cover, and rise until almost doubled, about 1 hour (or overnight in the fridge).
  6. Whisk filling ingredients together (brown sugar, cornstarch, cinnamon, salt); set aside.
  7. Roll risen dough into a rectangle (scale up from source's 16x18" for the 2x batch). Spread with softened butter, then the cinnamon-sugar filling, leaving a 1" border on one long edge.
  8. Roll into a tight cylinder; seal the seam. Slice into 24 portions (use dental floss for clean cuts). Arrange in greased pans, cut side up.
  9. Cover; proof until puffy and touching, 40-50 minutes. Preheat oven to 350°F during the last 15 minutes.
  10. Bake 20-25 minutes (tenting with foil halfway through), until internal temperature reaches ~190°F.
  11. Beat frosting ingredients until smooth; spread over warm rolls immediately after baking.
- **Food safety controls**: Standard egg/dairy/yeast handling; confirm doneness by internal temperature.
- **Allergens**: Gluten, dairy, egg.
- **Quality indicators**: Very soft, pillowy crumb (tangzhong effect), tight even spiral, structured (not runny) filling, tangy cream cheese frosting.
- **Holding/storage**: Best same-day; store completely cooled rolls in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 2 days.
- **Scaling notes**: Scaled 2x from the source's 12-roll yield to reach 24 rolls, matching the Classic Cinnamon Rolls entry's batch size.
- **Skill focus**: Tangzhong (cooked flour paste) technique for crumb softness, precision dough hydration, cream cheese frosting, comparison technique against the classic straight-dough method.

## Sweet Dinner Rolls

- **Course/use case**: Culinary 1/2, Baking - Enriched Dough unit; Bistro dinner service.
- **Source and revision date**: `17 - Advanced Baking & Pastry/Baking & Pastry/Sweet Dinner Rolls.docx`, standardized 2026-07.
- **Yield and portion size**: 27 rolls (source yield; already within the program's target range).
- **Equipment**: Mixing bowl, three greased cake pans (9 rolls each).
- **Ingredients**: 1/2 cup warm water (110°F), 0.25 oz active dry yeast, 1/2 cup warm milk, 1 egg, 1/3 cup melted butter, 1/3 cup white sugar, 1 tsp salt, 3 3/4 cups bread flour.
- **Procedure**:
  1. Mix yeast and warm water; let sit 10 minutes.
  2. In a separate bowl, mix milk, egg, butter, sugar, and salt.
  3. Combine with the yeast mixture; slowly add flour.
  4. Turn dough onto a lightly floured surface and knead. Divide into 27 pieces; place 9 into each of the 3 greased cake pans.
  5. Cover with a clean kitchen towel; let rise in a warm place 1 hour. Preheat oven to 400°F.
  6. Bake 10-15 minutes, until golden. Brush with melted butter.
- **Food safety controls**: Standard egg/dairy/yeast handling.
- **Allergens**: Gluten, dairy, egg.
- **Quality indicators**: Golden, glossy (butter-brushed) top; soft, pull-apart texture.
- **Holding/storage**: Best same-day; store loosely covered at room temperature.
- **Scaling notes**: Already at target batch size (27 portions). Scales by weight for larger batches.
- **Skill focus**: Basic enriched-dough mixing, portion-divide consistency, pan-rise technique.

## Bagels

- **Course/use case**: Culinary 2, Baking - Enriched Dough unit; Bistro breakfast/bake-sale item.
- **Source and revision date**: `17 - Advanced Baking & Pastry/Baking & Pastry/bagels.pdf` (professional straight-dough production formula), condensed with `5 - Baking - Enriched Dough/NYC Bagels.pdf` (Sophisticated Gourmet, home-style variant) noted as a variation. Standardized 2026-07. Both use the same core technique (boil, then bake); the professional version scales to a full batch with a dough conditioner and a malt-syrup boiling solution, while the home-style version simplifies for a smaller kitchen (no dough conditioner, optional barley malt) - treated as minor variance rather than a separate entry.
- **Yield and portion size**: 8 lb 2 oz dough, approximately 32 bagels (source yield; already above the program's target range and can be portioned down if needed).
- **Equipment**: Stand mixer, sheet pans, large pot for boiling, cornmeal for dusting.
- **Ingredients**: Dough: 5 lb high-gluten flour, 2 lb 14 oz water, 2 oz fresh yeast, 0.5 oz malt syrup or honey, 1.5 oz salt, 1.5 oz dough conditioner (optional - home-style variant omits this and adds ~1-2 hours to bulk fermentation). Boiling solution: 8 lb water, 6 oz malt syrup, honey, or brown sugar (home-style variant: barley malt, optional), 2 oz salt.
- **Procedure**:
  1. Combine all dough ingredients; mix 2 minutes on speed 1, then 6 minutes on speed 2.
  2. Divide into 4 oz portions; round, cover, and rest 5-10 minutes.
  3. Roll each portion into a log, wrap around a few fingers, and roll the ends together to seal into a ring.
  4. Place on a paper-lined sheet pan dusted with cornmeal.
  5. Proof 15-20 minutes. (After proofing, bagels can be frozen for later baking.)
  6. Bring the boiling solution to a simmer. Boil each bagel 20 seconds per side (up to 2 minutes per side for a chewier, more New York-style result).
  7. Dip in toppings if using; place on well-sprayed, paper-lined sheet pans.
  8. Bake immediately at 420°F until golden brown, about 20-25 minutes.
- **Food safety controls**: Standard yeast/gluten handling; maintain boiling solution at a food-safe simmer throughout service.
- **Allergens**: Gluten.
- **Quality indicators**: Shiny, chewy crust from the boil; dense, chewy interior (not bready/soft like a dinner roll); even ring shape.
- **Holding/storage**: Cool completely before bagging; freezes well after boiling (par-cooked) or fully baked.
- **Scaling notes**: Source already yields ~32 bagels; portion down (e.g., half-batch for ~16) if a smaller run is needed.

## Neapolitan-Style Pizza Dough (Cold Ferment)

- **Course/use case**: Culinary 2, Advanced Baking & Pastry unit (fermentation/technique focus).
- **Source and revision date**: `4 - Baking - Lean Dough/Pizza dough.pdf` ("Dan Richer's Everyday Pizza Dough," source: ooni.com). Standardized 2026-07 and scaled up. Kept as a distinct entry from the other pizza doughs in this bank - the 3-day cold ferment and windowpane-test technique are a deliberately different, more advanced process, not a minor variant of the same-day classroom dough.
- **Yield and portion size**: Scaled 3x from the source to ~20 dough balls at 250 g each (source yielded 7 balls at 250 g, or 2 balls at 890 g for pan pies).
- **Equipment**: Stand mixer with dough hook (or hand-mixing), individual lightly-oiled proofing containers, dough scraper, pizza peel, baking stone/steel.
- **Ingredients (3x batch)**: 3000 g "00" flour, 9 g instant yeast, 2040 g water (increase to 2280 g / 76% hydration if using a conventional oven rather than a high-heat pizza oven), 60 g fine sea salt. Rice flour or semolina for dusting.
- **Procedure**:
  1. Day 1: Mix flour and yeast; add water and mix until no dry bits remain. Rest, covered, 20 minutes to 1 hour.
  2. Sprinkle salt over the dough and work it in by hand.
  3. Perform a series of stretch-and-folds (lift and press dough onto itself, rotating the bowl) every 30 minutes for about 2 hours, until the dough passes the windowpane test (stretched thin, it shows light through without tearing).
  4. Cover and refrigerate overnight.
  5. Day 2: Stretch and fold once more. Return to the fridge.
  6. Before bed (at least 12 hours later), divide into 250 g pieces (or 890 g for pan pies). Round each into a tight ball, seam-side down. Place in individual lightly oiled containers.
  7. Refrigerate overnight (at least 12 hours).
  8. Day 3: Remove dough 2-3 hours before baking to come to room temperature; it should have visibly increased in volume and pass the poke test (springs back slowly, leaves a slight indentation).
  9. Preheat oven (and stone/steel, if using) to its highest temperature.
  10. Stretch each ball by hand to a 10-11" disc, leaving the rim untouched. Transfer to a floured peel and stretch further to 12".
  11. Top and bake: 1-2 minutes in a high-heat pizza oven, or 6-7 minutes in a home oven on a preheated stone/steel.
- **Food safety controls**: Standard yeast/gluten handling; refrigerate dough promptly between fermentation stages.
- **Allergens**: Gluten.
- **Quality indicators**: Passes the windowpane test before baking; leopard-spotted, blistered crust; open, airy rim ("cornicione"); pass the poke test before shaping.
- **Holding/storage**: Portioned dough balls hold well refrigerated for the full 2-3 day process; not intended for long-term storage beyond that.
- **Scaling notes**: Scaled 3x from the source's 7-ball yield to reach ~20 portions.
- **Skill focus**: Multi-day cold fermentation, stretch-and-fold gluten development, windowpane and poke tests for readiness, hand-stretching technique.

## Cinnamon King Cake

- **Course/use case**: Culinary 2, Baking - Enriched Dough unit; seasonal (Mardi Gras) Bistro item.
- **Source and revision date**: `5 - Baking - Enriched Dough/Cinnamon King Cake (Classic, Not Overly Sweet).docx`. Standardized 2026-07.
- **Yield and portion size**: 1 large ring, 12-16 slices (source yield).
- **Equipment**: Stand mixer with dough hook, parchment-lined sheet pan, rolling pin.
- **Ingredients**: Dough: 480 g all-purpose flour, 75 g granulated sugar, 7 g instant yeast, 6 g fine salt, 240 g warm whole milk (100-110°F), 2 large eggs (room temp), 85 g unsalted butter (very soft), 1 tsp vanilla extract, zest of 1/2 lemon (optional). Filling: 150 g packed brown sugar, 2.5 Tbsp cinnamon, 60 g very soft butter, 1 Tbsp flour, pinch of salt. Icing: 180 g powdered sugar, 2-3 Tbsp milk or lemon juice, purple/green/gold sanding sugar.
- **Procedure**:
  1. Warm milk, sugar, and yeast together; rest 5 minutes until lightly foamy.
  2. Add eggs, vanilla, lemon zest, and half the flour; mix until shaggy. Add salt and remaining flour.
  3. Add softened butter in pieces while mixing; knead 8-10 minutes until smooth, elastic, and slightly tacky.
  4. Place in an oiled bowl, cover, and rise 60-90 minutes until doubled (or cold-rise overnight for better flavor and easier classroom timing).
  5. Mix filling: brown sugar, cinnamon, flour, and salt, then blend in softened butter to a spreadable (not runny, not crumbly) paste.
  6. Roll dough into a 10x24" rectangle. Spread filling edge to edge, leaving a 1/2" border on one long side.
  7. Roll tightly into a log from the long side; transfer to a parchment-lined sheet pan and form into a ring, pinching the seam closed.
  8. Proof, loosely covered, 45-60 minutes until puffy and slightly jiggly.
  9. Bake at 350°F (175°C) for 25-30 minutes, until deep golden and internal temperature reaches ~190°F. Cool completely before icing.
  10. Whisk icing to a thick drizzle consistency; pour over the cooled cake and immediately add colored sanding sugar.
- **Food safety controls**: Standard egg/dairy/yeast handling; confirm doneness by internal temperature. *(If inserting a plastic baby figurine per tradition, do so after baking, from underneath, for food safety.)*
- **Allergens**: Gluten, dairy, egg.
- **Quality indicators**: Clean spiral when sliced, puffy (not dense - under-proofing is the most common cause of a dense king cake), evenly colored icing coverage.
- **Holding/storage**: Best same-day; store loosely covered at room temperature.
- **Scaling notes**: Already at a workable single-ring batch size for a bake-sale/seasonal item; double for a second ring if needed for a larger event.
- **Skill focus**: Enriched-dough kneading, filling consistency (stabilized with flour so it doesn't melt out), ring-shaping and seam-sealing, proofing judgment, decorative icing.

## Classic Buttermilk Biscuits (Lard, Hand Rub-In)

- **Course/use case**: Culinary 1, Baking - Lean Dough / Breakfast unit.
- **Source and revision date**: `6 - Breakfast/Breakfast/Buttermilk biscuits.doc`, standardized 2026-07.
- **Yield and portion size**: 12-16 biscuits (source yield). Scaled 2x below to ~24-32 biscuits to reach the program's target range.
- **Equipment**: Large bowl, rolling pin, 2 1/2-3" biscuit cutter, parchment-lined sheet pan.
- **Ingredients (2x batch)**: 5 cups sifted self-rising flour (measured after sifting), 2 Tbsp sugar, 1/2 cup + 2 Tbsp packed lard or butter (chilled), 2 cups chilled buttermilk, 6 Tbsp unsalted butter (melted, for brushing).
- **Procedure**:
  1. Heat oven to 500°F. Whisk flour and sugar together. Add lard, coating in flour; rub between fingertips until roughly half is coarsely blended and half remains in large (~3/4") pieces.
  2. Make a well in the center; add buttermilk all at once. Stir quickly with a large spoon just until blended and a sticky dough begins to form (add 1-2 Tbsp more buttermilk if dough seems dry).
  3. Turn dough onto a generously floured surface. Knead briskly 8-10 times until a cohesive ball forms. Flatten to an even thickness and roll to 3/4" thick.
  4. Dock the dough with a flour-dipped fork at 1/2" intervals. Cut rounds with a floured biscuit cutter; arrange on a parchment-lined sheet pan. Gently re-form scraps once for additional rounds.
  5. Bake on a rack in the upper third of the oven, 8-12 minutes, until crusty and golden brown.
  6. Brush with melted butter; serve hot.
- **Food safety controls**: Standard dairy/gluten handling.
- **Allergens**: Gluten, dairy.
- **Quality indicators**: Tall rise, craggy golden top, visible flaky layers from the lard pieces, tender interior.
- **Holding/storage**: Best straight from the oven; hold briefly warm, uncovered, to preserve crust; day-old biscuits reheat well split and toasted.
- **Scaling notes**: Scaled 2x from the source's 12-16 biscuit yield.
- **Skill focus**: Cold-fat rub-in technique (visible fat pieces for flaky layers), gentle dough handling, high-heat baking.

## The Food Lab's Buttermilk Biscuits (Laminated)

- **Course/use case**: Culinary 2, Advanced Baking & Pastry unit (lamination technique focus).
- **Source and revision date**: `6 - Breakfast/Biscuits.pdf` ("The Food Lab's Buttermilk Biscuits," source: Serious Eats). Standardized 2026-07. Kept separate from the other biscuit entries in this bank - this version uses a food-processor lamination (fold-and-roll) technique specifically for flaky layers, a different method and result from the simple rub-in or paddle-mix biscuits above.
- **Yield and portion size**: 8 biscuits (source yield). Scaled 2x below to 16 biscuits.
- **Equipment**: Food processor, rolling pin, bench scraper, 2 1/2" biscuit cutter, parchment-lined sheet pan.
- **Ingredients (2x batch)**: 1 cup (240 ml) buttermilk, 1 cup sour cream (8 oz), 20 oz all-purpose flour (4 1/2 cups), 2 Tbsp baking powder, 1/2 tsp baking soda, 3 tsp Diamond Crystal kosher salt (use half as much by volume for table salt), 16 Tbsp cold unsalted butter (cut into 1/4" pats), plus 4 Tbsp melted unsalted butter for brushing.
- **Procedure**:
  1. Adjust oven rack to middle position; preheat to 425°F.
  2. Whisk buttermilk and sour cream together in a small bowl.
  3. In a food processor, combine flour, baking powder, baking soda, and salt; process to blend, about 2 seconds. Scatter cold butter over the flour and pulse until the mixture resembles coarse meal with the largest butter pieces about 1/4". Transfer to a large bowl.
  4. Add the buttermilk mixture and fold with a rubber spatula until just combined. Turn onto a floured surface and knead just until it comes together, adding flour as necessary.
  5. Roll into a 12x8" rectangle. Fold in thirds (letter-fold) using a bench scraper, then fold in thirds again to a 4" square. Press down and re-roll into a 12x8" rectangle; repeat the folding process once more. (Variation: add shredded cheddar and sliced scallions during a fold for a savory version.)
  6. Roll again into a 10x6" rectangle, about 1" thick. Cut rounds with a floured biscuit cutter; transfer to a parchment-lined sheet pan, spacing 1" apart. Gently re-form scraps for additional rounds.
  7. Brush tops with melted butter; bake about 14 minutes, rotating the pan halfway, until golden brown and well risen. Cool 5 minutes before serving.
- **Food safety controls**: Standard dairy/gluten handling.
- **Allergens**: Gluten, dairy.
- **Quality indicators**: Distinct flaky layers visible on the cut side (from lamination), tall even rise, golden top.
- **Holding/storage**: Best straight from the oven; day-old biscuits reheat well split and toasted.
- **Scaling notes**: Scaled 2x from the source's 8-biscuit yield.
- **Skill focus**: Food-processor fat-cutting, letter-fold lamination for layered flakiness (contrast this technique against the hand rub-in and paddle methods in the other biscuit entries).
