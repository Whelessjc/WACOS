## Buttermilk Fried Chicken (Large-Batch, Brined)

- **Course/use case**: Culinary 2, Poultry unit; catering/large-event production.
- **Source and revision date**: `12 - Poultry/BUTTERMILK FRIED CHICKEN.docx`, standardized 2026-07.
- **Yield and portion size**: Already at production scale - brine sized for ~30 lb chicken; seasoned flour sized for 75-90 pieces.
- **Equipment**: Large stock pot, ice bath, cooler, steamer, fryer (peanut oil), breading station.
- **Ingredients**: Brine (per ~30 lb chicken): 15 lemons, 30 bay leaves, 3 bunches flat-leaf parsley, 30 g whole leaf thyme, 3 cups honey, 4 heads garlic (split), 1/2 cup whole/cracked black peppercorns, 5 cups kosher salt, 5 gallons water. Seasoned flour (for 75-90 pieces): 11 lb flour, 1.5 cups garlic powder, 1.5 cups onion powder, 3/4 cup paprika, 1/3 cup cayenne pepper, 3/4 cup kosher salt, 3 Tbsp ground black pepper.
- **Procedure**:
  1. Combine all brine ingredients in a large stock pot; bring to a boil for 1 minute. Remove from heat; stir to fully dissolve the salt.
  2. Divide the brine into small batches and cool quickly on ice baths.
  3. Brine cut chicken pieces overnight in the cooler.
  4. Remove from brine, rinse, and steam the chicken 30 minutes; cool completely.
  5. Combine all seasoned flour ingredients in a bowl; mix well.
  6. Bread using the sequence: plain flour, then buttermilk, then seasoned flour.
  7. Fry in peanut oil until golden brown and internal temperature reaches 165°F.
- **Food safety controls**: Standard poultry handling; brine and cool per HACCP cooling curve; confirm final internal temperature of 165°F after frying (the pre-steam step is a partial cook, not the final safety check).
- **Allergens**: Gluten.
- **Quality indicators**: Deeply golden, crisp double-breaded crust; moist interior from the brine/steam pre-treatment.
- **Holding/storage**: Hold hot for service per program's standard hot-holding procedure; cool and refrigerate leftovers promptly.
- **Scaling notes**: Already a full production batch (~30 lb chicken, 75-90 pieces). Scale down proportionally for smaller runs.
- **Skill focus**: Large-batch brining and cooling safety, steam pre-cooking before frying, double-dredge breading technique, batch fry management.

## Copycat KFC-Style Fried Chicken

- **Course/use case**: Culinary 1/2, Poultry unit.
- **Source and revision date**: `12 - Poultry/Poultry/KFC.docx` (source noted in file: Chicago Tribune). Standardized 2026-07. Kept separate from the Buttermilk Fried Chicken above - this is a herb-and-spice buttermilk soak with a single dredge, a different (and much smaller-batch) technique from the professional brine-steam-double-dredge method.
- **Yield and portion size**: 4 portions (1 whole chicken) per source. Scaled 5x below to ~20 portions (5 chickens).
- **Equipment**: Large bowl, wire rack, sheet pan, Dutch oven or heavy deep pot, deep-fry thermometer.
- **Ingredients (5x batch)**: 10 cups AP flour, 5 tsp salt, 7.5 tsp dried thyme, 7.5 tsp dried basil, 5 tsp dried oregano, 5 Tbsp celery salt, 5 Tbsp black pepper, 5 Tbsp dried mustard, 20 Tbsp paprika, 10 Tbsp garlic salt, 5 Tbsp ground ginger, 15 Tbsp white pepper, MSG (optional - source notes "??try 1 tsp" per original batch, scale/confirm to taste), 5 cups buttermilk, 5 eggs, 5 whole chickens, fryer oil.
- **Procedure**:
  1. Mix flour with all herbs and spices in a bowl; set aside.
  2. Whisk buttermilk and eggs together. Soak chicken in the buttermilk mixture at room temperature, 20-30 minutes.
  3. Remove chicken from buttermilk, letting excess drip off. Dredge in the herb-spice-flour mixture, coating all sides; shake off excess. Rest on a rack over a sheet pan, 20 minutes.
  4. Heat about 3" of oil in a Dutch oven to 350°F.
  5. Fry 3-4 pieces at a time (don't crowd the pot) until medium golden brown, turning once, 15-18 minutes, until internal temperature reaches 165°F. Drain on a paper-towel-lined sheet pan. Let oil return to temperature between batches.
- **Food safety controls**: Standard poultry/egg/dairy handling; confirm 165°F internal temperature before serving.
- **Allergens**: Gluten, dairy, egg.
- **Quality indicators**: Deep golden color, herb-flecked crust, audible crunch.
- **Holding/storage**: Best fresh from the fryer; hold hot briefly on a rack (not covered, to preserve crispness).
- **Scaling notes**: Scaled 5x from the source's 4-portion (1 chicken) yield.
- **Skill focus**: Buttermilk soak and single-dredge breading, oil temperature management across batches, herb/spice blending.

## Copycat Chick-fil-A Sandwich (Pickle Brine)

- **Course/use case**: Culinary 1/2, Poultry unit; Bistro sandwich item.
- **Source and revision date**: `12 - Poultry/Poultry/chic fil a.docx`, standardized 2026-07. Kept as a separate entry from the Serious Eats version below - genuinely different brine (pickle juice vs. salt-sugar) and breading approach, not a minor variant.
- **Yield and portion size**: 2 sandwiches (source yield). Scaled 8x below to 16 sandwiches.
- **Equipment**: Zip-top bags, skillet, tongs.
- **Ingredients (8x batch)**: 16 thin chicken breasts, 2 cups pickle juice, 8 eggs, 1 cup milk, 4 cups flour, 8 Tbsp powdered sugar, 4 tsp garlic salt, 4 tsp celery salt, 4 tsp basil, 2 cups peanut oil, 16 buns, 8 tsp paprika, salt and pepper to taste.
- **Procedure**:
  1. Marinate chicken breasts in pickle juice, in a bag, for 1 hour.
  2. Beat together egg and milk.
  3. Mix flour, powdered sugar, garlic salt, celery salt, basil, salt, and pepper.
  4. Heat oil in a skillet over medium-high heat.
  5. Coat each chicken piece in the egg mixture, then in the flour mixture.
  6. Fry 3-4 minutes per side, until golden brown and cooked through (confirm 165°F internal temperature).
  7. Drain on paper towels.
  8. Butter and toast the sandwich buns; assemble with condiments as desired.
- **Food safety controls**: Standard poultry/egg/dairy handling; confirm 165°F internal temperature.
- **Allergens**: Gluten, egg, dairy.
- **Quality indicators**: Golden, crisp exterior; tangy pickle-brined flavor throughout the meat.
- **Holding/storage**: Best fresh; assemble sandwiches to order.
- **Scaling notes**: Scaled 8x from the source's 2-sandwich yield.
- **Skill focus**: Pickle-brine flavoring technique, single egg-then-flour dredge, sandwich assembly/steaming (see the Serious Eats version's steam-rest step for a technique contrast).

## Copycat Chick-fil-A Sandwich (Salt-Sugar Brine)

- **Course/use case**: Culinary 2, Poultry unit; Bistro sandwich item.
- **Source and revision date**: `12 - Poultry/Poultry/Chick-fil-A.pdf` ("Homemade Chick-Fil-A Sandwiches," source: Serious Eats). Standardized 2026-07.
- **Yield and portion size**: 4 sandwiches (source yield, from 2 whole breasts split into 4 cutlets). Scaled 4x below to 16 sandwiches.
- **Equipment**: Zipper-lock bags, wok/deep fryer/Dutch oven, deep-fry thermometer.
- **Ingredients (4x batch)**: 2 cups plus 2 tsp kosher salt (divided), 1 cup plus 8 tsp granulated sugar (divided), 8 large boneless skinless chicken breasts (split horizontally into 16 cutlets), 8 Tbsp ground black pepper, 8 tsp paprika, 4 tsp powdered MSG (optional), 2 tsp cayenne pepper, 4 cups milk, 8 large eggs, 6 cups all-purpose flour, 8 Tbsp non-fat milk powder, 4 tsp baking powder, peanut oil for frying, 16 soft hamburger buns (toasted in butter), 32 dill pickle chips.
- **Procedure**:
  1. Dissolve the bulk of the salt and sugar in cold water; brine chicken breasts at least 6 hours, up to overnight, refrigerated.
  2. Combine black pepper, paprika, MSG (if using), and cayenne. Set aside.
  3. Whisk milk and eggs together.
  4. Combine flour, milk powder, baking powder, remaining salt and sugar, and 2 Tbsp of the spice mixture; whisk to combine. Drizzle in a little of the milk-egg mixture and rub in with fingertips until the flour mixture is coarse, like wet sand.
  5. Preheat oil to 350°F.
  6. Remove chicken from brine, pat dry, and season with remaining spice mixture. Dip in the milk-egg mixture, then in the flour mixture, pressing firmly to coat. Lower into hot oil.
  7. Fry, turning occasionally, until golden and cooked through, about 4 minutes total (confirm 165°F internal temperature).
  8. Assemble on toasted buns with pickle chips. Cover with foil or an inverted bowl for 2 minutes to steam the buns before serving.
- **Food safety controls**: Standard poultry/egg/dairy handling; confirm 165°F internal temperature; refrigerate during the full brine period.
- **Allergens**: Gluten, egg, dairy.
- **Quality indicators**: Craggy, well-adhered crust (from the wet-sand flour technique); juicy interior from the long brine; steamed, soft bun.
- **Holding/storage**: Best fresh; assemble to order.
- **Scaling notes**: Scaled 4x from the source's 4-sandwich yield.
- **Skill focus**: Long salt-sugar brining, "wet sand" flour-hydration technique for a craggy crust, bun-steaming finish.

## Nashville Hot Chicken Sandwiches

- **Course/use case**: Culinary 2, Poultry unit; Bistro sandwich item.
- **Source and revision date**: `12 - Poultry/Nashville Hot chicken.pdf` (source: Meat Church). Standardized 2026-07.
- **Yield and portion size**: 4 sandwiches (source yield). Scaled 4x below to 16 sandwiches.
- **Equipment**: Non-reactive container for brining, breading station (2 bowls), fryer or heavy pot, wire rack, thermometer.
- **Ingredients (4x batch)**: 16 chicken thighs, trimmed (dark meat preferred; white meat can be substituted - slice in half if using breast so it isn't too thick), 16 buns (buttered). Brine: 8 cups pickle juice, seasoned flour dredge (program's own seasoned-flour blend, e.g. the KFC-style or Buttermilk Fried Chicken seasoned flour above, may be substituted for the branded "Chicken Fried Breading" in the source), 8 large eggs, 4 cups buttermilk, 4 Tbsp hot sauce. Spicy coating: 2 cups hot frying oil (reserved from frying) or melted lard, 8 Tbsp cayenne pepper (adjust to taste), 4 Tbsp brown sugar, seasoning blend to taste (garlic/onion powder, paprika - source uses a branded "Holy Voodoo" blend).
- **Procedure**:
  1. Brine chicken thighs in pickle juice, fully submerged, 2-3 hours.
  2. Remove from brine, pat dry, and season lightly with the spice blend.
  3. Set up a dredge station: seasoned flour in one bowl, beaten eggs/buttermilk/hot sauce in another.
  4. Coat each piece in the seasoned flour, then the egg wash, then the seasoned flour again.
  5. Fry until golden brown and internal temperature reaches at least 165°F.
  6. Remove to a wire rack to drain and cool slightly.
  7. Whisk hot frying oil (or melted lard), cayenne, brown sugar, and seasoning together. Spoon over each piece of fried chicken to taste (careful - the oil is very hot).
  8. Serve on toasted, buttered buns with mayonnaise and pickles.
- **Food safety controls**: Standard poultry/egg/dairy handling; confirm 165°F internal temperature; handle the hot oil-based spicy coating with care.
- **Allergens**: Gluten, egg, dairy.
- **Quality indicators**: Deep red-orange spicy coating, audible crunch, juicy thigh meat, balanced heat (adjustable via cayenne level).
- **Holding/storage**: Best fresh; the spicy oil coating doesn't hold well once applied - dress to order.
- **Scaling notes**: Scaled 4x from the source's 4-sandwich yield.
- **Skill focus**: Long pickle-brine flavoring, double-dredge breading, hot-oil spice coating technique (a hallmark of the Nashville hot style), heat-level adjustment.

## Blackened Chicken

- **Course/use case**: Culinary 1, Poultry unit; Cajun/Creole flavor profile.
- **Source and revision date**: `12 - Poultry/Poultry/Blackened Chicken.docx`, standardized 2026-07. **Needs confirmation**: the source is a bare technique outline with no quantities at all (just "chicken," "Cajun seasoning," "clarified butter") - not filled in with assumed amounts. A standard blackening ratio (roughly 1-2 tsp Cajun seasoning per chicken breast) is suggested below but should be confirmed against program practice.
- **Yield and portion size**: Not specified in source. **Needs confirmation** - recommend testing and recording actual portions per batch of chicken breasts used.
- **Equipment**: Cast iron pan (must get very hot), oven.
- **Ingredients**: Chicken (breast or thigh, quantity not specified in source), Cajun seasoning (amount not specified - suggested starting point: 1-2 tsp per piece), clarified butter for dipping.
- **Procedure**:
  1. Preheat a cast iron pan until very hot.
  2. Dip chicken in clarified butter, then coat in Cajun seasoning.
  3. Carefully place in the hot pan.
  4. Flip after about a minute; cook until charred/blackened.
  5. Finish in a 450°F oven until internal temperature reaches 165°F.
- **Food safety controls**: Standard poultry handling; confirm 165°F internal temperature after the oven finish, since the pan step alone is a surface char, not full cooking for larger cuts.
- **Allergens**: Dairy (clarified butter).
- **Quality indicators**: Deeply charred, blackened spice crust without a burnt/bitter taste; moist interior.
- **Holding/storage**: Best fresh, immediately after the oven finish.
- **Scaling notes**: **Needs confirmation** - no source yield to scale from; establish a batch size and record it here.
- **Skill focus**: High-heat cast iron searing without burning, spice-crust ("blackening") technique, pan-to-oven finishing for even doneness.

## Chicken Sauté (Pan-Seared Chicken Breast)

- **Course/use case**: Culinary 1, Poultry unit - foundational technique.
- **Source and revision date**: Condensed from three sources, all the same core technique (season, pan-sear, rest a boneless chicken breast) with minor seasoning/method variance: `12 - Poultry/Poultry/Saute Chicken.docx` and `Ingredients.docx` (identical recipes, literal duplicates - lime-garlic pan finish), `11 - Cooking Methods/Sauteed chicken.pdf` (marshaeileen.com - Italian-herb spice blend, no pan finish), and `11 - Cooking Methods/Juicy chicken breast.pdf` (thecafesucrefarine.com - garlic-paprika-Italian rub with a specific sear-then-cover technique for juiciness). Standardized 2026-07.
- **Yield and portion size**: 4 chicken breasts (source yield). Scaled 4x below to 16 portions.
- **Equipment**: Large heavy skillet, meat thermometer.
- **Ingredients (4x batch, base lime-garlic version)**: 16 boneless, skinless chicken breast halves, 3 tsp salt, 1 tsp black pepper, 1 tsp cayenne pepper, 1/2 tsp paprika, 1 tsp garlic powder, 1/2 tsp onion powder, 1 tsp dried thyme, 1 tsp dried parsley, 8 Tbsp butter, 4 Tbsp olive oil, 8 tsp garlic powder (second addition), 12 Tbsp lime juice. *(Variant seasoning: swap the spice blend for 2 Tbsp each garlic powder, onion powder, and Italian seasoning, plus 1 Tbsp paprika, 1 Tbsp salt, and 1/2 Tbsp black pepper, and skip the lime-garlic finish, per the Sautéed Chicken/marshaeileen.com variant.)*
- **Procedure**:
  1. Mix the spice blend; sprinkle generously on both sides of the chicken breasts. For a more even cook, pound breasts to uniform thickness first, or slice in half lengthwise.
  2. Heat butter and olive oil in a large heavy skillet over medium heat.
  3. Sauté chicken until golden brown, about 6 minutes on each side.
  4. *(Lime-garlic version)* Sprinkle with the second addition of garlic powder and lime juice; cook 5 minutes more, stirring to coat evenly.
  5. *(Juicy/covered-finish variant)* After browning both sides (about 5 minutes per side), lower heat to medium-low, cover, and continue cooking 5-10 minutes until internal temperature reaches 165°F - the cover traps steam for a more evenly cooked, juicier result.
  6. Rest 3-5 minutes before slicing or serving.
- **Food safety controls**: Standard poultry handling; confirm 165°F internal temperature at the thickest part of the breast.
- **Allergens**: Dairy (butter).
- **Quality indicators**: Golden brown exterior, juicy interior, even doneness edge to edge (an indicator the covered-finish technique was used correctly).
- **Holding/storage**: Best fresh; hold briefly covered to retain moisture.
- **Scaling notes**: Scaled 4x from the source's 4-breast yield.
- **Skill focus**: Basic dry-rub seasoning, pan-sauté technique, doneness by thermometer, covered-finish technique for guaranteed juiciness (contrast against the uncovered version).

## Shallow-Fried Chicken with Shallot Pan Sauce

- **Course/use case**: Culinary 2, Poultry unit - pan sauce technique.
- **Source and revision date**: `11 - Cooking Methods/Shallow Fried Chicken with Shallot Pan Sauce.docx`, standardized 2026-07. Kept separate from the plain Chicken Sauté above - this version is dredged in flour before frying and finished with a proper deglazed pan sauce, a distinct two-part technique.
- **Yield and portion size**: 4 chicken cutlets (source yield). Scaled 4x below to 16 portions.
- **Equipment**: Large skillet (cast iron preferred), tongs, thermometer, shallow dishes for dredging.
- **Ingredients (4x batch)**: 16 boneless, skinless chicken cutlets, 2 cups all-purpose flour, 4 tsp salt, 2 tsp black pepper, 2 tsp garlic powder, 1 tsp paprika, 8 Tbsp vegetable or canola oil, 8 Tbsp butter, 8 shallots (minced), 2 cups chicken broth, 4 Tbsp lemon juice, 8 Tbsp fresh parsley (chopped).
- **Procedure**:
  1. Pat chicken cutlets dry; season with salt, pepper, garlic powder, and paprika.
  2. Dredge each cutlet fully in flour.
  3. Heat oil in the skillet to 350-375°F. Shallow-fry chicken 3-4 minutes per side, until golden brown and internal temperature reaches 165°F. Remove and drain on a rack.
  4. Pour off the frying oil, leaving the browned bits (fond). Melt butter in the pan over medium heat; add shallots and cook until fragrant and translucent, 2-3 minutes.
  5. Deglaze with chicken broth, scraping up the fond; simmer until slightly reduced, 3-5 minutes.
  6. Remove from heat; stir in lemon juice and parsley.
  7. Plate chicken and spoon the pan sauce over it.
- **Food safety controls**: Standard poultry handling; confirm 165°F internal temperature.
- **Allergens**: Gluten, dairy.
- **Quality indicators**: Crisp, golden crust from the flour dredge; glossy, well-balanced pan sauce (not too thin or too reduced); bright lemon-parsley finish.
- **Holding/storage**: Best fresh; sauce can separate on reheating - hold sauce and chicken together only briefly.
- **Scaling notes**: Scaled 4x from the source's 4-cutlet yield.
- **Skill focus**: Flour-dredge shallow frying, fond development and deglazing, pan sauce building and finishing (contrast against the pre-made sauces in the Marsala and Shallot entries).

## Chicken Marsala

- **Course/use case**: Culinary 2, Poultry unit - classic Italian-American technique.
- **Source and revision date**: `11 - Cooking Methods/Chicken marsala.pdf` (source: Rouxbe). Standardized 2026-07.
- **Yield and portion size**: Serves 3-4 (source yield). Scaled 5x below to ~15-20 portions.
- **Equipment**: Skillet, cooling rack, tongs.
- **Ingredients (5x batch)**: 15-20 boneless, skinless chicken breasts (sliced into 2-3 pieces each), kosher salt and black pepper to taste, 10 tsp grapeseed oil (approx., plus more as needed), 15 Tbsp minced shallots, 2.5 cups Marsala wine, 5 cups dark chicken stock, 10 Tbsp unsalted butter, 5 lemons, 1.25 cups chopped Italian flat-leaf parsley.
- **Procedure**:
  1. Slice each chicken breast into 2-3 equal pieces. Season both sides with salt and pepper.
  2. Heat a pan over medium-high heat; add oil, then chicken. Cook until golden underneath, flip, and cook through. Rest on a cooling rack, tented with foil, while cooking remaining batches.
  3. In the same pan, add a touch more oil if needed and the shallots; cook until soft and translucent.
  4. Deglaze with Marsala, scraping up the fond; reduce until almost all liquid has evaporated.
  5. Add the dark chicken stock; simmer and reduce by about half. Add any resting juices from the chicken; continue reducing to a sauce-like consistency.
  6. Swirl in cold butter to finish the sauce; season to taste.
  7. Return chicken to the pan, turning to coat in the sauce. Thin with a touch of water or stock if too thick.
  8. Finish with chopped parsley and a squeeze of lemon; plate individually or family-style.
- **Food safety controls**: Standard poultry handling; confirm 165°F internal temperature.
- **Allergens**: Dairy (butter); contains alcohol (Marsala wine - cooks off substantially but note for service).
- **Quality indicators**: Glossy, balanced sauce (not broken), tender chicken, good caramelized color on the initial sear.
- **Holding/storage**: Best fresh; reheat gently to avoid overcooking the chicken.
- **Scaling notes**: Scaled 5x from the source's 3-4 serving yield.
- **Skill focus**: Batch-searing and resting technique, fond development, wine reduction, mounting a sauce with cold butter (beurre monté-style finish).

## Panko-Crusted Chicken

- **Course/use case**: Culinary 1, Poultry unit - breading technique.
- **Source and revision date**: `11 - Cooking Methods/Panko crusted chicken.pdf` (source: orwhateveryoudo.com). Standardized 2026-07. Kept separate from the Sesame-Panko Chicken & Spinach Salad below - this is a plain seasoned panko crust served on its own, not part of a composed Asian-flavored salad.
- **Yield and portion size**: 6 servings (3 chicken breasts, source yield). Scaled 3x below to ~18 servings.
- **Equipment**: Meat mallet or rolling pin, 3 shallow dishes for breading station, skillet, wire rack.
- **Ingredients (3x batch)**: 9 chicken breasts, 2.25-3 cups cooking oil, 3 cups flour (divided), 2 cups panko, 1.5 tsp salt, 1.5 tsp garlic powder, 3/4 tsp seasoned salt, 3/8 tsp cayenne pepper, 3/8 tsp onion powder, 3 large eggs (beaten), 9 Tbsp milk, pinch of salt and pepper.
- **Procedure**:
  1. Pound chicken breasts to an even 1/2-3/4" thickness; cut into palm-sized pieces.
  2. Mix half the flour with panko, salt, garlic powder, seasoned salt, cayenne, and onion powder. In a separate bowl, mix egg and milk. In a third bowl, mix remaining flour with a pinch of salt and pepper.
  3. Dredge each piece of chicken: first in the plain flour, then the egg wash, then the panko mixture, coating completely at each step.
  4. Fry in preheated oil over medium-high heat until golden brown on both sides and cooked through, about 6-7 minutes.
  5. Rest on a wire rack 3-4 minutes before serving.
- **Food safety controls**: Standard poultry/egg/dairy handling; confirm 165°F internal temperature.
- **Allergens**: Gluten, egg, dairy.
- **Quality indicators**: Crisp, evenly golden panko crust; no soggy spots (indicates oil temperature was maintained).
- **Holding/storage**: Best fresh; hold uncovered on a rack to preserve crispness.
- **Scaling notes**: Scaled 3x from the source's 6-serving yield.
- **Skill focus**: Three-stage breading station technique, even pounding for uniform cooking, oil temperature management.

## Sesame-Panko Chicken & Spinach Salad

- **Course/use case**: Culinary 2, Poultry unit - composed salad/Asian flavor profile.
- **Source and revision date**: `11 - Cooking Methods/Sesame-panko chicken.pdf` (source: Rouxbe). Standardized 2026-07.
- **Yield and portion size**: Serves 4 (source yield). Scaled 4x below to ~16 portions.
- **Equipment**: 3 shallow dishes for breading, large skillet, cooling rack, salad bowl.
- **Ingredients (4x batch)**: Vinaigrette: 4" piece fresh ginger, 4 cloves garlic, 1 cup soy sauce, juice of 2 lemons, 4 Tbsp rice wine vinegar, 1.33 cups grapeseed or olive oil, 4 tsp chili flakes, 4 tsp sugar. Chicken: 16 boneless, skinless chicken breasts (sliced into 8 thin fillets each). Crumb coating: 4 cups panko, 1 cup sesame seeds, 2 tsp kosher salt, 1 tsp black pepper. Salad: 16 cups baby spinach, 2 English cucumbers, 2 bunches cilantro, 2 bunches mint, 8 green onions. Breading/frying: 12-20 Tbsp grapeseed or canola oil, 4-8 large eggs, 2 cups all-purpose flour, additional frying oil. Garnish: 4 Tbsp toasted sesame seeds (optional).
- **Procedure**:
  1. Mince ginger and garlic; whisk with soy sauce, lemon juice, rice wine vinegar, sugar, and chili flakes, then whisk in the oil. Set aside (can be made 1-2 days ahead).
  2. Slice each chicken breast horizontally into thin fillets. Toss with a few tablespoons of the vinaigrette; marinate at least 30 minutes (up to overnight, refrigerated).
  3. Combine panko and sesame seeds; season with salt and pepper.
  4. Prep the salad vegetables: clean and dry spinach, cilantro, and mint; slice cucumber and green onions. Combine in a large bowl.
  5. Set up a breading station: flour, beaten eggs, seasoned panko. Dredge each chicken fillet through all three, pressing to coat. Rest breaded pieces on a rack 10 minutes to set.
  6. Shallow-fry in 1/4-1/2" of oil over medium heat until golden brown and cooked through, in batches as needed. Drain on a rack.
  7. Toss the salad with a few tablespoons of vinaigrette (and sesame seeds, if using); taste and adjust.
  8. Plate salad topped with sliced chicken, alternating layers as desired. Serve immediately.
- **Food safety controls**: Standard poultry/egg handling; confirm 165°F internal temperature on the chicken.
- **Allergens**: Gluten, egg, soy, sesame.
- **Quality indicators**: Crisp panko-sesame crust, bright and well-dressed salad (not soggy), balanced sweet-sour-savory vinaigrette.
- **Holding/storage**: Best assembled just before service; fry chicken as close to service as practical.
- **Scaling notes**: Scaled 4x from the source's 4-serving yield.
- **Skill focus**: Butterflying/slicing technique for thin fillets, three-stage breading, composed salad plating, balancing an Asian-style vinaigrette.

## Mini Chicken Parmesan Crostini

- **Course/use case**: Culinary 2, Poultry unit; passed hors d'oeuvre/appetizer.
- **Source and revision date**: `12 - Poultry/Poultry/Mini Chicken Parms.docx`, standardized 2026-07.
- **Yield and portion size**: Not specified in source (depends on baguette length and how small the chicken pieces are cut). **Needs confirmation** - recommend a test batch to establish actual crostini count per the quantities below.
- **Equipment**: Sheet pan, skillet, mixing bowl.
- **Ingredients (3x batch)**: 3 lb chicken tenders or thinly sliced chicken breast, 6 eggs (lightly beaten), 6 cups seasoned bread crumbs, olive oil for frying, 3 French baguettes (thinly sliced), 3 cups ricotta cheese, 3/4 cup grated parmesan cheese, fresh parsley (chopped), 3 cups thick, smooth tomato sauce, 3 cups shredded mozzarella cheese.
- **Procedure**:
  1. Preheat oven to 375°F.
  2. Cut chicken into small (~1") pieces. Dip in beaten egg, then bread crumbs, pressing to coat.
  3. Heat a thin layer of olive oil in a pan over medium heat; fry breaded chicken pieces about 2 minutes per side, until golden and cooked through. Drain on paper towels.
  4. Mix ricotta, parmesan, and a bit of parsley in a small bowl.
  5. Arrange baguette slices on a baking sheet. Spread each with the ricotta mixture, top with a piece of cooked chicken, drizzle with tomato sauce (don't overdo it), and sprinkle with mozzarella.
  6. Bake about 10 minutes, until hot and melty.
  7. *(Make-ahead note from source: chicken can be cooked ahead and refrigerated; add about 5 minutes to the final bake time if starting from cold.)*
- **Food safety controls**: Standard poultry/egg/dairy handling; confirm chicken reaches 165°F during frying; reheat to 165°F if assembled from refrigerated cooked chicken.
- **Allergens**: Gluten, egg, dairy.
- **Quality indicators**: Melted, bubbly cheese topping; crisp baguette base; chicken hot through if reheated.
- **Holding/storage**: Best fresh from the oven for a passed appetizer.
- **Scaling notes**: Scaled 3x from the source (which had no stated yield); confirm actual crostini count in production.
- **Skill focus**: Small-format breading and frying, hors d'oeuvre assembly and portioning, make-ahead/reheat planning for passed appetizers.

## Chicken and Vegetable Stir-Fry

- **Course/use case**: Culinary 1/2, Poultry unit - stir-fry technique.
- **Source and revision date**: `12 - Poultry/Poultry/Chicken and vegetable stir-fry updated.docx`, condensed with the earlier `Chicken and vegetable stir-fry 2019.docx` (same dish, same portions/method - the "updated" file simplifies the cooking fat from sesame oil to vegetable oil, reduces the Szechuan sauce quantity, and drops the fresh herb garnish). Standardized 2026-07 using the updated version as primary, per its own revision marking.

## Chicken with Vinegar (Poulet au Vinaigre)

## Green Curry Duck

- **Course/use case**: Culinary 2, Poultry unit - competition/advanced technique.
- **Source and revision date**: Condensed from two complementary program documents on the same dish: `12 - Poultry/Poultry/Duck prostart.docx` (gives the curry sauce recipe and technique) and `19 - International Cuisine/International Cuisine/Green curry duck.doc` (a formal recipe/food-cost worksheet, West Ashley High, Educator: Jason Wheless, referencing "Curry sauce (see recipe)" without repeating it). Standardized 2026-07 by combining both.
- **Yield and portion size**: 2 portions at 10 oz per the worksheet source. Scaled 8x below to ~16 portions. *Note: duck breast is a premium-cost protein - consider whether this is a special-occasion/competition item rather than a routine batch-service dish before scaling this far in practice.*
- **Equipment**: Small nonstick pan (for rendering duck skin), saucepan, plating equipment.
- **Ingredients (8x batch)**: 16 duck breasts (6 oz each), salt and pepper to taste, 8 oz potato (medium dice), 32 oz green beans (blanched and shocked), 8 oz tomato (concasse), 1 bunch cilantro (chopped). Curry sauce: 8 Tbsp olive oil, 8 onions (finely chopped), 16 cloves garlic (minced), 8 Tbsp minced ginger, 8-16 Tbsp curry paste, 16 cups chicken broth, 4 cups coconut milk, 8 limes (juiced), 2 cups chopped cilantro, 8 tsp fish sauce, 8 tsp brown sugar, 8 potatoes (small dice), 48 grape tomatoes (halved).
- **Procedure**:
  1. Make the curry sauce: sweat onion, ginger, and garlic in olive oil until soft.
  2. Add curry paste; cook until fragrant.
  3. Add chicken broth and coconut milk; simmer 3 minutes.
  4. Stir in lime juice, cilantro, fish sauce, and brown sugar.
  5. Add the diced potatoes; simmer about 20 minutes, until tender.
  6. Add halved grape tomatoes; reserve.
  7. Remove the skin from the duck breasts and chop into small pieces. Render the skin in a small nonstick pan until crispy; remove and season - reserve as a garnish ("cracklings").
  8. Season the duck breasts with salt and pepper.
  9. Reheat the curry sauce; cook the diced potato garnish (separate from the sauce's own potato) until fork-tender. Remove and keep warm.
  10. Poach the duck breasts in the sauce until medium/medium-well. Remove and let rest 5 minutes before slicing.
  11. Reheat green beans in the sauce; add the tomato concasse.
  12. Slice the duck and season. Layer diced potatoes and green beans on the plate; top with sliced duck breast, sauce, duck cracklings, and cilantro.
- **Food safety controls**: Standard poultry handling; confirm duck reaches a safe internal temperature per program policy for poached poultry (duck is often served to a lower "medium" doneness than chicken - confirm the program's approved minimum temperature for duck specifically before serving at "medium/medium-well").
- **Allergens**: Fish (fish sauce), tree nut/coconut (coconut milk - note as an allergen-adjacent ingredient per program policy).
- **Quality indicators**: Duck skin rendered crisp with no rubbery fat left, duck breast cooked evenly to the specified doneness, sauce balanced between curry richness, lime acidity, and fish sauce savoriness.
- **Holding/storage**: Best fresh; duck breast doesn't hold or reheat well without overcooking - cook close to service.
- **Scaling notes**: Scaled 8x from the worksheet's 2-portion yield. See the cost/practicality note above given duck's premium price point.
- **Skill focus**: Duck skin rendering for cracklings, poaching a delicate protein to a precise doneness, building a Thai-style green curry sauce, combining a formal recipe/cost worksheet with its referenced sub-recipe.

## Grilled Duck Breasts with Cranberries

- **Course/use case**: Culinary 2, Poultry unit - nutritional cooking/composed plate.
- **Source and revision date**: `20 - Nutritional Cooking/From TTC/Grilled Duck Breasts with Cranberries.docx`. Standardized 2026-07. Kept separate from the Green Curry Duck above - a red-wine-cranberry pan sauce and a two-stage skin-crisping technique, a different dish and technique from the poached curry preparation.
- **Yield and portion size**: Servings 4 (source yield). Scaled 4x below to 16 servings. *Note: as with the Green Curry Duck, duck breast is a premium protein - consider batch size against program budget before scaling this far in routine production.*
- **Equipment**: Baking sheets (2, for weighting the skin), parchment paper, a baking weight or heavy pot, sauté pan, grill.
- **Ingredients (4x batch)**: 16 duck breasts, salt, freshly ground black pepper, 4 tsp nut oil, 4 Tbsp minced shallots, 2 cups red wine, 2 cups cranberry juice, 16 Tbsp fresh or frozen cranberries, 4 cups duck or chicken stock.
- **Procedure**:
  1. Preheat oven to 350°F.
  2. Peel the skin off the duck breasts; place on a baking sheet. Season the skin with salt and pepper. Cover with parchment; place another baking sheet on top, weighted down (a baking weight or heavy pot). Bake 15 minutes. Remove, flip the skin, drain excess fat into a bowl, and re-weight; bake another 10-15 minutes, until crisp.
  3. Blanch the cranberries in 1/2 cup of the duck or chicken stock, 5 minutes.
  4. Heat nut oil in a sauté pan over medium-high heat. Season duck breasts with salt and pepper; sauté on both sides, keeping the duck very rare. Remove to a plate.
  5. Sauté the shallots in the same pan less than 30 seconds. Deglaze with red wine and cranberry juice; simmer over medium heat until well reduced, 5-8 minutes.
  6. Add the remaining stock; reduce until thick, another 4-8 minutes. Add the blanched cranberries.
  7. About 15 minutes before service, grill the duck breasts briefly to create grill marks.
  8. Let breasts rest 5 minutes before slicing.
  9. Slice duck breasts; serve with the crisped skin as a garnish, sauced around the plate.
- **Food safety controls**: Standard poultry handling; confirm the program's approved minimum internal temperature for duck served rare/medium-rare before finalizing this technique for service - this recipe intentionally serves duck breast pink, which differs from standard chicken safe-temperature practice.
- **Allergens**: None inherent to this recipe (confirm any wine-reduction alcohol content is acceptable per program policy).
- **Quality indicators**: Very crisp, flat skin (from the weighted double-bake), rare-to-medium-rare interior, glossy well-reduced cranberry sauce.
- **Holding/storage**: Best fresh; duck breast doesn't hold or reheat well - cook close to service.
- **Scaling notes**: Scaled 4x from the source's 4-serving yield. See the cost/practicality note above.
- **Skill focus**: Weighted skin-crisping technique (a distinct method from simple pan-searing), cooking duck breast to a specific reduced doneness, building a wine-and-fruit-juice pan sauce, plating with a rendered-skin garnish.
