## Charleston Chewies

- **Course/use case**: Bistro dessert case; direct tie to the Charleston Wine & Food relationship referenced throughout Culinary 2's Tasting Menu unit.
- **Source and revision date**: Adapted from Charlestonwineandfood.com; already scaled 2x to a real batch. Standardized 2026-07.
- **Yield and portion size**: 24 servings (half-sheet pan, cut into squares).
- **Equipment**: Half-sheet pan, mixer.
- **Ingredients**:
  - 2 cups unsalted butter, softened
  - 2 cups granulated sugar
  - 2 cups light brown sugar
  - 4 eggs
  - 4 cups self-rising flour
  - 1.33 Tbsp vanilla extract
  - 0.5 tsp kosher salt
  - Baking spray, or 2 Tbsp each butter and flour (for the pan)
  - Powdered sugar for dusting (optional)
- **Procedure**:
  1. Preheat oven to 350F.
  2. Grease a half-sheet pan with baking spray or butter and flour.
  3. Cream butter and both sugars until smooth and well mixed.
  4. Add eggs one at a time, then the vanilla.
  5. Stir in flour until smooth; add salt.
  6. Spread evenly into the sheet pan.
  7. Bake 45 minutes or until golden brown.
  8. Cool completely before cutting into squares.
  9. Dust with powdered sugar if desired.
- **Food safety controls**: Standard egg/dairy handling; cool completely before cutting/storing.
- **Allergens**: Egg, dairy, gluten.
- **Quality indicators**: Golden brown top, chewy (not cakey or underbaked) interior, clean cut edges once cooled.
- **Holding/storage**: Store covered at room temperature; cut only once fully cooled to prevent tearing.
- **Scaling notes**: Already scaled to a real 24-portion batch on a half-sheet pan - this is the reference scale for other baked-good batches in this program.
- **Skill focus**: Creaming method, weight/volume batch baking, self-rising flour handling, portioning a sheet-pan bake into consistent squares.

## Brownies (2 Half Sheet Pans)

- **Course/use case**: Bistro dessert case; also the recipe referenced throughout Culinary 1 and Culinary 2's Week 2 mise en place/dish-systems labs ("brownie batter lab"). This is the real recipe behind that reference, standardized here for the first time.
- **Source and revision date**: Program recipe (originally a handwritten production card). Standardized 2026-07.
- **Yield and portion size**: 2 half-sheet pans.
- **Equipment**: 2 half-sheet pans, parchment paper, large mixing bowls.
- **Ingredients**:
  - Dry: 10 cups granulated sugar, 4 cups all-purpose flour, 2 2/3 cups cocoa powder, 2 Tbsp salt
  - Wet: 16 large eggs, 2 cups canola oil, 0.5 cup water, 4 tsp vanilla
- **Procedure**:
  1. Preheat oven to 350F. Line 2 half-sheet pans with parchment and lightly spray with cooking spray.
  2. In a medium bowl, combine sugar, flour, cocoa powder, and salt.
  3. In a large bowl, whisk together eggs, canola oil, water, and vanilla.
  4. Sprinkle the dry mix over the wet mix and stir until just combined.
  5. Pour batter into the prepared pans; smooth the top with a spatula.
  6. Bake 50 minutes.
- **Food safety controls**: Standard egg handling.
- **Allergens**: Egg, gluten; confirm cocoa powder processing for any cross-contact.
- **Quality indicators**: Set edges, fudgy (not raw/wet) center, even bake across the full pan.
- **Holding/storage**: Cool completely before cutting; store covered at room temperature.
- **Scaling notes**: Already a real 2-pan batch. This recipe is large enough to split for multiple lab sections in one day.
- **Skill focus**: Basic mise en place and measuring (this is the anchor recipe for Culinary 1/2's earliest brownie batter labs), dry/wet combination technique, full-batch station cleanup and dish-flow practice.

## Jos Louis Cake (Refined)

- **Course/use case**: Bistro dessert case (specialty/celebration item).
- **Source and revision date**: Program recipe, refined version. Standardized 2026-07.
- **Yield and portion size**: One 2-layer 9" cake.
- **Equipment**: 2x 9" cake pans, mixer, candy thermometer (for the filling).
- **Ingredients**:
  - Chocolate cake: 2 cups sugar, 1.75 cups all-purpose flour, 0.75 cup cocoa powder, 2 tsp baking powder, 1 tsp baking soda, 0.5 tsp salt, 1 tsp espresso powder (optional), 2 eggs, 1 cup buttermilk, 1 cup hot coffee, 0.25 cup vegetable oil, 0.25 cup melted butter, 1 tsp vanilla.
  - Marshmallow filling: 2 egg whites, 0.25 tsp cream of tartar, 1 tsp vanilla, 0.75 cup sugar, 2 Tbsp corn syrup, 1/3 cup water, pinch of salt.
  - Ganache: 8 oz chocolate, 8 oz heavy cream, pinch of salt.
- **Procedure**:
  1. Cake: Preheat oven to 350F. Combine dry ingredients; add wet ingredients and mix just until smooth. Divide into two greased 9" pans. Bake 28-32 minutes. Cool completely, then chill before assembling.
  2. Marshmallow filling: Heat sugar, corn syrup, and water to 240F. Whip egg whites and cream of tartar to soft peaks. Slowly stream the hot syrup into the whites while whipping. Whip to stiff peaks; add vanilla and salt. Use immediately.
  3. Ganache: Heat cream to just below a simmer; pour over chocolate and rest 2-3 minutes. Stir smooth; add salt; cool to a pourable consistency.
  4. Assembly: Level cake layers. Spread marshmallow filling evenly, leaving a small border. Top with the second layer; chill 20-30 minutes. Optional thin crumb coat, then chill again. Pour ganache over the cake in one pass; let set.
- **Food safety controls**: Egg whites in the marshmallow filling are cooked via the hot sugar syrup (a cooked meringue method), which addresses raw-egg safety concerns.
- **Allergens**: Egg, dairy, gluten.
- **Quality indicators**: Level layers, marshmallow filling holds its shape without weeping, smooth glossy ganache shell.
- **Holding/storage**: Refrigerate; bring to room temperature briefly before serving for the best texture.
- **Scaling notes**: This is a specialty single-cake item, not typically scaled to a large batch - if needed for a larger event, multiply full cake units rather than scaling the recipe internally.
- **Skill focus**: Cooked meringue (Italian meringue) technique, ganache consistency control, cake assembly and leveling.

## Rice Krispie Treats (Two Current Variants)

**Note**: two different rice krispie treat recipes exist in current use - documented here as separate entries rather than merged, since they may serve different purposes (a themed treat vs. a bakery-style upsell item). Confirm with the instructor whether both stay in rotation.

### Lucky Charms Rice Krispy Treats
- **Course/use case**: Bistro dessert case (themed/seasonal item).
- **Yield and portion size**: One half-sheet pan.
- **Ingredients**: 10 Tbsp butter; 20 cups mini marshmallows (16 cups melted, 4 cups folded in at the end); 8 cups Rice Krispies; 2 cups Fruity Pebbles; 2 cups Lucky Charms; 1 tsp kosher salt.
- **Procedure**: Melt butter and brown slightly. Add 16 cups marshmallows over low heat until melted. Stir in Rice Krispies, Fruity Pebbles, and salt. Cool 20-30 seconds. Fold in remaining 4 cups marshmallows and the Lucky Charms. Lightly press into a half-sheet pan (do not compact hard, or the treats turn dense). Cool completely, then cut.
- **Food safety controls**: Standard dairy handling.
- **Allergens**: Dairy, gluten (cereal).
- **Quality indicators**: Soft, not compacted or hard; even cereal distribution.
- **Holding/storage**: Store covered at room temperature; best within a day or two before marshmallow staling sets in.
- **Scaling notes**: Already a real half-sheet batch.
- **Skill focus**: Sugar/marshmallow melting without scorching, folding technique to preserve texture, timing the "cool 20-30 seconds" step correctly.

### Bakery-Style Giant Rice Krispie Treats
- **Course/use case**: Bistro dessert case (bakery-style upsell item).
- **Source and revision date**: Adapted from Abeautifulmess.com; standardized 2026-07.
- **Yield and portion size**: 12 treats (or 9, for genuinely "giant" treats).
- **Ingredients**: 18 Tbsp butter; 1.125 cups sweetened condensed milk; 2.25 tsp vanilla extract; 15 oz mini marshmallows (for melting); 9 oz mini marshmallows (unmelted, folded in); 13.5 cups Rice Krispies cereal.
- **Procedure**: Brown the butter until dark gold/amber and nutty-smelling (optional but recommended - or simply melt it). Add condensed milk and vanilla; stir well; reduce heat to medium-low. Add 10 oz of the marshmallows; stir until smooth. Add Rice Krispies; stir to combine (transfer to a large bowl first if needed). Fold in the remaining 6 oz marshmallows for texture. Pour into a buttered half-sheet pan; smooth with parchment paper pressed over the top. Cool at least an hour before cutting - 9 pieces for genuinely giant treats.
- **Food safety controls**: Standard dairy handling.
- **Allergens**: Dairy, gluten (cereal).
- **Quality indicators**: Rich caramelized flavor from browned butter (if used), soft chew, thick bakery-style portion.
- **Holding/storage**: Store covered at room temperature.
- **Scaling notes**: Already a real half-sheet batch.
- **Skill focus**: Browning butter without burning it, condensed milk incorporation, portioning "giant" vs. standard treat sizes.

## S'Mores Bar

- **Course/use case**: Bistro dessert case.
- **Source and revision date**: Program recipe. Standardized 2026-07.
- **Yield and portion size**: One 9"x13" pan.
- **Equipment**: 9"x13" baking dish.
- **Ingredients**:
  - Crust: 1.5 cups graham cracker crumbs, 0.5 cup melted butter, 0.25 cup sugar.
  - Cookie layer: 1 cup unsalted butter (softened), 1 cup brown sugar, 0.5 cup granulated sugar, 2 large eggs, 2 tsp vanilla extract, 2.5 cups all-purpose flour, 1 tsp baking soda, 0.5 tsp salt, 1 cup chocolate chips, 1 cup mini marshmallows.
- **Procedure**:
  1. Preheat oven to 350F. Grease a 9"x13" baking dish.
  2. Mix graham cracker crumbs, melted butter, and sugar; press into the bottom of the dish to form the crust.
  3. Cream butter and both sugars until fluffy. Add eggs and vanilla; mix well.
  4. Whisk flour, baking soda, and salt; gradually add to the wet mixture until just combined.
  5. Fold in chocolate chips.
  6. Spread half the cookie dough over the graham cracker crust. Sprinkle chocolate chips and mini marshmallows evenly on top. Dollop the remaining cookie dough over the marshmallows.
  7. Bake 25-30 minutes, until edges are golden and the center is set.
  8. Optional: broil 1-2 minutes to toast the marshmallows.
  9. Cool completely before cutting into bars.
- **Food safety controls**: Standard egg/dairy handling.
- **Allergens**: Egg, dairy, gluten.
- **Quality indicators**: Set center, toasted marshmallow top if broiled, clean-cut bars once fully cooled.
- **Holding/storage**: Store covered at room temperature; cool completely before cutting to avoid a gooey mess.
- **Scaling notes**: Already a real 9x13 batch; double for a half-sheet pan if a larger batch is needed.
- **Skill focus**: Layering technique (crust, dough, filling, dough), timing a bake around a gooey marshmallow layer, optional broiler use.

## Overnight Chocolate Chip Cookies

- **Course/use case**: Bistro dessert case; Culinary 1/2 creaming-method vehicle.
- **Source and revision date**: Program recipe, already production-ready.
- **Yield and portion size**: 2 dozen, 2 oz portions.
- **Equipment**: Mixer with paddle attachment, blue portion scoop (2 oz), sheet pans, plastic wrap.
- **Ingredients**:
  - Cake flour: 8.5 oz
  - Bread flour: 8.5 oz
  - Baking powder: 8 g
  - Baking soda: 6 g
  - Coarse salt: 8 g
  - Butter, room temperature: 10 oz
  - Light brown sugar: 10 oz
  - Granulated sugar: 8 oz
  - Eggs, room temperature: 2 each (3.5 oz)
  - Vanilla extract: 10 g
  - Chocolate chips: 1 lb 4 oz
- **Procedure**:
  1. Sift together flours, baking soda, and baking powder; add salt.
  2. Cream butter and sugars with a paddle until light and fluffy; scrape bowl and paddle.
  3. Add eggs and vanilla slowly.
  4. Add dry ingredients.
  5. Stir in chocolate chips.
  6. Portion with a 2 oz blue scoop onto paper-lined sheet pans (8 per pan), or roll into a log, wrap, and chill for slicing.
  7. Wrap pans well with plastic wrap and refrigerate 24-36 hours.
  8. Remove from the walk-in and let sit at room temperature 15 minutes before baking.
- **Food safety controls**: Standard egg/dairy handling; keep dough refrigerated during the required rest, not left at room temperature.
- **Allergens**: Egg, dairy, gluten.
- **Quality indicators**: Even chip distribution, consistent portion size, chewy center with a slightly crisp edge after baking.
- **Holding/storage**: Dough holds refrigerated 24-36 hours as part of the method (this rest is required, not optional - it develops flavor and texture). Baked cookies store at room temperature in a covered container.
- **Scaling notes**: Scales by weight - straightforward to double or halve using the gram/ounce measures given.
- **Skill focus**: Creaming method, precise scaling by weight, portion control, planning production around a required overnight rest.

## Chocolate Chip Muffins

- **Course/use case**: Bistro dessert/bakery case; Culinary 2 Week 20 (Scaling by Weight) anchor recipe.
- **Source and revision date**: Program recipe, already in weight-based format. Standardized 2026-07 - yield was not previously specified.
- **Yield and portion size**: Not stated in the source; estimate ~18-24 standard muffins depending on portion size. Confirm and record actual yield the next time this is produced.
- **Equipment**: Muffin tin, liners, scale.
- **Ingredients**:
  - Dry: 260 g all-purpose flour, 160 g sugar, 10 g baking powder, 3 g baking soda, 5 g kosher salt
  - Wet: 2 large eggs (100 g), 180 g buttermilk, 105 g vegetable oil, 8 g vanilla extract
  - Fold-in: 180-200 g chocolate chips
- **Procedure**:
  1. Preheat oven to 375F.
  2. Whisk dry ingredients together.
  3. Whisk wet ingredients together.
  4. Combine wet into dry; fold just until combined (batter will be lumpy).
  5. Fold in chocolate chips.
  6. Rest batter 15-20 minutes.
  7. Portion into a lined muffin tin, filling 3/4 to slightly over.
  8. Bake 5 minutes at 375F, then reduce to 350F and bake 12-15 minutes more.
  9. Cool before removing from the pan.
- **Food safety controls**: Standard egg/dairy handling.
- **Allergens**: Egg, dairy, gluten.
- **Quality indicators**: Domed top, even chip distribution, moist crumb (not dense or gummy).
- **Holding/storage**: Store covered at room temperature; cool completely before wrapping to avoid trapped steam.
- **Scaling notes**: Already in a clean weight-based format, ready to scale directly - this is the reference recipe for teaching Culinary 2's scaling-by-weight lesson. Record the actual yield next production run so this entry can be corrected.
- **Skill focus**: Weight-based scaling, batter rest time, oven temperature step-down technique.

## M&M Cookies

- **Course/use case**: Bistro dessert case.
- **Source and revision date**: Adapted from a Reddit recipe; standardized 2026-07. Yield not stated in source.
- **Yield and portion size**: Not specified; a standard batch of this size typically yields ~24 large cookies. Confirm and record actual yield next production run.
- **Equipment**: Stand mixer, sheet pans.
- **Ingredients**:
  - 2.25 cups all-purpose flour
  - 1 tsp baking soda
  - 1 tsp salt
  - 0.5 cup shortening
  - 0.5 cup unsalted butter, softened
  - 0.75 cup granulated sugar
  - 0.75 cup brown sugar
  - 1 tsp vanilla extract
  - 2 eggs
  - 1.5 cups candy-coated chocolate pieces
- **Procedure**:
  1. Preheat oven to 375F.
  2. Whisk flour, baking soda, and salt.
  3. Cream shortening, butter, both sugars, and vanilla until well combined.
  4. Add eggs one at a time, blending fully after each.
  5. Gradually add the flour mixture, blending evenly.
  6. Gently stir in the candy pieces.
  7. Portion large scoops onto sheet pans, leaving space between each.
  8. Bake about 12 minutes at 375F (edges golden), or 350F for 10-12 minutes for a softer cookie.
  9. Cool on the pan briefly, then transfer to a rack to cool completely.
- **Food safety controls**: Standard egg/dairy handling.
- **Allergens**: Egg, dairy, gluten; confirm current candy supplier for any additional allergens (may contain peanut traces depending on brand/facility).
- **Quality indicators**: Golden edges, soft center, even candy distribution.
- **Holding/storage**: Store covered at room temperature.
- **Scaling notes**: No confirmed yield on file - record actual cookie count next time this is produced so this entry can be corrected with a real number.
- **Skill focus**: Creaming method, consistent portioning, adjusting bake time for texture preference.

## Bacon Jam

- **Course/use case**: Bistro condiment/component (e.g., a Bodega upgrade or side condiment).
- **Source and revision date**: Adapted from Leitesculinaria.com; standardized 2026-07.
- **Yield and portion size**: 12 servings (as a condiment, roughly 1-2 Tbsp per serving).
- **Equipment**: Large skillet, food processor, slow cooker (optional).
- **Ingredients**:
  - 1.5 lb sliced bacon, cut crosswise into 1" pieces
  - 2 medium yellow onions, small dice
  - 3 garlic cloves, smashed and peeled
  - 0.75 cup strongly brewed coffee
  - 0.5 cup cider vinegar
  - 0.5 cup packed dark brown sugar, or less to taste
  - 0.25 cup maple syrup
- **Procedure**:
  1. Cook bacon in a skillet over medium heat until crisp, about 20 minutes, stirring occasionally. Transfer to paper-towel-lined plates to drain.
  2. Pour off all but 1 Tbsp drippings. Add onion and garlic; cook until translucent, about 6 minutes.
  3. Add coffee, vinegar, brown sugar, and maple syrup; bring to a boil, scraping up browned bits, for 2 minutes. Add bacon back in and stir to combine.
  4. Stovetop method: reduce to a bare simmer, cook uncovered, stirring occasionally, until the liquid nearly evaporates and turns syrupy, 1-1.5 hours.
  5. Slow-cooker method: transfer to a 6-quart slow cooker, cook on high uncovered until syrupy, 3.5-4 hours.
  6. Cool slightly, then pulse in a food processor until coarsely chopped.
- **Food safety controls**: Bacon cooked to visibly rendered/crisp; cool before refrigerating.
- **Allergens**: None inherent; confirm bacon supplier.
- **Quality indicators**: Thick, syrupy, jammy texture - not soupy or dry; balanced sweet/savory/acid.
- **Holding/storage**: Refrigerate in a covered container; this is a slow-cook, make-ahead item, not a same-day production item.
- **Scaling notes**: Scales linearly. Long cook time makes this a good "start early, finish later" prep-day item rather than a same-service production task.
- **Skill focus**: Long reduction/caramelization technique, balancing sweet/acid/savory, batch scheduling around a multi-hour cook.

## Blueberry Coulis

- **Course/use case**: Bistro topping/sauce (e.g., for Belgian Waffles).
- **Source and revision date**: Adapted from Chefnotrequired.com; standardized 2026-07.
- **Yield and portion size**: About 500 g finished sauce (roughly 16-20 topping portions at 1-2 Tbsp each).
- **Equipment**: Saucepan, immersion blender or food processor, fine sieve.
- **Ingredients**:
  - 500 g blueberries (fresh or frozen)
  - 40 g granulated sugar, plus more to taste
  - 2 Tbsp fresh lemon juice
- **Procedure**:
  1. Combine blueberries, sugar, and lemon juice in a saucepan; stir well.
  2. Bring to a simmer; simmer uncovered 10-15 minutes, stirring occasionally, until fruit is soft and the sauce has thickened.
  3. Adjust sugar to taste.
  4. Remove from heat; blend until smooth (immersion blender off the heat, or transfer to a blender/processor once slightly cooled).
  5. Pass through a fine sieve for a smooth finish.
- **Food safety controls**: Standard produce handling; cool before refrigerating.
- **Allergens**: None inherent.
- **Quality indicators**: Smooth, pourable but not watery consistency; balanced sweet/tart.
- **Holding/storage**: Refrigerate in a covered container; thin with a small amount of water if it thickens too much on standing.
- **Scaling notes**: Scales linearly by weight - simple to double or triple for a larger batch.
- **Skill focus**: Reduction and thickening by evaporation, blending safety (blending hot liquid off heat), straining technique.

## Crème Anglaise (Ice Cream Base Ratio)

- **Course/use case**: Culinary 2, Baking & Pastry unit - foundational custard technique; base for churned ice creams (e.g., the Pacojet/ice cream items in this bank).
- **Source and revision date**: `8 - Stocks & Sauces/CRÈME ANGLAISE (RATIO FOR ICE CREAM BASE).docx`, standardized 2026-07.
- **Yield and portion size**: Base ratio as written yields about 1 cup; scales linearly as a ratio for larger ice cream batches.
- **Equipment**: Stainless steel saucepan, mixing bowl, whisk, fine strainer, ice bath.
- **Ingredients**: 1 cup milk, 1/8 vanilla bean, 3 egg yolks, 2 oz sugar.
- **Procedure**:
  1. Split the vanilla bean; scrape out the flecks. Add both pod and flecks to a saucepan with the milk.
  2. Bring to a simmer; remove from heat.
  3. In a separate bowl, whisk egg yolks and sugar until light and thick (ribbon stage).
  4. Temper the hot milk into the egg mixture gradually, whisking constantly.
  5. Return to the pan; heat until nappe consistency (coats the back of a spoon).
  6. Strain into a bowl set over an ice bath to stop the cooking.
- **Food safety controls**: Cook the custard to a safe egg temperature (nappe stage) without scrambling; cool promptly over an ice bath.
- **Allergens**: Dairy, egg.
- **Quality indicators**: Smooth, nappe consistency, no scrambled egg bits (a sign of overheating), balanced vanilla flavor.
- **Holding/storage**: Refrigerate; use as an ice cream base within a few days.
- **Scaling notes**: This is a ratio recipe (roughly 1 cup milk : 3 yolks : 2 oz sugar) - scale proportionally to any batch size.
- **Skill focus**: Tempering egg yolks without scrambling them, judging nappe/ribbon stages by feel and sight, rapid cooling technique to halt residual cooking.

## Strawberry Panna Cotta

- **Course/use case**: Culinary 2, Baking/Garde Manger - Desserts unit; portioned molded gelatin dessert.
- **Source and revision date**: Program recipe, "20 unmolded 2 oz servings." Standardized 2026-07.
- **Yield and portion size**: 20 portions at 2 oz each, plus a small buffer - within the program's target range.
- **Equipment**: Saucepan, fine mesh strainer, 20 two-ounce molds or ramekins, blender or immersion blender.
- **Ingredients**: **Strawberry purée**: 680 g strawberries (hulled), 150 g sugar, 20 g lemon juice, 2 g kosher salt. **Panna cotta base**: 720 g heavy cream, 360 g whole milk, 150 g sugar, 14 g powdered gelatin (about 5.5 tsp / 2.25 standard packets), 90 g cold water, 10 g vanilla extract, 2 g kosher salt, 300 g strained strawberry purée (from above).
- **Procedure**: Combine the strawberries, sugar, lemon juice, and salt in a saucepan; cook over medium heat until the berries collapse and release juice, 6-8 minutes. Blend smooth, then return to the pan and simmer gently until at least 300 g strained purée remains (extra puree may be reserved for other uses). Strain through a fine mesh strainer; cool until warm, not hot. Sprinkle the gelatin evenly over the cold water; let stand 5-10 minutes to bloom (don't dump it in a pile). Combine the cream, milk, sugar, vanilla, and salt; heat until steaming and the sugar dissolves, without boiling (high heat weakens gelatin's setting power). Remove from heat; whisk in the bloomed gelatin until fully dissolved. Whisk in the 300 g strained strawberry purée. Strain the finished mixture into a pitcher. Lightly spray the molds, wiping out most of the spray with a paper towel; fill each with 2 fl oz (about 60 g) of the panna cotta mixture. Cover and chill at least 8 hours, ideally 24 hours for full setting. **Unmolding**: dip each mold in warm water 3-5 seconds, dry the outside, place a plate on top, invert, and give one firm downward shake; dip again briefly if it doesn't release.
- **Food safety controls**: Standard dairy/gelatin handling; keep chilled throughout setting.
- **Allergens**: Dairy.
- **Quality indicators**: Silky, fully-set texture (not rubbery from over-gelatin or weeping from under-set), clean unmolding with an intact shape, balanced fresh-strawberry flavor.
- **Holding/storage**: Refrigerate at least 8 hours before serving (24 hours ideal per source, citing David Lebovitz on gelatin-dessert set times); keeps several days chilled.
- **Scaling notes**: Source yield of 20 portions at 2 oz is within the program's target range; scales linearly by gram weight for a larger batch.
- **Skill focus**: Proper gelatin blooming and dissolving technique, controlled dairy heating to protect gelatin's set, clean unmolding technique via a brief warm-water dip.

## Salted Caramel Ganache Dark Chocolate

- **Course/use case**: Culinary 2, Baking/Chocolate work - bonbon filling.
- **Source and revision date**: Isugarcoatit.com, "Salted Caramel Ganache Dark Chocolate." Standardized 2026-07.
- **Yield and portion size**: Serves 20 (source yield); a filling/component yield within the program's target range.
- **Equipment**: Deep pot, small pot, medium bowl, hand whisk, piping bag, prepared molded chocolate shells.
- **Ingredients**: 40 g water, 10 g glucose, 50 g sugar, 137.5 g cream (35%), 250 g dark chocolate (such as Valrhona Caraibe or a similar 66% dark chocolate), fleur de sel to taste.
- **Procedure**: In a deep pot, combine the water, glucose, and sugar; cook until caramelized. Remove from heat. Warm the cream in a separate pot; remove from heat and gradually add to the caramelized sugar. Stir in salt to taste. Place the chocolate in a medium bowl (chop finely if using blocks, for faster melting). Pour the salted caramel over the dark chocolate and use a hand whisk to stir until combined and smooth. Allow to cool at room temperature. Once cooled, pipe into prepared molded chocolate shells, allow to set, and then cap.
- **Food safety controls**: Standard handling; caramelized sugar is extremely hot - handle with care when combining with cream.
- **Allergens**: Dairy.
- **Quality indicators**: Smooth, glossy ganache (fully emulsified, not split or grainy), balanced salted-caramel flavor, clean set for capping bonbons.
- **Holding/storage**: Cool to room temperature before piping; filled/capped bonbons store per standard chocolate-work practice (cool, dry conditions).
- **Scaling notes**: Source yield of 20 servings scales linearly by ingredient ratio for a larger batch.
- **Skill focus**: Dry-caramelizing sugar with glucose to control crystallization, emulsifying a hot caramel into chocolate for a smooth ganache, piping and capping molded bonbons.
