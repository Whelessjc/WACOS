## Classic Chicken Salad

- **Course/use case**: Culinary 1/2, Garde Manger unit - cold chicken salad for sandwich or salad-plate service.
- **Source and revision date**: spendwithpennies.com, "Classic Chicken Salad." Standardized 2026-07.
- **Yield and portion size**: Serves 6 (source yield); a small-batch cold-prep yield, left as written - scale up for program batch service (see Scaling notes).
- **Equipment**: Mixing bowl, knife and board.
- **Ingredients**: 2 cups cooked chicken (chopped), 1/2 cup mayonnaise, 1 stalk celery (chopped), 1 green onion (diced; or chives or red onion), 1 tsp Dijon mustard, 1/2 tsp seasoned salt, pepper to taste, 1 tsp fresh dill (optional).
- **Procedure**: Combine all ingredients in a bowl and mix well. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Serve as a sandwich filling or over salad greens.
- **Food safety controls**: Standard cooked-poultry and mayonnaise-based salad handling; cool cooked chicken fully before combining; hold cold (41°F or below).
- **Allergens**: Egg (mayonnaise), mustard.
- **Quality indicators**: Moist but not soupy (mayonnaise coats without pooling), visible celery/onion dice for texture, balanced tang from mustard and optional dill.
- **Holding/storage**: Refrigerate airtight; best within 3-4 days.
- **Scaling notes**: Source yield of 6 servings is a small-batch cold-prep yield; scale linearly by chicken weight for a program-size batch (16-30 portions).
- **Skill focus**: Basic cold-salad assembly technique - even dice work on aromatics, binding a protein salad with mayonnaise without breaking or over-saturating it.

- **Course/use case**: Culinary 1/2, Garde Manger unit - fruit-and-nut chicken salad, a distinct flavor direction from the Classic Chicken Salad above (Waldorf-style sweet/savory/crunch vs. a straightforward mayo-mustard-dill salad) - kept as a separate entry per the dedup rule for genuinely different flavor profiles, not a minor variance.
- **Source and revision date**: Program recipe (no external attribution). Standardized 2026-07. **Needs confirmation** - source gave no ingredient quantities at all; amounts below are placeholders and must be set by an instructor before production use.
- **Yield and portion size**: Not specified in source. **Needs confirmation** for a target yield and exact quantities.
- **Equipment**: Oven or roasting pan, mixing bowl, knife and board.
- **Ingredients**: Chicken (quantity **needs confirmation**), salt and pepper to taste, onion (quantity **needs confirmation**), celery (quantity **needs confirmation**), grapes (quantity **needs confirmation**), cashews (quantity **needs confirmation**), mayonnaise (quantity **needs confirmation**), red wine vinegar (quantity **needs confirmation**).
- **Procedure**: Season and roast the chicken at 450°F for 1 hour. Pull the meat and combine with the remaining ingredients. Season to taste.

- **Allergens**: Egg (mayonnaise), tree nuts (cashews).
- **Quality indicators**: Well-shredded/pulled chicken (not stringy), grapes and cashews distributed for sweetness and crunch in every bite, balanced tang from the vinegar against the mayonnaise's richness.

## Duck "Prosciutto"

- **Course/use case**: Culinary 2, Garde Manger unit - basic dry-cure charcuterie technique.
- **Source and revision date**: Program recipe. Standardized 2026-07.
- **Yield and portion size**: 1 duck breast (single-item cure); not scaled to the program's 16-30 portion batch convention since curing is typically done per-breast with a fixed cure ratio - see Scaling notes.
- **Equipment**: Sanitized side towels, sheet pan or tray, weight (e.g., 1 lb butter block) for pressing, refrigerator space, slicer or sharp knife.
- **Ingredients**: 1 duck breast, 1/2 cup kosher salt, 1/4 cup sugar, 1 Tbsp dried thyme.
- **Procedure**: Combine the cure ingredients (salt, sugar, thyme). Lay a sanitized side towel flat on a bench and place a 1/2" layer of cure in the center. Place the duck breast on the cure and cover with another 1/2" layer, then wrap the towel tightly around the breast so it is fully encased in cure. Press the wrapped breast with a 1 lb weight (e.g., a block of butter) in the refrigerator. Turn the breast after 12 hours. Cure for a total of 24-36 hours, or until the meat is firm. Rinse off the cure and pat dry. Slice ultra-thin by hand or with a slicer, keeping the prosciutto very cold while slicing.
- **Food safety controls**: Standard dry-curing food safety - keep refrigerated throughout the entire cure (never at room temperature), use a scale to confirm cure ratios are food-safe rather than eyeballing, discard the used side towel/cure after single use, keep the cured product cold when slicing and serving.
- **Allergens**: None inherent to this recipe.
- **Quality indicators**: Firm, dense texture throughout after curing (no soft/raw spots at the center), even seasoning penetration, ultra-thin, non-tearing slices.
- **Holding/storage**: Refrigerate the finished cured breast; slice to order for best texture. Use within about a week of curing.
- **Scaling notes**: Scales by duck breast count, keeping the cure ratio (salt : sugar : thyme) constant per breast; not a batch-yield recipe in the same sense as a cooked dish.
- **Skill focus**: Basic dry-cure charcuterie method - building and applying a salt/sugar cure, weighting during the cure, and slicing a cured product paper-thin.

## Smoked Salmon Platter

- **Course/use case**: Bistro/brunch service, Garde Manger unit - composed cold platter, no-cook assembly (aside from soft-boiling eggs).
- **Source and revision date**: howsweeteats.com, "Smoked Salmon Platter." Standardized 2026-07.
- **Yield and portion size**: Serves 6 (source yield); within the program's target range on the low end - scale up proportionally for a larger batch.
- **Equipment**: Small saucepan, slotted spoon, ice bath, serving platter.
- **Ingredients**: 4 eggs (soft-boiled), 1-2 lb smoked salmon, 1 (8 oz) block cream cheese, 1 English cucumber (sliced into rounds or lengthwise), 1 red onion (diced or sliced into rounds), 1 avocado (thinly sliced), 1 pint cherry tomatoes (halved), 1/2 cup fresh dill (some chopped), 1/3 cup capers, 1/4 cup everything seasoning, 1 lemon (thinly sliced), bagels/toast/crackers for serving, freshly cracked pepper for serving, extra whipped cream cheese if desired.
- **Procedure**: Bring about 3" of water to a boil in a small saucepan; reduce to a bare simmer, gently add the eggs, and cook 6 minutes. Remove with a slotted spoon into an ice bath; let cool completely before peeling. To assemble, place the block of cream cheese in the center of a platter and top with cracked pepper, fresh dill, everything seasoning, and capers. Surround with the salmon, peeled soft-boiled eggs, cucumber, onion, tomatoes, extra dill, lemon slices, and bagels/toast/crackers. Serve immediately.
- **Food safety controls**: Standard smoked-fish and dairy handling; keep the platter cold until service; cool the soft-boiled eggs fully in an ice bath before peeling and holding.
- **Allergens**: Fish, dairy, egg, tree nuts (if an everything-seasoning blend includes them - confirm blend used).
- **Quality indicators**: Neatly composed platter with distinct color blocks (not overlapping/muddled), soft-boiled eggs with a just-set white and jammy yolk, cream cheese soft enough to spread easily.
- **Holding/storage**: Best assembled just before service and held cold; not a dish that holds well pre-composed for long periods.
- **Scaling notes**: Source yield of 6 servings is on the low end of the program's target range; scales linearly by ingredient ratio and platter size for a larger batch.
- **Skill focus**: Soft-boiling and peeling eggs cleanly, composing a visually balanced cold platter from multiple components without a cooked sauce or dressing to unify it.
