## The Best Salsa in the Universe

- **Course/use case**: Bistro condiment (chips, tacos, general use).
- **Source and revision date**: Program recipe. Standardized 2026-07.
- **Yield and portion size**: 1 quart.
- **Equipment**: Sheet pan (for roasting), food processor or blender, saucepan.
- **Ingredients**: 2 lb fresh tomatoes, 2 cups crushed canned tomatoes, 0.5 large red onion (quartered), 1 red bell pepper, 2-3 canned jalapenos (seeded for milder heat), 3 cloves garlic (minced), 0.5 bunch fresh cilantro (stems and leaves), juice of 2 limes, 2 tsp kosher salt, 1 tsp black pepper, 1.5 tsp ground cumin, 1 tsp chili powder, 1 tsp oregano.
- **Procedure**:
  1. Toss tomatoes, onion, bell pepper, and garlic with a little salt; roast at 425F until lightly blistered, about 20 minutes (adds char and sweetness - skip and go raw for a brighter salsa if time is short).
  2. Add roasted vegetables, cilantro, lime juice, jalapenos, and seasonings to a food processor; pulse until mostly smooth but still textured. Add a splash of crushed tomatoes if it needs more body.
  3. Simmer in a pot 20-30 minutes to marry the flavors.
  4. Taste and adjust: more salt if flat, more lime if dull, more cilantro if you want it grassier, a splash of water if it feels heavy.
- **Food safety controls**: Standard produce handling; cool before refrigerating.
- **Allergens**: None inherent.
- **Quality indicators**: Balanced heat, acid, and salt; texture still has some chunk, not a puree.
- **Holding/storage**: Refrigerate in a covered container.
- **Scaling notes**: Scales linearly by the quart.
- **Skill focus**: Roasting for flavor development, balancing acid/heat/salt by taste, food processor texture control (pulsing vs. pureeing).

## Roasted Tomatillo Salsa (Salsa Verde)

- **Course/use case**: Bistro condiment (pairs with Smoked Carnitas Tacos in Lunch.md).
- **Source and revision date**: Adapted from an external source; standardized 2026-07.
- **Yield and portion size**: About 2 cups.
- **Equipment**: Baking sheet, blender or food processor.
- **Ingredients**: 6 medium tomatillos (husked and rinsed), 1 small white onion (quartered), 2 cloves garlic (peeled), 1-2 jalapenos (stemmed, seeds removed for less heat), 0.5 cup chopped fresh cilantro, 1 Tbsp fresh lime juice, 0.5 tsp salt, 1 Tbsp olive oil.
- **Procedure**:
  1. Preheat oven to 450F or set a broiler on high.
  2. Place tomatillos, onion, garlic, and jalapenos on a baking sheet; drizzle with olive oil.
  3. Roast 10-12 minutes, flipping halfway, until slightly charred.
  4. Transfer to a blender or food processor; add cilantro, lime juice, and salt.
  5. Pulse until smooth but still slightly chunky.
  6. Taste and adjust seasoning with more salt or lime as needed.
- **Food safety controls**: Standard produce handling.
- **Allergens**: None inherent.
- **Quality indicators**: Balanced tang from tomatillos and lime, visible slight char flavor, not over-blended.
- **Holding/storage**: Refrigerate in an airtight container up to 5 days.
- **Scaling notes**: Scales linearly by tomatillo count.
- **Skill focus**: Roasting/charring for flavor, blending to a controlled texture, tasting and adjusting acid/salt balance.

## Savory Onion Jam (Chicken + Mozzarella Focaccia)

- **Course/use case**: Bistro condiment (sandwich component, pairs with Focaccia in Lunch.md).
- **Source and revision date**: Program recipe, written in a student-friendly, batch-consistent style. Standardized 2026-07.
- **Yield and portion size**: About 2.5-3 cups (600-700 g), depending on reduction.
- **Equipment**: Wide saute pan or rondeau.
- **Ingredients**: 1.5 lb yellow onions (thinly sliced, 680 g), 2 Tbsp olive oil, 1 Tbsp unsalted butter, 1 tsp kosher salt, 1 Tbsp granulated sugar, 3 Tbsp white wine or champagne vinegar, 2 Tbsp water, 0.5 tsp black pepper, optional 1 tsp fresh thyme (finely chopped).
- **Procedure**:
  1. Heat olive oil and butter in a wide pan over medium-low heat.
  2. Add onions and salt; cook 30-40 minutes, stirring every few minutes, until fully soft and lightly golden - no aggressive browning; the goal is tenderness and natural sweetness, not deep caramel bitterness.
  3. Add sugar; cook 5 more minutes to build gentle sweetness without making it dessert-like.
  4. Add vinegar and water; stir and scrape the pan well. Reduce on low heat 15-20 minutes until jammy but still soft and spreadable.
  5. Stir in black pepper and thyme if using. Taste and adjust salt or add a splash more vinegar if it feels flat.
- **Food safety controls**: Standard produce handling; cool before refrigerating.
- **Allergens**: Dairy (butter).
- **Quality indicators**: Jammy, spreadable texture - not caramelized to a deep brown or bitter; balanced sweet/acid.
- **Holding/storage**: Refrigerate in a covered container.
- **Scaling notes**: Scales linearly by onion weight; the long, low cook time is the real constraint on batch timing, not the ratios.
- **Skill focus**: Low-and-slow sweating (distinct from caramelizing), deglazing and reduction, tasting for balance rather than following a fixed cook time.

## Smash Burger Sauce

- **Course/use case**: Bistro condiment.
- **Source and revision date**: Program recipe. Standardized 2026-07.
- **Yield and portion size**: 3-4 cups.
- **Equipment**: Mixing bowl.
- **Ingredients**: 2 cups mayonnaise, 0.5 cup ketchup, 3 Tbsp all-purpose seasoning, 0.5 cup pickle juice, 0.25 cup chopped pickles.
- **Procedure**: Mix all ingredients together until smooth.
- **Food safety controls**: Standard mayonnaise-based sauce handling; hold cold.
- **Allergens**: Egg (mayonnaise).
- **Quality indicators**: Smooth, well-combined, balanced tang from pickle juice.
- **Holding/storage**: Refrigerate in a covered container; standard mayonnaise-based product shelf life applies.
- **Scaling notes**: Scales linearly - a simple mix-only recipe with no real batch ceiling.
- **Skill focus**: Ratio-based sauce building, tasting and adjusting a no-cook condiment.
