## Braised Pork Shoulder

- **Course/use case**: Culinary 2, Advanced Cooking Techniques unit - oven-braising technique (contrast to the low-and-slow smoked pulled pork entries below).
- **Source and revision date**: Program recipe ("My team"). Standardized 2026-07.
- **Yield and portion size**: 4 portions at 4 oz each.
- **Equipment**: Sauté pan, hotel pan, oven, sauce pan, fine strainer.
- **Ingredients**: 1 oz vegetable oil, 1 lb pork shoulder, salt and pepper (to taste), 1 onion (julienne), 1 carrot (rough chop), 2 cloves garlic (smashed), 1 sprig rosemary, 1 sprig thyme, 2 cups chicken stock, 2 Tbsp butter.
- **Procedure**: Heat the oil in a sauté pan. Cut the pork into 4 portions and season. Brown the pork on all sides; transfer to a hotel pan. Add the vegetables to the sauté pan and cook until caramelized. Deglaze with the stock and bring to a boil. Pour over the pork, cover, and cook at 300°F for 3 hours. Remove the pork (and vegetables, if desired) and strain the sauce into a new sauce pan. Reduce the sauce until somewhat thick. Remove from heat and swirl in the cold butter. Serve the sauce over the pork.
- **Food safety controls**: Standard pork handling; confirm the pork is fork-tender and fully cooked before serving.
- **Allergens**: Dairy (butter).
- **Quality indicators**: Fork-tender pork from the long braise, glossy monté au beurre sauce (not broken/oily), well-caramelized aromatic base carrying through the sauce.
- **Holding/storage**: Holds and reheats well in its sauce, refrigerated a few days.
- **Scaling notes**: Scales linearly by pork weight and proportional stock/vegetable ratio.
- **Skill focus**: Classic braise sequence (sear, build aromatic base, deglaze, low slow cook, reduce and mount the sauce with cold butter), a contrast to the dry-rub-and-smoke technique used in the pulled pork entries below.

## Carolina Pulled Pork Barbecue Sandwich

- **Course/use case**: Culinary 2, Advanced Cooking Techniques unit - Carolina-style dry rub, mop, and vinegar-mustard BBQ sauce system.
- **Source and revision date**: Program recipe (competition/lab-style recipe card). Kept as a separate entry from the North Carolina cookbook pulled pork below - this version oven-roasts at 300°F with a cider-vinegar mop and a dry-mustard-based sauce; the cookbook version smokes low-and-slow over hickory at 325°F with a thicker, sweeter vinegar-pepper-flake sauce. Genuinely different techniques and results, not a minor variance. Standardized 2026-07.
- **Yield and portion size**: 4 portions (sandwiches).
- **Equipment**: Oven, basting brush, 2 forks (for shredding), sauce pan.
- **Ingredients**: **Rub**: 1.5 Tbsp black pepper (fresh ground), 1.5 Tbsp dark brown sugar, 1.5 Tbsp paprika, 2 Tbsp salt, 4.5 tsp cayenne pepper. **Pork and buns**: 2 lb boneless pork shoulder, 4 hamburger buns or kaiser rolls. **Mop**: 1.5 cups apple cider vinegar and water combined (0.5 cup vinegar per source proportions - **needs confirmation**, quantities were unclear in the source table), 1 Tbsp Worcestershire sauce, 0.5 Tbsp black pepper (fresh ground), 0.5 tsp coarse salt, 1 tsp vegetable oil. **Carolina BBQ sauce**: 1 tsp dry mustard, 1 tsp coarse salt, 1 Tbsp sweet Hungarian paprika, 1 Tbsp granulated garlic, 1 Tbsp brown sugar, 0.5 tsp cayenne, 6 oz water, 2 oz Worcestershire sauce, 4 oz red wine vinegar. **Sandwich slaw**: 2 cups shredded green cabbage, 2 Tbsp minced onion, 2 Tbsp cider vinegar, 2 Tbsp mayonnaise, 1 clove garlic (minced), 1 tsp black pepper (fresh ground), salt to taste.
- **Procedure**: **Rub**: combine all ingredients. Sprinkle over the pork shoulder, using all of it and pressing into the crevices; let stand at room temperature 1 hour. **Mop**: combine all ingredients and set aside. **Cooking**: roast the pork at 300°F for 3 hours, brushing with the cold mop every 45 minutes. When fork-tender, remove from the oven and rest 45 minutes. Shred the meat with 2 forks. **Sauce**: combine the dry ingredients in a sauce pan; stir in the water and heat to boiling, then remove from heat. Stir in the Worcestershire sauce and red wine vinegar. Combine the shredded pork with the BBQ sauce; cover tightly and simmer 20-30 minutes, adding more water if it dries out. **Slaw**: mix all ingredients and refrigerate 1 hour. **To serve**: pile the pork on a toasted bun with a heaping spoon of slaw and additional BBQ sauce.
- **Food safety controls**: Standard pork handling; confirm the pork is fork-tender and reaches a safe internal temperature over the 3-hour roast; discard any mop that contacted raw pork if reusing.
- **Allergens**: Gluten (buns), egg (mayonnaise in slaw).
- **Quality indicators**: Fork-tender, easily shredded pork; tangy-sweet mustard-based Carolina sauce fully absorbed into the meat; crisp, tangy slaw as a textural contrast.
- **Holding/storage**: Shredded pork holds and reheats well in its sauce, refrigerated a few days; slaw is best within 1-2 days.
- **Scaling notes**: Scales linearly by pork weight; rub, mop, sauce, and slaw all scale by their own ratios.
- **Skill focus**: Dry-rub-then-mop technique for a long oven roast (keeping the surface moist without a full braise), building a thin, vinegar-forward Carolina-style BBQ sauce distinct from a thick tomato-based sauce, simmering shredded meat directly in its sauce to finish.

## North Carolina Pulled Pork with Pig Picker Pucker Sauce

- **Course/use case**: Culinary 2, Advanced Cooking Techniques unit - true low-and-slow smoked North Carolina barbecue.
- **Source and revision date**: Cookbook excerpt (page scan, OCR-garbled in places; publication not identified in the extracted pages - **needs confirmation** if exact attribution is wanted). Kept as a separate entry from the Carolina Pulled Pork Barbecue Sandwich above - this version smokes the pork low-and-slow over hickory chips on a grill (4-6 hours to 195°F internal) rather than oven-roasting at 300°F, and uses a thin, un-thickened cider vinegar sauce with sliced onion rather than the other entry's cooked mustard-based sauce. Standardized 2026-07.
- **Yield and portion size**: Serves 10-12 (source yield); within the program's target range.
- **Equipment**: Charcoal or gas grill set up for indirect cooking, smoker box or foil packet for wood chips, aluminum foil pan.
- **Ingredients**: **Pork**: 1 Boston butt (5 lb, bone-in pork shoulder roast), 1 Tbsp coarse salt (kosher or sea), 1 Tbsp freshly ground black pepper, 1 Tbsp paprika, 1 recipe Pig Picker Pucker Sauce (below), 6 cups shredded cabbage, 10-12 hamburger buns (split). **Special equipment**: 4 cups hickory chips (soaked in cold water 1 hour). **Pig Picker Pucker Sauce**: 1.5 cups cider vinegar, 1 cup cold water, 2 Tbsp sugar (or to taste), 1 Tbsp hot pepper flakes, 1 small onion (thinly sliced), 1.5 Tbsp coarse salt (kosher or sea, or to taste), 1/2 tsp freshly ground black pepper.
- **Procedure**: **Pork**: Generously season the pork all over with salt, pepper, and paprika. Set up the grill for indirect cooking and preheat to 325°F. Place the pork shoulder, skin side up, on the grate over a drip pan. If using charcoal, toss a quarter of the wood chips on the coals; if using a gas grill, place the chips in the smoker box - don't grill until you see smoke. Cover the grill. Smoke-cook the pork shoulder until fall-off-the-bone tender with an internal temperature of 195°F, 4-6 hours; if using charcoal, add 10 fresh coals and 1 cup wood chips per side every hour. Transfer the cooked pork to a cutting board, tent with foil, and let rest 15 minutes. Pull off and discard any skin. Pull the pork into thin shreds by hand (heavy-duty rubber gloves recommended) or finely chop with a cleaver, discarding bones and fat. Transfer the pork to a large foil pan and stir in 1 to 1.5 cups of the sauce, enough to keep the pork moist; cover with foil and keep warm on the grill. **Slaw**: place the cabbage in a bowl, add 1/2 to 3/4 cup of the remaining sauce, and toss to mix. **Sauce**: combine all ingredients in a bowl and stir until the sugar and salt dissolve; correct the seasoning with more salt if needed; best made fresh rather than ahead. **To serve**: pile the pork and cabbage on the buns, spooning on additional sauce if desired.
- **Food safety controls**: Standard pork handling; confirm the pork reaches 195°F internal and is fall-off-the-bone tender before pulling; monitor smoker temperature to keep it in the low-and-slow range throughout the long cook.
- **Allergens**: Gluten (buns).
- **Quality indicators**: Smoky, tender, easily-pulled pork; thin, sharply tangy vinegar-pepper sauce that soaks into the fine shreds rather than coating them like a thick sauce; crisp cabbage slaw dressed in the same sauce.
- **Holding/storage**: Pulled pork holds warm, covered, on the grill for service; refrigerate and reheat leftovers gently with a splash of extra sauce to re-moisten.
- **Scaling notes**: Source yield of 10-12 servings is within the program's target range; scales by pork weight, though total smoke time (4-6 hours) will not shorten with a bigger cut.
- **Skill focus**: True low-and-slow indirect grilling/smoking technique with wood-chip smoke management, hand-pulling meat into fine shreds (vs. chopping), building a thin, un-thickened vinegar mop-style sauce characteristic of eastern North Carolina barbecue - a genuinely different result from the mustard-based Carolina sauce in the other pulled pork entry in this file.

## Basic BBQ Sauce

- **Course/use case**: Culinary 1/2, Advanced Cooking Techniques unit - quick tomato-vinegar BBQ sauce.
- **Source and revision date**: Program recipe (no external attribution). Standardized 2026-07.
- **Yield and portion size**: Not specified in source. **Needs confirmation** for a target yield.
- **Equipment**: Saucepan.
- **Ingredients**: 1 cup ketchup, 1/4 cup water, 1/4 cup vinegar, 1/4 cup brown sugar, 3 Tbsp olive oil, 2 Tbsp paprika, 1 Tbsp chili powder, 2 cloves garlic (minced), 1 tsp cayenne.
- **Procedure**: Heat the oil in a saucepan. Add the garlic and sauté until browned. Add the remaining ingredients and reduce heat. Simmer 15 minutes until thickened.
- **Food safety controls**: Standard handling.
- **Allergens**: None inherent to this recipe.
- **Quality indicators**: Thickened, glossy consistency (not thin/watery), balanced sweet-tangy-smoky flavor without a raw ketchup taste.
- **Holding/storage**: Refrigerate airtight; holds well for a week or more.
- **Scaling notes**: No yield stated in source - needs confirmation on a target batch size; scales linearly.
- **Skill focus**: Building a quick tomato-based BBQ sauce by sautéing aromatics before adding liquid ingredients, simmering to the correct thickened consistency.
