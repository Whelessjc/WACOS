# Recipe Bank: Beverages

Current as of 2026-07. Built from real recipes and the Barista Station Recipe Sheet already in use. Single-serving drink builds are documented per-drink (standard for a barista program) rather than scaled to a batch, except where a large-format batch (like sweet tea) is the norm.

## Bistro Sweet Tea

- **Course/use case**: Bistro, all-day beverage service.
- **Source and revision date**: Program recipe, already a real batch size.
- **Yield and portion size**: 1 gallon.
- **Equipment**: Large pot, gallon pitcher.
- **Ingredients**: 1 gallon water (divided), 1 large tea bag, 2 cups sugar.
- **Procedure**:
  1. Boil half the water; add the tea bag and remove from heat.
  2. Steep 5 minutes.
  3. Remove the bag; whisk in sugar; pour into a gallon pitcher.
  4. Add the remaining half-gallon as ice water and chill.
- **Food safety controls**: Standard beverage handling; chill promptly after steeping.
- **Allergens**: None.
- **Quality indicators**: Fully dissolved sugar, no over-steeped bitterness, properly chilled before service.
- **Holding/storage**: Refrigerate in the pitcher; hold cold until service.
- **Scaling notes**: Scales linearly by the gallon - straightforward to double for higher-volume service.
- **Skill focus**: Basic ratio work (sugar-to-water), steep timing, batch beverage production.

## Lemonade

- **Course/use case**: Bistro, all-day beverage service.
- **Source and revision date**: Program recipe. Standardized 2026-07 - original was a small single-batch recipe, scaled here to match the sweet tea batch size.
- **Yield and portion size**: Scaled to roughly 1 gallon (4x the original).
- **Equipment**: Saucepan, gallon pitcher.
- **Ingredients (scaled x4)**: 4 cups lemon juice, 4 cups sugar, 4 cups water (for simple syrup), 16 cups cold water.
- **Procedure**:
  1. Boil the sugar and water (simple syrup portion) until sugar dissolves.
  2. Add lemon juice and the remaining cold water.
- **Food safety controls**: Standard beverage handling.
- **Allergens**: None.
- **Quality indicators**: Balanced sweet/tart, no undissolved sugar.
- **Holding/storage**: Refrigerate; hold cold until service.
- **Scaling notes**: Scales linearly. Confirm actual lemon juice source (fresh-squeezed vs. bottled) since that affects both flavor and food-safety handling (fresh juice should be made same-day).
- **Skill focus**: Simple syrup technique, basic ratio work, taste-balancing sweet against acid.

## Espresso-Based Drinks (Barista Station Recipe Sheet)

- **Course/use case**: Bistro, Barista station.
- **Source and revision date**: Program recipe, already production-ready.
- **Yield and portion size**: Per drink, as noted below.
- **Equipment**: Espresso machine, grinder, steaming pitcher, thermometer (or feel-based technique).

### Drip Coffee
Fill the grinder with beans, grind between Extra Fine and Fine, timer setting #12. Pour ground coffee into a filter-lined basket; insert into the machine. Fill the carafe with water; lift the black flap and pour water in. Confirm basket and carafe are in place; press START. Serve black or with cream, half-and-half, milk, and/or sugar.

### Americano (Hot)
2 oz espresso (double shot) + 6-8 oz hot water. Pull the shots, then add hot water for a smooth, rich coffee.

### Espresso
Double (2 oz) = 14 g coffee. Push the left button, programmed for a 2 oz pull.

### Cappuccino
Ratio 1:1:1 - 2 oz espresso, 2 oz steamed milk, 2 oz thick milk foam.

### Latte
More milk than a cappuccino - espresso with mostly steamed milk (about 6 oz) and a thin cap of foam.

### How to Steam Milk (for Latte/Cappuccino)
Pour cold milk into the steaming pitcher (whole milk gives the smoothest texture). Place the steam wand just under the milk's surface; turn on steam. Listen for a tearing-paper sound as microfoam forms. As milk rises and thickens slightly, lower the wand deeper to heat evenly. Stop around 150F (pitcher hot to the touch). Tap and swirl to smooth large bubbles.

### Building a Latte or Cappuccino
1. Pull the espresso (2 oz); set aside.
2. Add sweetener to the cup if requested (3 pumps).
3. Pour espresso into the cup; swirl gently.
4. Steam milk to the amount above for the drink being made.
5. Pour steamed milk over espresso slowly; use a spoon to hold back foam if you want to control it.
6. Top with a layer of foam - thicker for cappuccino, thinner for latte.
7. Optional: cocoa powder, cinnamon, nutmeg, or simple latte art.

### Iced Latte
1. Pull one or two shots of espresso; set aside.
2. Add sweetener to a take-out cup if requested (3 pumps).
3. Pour in the hot espresso; swirl gently to dissolve sweetener.
4. Fill with cold milk to the marked line (add milk before ice so it blends smoothly).
5. Fill with ice to the top, leaving room to avoid overflow.
6. Pour into a tall glass if serving in-house, or seal the lid if to-go.

- **Food safety controls**: Standard dairy handling; discard unused steamed milk per shift-end cleaning.
- **Allergens**: Dairy (unless a non-dairy milk is requested - oat, almond, soy, coconut are stocked per the Barista Team SOP).
- **Quality indicators**: Espresso shot timed 18-22 seconds; milk microfoam smooth and glossy, not large-bubbled; correct ratio for the drink ordered.
- **Holding/storage**: Espresso and steamed milk are made to order, not held.
- **Scaling notes**: These are single-drink builds by design - a barista program scales by volume of orders, not by batch recipe.
- **Skill focus**: Espresso extraction timing, milk steaming/microfoam technique, drink-to-drink ratio consistency.

## Pumpkin Pie Spiced Syrup (1 Quart Batch)

- **Course/use case**: Bistro, Barista station (flavor syrup for lattes, teas, sodas; also usable as a French toast/pancake drizzle).
- **Source and revision date**: Program recipe, already production-ready.
- **Yield and portion size**: About 1 quart (32 fl oz).
- **Equipment**: Medium saucepan, fine mesh sieve, cheesecloth, squeeze bottles or quart containers.
- **Ingredients**: 4 cups water, 4 cups sugar (white or light brown), 0.25 cup pumpkin pie spice blend, 1 Tbsp vanilla extract (optional).
- **Procedure**:
  1. Combine water and sugar in a saucepan; heat over medium, stirring until sugar fully dissolves.
  2. Whisk in pumpkin pie spice; simmer gently 5 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  3. Remove from heat; stir in vanilla if using. Let steep 10-15 minutes.
  4. Strain through a fine mesh sieve lined with cheesecloth for a smooth syrup (for drink use); leave some sediment for a more rustic drizzle use.
  5. Transfer to squeeze bottles or quart containers. Refrigerate up to 2 weeks.
- **Food safety controls**: Standard syrup handling; refrigerate and date.
- **Allergens**: None inherent; confirm spice blend for any cross-contact.
- **Quality indicators**: Fully dissolved sugar, balanced spice level, smooth strain for drink applications.
- **Holding/storage**: Refrigerate up to 2 weeks in a labeled, dated container.
- **Scaling notes**: Scales linearly; strain finer for drink use, coarser for a rustic drizzle.
- **Skill focus**: Simple syrup technique, infusion/steeping, straining for two different end uses from one base.

## Blackberry Black Magic Lemonade (No iSi, Sprite Finish)

- **Course/use case**: Bistro, beverage station (specialty drink).
- **Source and revision date**: Program recipe, already production-ready.
- **Yield and portion size**: 1 quart batch base; built per drink at service.
- **Equipment**: Blender, fine strainer, pitcher.
- **Ingredients (base)**: 1 quart pre-mixed cold lemonade, 1 cup frozen blackberries, a very small pinch of activated charcoal powder, optional 1-2 oz simple syrup if the lemonade is very tart.
- **Procedure**:
  1. Blend lemonade and frozen blackberries briefly - just enough to break the berries and release color, not fully puree.
  2. Strain through a fine strainer into a pitcher to remove seeds and pulp.
  3. Whisk in a tiny pinch of charcoal, adding slowly until the color is deep purple-black (not solid black).
  4. Taste; adjust sweetness if needed.
  5. Store cold in a sealed, labeled, dated container.
  6. **To serve**: fill a clear 16 oz cup with ice, pour 8-10 oz of the base, top with 2-4 oz Sprite to taste.
- **Food safety controls**: Standard beverage handling.
- **Allergens**: None.
- **Quality indicators**: Deep purple-black color (not muddy or solid black from over-adding charcoal), balanced sweetness, visually striking when served.
- **Holding/storage**: Refrigerate the base; build individual drinks to order.
- **Scaling notes**: Base scales linearly by the quart; charcoal amount should be added gradually regardless of batch size - it does not scale linearly the same way (a little goes a long way).
- **Skill focus**: Color control with a small-quantity ingredient (charcoal), balancing tartness, build-to-order beverage service.

## Hot Chocolate

- **Course/use case**: Bistro, Barista/beverage station.
- **Source and revision date**: Program recipe. Standardized 2026-07.
- **Yield and portion size**: About 4 cups (serves 3-4).
- **Equipment**: Small saucepan, whisk.
- **Ingredients**: 2 cups milk (whole or 2%), 2 cups water, 0.5 cup milk powder, 0.25 cup unsweetened cocoa powder, 0.25 cup granulated sugar, 0.5 cup bittersweet or semisweet chocolate chips (or chopped chocolate), 0.25 tsp vanilla extract.
- **Procedure**:
  1. Whisk milk powder into the water until fully dissolved; set aside.
  2. Combine liquid milk, the reconstituted milk powder mixture, cocoa powder, and sugar in a small saucepan.
  3. Heat over medium/medium-low, whisking frequently, until warm (not boiling).
  4. Add chocolate chips; whisk constantly until melted and fully combined.
  5. Stir in vanilla; serve immediately.
- **Food safety controls**: Standard dairy handling; do not boil (can scorch and separate).
- **Allergens**: Dairy.
- **Quality indicators**: Smooth, fully melted chocolate with no graininess, not boiled or scorched.
- **Holding/storage**: Best served immediately; does not hold well long-term without separating.
- **Scaling notes**: Scales linearly; batch ahead of a cold-weather service rush if needed, but expect to whisk again before serving if it sits.
- **Skill focus**: Milk powder reconstitution, gentle heat control to prevent scorching/boiling, melting chocolate into a hot liquid smoothly.

## How to Make Matcha

- **Course/use case**: Bistro, Barista station (menu item: "Matcha with a 1/4 Zip").
- **Source and revision date**: Adapted from Loveandlemons.com; standardized 2026-07.
- **Yield and portion size**: 1 drink.
- **Equipment**: Matcha whisk (or small regular whisk), mug or small bowl.
- **Ingredients**: 0.25 tsp matcha, 2 oz hot water (175F ideal), 6 oz additional hot water or steamed milk (dairy or non-dairy), maple syrup/honey/sweetener (optional).
- **Procedure**:
  1. Sift matcha into a mug or small bowl to remove lumps.
  2. Pour in the 2 oz of hot water. Whisk briskly side to side until fully dispersed with a foamy layer on top.
  3. Add the remaining 6 oz hot water or steamed milk; whisk again until foamy. Sweeten to taste if desired.
- **Food safety controls**: Standard beverage handling; if using steamed milk, follow dairy handling as above.
- **Allergens**: Dairy if steamed milk is used; none if made with water or a non-dairy milk.
- **Quality indicators**: No visible lumps, foamy top layer, vibrant green color (not muddy/brown, which signals over-whisking or low-quality matcha).
- **Holding/storage**: Made to order.
- **Scaling notes**: Single-drink build; no batch scaling needed.
- **Skill focus**: Whisking technique, sifting to prevent lumps, balancing bitterness with sweetener.

## Lemon Ginger Kombucha (with Lemon Purée & Ginger Juice)

- **Course/use case**: Bistro, all-day beverage service - fermentation technique.
- **Source and revision date**: Program recipe. Standardized 2026-07.
- **Yield and portion size**: 1 gallon batch.
- **Equipment**: Large glass fermentation jar, breathable cloth cover and rubber band, airtight bottles.
- **Ingredients**: **Primary fermentation**: 1 gallon filtered water, 8-10 g tea (4-5 tea bags, black or green), 1 cup (200 g) sugar, 2 cups starter liquid (from a previous batch or raw store-bought kombucha), 1 healthy SCOBY. **Secondary fermentation (per gallon)**: 3/4 cup fresh lemon purée (about 4-5 lemons, blended smooth), 1/2 cup fresh ginger juice (about 6-8 inches of ginger, juiced), 2-4 Tbsp sugar (optional, for extra carbonation).
- **Procedure**: **Primary fermentation**: Boil the water and steep the tea 5-7 minutes; remove the tea bags/leaves and stir in the sugar until dissolved. Let the sweet tea cool to room temperature. Pour into a glass jar, add the starter liquid, and gently place the SCOBY on top. Cover with a breathable cloth secured with a rubber band. Ferment in a warm, dark place (ideally 75°F/24°C) for 7-14 days, tasting as needed. **Secondary fermentation**: Remove the SCOBY and reserve 2 cups of kombucha as starter for the next batch. Mix the lemon purée, ginger juice, and sugar (if using) into the kombucha. Pour into airtight bottles, leaving about 1 inch of headspace. Store at room temperature 3-7 days to carbonate, burping the bottles daily to release excess pressure. Once fizzy, refrigerate and serve chilled.
- **Food safety controls**: Maintain a healthy, active SCOBY and clean equipment throughout fermentation to avoid contamination; discard if mold appears. Burp bottles daily during carbonation to prevent pressure buildup.
- **Allergens**: None inherent to this recipe.
- **Quality indicators**: Bright, zesty, slightly spicy flavor with a smooth texture from the purée; properly carbonated (fizzy, not flat or over-pressurized).
- **Holding/storage**: Refrigerate once carbonated; keeps well chilled for continued enjoyment over time.
- **Scaling notes**: Recipe as written is already a full 1-gallon batch; scales by multiplying fermentation vessels rather than one oversized container.
- **Skill focus**: Two-stage fermentation (primary sweet-tea fermentation, then flavored secondary bottle-conditioning), SCOBY handling and starter-liquid maintenance, controlled carbonation via bottle burping. **Adjustments**: reduce lemon purée or add a little honey/sugar if too tart; increase ginger juice slightly if too mild; add a sprig of fresh thyme or rosemary before bottling for extra complexity.
