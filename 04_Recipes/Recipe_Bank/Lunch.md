## Flaky Puff Crust Pizza

- **Course/use case**: Bistro, lunch service; strong CA2 baking/lamination vehicle.
- **Source and revision date**: Adapted from Kingarthurbaking.com; standardized 2026-07. Already a real 16-serving batch.
- **Yield and portion size**: 16 servings (one half-sheet-pan pizza, cut into 16 pieces).
- **Equipment**: 13"x18" pan (half-sheet or a dedicated pizza pan), box grater, rolling pin, parchment.
- **Ingredients**:
  - 4 Tbsp (57 g) cold butter
  - 2.5 cups + 1 Tbsp (300 g) '00' pizza flour, or all-purpose flour
  - 2.25 tsp instant yeast
  - 1.25 tsp (8 g) table salt
  - 0.5 tsp granulated sugar
  - 0.75 cup (170 g) lukewarm water (98-110F)
  - 1 Tbsp (12 g) extra-virgin olive oil
  - 2/3 cup (151 g) pizza sauce
  - 0.25 cup (25 g) grated parmesan, plus more for sprinkling
  - 8 oz (227 g) shredded whole-milk mozzarella
  - 4 oz (114 g) fresh mozzarella, cubed 1/4"
  - 0.25 cup (50 g) extra-virgin olive oil (for garlic-basil oil)
  - 2 heaping Tbsp (10 g) fresh minced basil
  - 0.5-1 tsp lemon juice, to taste
  - 0.5 clove garlic, grated
  - Pinch table salt, pinch red pepper flakes (optional)
- **Procedure**:
  1. Grate cold butter onto parchment in an even layer; freeze at least 30 minutes (lamination fat).
  2. Whisk flour, yeast, salt, and sugar. Add water and oil; mix until a few dry patches remain. Knead briefly to a shaggy dough. Rest 30 minutes at room temperature.
  3. Roll dough to roughly 10"x13". Sprinkle most of the frozen grated butter over the top; fold letter-style (left third to center, right third over). Repeat with remaining butter and one more letter fold.
  4. Continue rolling and resting (15 minutes) until the dough reaches roughly 13"x18", filling the pan.
  5. Let dough rest in the pan 15-30 minutes until visibly puffy at the edges.
  6. Preheat oven to 475F. Spread sauce, then parmesan, then shredded and cubed mozzarella.
  7. Bake 15-17 minutes on the center rack until cheese is melted/browned and dough is golden.
  8. While baking, combine garlic-basil oil ingredients.
  9. Cool 3-5 minutes on a rack; drizzle with garlic-basil oil and a little parmesan before slicing.
- **Food safety controls**: Standard dairy/gluten handling; cool before wrapping leftovers.
- **Allergens**: Gluten, dairy.
- **Quality indicators**: Golden, flaky, laminated crust (visible layers); cheese melted and browned in spots, not burnt.
- **Holding/storage**: Best served fresh. Reheat leftover slices wrapped in foil at 350F until warmed through, about 10 minutes.
- **Scaling notes**: This is a real, tested 16-portion batch on a specific pan size - scaling up requires proportional pan size and may need dividing into multiple half-sheets rather than one oversized batch.
- **Skill focus**: Lamination technique, yeast dough handling, timing a bake around cheese doneness, weight-based scaling.

## Smoked Chicken Mac and Cheese (base: Creamy Multi-Cheese Mac)

- **Course/use case**: Bistro, lunch service.
- **Source and revision date**: Program recipe (Creamy Multi-Cheese Mac base), already production-ready; smoked chicken and scallion finish per the Kitchen Team SOP. Standardized 2026-07.
- **Yield and portion size**: ~15-18 cups finished mac - use 1 full-size hotel pan or 2 standard 9x13 pans (as a side dish; roughly 30 side-dish portions).
- **Equipment**: Large stockpot, whisk, hotel pan or 2x 9x13 pans.
- **Ingredients**:
  - Pasta: 3 lb elbow macaroni or cavatappi (dry weight)
  - Roux base: 1.5 sticks (0.75 cup) unsalted butter; 0.75 cup all-purpose flour; 6 cups whole milk; 3 cups heavy cream; 1.5 Tbsp Dijon mustard; 1.5 tsp garlic powder; 1.5 tsp onion powder; 1 tsp smoked paprika; salt and pepper to taste
  - Cheese blend (shredded): 3 cups sharp white cheddar; 2 cups gruyere; 1 cup mozzarella; 1 cup fontina; 1 cup parmesan or pecorino; optional 6 oz cream cheese
  - Finish: smoked chicken (diced or shredded, quantity per current supply), sliced scallions
- **Procedure**:
  1. Cook pasta just under al dente.
  2. Make a roux with the butter and flour; whisk in milk and cream to build a bechamel. Season with mustard, garlic powder, onion powder, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper.
  3. Melt the cheese blend into the bechamel off heat.
  4. Combine sauce with pasta; fold in smoked chicken.
  5. Pan up in the hotel pan or 9x13s. Cover and bake to reheat, or serve stovetop-style if serving immediately.
  6. Top with sliced scallions at service.
- **Food safety controls**: Chicken must already be fully cooked (smoked) and reheated to 165F if held or refrigerated before combining. Cheese sauce held above 135F for service.
- **Allergens**: Dairy, gluten.
- **Quality indicators**: Smooth, coating sauce consistency (not broken or greasy); pasta al dente, not mushy; even cheese melt.
- **Holding/storage**: Holds reasonably well covered in a hotel pan; stir occasionally to prevent sauce separation. Reheat gently, adding a splash of milk if it's tightened up.
- **Scaling notes**: Already a real ~30-portion batch. Scale the base recipe linearly; scale smoked chicken to taste/supply rather than a fixed ratio.
- **Skill focus**: Roux and bechamel control, pasta timing, sauce holding quality, batch production.

## Shrimp Salad Roll

- **Course/use case**: Bistro, lunch service; also a Culinary 2 knife-skills and cold-station vehicle.
- **Source and revision date**: Program recipe. Standardized 2026-07 - yield was not previously specified; documented here as written pending instructor confirmation of actual batch size used.
- **Yield and portion size**: As written, yields roughly 6-8 sandwich portions (based on 1.5 lb shrimp). Confirm against actual roll count needed and scale accordingly.
- **Equipment**: Hot pan for blackening, mixing bowl, knife and board for brunoise cuts.
- **Ingredients**:
  - 1.5 lb shrimp
  - 4 Tbsp blackening seasoning
  - 0.25 red bell pepper, brunoise
  - 0.25 green bell pepper, brunoise
  - 0.25 red onion, brunoise
  - 1 celery rib, brunoise
  - 0.5 cup mayonnaise
  - Rolls, as needed for portioning
- **Procedure**:
  1. Season the shrimp with blackening seasoning; cook in a hot pan until done.
  2. Cool completely.
  3. Add remaining ingredients (brunoise vegetables, mayonnaise) and mix.
  4. Portion onto rolls for service.
- **Food safety controls**: Shrimp cooked to 145F; cooled to 41F or below before combining with mayonnaise-based dressing; hold cold (41F or below) until service.
- **Allergens**: Shellfish, egg (mayonnaise).
- **Quality indicators**: Shrimp firm and well-seasoned (not overcooked/rubbery), vegetables cut to a consistent fine brunoise, dressing binds without being soupy.
- **Holding/storage**: Cold-hold at 41F or below; discard per standard TCS food time limits if left out.
- **Scaling notes**: Scales linearly per the ratios above. Needs a confirmed target yield - this is a real gap worth closing with the instructor, since the source recipe never stated one.
- **Skill focus**: Blackening technique, brunoise knife cuts, cold-station composition, food safety for a mayonnaise-based cold item.

## Focaccia

- **Course/use case**: Bistro, lunch service (sandwich component - e.g., Chicken + Mozzarella Focaccia); direct tie to Culinary 2 Semester 2 Weeks 22-23 (Yeast Fermentation).
- **Source and revision date**: Program recipe, already production-ready. Standardized 2026-07.
- **Yield and portion size**: One half-sheet pan (with pan extender).
- **Equipment**: Half-sheet pan with pan extender, scale.
- **Ingredients**: 40 g yeast, 28 g sugar, 800 g warm water (100F), 1400 g all-purpose or bread flour, 35 g salt.
- **Procedure**:
  1. Whisk yeast, sugar, and warm water together; let sit 5 minutes to activate.
  2. Add flour; mix until a smooth dough forms.
  3. Rest covered 30 minutes.
  4. Add salt; mix until absorbed and dough is smooth again.
  5. Rest covered 1 hour to rise.
  6. Oil a half-sheet pan with pan extender generously (shallow pool of oil, sides wiped too).
  7. Stretch dough into the pan after the rise, working it to the edges.
  8. Let rise covered in the pan 1 more hour.
  9. Bake at 425F (convection) for 15-20 minutes, or until internal temperature reaches 210F.
- **Food safety controls**: Standard gluten/yeast handling; confirm doneness by internal temperature, not just color.
- **Allergens**: Gluten.
- **Quality indicators**: Golden top, open airy crumb, internal temp of 210F confirmed with a thermometer.
- **Holding/storage**: Cool before slicing for sandwiches; store wrapped at room temperature for same-day use.
- **Scaling notes**: Already a real production-scale batch (1400 g flour fills a half-sheet). Scales by weight if a larger batch is needed. **Alternate hydration noted**: the "Artisan Bread I" board notes (`17 - Advanced Baking & Pastry/artisanBread.boardNotes.pdf`) give a second, higher-hydration plain focaccia formula for a smaller (2.0 lb) batch - 9 g dry yeast, 340 g (12 oz) water, 12 g sugar, 510 g (1 lb 2 oz) flour, 5 g salt - about 67% hydration vs. this entry's 57%, mixed 6 minutes, rounded, given three 20-minute proofs, and baked at 360°F for 20+ minutes. Same plain oil-and-water style dough as this entry (not the herbed Rosemary Milk-Dough Focaccia in `Breads.md`, which is a genuinely different recipe); likely program scale/ratio drift on the same base rather than a separate dish, so folded in here as an alternate hydration rather than a duplicate entry. Use the wetter (67%) version for a more open, airy crumb if preferred.
- **Skill focus**: Yeast activation, two-stage rise timing, oiling technique to prevent sticking, confirming doneness by temperature rather than guessing.

## Smoked Carnitas Tacos (Lunch Version)

- **Course/use case**: Bistro, lunch service.
- **Source and revision date**: Program recipe. Standardized 2026-07.
- **Yield and portion size**: 6 tacos (serves 2-3) as written; scale up for full service.
- **Equipment**: Smoker, oven, grill or saute pan.
- **Ingredients**:
  - Carnitas: 2 lb pork shoulder (chunked), 1 Tbsp salt, 1 tsp black pepper, 1 Tbsp smoked paprika, 1 Tbsp cumin, 1 tsp dried oregano, 3 cloves garlic (minced), 1 small onion (chopped), 1 cup orange juice, 0.5 cup lime juice, 1 cup chicken broth.
  - Tacos: 6 small corn tortillas, 0.5 cup pickled red onions, 0.5 cup chopped cilantro, 0.5 cup crumbled queso fresco, lime wedges, 0.5 cup Roasted Tomatillo Salsa (see Condiments and Sauces).
- **Procedure**:
  1. Season pork with salt, pepper, smoked paprika, cumin, and oregano.
  2. Smoke at 225F for 3-4 hours (hickory or applewood).
  3. Transfer to a pan with garlic, onion, orange juice, lime juice, and chicken broth. Cover and braise in the oven at 300F for 1.5 hours until fork-tender.
  4. Shred; crisp on the grill or in a saute pan over medium-high heat before serving.
  5. Warm tortillas with slight charring; fill with carnitas, top with pickled onions, cilantro, and queso fresco.
  6. Serve with lime wedges and tomatillo salsa.
- **Food safety controls**: Pork cooked/held to safe internal temperature throughout the smoke-then-braise process; verify with a thermometer before shredding.
- **Allergens**: Dairy (queso fresco).
- **Quality indicators**: Fork-tender pork with crisped edges after the final sear, balanced acidity from citrus.
- **Holding/storage**: Shredded pork holds well covered and warm; crisp just before service for best texture.
- **Scaling notes**: Scales linearly by pork weight; smoker capacity is the real constraint on batch size, not the recipe ratios.
- **Skill focus**: Low-and-slow smoking, braising for tenderness, timing a multi-stage cook (smoke, braise, sear) around a single service window.

## Smoked Mac and Cheese

- **Course/use case**: Bistro, lunch service.
- **Source and revision date**: Program recipe. Standardized 2026-07. **Note: this is a second, distinct mac and cheese recipe alongside the Creamy Multi-Cheese Mac base used for Smoked Chicken Mac and Cheese above - confirm with the instructor which is current, or whether both are intentionally kept as different menu executions.**
- **Yield and portion size**: 8 servings.
- **Equipment**: Large pot, whisk, saucepan for roux.
- **Ingredients**:
  - 16 oz raw pasta, cooked in salted boiling water
  - Roux: 6 Tbsp butter, 6 Tbsp flour, 4 cups milk
  - Seasoning: 2 tsp onion powder, 2 tsp MSG, 1 tsp mustard powder, 1 tsp white pepper, 1 tsp salt
  - Cheese: 3 cups smoked shredded cheddar, 1 cup smoked shredded mozzarella
  - Soaked mustard seeds: 1/3 cup mustard seeds, 1/6 cup apple cider vinegar, 1/6 cup water (soak 1-2 hours)
- **Procedure**:
  1. Soak mustard seeds ahead of time (1-2 hours) to allow absorption.
  2. Melt butter, add flour to make a roux; cook to a light brown color.
  3. Add milk slowly over medium-low heat; once combined, raise to medium heat. Add onion powder, MSG, white pepper, salt, mustard powder, and soaked mustard seeds.
  4. Add cheddar first and let it melt before adding mozzarella. Thin with extra milk if too thick.
  5. Combine with cooked pasta.
- **Food safety controls**: Standard dairy handling; hold above 135F for service.
- **Allergens**: Dairy, gluten.
- **Quality indicators**: Smooth coating sauce, visible mustard seed texture, balanced smoke flavor from the cheese.
- **Holding/storage**: Holds covered; stir occasionally and thin with milk if it tightens.
- **Scaling notes**: Scales linearly from an 8-serving base - smaller than the Creamy Multi-Cheese Mac's ~30-serving batch.
- **Skill focus**: Roux and bechamel control, ingredient soaking/prep timing, building smoke flavor through a cheese choice rather than a cooking method.

## The Viral Chopped Italian Sandwich (ILT)

- **Course/use case**: Bistro, lunch service (sandwich).
- **Source and revision date**: Adapted from theninjacue.com; standardized 2026-07.
- **Yield and portion size**: 4 sandwiches.
- **Equipment**: Large cutting board, chef's knife.
- **Ingredients**: 4 hoagie/sub rolls, 2 cups shredded lettuce, 6 slices pepperoni, 6 slices salami, 6 slices ham, 6 slices tomato, 6 slices provolone, 0.25 cup banana peppers, 0.25 cup thinly sliced red onion, 0.25 cup mayonnaise, 3 Tbsp olive oil, 3 Tbsp red wine vinegar, 1 Tbsp dried Italian seasoning, salt and pepper to taste.
- **Procedure**:
  1. Build a base layer of lettuce, then pile on meats, cheese, tomato, banana peppers, and red onion.
  2. Chop everything together on the board until well mixed.
  3. Add mayonnaise, olive oil, vinegar, salt, pepper, and Italian seasoning; chop once more to incorporate.
  4. Pile onto a hoagie roll and serve.
- **Food safety controls**: Standard deli-meat handling; keep cold until assembly.
- **Allergens**: Dairy (cheese), gluten (roll); confirm deli meats for additional allergens.
- **Quality indicators**: Evenly chopped, dressing distributed throughout (not pooling at the bottom), roll not soggy.
- **Holding/storage**: Assemble close to service; the chopped salad component can be held cold briefly, but the sandwich itself doesn't hold well once built.
- **Scaling notes**: Scales linearly per sandwich.
- **Skill focus**: Knife-and-board "chopping" technique as an assembly method (distinct from standard mise en place cuts), dressing-to-filling ratio.

## Savory Martha White Cornbread (Half Sheet Pan)

- **Course/use case**: Bistro, lunch service (side item).
- **Source and revision date**: Program recipe, already production-ready.
- **Yield and portion size**: 30-36 pieces (1 half-sheet pan).
- **Equipment**: Half-sheet pan.
- **Ingredients**: 6 cups corn meal mix, 6 large eggs, 4.5 cups buttermilk, 1 cup vegetable oil, 0.5 cup melted butter, 1 Tbsp shortening (for the pan). Optional: 0.25-0.5 cup scallions, 1 tsp black pepper.
- **Procedure**:
  1. Heat oven to 375-400F.
  2. Melt shortening in the pan.
  3. Mix eggs, buttermilk, oil, and butter.
  4. Add corn meal mix; stir until just combined.
  5. Fold in scallions/pepper if using.
  6. Pour into the pan; bake 20-25 minutes until center is set with moist crumbs.
  7. Brush with melted butter and salt; rest 10 minutes before cutting into 30-36 pieces.
- **Food safety controls**: Standard egg/dairy handling.
- **Allergens**: Egg, dairy, gluten.
- **Quality indicators**: Moist crumb, not dry or crumbly; evenly baked with a set center.
- **Holding/storage**: Store covered at room temperature; best same-day.
- **Scaling notes**: Already a real half-sheet-pan batch.
- **Skill focus**: Batch batter mixing, savory quick-bread technique, add-in distribution (scallions).

## Minestrone Soup

- **Course/use case**: Bistro, lunch service; CA2 soup-structure and simmer-control vehicle (Semester 1 Week 13).
- **Source and revision date**: Adapted from Loveandlemons.com; standardized 2026-07. Original recipe served 4 - rescaled here to a real batch.
- **Yield and portion size**: Scaled to 24 servings (6x).
- **Equipment**: Large stockpot.
- **Ingredients (scaled x6)**:
  - 0.75 cup extra-virgin olive oil
  - 6 medium yellow onions, diced
  - 12 medium carrots, chopped
  - 12 celery ribs, thinly sliced
  - 6 tsp sea salt, plus more to taste
  - Freshly ground black pepper
  - 18 garlic cloves, grated
  - 6 (28 oz) cans diced tomatoes
  - 9 cups cooked white beans or kidney beans, drained and rinsed
  - 6 cups chopped green beans
  - 24 cups vegetable broth
  - 12 bay leaves
  - 6 tsp dried oregano
  - 6 tsp dried thyme
  - 4.5 cups small pasta (elbows, shells, or orecchiette)
  - 3 cups chopped fresh parsley
  - Red pepper flakes, to taste
  - Grated parmesan, for serving (optional)
- **Procedure**:
  1. Heat oil in a large pot; add onion, carrot, celery, salt, and pepper. Cook, stirring occasionally, about 8 minutes until vegetables soften.
  2. Add garlic, tomatoes, beans, green beans, broth, bay leaves, oregano, and thyme. Cover and simmer 20 minutes.
  3. Stir in pasta; cook uncovered 10 more minutes until pasta is cooked through.
  4. Season to taste; serve with parsley, red pepper flakes, and parmesan if desired.
- **Food safety controls**: Bring to a full simmer/boil during cooking; cool in shallow batches before refrigerating if made ahead; reheat to 165F before service.
- **Allergens**: Gluten (pasta); confirm broth for any hidden allergens.
- **Quality indicators**: Vegetables tender but not mushy, pasta cooked through without being overcooked and starchy, well-balanced seasoning.
- **Holding/storage**: If holding for service, keep pasta separate and add just before service to prevent it from over-absorbing broth and going soft. Cool in shallow pans if refrigerating.
- **Scaling notes**: Scales linearly per the ratios above. Consider cooking pasta separately and adding to individual bowls at pickup for a large batch, rather than in the full pot, to protect texture over a long service window.
- **Skill focus**: Soup structure and sequencing, simmer control, batch seasoning, holding-quality decisions for a pasta-based soup.
