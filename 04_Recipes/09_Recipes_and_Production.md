# Recipes and Production

## Purpose
Standardize recipe documentation, scaling, production planning, prep sheets, and kitchen math.

## Key Principle
Recipes are vehicles for skill practice. The curriculum is the skill, habit, and judgment being developed through the recipe.

## Standard Recipe Format
Each recipe should include:
- Name.
- Course/use case: Culinary 1, Culinary 2, Bistro, catering, assessment.
- Source and revision date.
- Yield and portion size.
- Equipment.
- Ingredients with units.
- Procedure.
- Food safety controls.
- Allergens.
- Quality indicators.
- Holding, cooling, storage, reheating.
- Scaling notes.
- Skill focus.

## Production Packet
For Bistro or larger labs, attach:
- Menu item.
- Target yield.
- Prep list.
- Station assignments.
- Production timeline.
- Shopping/inventory notes.
- Labels needed.
- Storage/holding plan.
- Service handoff.
- Cleanup responsibilities.

## Kitchen Math
Students should repeatedly practice:
- Fractions and decimals.
- Weight and volume conversions.
- Recipe scaling factor.
- Yield and portion size.
- Cost per portion where feasible.
- Menu pricing and food cost in Culinary 2.

## Cook-What-You-Cut Model
In Culinary 1, knife practice should become food whenever possible. Examples:
- Carrots become saute, stir fry, soup garnish, or roasted vegetables.
- Potato batonnet becomes fries or roasted potatoes.
- Onion and garlic become the base of simple dishes.
