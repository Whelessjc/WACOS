# Recipe Use Guidelines

Recipes are vehicles for skill practice.

They are not the curriculum.

## Recipe Selection Rules

Use a recipe when it gives students meaningful reps in one or more core habits:

- recipe reading and following
- knife control
- mise en place
- clean-as-you-go
- purposeful movement
- awareness and scanning
- tasting and seasoning
- communication
- follow-through
- composure under pressure

## Good Culinary 2 Recipes

A good Culinary 2 recipe:

- has a clear skill target
- fits the class block
- allows teacher circulation and coaching
- works for groups of 3
- creates visible quality differences
- can be repeated or varied
- supports Bistro when possible
- requires students to make decisions

## Avoid Recipes That

- are mostly novelty
- require too much teacher rescue
- hide the skill target
- take longer than the block can honestly support
- create cleanup chaos without enough learning value
- turn the class into assembly instead of cooking
- depend on expensive ingredients without enough reps

## Time Check

Before using a recipe, ask:

- Can students read and plan it?
- Can they complete the real production in about 60 minutes?
- Can flames be off 15 minutes before the bell?
- Can the kitchen be reset?
- What step is most likely to fail?
- What will I coach during circulation?

## Recipe Marking Routine

Students should mark:

- yield
- oven or cooking temperature
- cooking times
- equipment
- knife cuts
- raw protein or allergen risks
- steps that must happen first
- final quality cues

## Group of 3 Role Fit

Default roles:

- lead cook: tracks method, timing, and final quality
- mise cook: gathers, measures, cuts, labels, and stages
- sanitation/service cook: manages dishes, towels, station reset, and service readiness

For longer labs, rotate roles halfway or assign by task.

## Recipe Debrief

Keep debriefs practical.

Ask:

- What step controlled the outcome?
- What did heat do?
- What did seasoning do?
- Where did timing break down?
- What would you prep earlier next time?
- What would make this service-ready?

## Anchor Recipe Categories

Use these categories across the year:

- sweated onion base
- sauteed vegetables
- rice and grains
- vinaigrettes and emulsified dressings
- pan-roasted chicken
- seared steak
- fried or seared fish
- pan sauce
- mac and cheese
- soups
- braised dishes
- roasted or blanched vegetables
- muffins or quick breads
- focaccia or simple bread
- custard dessert
- ice cream or frozen base

## Recipe Revision Notes

After a lab, write one short note:

- Keep
- Revise
- Replace

Include why:

- timing
- cost
- cleanup
- skill value
- student success
- Bistro usefulness
